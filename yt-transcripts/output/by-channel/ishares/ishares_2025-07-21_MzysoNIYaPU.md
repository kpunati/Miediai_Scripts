# What should I look for before making an investment?

## Summary

Joanna Abukaza argues the most important pre-investment question is suitability — whether an investment fits your goals, time horizon, and risk tolerance. Professional investors apply this same framework: define what you're investing for, when you'll need the money, and your tolerance for volatility. Personal goals matter more than chasing fancy metrics or worrying about quarterly performance — a goals-first frame helps investors ignore the short-term Wall Street noise.

## Key Takeaways

- The first question before any investment is suitability: does it fit YOUR goals?
- Three dimensions define suitability: mission (what for?), time horizon (when needed?), and risk tolerance (how much volatility?).
- Retirement investing looks very different from a 5–10 year home-savings goal.
- Personal goals matter more than fancy financial metrics or quarterly performance comparisons.
- A goals-first frame helps investors stay long-term and ignore short-term Wall Street noise.
