## Summary

Kevin Lee explains crypto investing via ETPs as a simpler alternative to direct purchases. Bitcoin (launched 2009) is by far the largest crypto by market cap; Ether is the second-largest and powers the Ethereum blockchain. Surveys iShares Bitcoin Trust (IBIT) and iShares Ethereum Trust (ETHA) as ways to gain exposure without direct-purchase burdens (storage, security, tax reporting). Cautions that crypto remains highly volatile and warrants small exposure.

## Key Takeaways

- Crypto ETPs avoid the operational burdens of direct crypto purchases (storage, security, tax reporting).
- Bitcoin (launched 2009) remains by far the largest cryptocurrency by market cap.
- Ether is the second-largest cryptocurrency and powers the Ethereum blockchain.
- IBIT and ETHA provide ETP exposure to Bitcoin and Ether respectively.
- Crypto is highly volatile — small exposures may diversify a portfolio but exposure should be sized carefully.

## Detailed Notes

### Crypto landscape and the top two [00:00:01]

Cryptocurrencies are one type of digital asset. Millions exist but most are inactive or trivially capitalized. Bitcoin (launched 2009) is by far the largest cryptocurrency by market cap and serves as a foundational digital asset.

### Ether, Ethereum, and ETPs as an access route [00:00:41]

Ether is the second-largest cryptocurrency, powering the Ethereum blockchain (analogous to an open-source app store). Direct crypto purchases bring storage, security, and tax complexity. ETPs (IBIT for Bitcoin, ETHA for Ether) offer stock-like trading, low costs, and tax efficiency. Small allocations may diversify portfolios; crypto remains highly volatile.
