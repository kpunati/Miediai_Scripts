## Summary

Kyle Axelrod argues derivative ("outcome") ETFs are especially relevant in volatile markets. They use options to target growth, income, or downside protection. Buffer ETFs like MMAX use options for up to 100% downside protection with market participation up to a cap over a one-year period — giving up some upside for downside cushion. Helps investors stay invested through volatility, since the market's best days cluster during volatile periods.

## Key Takeaways

- Derivative (outcome) ETFs use options to target specific goals: growth, income, or downside protection.
- Buffer ETFs like MMAX provide up to 100% downside protection over a one-year period in exchange for capped upside.
- More volatility = more relevance for derivative ETFs.
- The market's best days tend to occur during periods of heightened volatility.
- Missing even a handful of best days can hurt long-term performance.
- Buffer ETFs help investors stay invested when sell-offs would otherwise tempt them out.

## Detailed Notes

### Why derivative ETFs in volatile markets [00:00:01]

Kyle Axelrod argues derivative ETFs (also called outcome ETFs) become more relevant as market volatility rises. They use options to target specific objectives: growth, income, or downside protection. Buffer ETFs like MMAX provide up to 100% downside protection over a one-year period in exchange for participation capped at a defined upside.

### Buffer ETFs and staying invested [00:01:04]

Trying to time exits around sell-offs is extremely difficult; the market's best days historically cluster during periods of heightened volatility. Missing even a handful of those days hurts long-term performance. Buffer ETFs help investors stay invested through short-term volatility without panic exits.
