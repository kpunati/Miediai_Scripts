## Summary

Anna Nerys explains Bitcoin: a digital cryptocurrency running on blockchain, capped at 21 million coins ever, that enables peer-to-peer value transfer without banks. Bitcoin ETPs bridge traditional investing and crypto by holding Bitcoin in a custodied wrapper, sidestepping the need for users to manage private keys themselves. ## Key Takeaways

- Bitcoin is a digital cryptocurrency running on blockchain — peer-to-peer value transfer without banks/governments.
- Bitcoin supply is hard-capped at 21 million coins ever — by design.
- Two pizzas bought for ~$41 in 2010 would be worth over $1 billion today — illustrates Bitcoin's price history.
- Direct Bitcoin ownership requires managing private keys; losing them = losing access permanently.
- Bitcoin ETPs hold Bitcoin via a professional custodian, accessible like a stock.
- iShares Bitcoin Trust ETF (IBIT) is the iShares vehicle for Bitcoin exposure.

## Detailed Notes

### The pizza hook [00:00:00]

Opens with the famous 2010 Bitcoin pizza purchase: ~$41 in BTC for two pizzas. At current Bitcoin value those coins would be worth over $1 billion. Sets up the broader story of Bitcoin as an asset class.

### What Bitcoin is [00:00:50]

Bitcoin = entirely digital cryptocurrency running on blockchain. Lets people send value to each other over the internet without traditional middlemen (banks, governments). Hard-capped at 21 million coins ever — scarcity by design has attracted investor interest.

### Custody risk for direct ownership [00:01:33]

Owning Bitcoin directly means you're responsible for safely storing private keys (essentially the password to move/spend your Bitcoin). Lost, stolen, or mishandled keys = lost access permanently. A known major risk for retail investors.

### Bitcoin ETPs as a bridge [00:02:41]

Bitcoin ETPs hold Bitcoin via a professional custodian and trade like a stock. Investors get price exposure without private-key custody burden. iShares Bitcoin Trust ETF (IBIT) is the iShares vehicle — accessible alongside 450+ other ETFs.
