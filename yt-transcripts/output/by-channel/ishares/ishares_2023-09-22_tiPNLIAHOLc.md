## Summary

Gargi Chaudhuri explains what bonds are — investments paying scheduled interest — and busts the myth that they're only for retirees. After Federal Reserve rate hikes pushed yields higher, bonds serve many investor needs: inflation protection (TIPS ETFs), short-horizon savings (short-term bond ETFs), or stock-portfolio diversification (broad US bond market ETFs). ## Key Takeaways

- A bond is an investment that pays the holder interest on a fixed schedule — hence "fixed income."
- Bonds add diversification: they can work when stocks struggle, helping preserve capital.
- After Fed rate hikes pushed yields higher, bonds now suit investors of any age, not just retirees.
- Bond ETFs cover specific needs: TIPS for inflation protection, short-duration for short horizons, broad-market for stock-portfolio diversification.
- The bond universe is wide — short/long duration, corporate, government, mortgage, inflation-linked — each with distinct characteristics.
- Example: LQD provides exposure to ~1,000 investment-grade corporate bonds in a single ETF.
