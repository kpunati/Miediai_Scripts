# Market Mythbusting with Gargi Chaudhuri: Is it hard to start investing?

**Channel:** iShares by BlackRock
**Published:** 2023-07-25
**URL:** https://youtube.com/watch?v=nFY8QmZT8Xk
**Duration:** 1:40

## Summary

Gargi Chaudhuri addresses whether starting to invest is hard: argues that picking individual winners consistently is nearly impossible, but ETFs do that research and exposure work for investors. Suggests rotating at the margin toward more attractive areas of the market while staying diversified. Notes that thematic investing (sub-sectors, themes, countries) is valid when not over-concentrated.

## Key Takeaways

- Consistently picking individual stock winners is nearly impossible — ETFs do that work for you via index exposure.
- Rotate your portfolio at the margin toward more attractive areas of the market rather than wholesale moves.
- Stay invested for the long term; ETFs make diversified exposure accessible.
- Investing can be aligned with what you care about — themes, sub-sectors, or specific countries you find compelling.
- Even with thematic investing, don't put all eggs in one basket — stay diversified across themes.

## Detailed Notes

### Why picking winners is hard [00:00:19]

Argues consistently picking individual stock winners is nearly impossible. ETFs can do the research and provide diversified index exposure on the investor's behalf. Rotating at the margin toward attractive areas of the market is a reasonable middle ground.

### Thematic investing with diversification [00:01:09]

Notes that investing can reflect personal values or interests — themes, sub-sectors, specific countries. Cautions against over-concentration: even thematic investors should stay diversified rather than betting everything on one trend.
