## Summary

Sam Mullen argues AI stocks are NOT in a bubble. Three reasons: (1) real demand for AI services exceeds compute supply, persisting for years; (2) AI capex is funded by cash flows from highly profitable companies, not borrowed money; (3) valuations are reasonable vs the dot-com peak — today's four largest AI hyperscalers (Microsoft, Alphabet, Amazon, Meta) trade at ~24x forward earnings vs 88x for Microsoft/Cisco/Intel/Oracle at the dot-com peak. Positions BAI and ARTY for exposure.

## Key Takeaways

- iShares argues AI is NOT a bubble, citing three reasons rooted in fundamentals.
- AI demand exceeds compute supply — the imbalance is expected to persist for years.
- AI capex is largely funded by corporate cash flows, not debt.
- Today's hyperscaler valuations (~24x forward earnings) are far below dot-com peak (~88x).
- BAI (active) and ARTY (broad index) are positioned as AI-exposure vehicles for selective vs broad investors.
