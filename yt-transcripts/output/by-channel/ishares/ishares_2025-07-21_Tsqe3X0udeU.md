## Summary

Caroline Bias addresses defensive investing in volatile markets, noting there's no single "best" defensive ETF since goals and risk tolerance differ. Surveys three iShares ETFs for different defensive needs: IAU (gold for macro/inflation hedge), BINC (active flexible-income for hard-to-reach yield), and BALI (large-cap with covered-call income for lower volatility than the broader market). Closes with the standard advice to stay invested rather than time rebounds.

## Key Takeaways

- There's no single "best" defensive ETF — goals and risk tolerance differ.
- IAU offers cost-effective gold exposure as a macro/inflation hedge.
- BINC seeks income in harder-to-reach segments of the bond market for higher yields.
- BALI uses covered-call option strategies to dampen equity volatility while generating income.
- Staying invested through volatility avoids missing rebounds that often come quickly.
