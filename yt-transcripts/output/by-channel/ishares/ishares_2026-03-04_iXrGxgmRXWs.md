## Summary

Anna Nerys defines long-term investing as active and consistent — regular contributions sustained through market ups and downs. Uses a gym/workout analogy. Shows the difference between a savings account (avg ~0.4%) vs S&P 500 (avg ~11% over last two decades) over 30 years with $100/month: huge gap thanks to compounding. Argues diversification (like working every muscle group) is the foundation of any long-term plan and iShares ETFs make it simple.

## Key Takeaways

- Long-term investing requires active consistency — regular contributions through ups and downs.
- Time in the market beats timing it; compounding is the secret ingredient.
- Compounding example: a penny doubling daily for 30 days reaches over $5 million.
- 30-year compounding gap: $100/mo in a savings account (~0.4%) vs S&P 500 (~11%/yr) shows dramatic difference.
- Diversification is the long-term investor's "working every muscle group" rule — no skipping leg day.
- iShares 450+ ETFs make broad diversification accessible in single purchases.

Long-term investing is active: regular contributions sustained through market ups and downs. Historically, the longer you stay with a diversified plan, the higher the likelihood of positive returns. Gym analogy: stick to the plan whether market is up, down, raining, or snowy.

Savings Anna puts $100/month in a savings account averaging 0.4%. Investing Anna puts the same $100/month in a diversified S&P 500-tracking fund averaging ~11%/yr. Over 30 years the difference is dramatic — past performance disclaimer applies but the math is consistent.
