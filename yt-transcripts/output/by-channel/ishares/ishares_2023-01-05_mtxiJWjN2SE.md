# Thematic investing with ETFs

**Channel:** iShares by BlackRock
**Published:** 2023-01-05
**URL:** https://youtube.com/watch?v=mtxiJWjN2SE
**Duration:** 19:43

## Summary

iShares presentation on thematic/megatrend investing. Single stocks (eToys vs Amazon) are too risky; broad indexes (NASDAQ Composite ~6x in 20 years) are too diffuse; theme-specific ETFs are the Goldilocks middle (MSCI e-commerce benchmark ~27x). Surveys five BlackRock megatrends: technological breakthroughs, demographics/genomics, rapid urbanization, climate transition, emerging global wealth. Lists iShares megatrend ETFs: IHAK, IRBO, IDNA, IFRA, ICLN, IDRV, CNYA, and XT for one-trade access.

## Key Takeaways

- Megatrends are long-term structural forces — different from cyclical themes, timing matters less.
- Tech adoption is accelerating: phone 35yr, PC 16yr, web 7yr, Facebook 4yr, TikTok 2yr to mainstream.
- Single-stock theme bets are high variance — Amazon (51x) vs eToys (bankruptcy) shows the risk.
- Thematic ETFs are the Goldilocks middle — e-commerce-specific index gave ~27x vs NASDAQ Composite's ~6x over the same period.
- Five BlackRock megatrends: tech breakthroughs, demographics, urbanization, climate transition, emerging wealth.
- iShares megatrend ETFs: IHAK (cyber), IRBO (robotics/AI), IDNA (genomics), IFRA (infrastructure), ICLN (clean energy), IDRV (EVs), CNYA (China A-shares), XT (one-fund access to all five megatrends).
- Three rules: wait-for-tomorrow (innovators, not incumbents), connect-the-value-chain (Tesla AND Valeo, Albemarle, ABB), think-beyond-borders.

## Detailed Notes

### Accelerating tech adoption [00:00:03]

Technological adoption is compressing: telephone took 35 years to mainstream, PC 16 years, web 7 years, Facebook 4 years, TikTok only 2 years to reach 500M users. Long-term structural opportunities now play out in shorter windows.

### Structural vs cyclical themes [00:01:06]

Structural themes (aging populations, EM middle-class consumer, tech adoption) play out over 5–20 years and feel inevitable — timing matters less. Cyclical themes are short-term, driven by rates or policy — timing matters a lot.

### The Goldilocks problem [00:02:09]

Single-stock picking too narrow; broad indexes too diffuse. Theme-targeted ETFs are the middle ground.

### eToys vs Amazon: single-stock risk [00:03:11]

Both rode e-commerce: eToys (online toys) went bankrupt; Amazon delivered ~51x return over 20 years. Picking the right single theme stock is hard even when the theme is right.

### NASDAQ Composite as too-broad alternative [00:04:15]

Over the same 20 years the NASDAQ Composite returned ~6x — a 5x improvement, but a far cry from 51x. Broad tech captures e-commerce only indirectly.

### MSCI e-commerce benchmark as middle ground [00:05:17]

An e-commerce-targeted index delivered ~27x over the same period — most of Amazon's upside without eToys-style single-stock risk.

### Trends are intuitive [00:06:22]

The question isn't picking the next FANG-M, it's spotting the trend. Self-driving cars, AI, clean energy — these are intuitive and relatable.

### Five BlackRock megatrends [00:07:25]

(1) Technological breakthroughs (cyber, AI). (2) Demographics (aging, genomics, immunology). (3) Rapid urbanization (infrastructure). (4) Climate change (EVs, clean energy). (5) Emerging global wealth (EM consumer).

### Smartphone vs dessert/alcohol survey [00:08:30]

~50% of Americans would give up dessert for a week before their smartphone; 74% would give up alcohol. The smartphone has become essential in a short time — illustrates connectivity as a megatrend.

### Connectivity by the numbers [00:09:33]

Internet-connected devices: 35B at start of 2021, projected to 75B by 2025 — more than doubling. Drives opportunities in AI and cybersecurity.

### Mega Rule 1 — wait for tomorrow [00:10:36]

2001 S&P 500 top five: GE, Microsoft, ExxonMobil, Walmart, Citigroup. 2021: Microsoft remains; Apple, Amazon, Facebook rose to top. iShares megatrend ETFs target tomorrow's leaders by focusing on small/mid-cap innovators.

### Demographics and genomics [00:11:39]

Aging populations: by next decade, more US grandparents than grandchildren for the first time. Cost to sequence a human genome: $2.7B in 2003, less than $1,000 today. Enables personalized medicine and mRNA vaccines.

### Cancer therapy via immunology [00:12:43]

Precision medicine and immunology advancing — extended lives via lung, renal, brain, and skin cancer survival improvements.

### Rapid urbanization and infrastructure [00:13:45]

World Bank estimates $100T of infrastructure spending over the next 20 years. Cities like Delhi and Dhaka adding ~9M citizens each in 10 years; Shanghai +7M; Cairo +5M. Developed markets also need infrastructure revitalization.

### Climate change and Mega Rule 2 — value chain [00:14:49]

Electric vehicles need more than Tesla: Valeo (software/sensors), Albemarle (lithium), ABB (charging networks). iShares megatrend ETFs invest across the whole value chain.

### Emerging global wealth [00:15:52]

China mints two billionaires a week. China and India may each have 1.5B people by 2026. 90% of new middle-class entrants are in Asia. 44 people escape extreme poverty in India per minute. Drives consumption and innovation.

### Mega Rule 3 — think beyond borders [00:16:54]

China and India have 4x as many internet users as the US combined — but only ~50% of China's population is online, and India has even more runway. Megatrend ETFs must include emerging-market exposure.

### The iShares megatrend ETF lineup [00:17:56]

IHAK (cybersecurity), IRBO (robotics/AI), IDNA (genomics/immunology), IFRA (US infrastructure), ICLN (clean energy), IDRV (EVs/autonomous), CNYA (China A-shares), XT (exponential tech — single-fund access to all five megatrends).

### Outro [00:18:58]

Megatrend ETFs as a way to reimagine portfolios for the future. Standard prospectus disclaimer.
