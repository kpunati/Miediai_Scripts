# 2026 Market Outlook: Seven Key Questions

**Channel:** iShares by BlackRock
**Published:** 2026-01-29

**Duration:** 1:50

## Summary

Q&A with Gargi Chaudhuri on iShares' 2026 market outlook. Defines 2026 as: above-trend growth, easing policy, accelerating productivity, and a need for selectivity. AI remains dominant — $500B+ was spent on data centers in 2025, with trillions more expected through 2030. Concentration is the key risk: the largest AI-driven companies now exceed 40% of the S&P 500, so diversification matters more than ever. Argues for broader income sourcing and diversification beyond traditional stocks/bonds.

## Key Takeaways

- 2026 outlook: above-trend growth, easing policy, accelerating productivity — but selectivity required.
- AI remains a dominant theme; $500B+ spent on data centers in 2025, trillions more expected through 2030.
- Concentration risk: top AI-driven companies now exceed 40% of the S&P 500.
- As rates fall, cash will deliver less income — investors need to source income across the portfolio.
- Traditional stock/bond diversification may not be enough — look beyond.
- Improving fundamentals across US equities open opportunities beyond pure AI.

## Detailed Notes

### 2026 outlook in one sentence [00:00:00]

Gargi Chaudhuri's 2026 framing: above-trend growth, easing policy, accelerating productivity, and a need to be more selective. Equities favored but selectively given rich valuations, labor market risks, and rate uncertainty.

### AI dominance and concentration risk [00:00:33]

AI is still in early stages of data-center buildout: over $500B spent in 2025, trillions more expected through 2030. But the largest AI-driven companies now make up over 40% of the S&P 500 — concentration is the headline risk. Broader US equity fundamentals are improving, opening opportunities beyond AI.

### Biggest challenges: income and diversification [00:01:35]

Two big challenges for investors in 2026: (1) as rates fall, cash income shrinks — income must be sourced across the portfolio; (2) AI-driven concentration means portfolios may be less diversified than they appear. Argues for non-traditional diversification beyond stocks and bonds.
