# Income investing with ETFs

**Channel:** iShares by BlackRock
**Published:** 2023-01-05
**URL:** https://youtube.com/watch?v=UHu0-vASYUI
**Duration:** 24:11

## Summary

iShares presentation on income investing with ETFs in a high-inflation environment. CPI ran ~8% YoY (fastest in 40 years; long-run US avg ~2% since 1871) while Fed funds rate was just lifting off zero. Walks through income tools — bonds (interest-rate and credit risks), dividend-paying stocks, and dividend Growers that historically outperformed through inflation. Surveys iShares income ETFs: USIG, USHY, GOVT, TIP, STIP, HDV, and IYLD multi-asset.

## Key Takeaways

- Inflation eats purchasing power — CPI ~8% (fastest in 40 years) vs long-run US average of ~2% since 1871.
- 5-year CD nominal returns are typically far below inflation — preserving capital but losing real value.
- Bonds have two key risks: interest-rate risk (longer duration = bigger price swing) and credit risk (default).
- S&P credit ratings: AAA to D. Investment grade = BBB or higher; below BBB = high-yield (a.k.a. junk).
- Bond ETFs by category: USIG (investment-grade corp), USHY (high-yield corp), GOVT (Treasuries), TIP/STIP (inflation-protected).
- Dividend Growers outperformed in bear markets (~+8% vs broader stock market) and across full cycles.
- Yield trap: a rising dividend yield can mean dividend strength OR a falling stock price signaling distress.
- HDV screens for dividend payers with quality/moat characteristics; IYLD is a multi-asset income ETF that includes stocks, bonds, REITs, and preferreds.

## Detailed Notes

### Inflation as the income hurdle [00:00:03]

Inflation eats purchasing power: a movie ticket cost ~50¢ in 1950, now over $9. CPI rose ~8% YoY recently — fastest in 40 years; long-run US average is ~2% since 1871.

### Fed funds and Treasury yields [00:01:06]

The Fed funds rate had been near zero in 2020 and only recently lifted to 0.25-0.5%. The 2-year Treasury yield moves with Fed funds — bond yields broadly follow.

### Why bonds still matter despite low yields [00:02:00]

Even with low nominal yields, bonds add diversification. They behave differently from stocks across economic cycles.

### Tech-bubble diversification example [00:03:02]

Tech bubble: S&P 500 fell more than 40% while long-term US Treasury bonds rose about the same amount. Treasuries delivered positive returns in most periods over 20 years where stocks fell ≥15%, including the global financial crisis and the early-2020 COVID sell-off.

### Income tools landscape [00:04:06]

Spectrum from savings/CDs (short-term, low risk, low yield) to bonds (fixed income, moderate risk/yield) to dividend-paying stocks (higher risk, capital appreciation, potentially higher income).

### Bond risks I: interest-rate risk [00:05:02]

Duration measures interest-rate sensitivity. Longer maturity bond = higher duration = bigger price movement when rates change. Locking up money longer means accepting higher interest-rate risk.

### Bond risks II: credit risk [00:06:04]

Credit risk = the borrower won't pay back. US government has high credit quality (can raise taxes). Companies vary — rating agencies (S&P) score AAA to D. BBB+ = investment grade; below = high-yield (junk).

### Interest-rate risk in 2018 vs 2020 [00:07:07]

2018 Fed hikes (3x): short-term Treasury index flat; intermediate lost ~1%; long-term lost more than 4%. 2020 Fed cut to zero: short-term up ~3%; long-term up ~17%. Duration cuts both ways.

### Credit risk in 2020 COVID [00:08:10]

COVID flight-to-quality Q1 2020: Treasury index +8%; investment-grade corp -4%; high-yield corp -13%. Then risk-on rally Q2-Q4 2020: high-yield +23% while Treasuries flat.

### Dividend stocks in the income mix [00:09:12]

Over the last 30 years for a 60/40 portfolio, bond coupons dominated income early on. As rates fell, stock dividends became the larger share. Unlike bond coupons, dividends can grow over time — helping keep pace with inflation.

### Dividend Growers historically [00:10:14]

Stocks classified by dividend policy: dividend Growers outperformed broader market in bear markets (~+8% above) and trailed slightly in bull markets but won overall. Cutters underperformed sharply.

### Dividend strategies in inflation [00:11:16]

During high inflation, dividend Growers outpaced peers — suggests dividend-growth strategies may add inflation-resilient income.

### Yield trap warning [00:12:19]

Two stocks at $10 with $0.50 dividends both yield 5%. If A's price rises to $11 on good news (yield drops to 4.5%) while B's price falls to $9 (yield rises to 5.5%), naive yield ranking would prefer B — but B may be the company in trouble. Add quality screens.

### ETFs as income tools [00:13:23]

ETFs offer diversification, low cost, and tax efficiency. Investment-grade bond ETFs like USIG (9,000+ bonds) provide broad core exposure.

### High-yield and Treasury ETFs [00:14:25]

USHY holds 2,000+ high-yield corporate bonds. GOVT holds Treasuries across maturities with low correlation to other bonds. iShares slices duration into short/intermediate/long for different risk profiles.

### Inflation-protected with TIPS [00:15:27]

TIPS pay a fixed coupon on a principal that adjusts with inflation. TIP holds TIPS of various maturities; STIP holds short-duration TIPS (under 5 years). Distributions rise with monthly inflation adjustments.

### Dividend ETFs and quality screens [00:16:30]

HDV screens for US stocks with economic moats — companies likely to fend off competition and sustain dividends. Holds top 75 dividend yields meeting quality criteria, rebalanced quarterly.

### International dividends [00:17:32]

International stocks have historically paid higher dividend yields than US peers. Many international dividend payers are household names (e.g., Nestlé). Diversification benefit plus yield enhancement.

### Multi-asset income ETFs [00:18:36]

IYLD provides a one-fund multi-asset income approach: stocks, bonds, REITs, preferred stocks. Rebalanced over time. For investors who want a turnkey income portfolio in one purchase.

### Recap and outro [00:19:39]

Inflation poses real challenges, but income-generating ETFs across bonds, dividend stocks, and multi-asset approaches can help. Higher income = higher risk — evaluate interest-rate, credit, and quality dimensions. Stick with the plan over time.
