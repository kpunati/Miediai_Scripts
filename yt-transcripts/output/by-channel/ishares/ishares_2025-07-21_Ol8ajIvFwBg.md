## Summary

Anna Nerys explains digital assets as anything in the digital world with monetary value — covering data, intellectual property, and cryptocurrencies. Distinguishes Bitcoin (the largest cryptocurrency by market value, using cryptography, peer-to-peer networks, and a public blockchain to verify transactions without intermediaries) from Ethereum (a technology platform enabling decentralized applications, with Ether as its native cryptocurrency).

## Key Takeaways

- Digital assets are anything in the digital world with monetary value — including data, IP, and cryptocurrencies.
- Bitcoin is the largest cryptocurrency by market value and operates as an emerging global monetary alternative.
- Bitcoin uses cryptography, peer-to-peer networks, and a public blockchain — verifying transactions without banks, governments, or other intermediaries.
- Ethereum is a technology platform enabling decentralized applications (e.g., peer-to-peer financial networks).
- Ether is the cryptocurrency that powers the Ethereum blockchain — distinct from Bitcoin.

## Detailed Notes

### Defining digital assets and Bitcoin [00:00:00]

Digital assets is an umbrella term for anything in the digital world with monetary value: data, intellectual property, and cryptocurrencies. ### Ethereum and Ether [00:01:03]

Ethereum is a technology platform for decentralized applications (e.g., peer-to-peer financial networks). Ether is the cryptocurrency used on the Ethereum blockchain. Frames Ethereum as analogous to an app store for decentralized apps.
