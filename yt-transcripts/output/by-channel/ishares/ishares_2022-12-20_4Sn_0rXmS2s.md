# Market Mythbusting with Gargi Chaudhuri: Is Investing Expensive?

**Channel:** iShares by BlackRock
**Published:** 2022-12-20
**URL:** https://youtube.com/watch?v=4Sn_0rXmS2s
**Duration:** 1:35

## Summary

Gargi Chaudhuri argues investing isn't expensive — you can start with whatever you can comfortably afford, since ETFs provide diversified exposure at low cost. Cautions against frequent buy/sell churn (eats into returns via the difficulty of market timing) and positions ETFs as both low-fee and tax-efficient, making them well-suited to long-term investors.

## Key Takeaways

- You don't need a lot of money to start investing — start with what's affordable, then keep at it consistently.
- Frequent buy/sell churn erodes returns because timing the market correctly every time is nearly impossible.
- ETFs offer diversified exposure at low cost, which suits beginners with limited capital.
- ETFs are typically more tax-efficient than other vehicles, helping long-term investors keep more of what they earn.
- Stay invested through the cycles rather than trying to time entries and exits.

## Detailed Notes

### The case for starting small [00:00:18]

Argues that beginners can start with whatever they can comfortably afford — there's no minimum that makes investing worthwhile. ETFs provide diversified exposure at a low cost, which suits modest starting balances. Cautions against frequent churn: in-and-out trading eats into returns over time.

### ETFs and tax efficiency [00:00:55]

Notes that market timing is consistently hard to do well. Positions ETFs as both low-fee and tax-efficient compared to alternative vehicles — a combination especially valuable for long-term investors who let returns compound.
