# How to build a DIY portfolio with ETFs

**Channel:** iShares by BlackRock
**Published:** 2023-01-05
**URL:** https://youtube.com/watch?v=eWXmrjDqifo
**Duration:** 18:07

## Summary

This iShares Investment Insights episode walks DIY investors through ETF portfolio construction. Covers starting early (compounding), the case against market timing, diversification across stocks and bonds, asset allocation (e.g., the 60/40 mix), rebalancing, and a survey of ETF categories — core, factor, thematic, and active. Closes with four criteria for evaluating any ETF.

## Key Takeaways

- Time in the market beats timing the market: a 30-year hypothetical shows investor A (contributing $1K/yr for 15 years then stopping) outperforms investor B (3x larger contributions but starting 15 years later).
- Missing the 5 best market days over 20 years would have cost ~1/3 of portfolio value; missing 25 best days leaves you barely above your starting amount.
- Diversification across stocks reduces single-stock risk: in 2021, 36% of individual US stocks lost money even as the market rose 25%, but only 2% of US-stock funds lost money.
- Cross-asset diversification buffers downturns: 10-year US Treasuries have delivered positive returns in every period over 20 years where stocks fell 15%+.
- Asset allocation should match your goals, timeline, and risk tolerance; rebalancing keeps the allocation on target as markets move.
- ETF categories cover different needs: core (broad-market), factor (value/quality/momentum/size/min-vol), thematic (clean energy, cybersecurity), active, and asset-allocation (all-in-one).
- Four criteria for evaluating any ETF: fund-manager experience, fund structure, exposure (what it actually holds), and cost (including expense ratio, trading costs, and tax efficiency).

## Detailed Notes

### Time advantage and compounding [00:00:04]

Opens with a 30-year hypothetical: investor A puts in $1K/yr for 15 years and stops; investor B starts 15 years later with $3K/yr. Despite investing 3x more, B ends up ~$37K behind because A's earlier dollars compounded longer.

### Personal anecdote and Einstein quote [00:01:06]

Host's father opened a brokerage account when host was 14. Cites the apocryphal Einstein attribution: 'compound interest is the eighth wonder of the world.' Sets up why starting sooner beats waiting.

### The case against market timing [00:02:08]

Missing the 5 best market days over 20 years would have cost ~1/3 of portfolio value. Missing 25 best days = barely above starting amount. Many of the best days came within weeks of crises — the scariest moments to invest.

### Diversification — the 'one free lunch' [00:03:11]

Harry Markowitz: diversification is the only free lunch in investing. Spreading risk across companies, sectors, countries, and styles via mutual funds and ETFs.

### Why single-stock diversification matters [00:04:15]

2021 paradox: market up 25%+, but 36% of individual stocks lost money (average decliner: -35%). Only 2% of US-stock mutual funds and ETFs lost money that year — diversification did the work.

### Cross-asset diversification with bonds [00:05:18]

High-quality bonds (especially US Treasuries) historically rise when the economy slows. Tech bubble: S&P 500 -40% while long-term Treasuries +40%. 10-year Treasuries have delivered positive returns in every 20-year period where stocks fell ≥15%.

### Asset allocation and the 60/40 [00:05:58]

Asset allocation = how money is spread across asset classes. 60/40 (60% stocks, 40% bonds) is a common starting point. Mix should reflect goals, timeline, and risk tolerance.

### Rebalancing [00:07:02]

Allocations drift as markets move. In 2020 a 60/40 portfolio drifted to ~50/50 during the Feb-Mar sell-off; rebalancing back to 60/40 would have boosted participation in the rebound.

### Asset-allocation ETFs (all-in-one) [00:08:03]

iShares Core Allocation ETFs are diversified portfolios in a single fund, with conservative/moderate/growth/aggressive risk targets. Auto-rebalanced twice a year. Sustainable variants incorporate ESG criteria.

### Core ETFs (broad market) [00:09:06]

Individual core ETFs (e.g., S&P 500 trackers) offer total-market or market-segment exposure at the lowest cost. Building blocks for custom allocations.

### Factor ETFs [00:10:08]

Factor strategies screen for specific stock attributes: value (cheap stocks), quality (healthy balance sheets), momentum (rising prices), size (small-cap), and minimum volatility (lower risk).

### Min-vol and factor use cases [00:11:11]

Min-vol ETFs help risk-averse investors stay invested in stocks without full market volatility. Factor ETFs deliver traditionally active-management strategies in a transparent, low-cost, tax-efficient wrapper.

### Thematic / megatrend ETFs [00:12:15]

Thematic ETFs bundle companies that fit a trend (clean energy, cybersecurity, etc.). Diversifies the bet on a trend without picking the single winning stock.

### Four criteria for evaluating any ETF [00:13:17]

Manager (experience in the specific market), structure (does the objective fit your goals?), exposure (what asset classes/markets does it hold?), cost. The four-question framework runs through the rest of the episode.

### Stock vs bond fund exposure details [00:14:21]

For stock funds: company size, value vs growth, sectors (tech vs financials), developed vs emerging markets. For bond funds: credit quality, sector (government vs corporate), duration (interest-rate sensitivity).

### Alternatives, active, leveraged/inverse [00:15:23]

Beyond index ETFs: commodities ETFs (gold, silver), actively managed ETFs, and leveraged/inverse ETPs (which iShares does not offer; for sophisticated short-term institutional use only).

### Evaluating cost beyond the expense ratio [00:16:26]

Trading costs (bid-ask spread, volume), distribution frequency (capital gains tax exposure), tracking difference vs the benchmark. Holistic cost view, not just the headline expense ratio.

### Wrap-up [00:17:28]

Recap: compounding, diversification, asset allocation, rebalancing. Evaluate any ETF on manager/structure/exposure/cost.
