# ETFs: An Innovation of Index Investing

**Channel:** iShares by BlackRock
**Published:** 2023-01-05
**URL:** https://youtube.com/watch?v=2DVamz9_nBQ
**Duration:** 13:55

## Summary

This iShares Investment Insights episode introduces what an index is — a rules-based measurement of a group of securities — and explains how index ETFs make broad market exposure accessible with a single purchase. Walks through major indexes (Dow Jones, S&P 500, NASDAQ 100, MSCI EAFE, Bloomberg US Aggregate Bond) and surveys iShares ETFs across size, asset class, dividend, and thematic strategies.

## Key Takeaways

- An index is a rules-based measurement that tracks the performance of a defined group of securities.
- Indexes differ in inclusion rules and weighting: the Dow Jones is price-weighted with 30 stocks; the S&P 500 is market-cap-weighted with 500 stocks; MSCI EAFE covers developed regions outside North America.
- Index ETFs let an investor own all the constituents of an index with a single trade — solving the complexity of buying and rebalancing hundreds of individual stocks.
- ETFs offer three core benefits: diversification, low cost, and tax efficiency (fewer capital gains distributions than typical mutual funds).
- Global ETF assets crossed $10 trillion by end of 2021, with about $5.4 trillion in US ETFs.
- Beyond broad-market indexes, ETFs now target specific attributes (dividend growers, factor screens) and themes (clean energy, autonomous vehicles).
- iShares offers ETFs across these strategies: IVV (S&P 500), IJR (S&P Small-Cap), AGG (US bonds), HYG (high-yield), DGRO (dividend growth), HDV (high dividend), IDRV (self-driving/EV), ICLN (clean energy).

## Detailed Notes

### What an index is [00:00:05]

Defines an index as a rules-based measurement of a group of securities. Used to benchmark performance, track market segments, and define investment strategies.

### How index recipes differ [00:01:09]

Indexes have rules for inclusion (e.g., US-incorporated, above a size threshold) and weighting (e.g., by price or by market cap). Different recipes produce different exposures even from similar ingredients.

### Common stock indexes [00:02:13]

The Dow Jones Industrial Average (30 large-cap US stocks, price-weighted). The S&P 500 (500 large US stocks, market-cap-weighted). The NASDAQ 100 (US tech-focused).

### International and bond indexes [00:02:58]

MSCI EAFE covers large/mid-cap companies in developed regions outside North America (Europe, Australasia, Japan). The Bloomberg US Aggregate Bond index (AGG) covers US investment-grade bonds. Mentions thematic indexes like the NYSE Global Autonomous Driving and Electric Vehicle index.

### Index investing as a strategy [00:04:02]

Index investing means replicating an index's holdings rather than picking individual securities. BlackRock's predecessor firm created the first investable index ~50 years ago, enabling exposure to an entire index in a single purchase.

### ETFs vs mutual funds [00:05:04]

ETFs and mutual funds both offer professional management and diversification, but ETFs trade intraday and most are passively managed. IVV (iShares Core S&P 500 ETF) replicates the S&P 500 at low cost.

### Three core ETF benefits [00:06:06]

Diversification (hundreds of securities per fund), low cost (less revenue dragged from returns), and tax efficiency (ETFs distribute capital gains less often than mutual funds). Global ETF assets crossed $10T by end of 2021, ~$5.4T in US ETFs.

### Stock ETFs by company size [00:07:10]

Large-cap exposure via IVV (iShares Core S&P 500). Small-cap via IJR (iShares Core S&P Small-Cap 600). Small caps are riskier but historically offer more growth potential.

### Bond ETFs [00:08:14]

Bond indexes are typically built by credit risk rather than issuer size. AGG covers investment-grade bonds (US government and high-grade corporate). HYG covers high-yield (junk) bonds with higher default risk and higher yields.

### Dividend ETFs [00:09:17]

DGRO (iShares Core Dividend Growth ETF) screens for US stocks with multi-year dividend-growth records. HDV (iShares Core High Dividend ETF) screens for companies with sustainable competitive advantages (Coca-Cola cited as an example).

### Thematic / megatrend ETFs [00:10:20]

IDRV (autonomous driving and EVs) holds not just Tesla but adjacent companies: ABB (charging stations), LG Chem (batteries), Albemarle (lithium). ICLN tracks clean energy infrastructure beyond pure providers.

### Wrap-up [00:11:22]

Recap of indexing as a style of investing, ETFs as the most accessible vehicle, and how ETF evolution now covers asset classes, attributes, and themes.
