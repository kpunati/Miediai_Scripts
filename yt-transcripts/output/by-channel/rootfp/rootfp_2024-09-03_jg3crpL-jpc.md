# Facing Health Challenges in Retirement and Learning to Reconnect with Your Spouse

**Channel:** James Conole, CFP®
**Published:** 2024-09-03
**URL:** https://youtube.com/watch?v=jg3crpL-jpc
**Duration:** 30:07

## Summary

James Conole interviews Derek Tinky, a recently retired federal law enforcement officer who spent 34 years in law enforcement — 13 years local and over 20 years federal. Derek explains that witnessing his father's health insurance company go bankrupt during cancer treatment, which depleted his parents' retirement savings and left his mother in financial hardship, was the primary motivation for switching to federal employment to secure guaranteed post-retirement healthcare. The conversation covers Stephanie's ankle surgery and Derek's own prostate cancer diagnosis shortly after retirement, how those health events reshaped their retirement outlook, and the intentional conversations they had to navigate the marital dynamics of spending 50 or more additional hours per week together.

## Key Takeaways

- Healthcare coverage continuity was Derek's primary reason for switching from local to federal law enforcement, illustrating how employer benefits can drive major career decisions for retirement-minded workers.
- Derek's father's insurance company went bankrupt during a third cancer bout, depleting the family's retirement savings — a firsthand cautionary tale about relying on a single insurer in retirement.
- Facing prostate cancer and a spouse's ankle surgery within months of retiring shifted the couple's focus toward living intentionally and appreciating experiences rather than accumulating more.
- Pre-retirement conversations about how spouses will coexist with 50 or more additional hours per week together significantly reduced friction and actually deepened the couple's relationship.
- Derek attributes their financial peace of mind to living on about 48% of income during their working years, enabling Stephanie to maintain her lifestyle if Derek passes.
- Interviewing multiple advisors before choosing Root Financial gave Derek confidence that his wife would be financially cared for, reinforcing the value of a holistic financial planning relationship.

## Detailed Notes

### Career Background and Healthcare Motivation [00:01:03]

Derek spent 13 years in local law enforcement before switching to a federal agency at age 39, primarily to secure post-retirement healthcare benefits. Federal law enforcement officers must retire between 57 and 60; an exemption allowed Derek to work until 60. He entered under an exemption as entry is normally capped at age 37.

### Father's Healthcare Disaster and Its Influence [00:03:11]

Derek's father battled cancer three times over 16 years. The third bout occurred after the family's insurer went bankrupt; uninsurable due to pre-existing conditions, his father's final treatment depleted all financial reserves. After his father died in 2003, Derek's mother had to take a second mortgage and live on a very tight budget. This experience made healthcare coverage in retirement a non-negotiable priority for Derek.

### Health Events Early in Retirement [00:06:19]

In August 2023, just months after retiring, Stephanie required ankle reconstruction surgery for a torn peroneal tendon. Simultaneously, Derek received concerning results from active surveillance of a pre-existing prostate cancer diagnosis and underwent further testing. Their daughter Caitlyn helped transport both to appointments, marking the first time they felt truly dependent on their child.

### Marriage Adjustments and Pre-Retirement Conversations [00:16:54]

Derek was concerned about disrupting Stephanie's established routines when he went from a commute of 90 minutes each way to being home full-time. Before retiring, the couple had deliberate conversations about personal space and expectations. Key findings included Stephanie encouraging Derek to maintain friendships and solo activities, which strengthened rather than strained the relationship. Derek, by nature a quieter person, reports significantly improved communication now that he has time to engage.
