# $10M+ Net Worth Case Study: What to Do First

**Channel:** James Conole, CFP®
**Published:** 2025-10-11
**URL:** https://youtube.com/watch?v=BXvtTM8d9Yo
**Duration:** 16:49

## Summary

James Conole (CEO) and Ari Towi (CGO) of Root Financial present a high-level $14 million case study featuring a couple with $4.65 million in retirement accounts and $8.5 million in inherited Apple and Amazon stock. The episode focuses on the first questions such clients should address: how much they can safely spend, how investment mix dramatically affects long-term outcomes, and what a spending plan designed around life experiences looks like. It serves as an introduction to a multi-part series that will dive deeper into tax, estate, and investment optimization.

## Key Takeaways

- A preservation-tilted portfolio can cause even a $14 million portfolio to run out of money by age 75, illustrating that being too conservative is a real financial risk.
- Spending $50,000 per month versus $25,000 per month results in $33 million less at end of life, demonstrating the enormous compounding impact of spending decisions.
- At $25,000 per month, the couple is on track for $70 million at end of life—a figure that for most would represent significant missed opportunity for experiences and giving.
- Concentrated inherited stock carries not only financial risk but emotional complexity, as clients often feel a connection to the stock through the person who left it.
- Adding $150,000 per year in first-class family travel for 30 years reduces the projected estate by $11 million—but still leaves the couple in a highly comfortable position.
- The first planning question for ultra-high-net-worth clients is not 'will I run out of money' but 'how do I avoid regretting not using this wealth while I had energy and health'.

## Detailed Notes

### Ultra High Net Worth in Context [00:00:42]

Fidelity data from 2023 shows 147,000 individuals with $30 million or more in investable assets in the U.S., far more than most people would estimate. The case study couple has a total net worth of $14 million: $2.4 million in retiree's 401k, $2.2 million in spouse's 401k, $250,000 in Roth IRA, $50,000 HSA, and $8.5 million in inherited Apple and Amazon stock.

### Spending Scenarios and Lifestyle Design [00:05:11]

At $25,000 per month the couple projects to $70 million at end of life, suggesting significant underutilization of wealth. Doubling to $50,000 per month reduces that by $33 million. Adding $150,000 per year in travel for 30 years reduces the estate by $11 million but remains financially comfortable. The advisors emphasize that the real risk is the regret of not spending on experiences while health and energy are available.

### Investment Mix and Sequence Risk [00:08:53]

Switching the portfolio from a growth-oriented mix to a preservation-oriented allocation causes the model to project insolvency by age 75, even starting from $14 million. This illustrates the non-obvious danger that excessive conservatism—not just market downturns—can erode a large portfolio. The advisors stress that projections depend heavily on return assumptions, inflation, and spending, none of which are guaranteed.

### Upcoming Content and Planning Priorities [00:14:52]

The episode is explicitly a first in a series; future installments will cover tax strategy (Roth conversions timing, capital gains harvesting, QCDs, donor-advised funds), investment strategy (managing concentrated stock, sequence risk), and estate planning (SLATs, grantor trusts, right-sized inheritances). The advisors invite viewers in this situation to contact Root Financial's private wealth team.
