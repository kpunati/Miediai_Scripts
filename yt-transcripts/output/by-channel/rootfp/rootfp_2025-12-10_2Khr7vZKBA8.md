# Freedom at 60 Why He Left a Job He Loved (and Doesn’t Regret It) | Retirement Reality

**Channel:** James Conole, CFP®
**Published:** 2025-12-10
**URL:** https://youtube.com/watch?v=2Khr7vZKBA8
**Duration:** 38:05

## Summary

This episode of Retirement Reality features Steve, a former video game programmer who retired at 60 after a 2021 heart attack accelerated his long-held desire for early retirement. Steve recounts how budgeting software (YNAB), maximizing retirement accounts, and leveraging an HSA helped him achieve financial independence, while discussing the psychological shift from earning to spending in retirement. Now two and a half years in, Steve describes an active, purposeful retirement filled with jazz performance, acting classes, VR-based fitness, and open-ended international travel.

## Key Takeaways

- A health crisis can serve as a forcing function to translate vague retirement aspirations into a concrete financial and life plan.
- Learning to budget was Steve's foundational step; YNAB changed how he managed money and enabled him to build savings after a costly divorce.
- Maximizing 401k catch-up contributions and HSA after age 50 significantly accelerated Steve's retirement readiness.
- Roth conversions in retirement allow Steve to manage his tax bracket while building tax-free wealth for the future.
- Delaying Social Security to age 70 is Steve's plan to maximize lifetime income and cover core expenses in later retirement.
- Experimenting with new activities—acting, VR fitness, jazz bands—illustrates the value of entering retirement with an open mindset rather than a rigid plan.
- The freedom to be present for family (supporting his mother through a cancer diagnosis) was an unanticipated but deeply meaningful benefit of early retirement.

## Detailed Notes

### Career Background and Retirement Decision [00:00:00]

Steve spent 23 years at the same video game company, loved his work and colleagues, and was earning peak salary when he chose to retire at 60. The decision was not driven by dissatisfaction but by a desire for autonomy over his time. A 2021 heart attack, followed by a period of depression, crystallized his longstanding interest in early retirement into concrete action.

### Financial Journey: Divorce, Budgeting, and Saving [00:00:00]

A divorce at 45-47 wiped out half of Steve's 401k, forcing him to rebuild from a low base. Discovering YNAB (You Need a Budget) taught him to budget for the first time and became the foundation of his financial turnaround. After age 50 he began maxing out his 401k including catch-up contributions and also maxed his HSA, describing the HSA as a major enabler of early retirement by removing healthcare cost anxiety.

### Retirement Transition and Notification [00:00:00]

Steve gave his employer nine months' notice to allow time to hire and train a replacement programmer. This unusually generous timeline reflected his loyalty to a company he valued, and it enabled a smooth knowledge transfer. After retiring he remained in San Diego for a year before relocating to Arizona for lower cost of living and to be near family.

### Life in Retirement: Activities and Travel [00:00:00]

Steve joined jazz bands, took improv and acting classes, and pursued open-ended international travel including a trip to Scotland, France, and Italy. He describes having abundant energy that was absent during working years. His approach to retirement activities mirrors his financial philosophy: experiment broadly, iterate quickly, and stick with what works.

### Health Transformation and Fitness Routine [00:00:00]

Starting after his heart attack, Steve gradually lost over 70 pounds over four years through caloric deficit, dietary changes (reducing red meat), resistance band training, and ultimately VR fitness using Supernatural on a MetaQuest 3 headset. Switching from traditional weights to heavy resistance bands eliminated chronic joint pain. The freedom to exercise at any hour in retirement has made consistency far easier.

### Retirement Income Strategy [00:00:00]

Steve manages spending intuitively, spending more freely when markets are up and planning to reduce if conditions deteriorate. He is conducting Roth conversions up to a target tax bracket and plans to delay Social Security until 70, expecting it to cover core expenses. He used three years of COBRA for health coverage and expects only about 20 months on ACA or private insurance before Medicare eligibility.

### Key Advice for Prospective Retirees [00:00:00]

Steve recommends budgeting as the essential first step, citing YNAB as transformational. He urges people to maximize all available retirement vehicles including 401k catch-up contributions and HSA. He encourages envisioning what retirement will look like—the lifestyle, not just the numbers—and advises not letting healthcare concerns prevent early retirement given available options like COBRA and ACA.
