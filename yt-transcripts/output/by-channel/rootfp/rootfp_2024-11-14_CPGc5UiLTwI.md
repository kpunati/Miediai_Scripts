# Here's How To Discuss Money Effectively With Your Parents

**Channel:** James Conole, CFP®
**Published:** 2024-11-14
**URL:** https://youtube.com/watch?v=CPGc5UiLTwI
**Duration:** 20:12

## Summary

James Conole and Ari Taublieb launch a joint episode inspired by a listener comment asking how to talk to elderly parents about money and inheritance. The hosts share personal experiences with family financial conversations and offer practical strategies for starting these difficult discussions, emphasizing leading with concern for the parent's wellbeing rather than interest in inheriting assets. They highlight that many families avoid the topic entirely, creating risks of family conflict, missed estate planning, and parents sacrificing quality of life unnecessarily. The episode encourages using low-stakes entry points — like long-term care preferences or digital account access — to open broader financial conversations.

## Key Takeaways

- Approach conversations about parental finances from a place of care for their wellbeing, not curiosity about inheritance.
- Long-term care preferences (staying home vs. care community) are a low-stakes entry point that naturally leads to broader financial discussions.
- Many families conflate owning estate documents with having an actual estate plan — the latter involves intentional decisions about spending, gifting, and wealth transfer.
- Parents often sacrifice personal spending to leave inheritances that their children don't actually want or need, highlighting the value of open communication.
- Practical access questions — passwords, account locations, advance directives — are as important as legal documents in estate preparedness.
- Unresolved or unspoken family financial dynamics can lead to litigation and broken relationships after a parent's death.

## Detailed Notes

### Episode Introduction and Show Concept [00:00:00]

James Conole and Ari Taublieb, both CFPs at Root Financial, launch a new joint show to address common financial questions from their audiences. The format draws topics from viewer and listener comments. This first episode is prompted by a comment from a viewer asking how to discuss elderly parents' finances and eventual estate.

### Why These Conversations Are Hard [00:05:21]

Money is rarely discussed openly in most households, making the topic feel taboo. Children worry that bringing up inheritance will make them appear to be waiting for their parents to die. Ari notes that stress and shame around money are common family dynamics, and the goal is to approach the conversation with care rather than avoidance.

### Strategies for Approaching the Conversation [00:06:22]

Ari suggests indirect warm-up approaches such as talking to a parent's close friend first to gauge openness, or asking a parent what they would want done with their money if something happened tomorrow — framed as honoring their wishes rather than anticipating inheritance. Starting with long-term care preferences (home care vs. facility) is recommended as a natural, lower-stakes entry point.

### Estate Planning vs. Estate Documents [00:14:44]

Many clients assume having a trust, will, and medical directives constitutes an estate plan — but James distinguishes documents from the actual plan. An estate plan includes decisions about how to spend during life, what to gift, how to avoid family conflict over assets like real estate, and whether parents are sacrificing their lifestyle unnecessarily to leave money to children who would rather see them enjoy it.
