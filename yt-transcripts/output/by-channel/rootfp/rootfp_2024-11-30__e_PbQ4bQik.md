description: |
  Retiring as a couple with an age gap brings unique challenges that require careful planning. Daniel (53) and Dione (64) illustrate the complexities. They’ve built a solid financial foundation with investments, properties, and savings. Dione earns a significant salary, while Daniel currently doesn’t, adding to the financial dynamics.

  Here are three critical principles for couples in similar situations:
  1. Account for the Younger Spouse’s Longevity: With an 11-year age gap, Dione’s retirement at 69 might leave Daniel financially vulnerable later. Retirement plans should extend beyond the older spouse’s expected lifespan to ensure assets last for the younger spouse.
  2. Plan for Premature Death: If Dione, the primary earner, were to pass away, their financial plan could unravel without her income or additional life insurance. Couples should prepare for this possibility by assessing life insurance or creating backup plans.
  3. Consider Long-Term Care Costs: A long-term care event could deplete resources, leaving the surviving spouse without support. Planning for such expenses, through insurance or savings adjustments, is essential.

  Running projections and preparing for “what-ifs” is key. Scenarios like retiring earlier, altering spending, or reevaluating investments highlight the need for flexible strategies. With careful planning, couples can protect their futures and maintain financial security.
