  You can retire with enough money — and still feel unhappy, unfulfilled, or lost. This is one of the most overlooked retirement planning mistakes, and it has nothing to do with running out of money.

  James explains why many retirees who are financially prepared struggle in retirement, even with no debt, strong Social Security benefits, pensions, and seven-figure portfolios. Traditional retirement planning focuses on numbers, probabilities, and portfolio success, but rarely addresses what life actually looks like after work ends. 

  Using a real retirement case study, James shows how retirement planning software can answer whether you can retire, but not whether you’ll enjoy retirement. Without planning for purpose, structure, relationships, and personal fulfillment, many retirees experience identity loss, boredom, and strained relationships once the honeymoon phase of retirement fades. 

  This video breaks down the most common emotional and lifestyle risks in retirement, including losing a sense of identity, having too much unstructured time, and misaligned expectations between spouses. Ari explains why money should be a tool that supports health, freedom, relationships, and personal growth — not the sole focus of retirement planning.

  If you’re approaching retirement or thinking about early retirement, this is a reminder that financial independence alone doesn’t guarantee a fulfilling retirement. Before asking if you can retire, it’s worth asking if you’ll like the life you’re building.
