# How Should You Invest $10M+? Most Underutilized Strategy Revealed

**Channel:** James Conole, CFP®
**Published:** 2025-01-14
**URL:** https://youtube.com/watch?v=T4rtE-qthMQ
**Duration:** 22:52

## Summary

This video explains direct indexing — a strategy where investors own each individual component stock of an index such as the S&P 500 rather than a single ETF — and focuses specifically on the tax advantages it unlocks through systematic tax-loss harvesting. By owning individual stocks, investors can identify and sell underperformers even in rising markets, bank those losses, and deploy them to offset realized gains from other sources such as business or real estate sales. The video cites Dimensional Fund Advisors research covering 1990-2020 showing the strategy generates 0.5% to 1.85% per year in tax alpha. Five criteria for suitability are detailed: high tax bracket, existence of outside capital gains, concentrated stock positions, values-based investing preferences, and significant charitable giving.

## Key Takeaways

- Direct indexing owns the individual constituent stocks of an index rather than an ETF, enabling granular tax-loss harvesting that is impossible with a single fund.
- Tax-loss harvesting captures realized losses, which can be used to offset ordinary income (within annual limits) or capital gains from stock, real estate, or business sales.
- Even in years when the overall index rises, individual stocks with large losses exist and can be harvested — making direct indexing more powerful than ETF-based harvesting.
- Research spanning 30 years shows the strategy can add 0.5%-1.85% in annual tax alpha without changing investment risk or expected returns.
- Charitable givers benefit doubly: they harvest losses to offset other gains while also donating appreciated securities tax-free, eliminating capital gains at the time of gifting.
- Technological advances have lowered the entry point from $20 million or more to approximately $500,000, but the strategy still requires a sophisticated daily trading system and tax-sensitivity infrastructure.

## Detailed Notes

### What Direct Indexing Is [00:00:00]

Instead of buying an S&P 500 ETF, direct indexing means purchasing each of the 500 constituent stocks in their index-proportional weights. Apple represents about 7% of the S&P 500, so $1 million would mean $70,000 in Apple, and so on. The result is economically equivalent market exposure, but with individual stock ownership that enables more sophisticated tax management.

### Tax-Loss Harvesting Mechanics [00:04:09]

When any individual stock falls in value, it can be sold to lock in a loss, then replaced after 31 days (or replaced immediately with a similar but not identical security to avoid wash sale rules). These harvested losses can offset ordinary income up to annual limits, or can be banked and used against future realized gains from any source. In 2022, a $1 million S&P 500 portfolio could have yielded roughly $250,000 in harvestable losses; even in the strong 2023 market, InPhase Energy fell 50% while the index rose 26%.

### Research on Tax Alpha [00:09:21]

A white paper from Dimensional Fund Advisors studying three distinct decades (1990-2000, 2000-2010, 2010-2020) found that depending on the investor's tax situation and bracket, the strategy produced 0.5% to 1.85% in annual tax alpha — additional net returns attributable purely to tax management rather than investment selection or added risk. The approach is not limited to the S&P 500 and can be applied to small-cap, mid-cap, international, or any index-based allocation.

### Five Criteria for Suitability [00:12:28]

Criterion 1: High tax bracket — the higher the marginal rate, the more valuable each dollar of harvested loss. Criterion 2: Outside capital gains — from real estate, business sales, or other accounts where losses can be applied. Criterion 3: Concentrated stock positions — the SMA can be customized to exclude additional purchases of a stock already overweighted in the investor's total portfolio. Criterion 4: Values-based investing — individual components can be excluded by sector or company, impossible with a standard ETF. Criterion 5: Significant charitable giving — appreciated securities accumulated in the account can be donated directly, avoiding capital gains while still receiving the full charitable deduction.

### Operational Requirements and Accessibility [00:11:26]

The strategy demands a daily trading system scanning hundreds of stocks, precise wash-sale coordination across accounts, and a sophisticated algorithmic trading platform. It cannot be replicated by manually purchasing stocks on a retail brokerage. Technology improvements have brought the minimum portfolio size from $20 million (20+ years ago) to approximately $500,000 today for providers like Dimensional Fund Advisors.
