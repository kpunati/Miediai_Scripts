# How to Travel the Country and Start Living Before Actually Being Fully Retired

**Channel:** James Conole, CFP®
**Published:** 2024-12-31
**URL:** https://youtube.com/watch?v=w1KhUYUH0y4
**Duration:** 36:45

## Summary

James Conole interviews LJ and Kelly, a couple who sold their home and traveled the U.S. for a full year (August 2021 to August 2022) before Kelly formally retired, staying in Airbnbs for month-long stretches from San Diego to Portland, Maine, down the East Coast, across the South, and back. They share how total travel costs were roughly equivalent to their San Diego rent because housing costs replaced rather than supplemented their previous expenses, and they cooked most meals at Airbnbs. Kelly, a physical therapist, emphasizes that she witnessed too many patients who waited until retirement to travel only to find their health had declined, reinforcing the couple's philosophy of traveling while healthy. The video concludes with advice on budgeting intentionally for travel, ignoring naysayers, finding hobbies and creative outlets, and treating retirement as an ongoing experiment.

## Key Takeaways

- Slow travel — staying one month per location — costs no more than fixed city rent when you sell or vacate your home, because housing costs replace rather than add to existing expenses.
- Booking Airbnbs well in advance is critical, especially for peak-season destinations; cancellations can force significantly higher last-minute costs.
- Coordinating healthcare coverage before extended travel — including calling your insurer's travel nurse — is essential to ensure medical care while away from your home network.
- Kelly's PT experience shows that patients who delay travel until full retirement often find their health prevents them from enjoying it, making early-retirement or pre-retirement travel strategically important.
- Having hobbies and creative outlets before retiring prevents the boredom and purposelessness that causes many early retirees to return to work.
- Travel insurance is strongly recommended to cover medical evacuation, trip cancellations, and overseas injuries.
- Experimenting with retirement activities — treating failures as data rather than setbacks — allows for an evolving, meaningful retirement.

## Detailed Notes

### The Year-Long Road Trip: Route and Logistics [00:01:02]

LJ and Kelly left San Diego in August 2021, put belongings in storage, and drove a Subaru Forester across the country for 12 months. Their primary destination was Portland, Maine (LJ's mother), and they mapped the trip to include national parks and visits to friends along the way — Colorado, Nebraska, Massachusetts — then traveled down the Eastern Seaboard to Florida and back across the South to California. They kept daily drives under four to five hours and pre-planned most Airbnb bookings months ahead.

### Slow Travel: Benefits of Month-Long Stays [00:09:28]

Staying one month in each city allowed LJ and Kelly to experience neighborhoods, local transit, and surrounding rural areas rather than rushing through tourist highlights. Washington D.C. is cited as an example where a 30-day stay enabled walking every neighborhood and visiting museums at a relaxed pace. Extended stays also unlock significant Airbnb monthly discounts, making it cheaper per night than shorter bookings.

### Cost Comparison: Travel vs. Staying in San Diego [00:13:37]

Their former San Diego rent was $3,000-$3,500 per month. Because they sold their home and put possessions in storage, they eliminated roughly $15,000 per year in home-maintenance costs and eliminated utilities and internet bills. Many Airbnb stays matched or came in under their previous rent, making the total annual cost comparable. Food costs were also similar because they grocery-shopped and cooked in Airbnb kitchens rather than dining out.

### Healthcare and Travel Insurance on the Road [00:17:48]

The couple used Kaiser insurance, which has limited coverage outside its service states; they proactively called a travel nurse before departing to document their itinerary and receive health advice. Based on patient cases in her PT practice, Kelly strongly recommends purchasing travel insurance for any major trip to cover medical evacuation, overseas surgery complications, and trip-cancellation reimbursement.

### Physical Therapist Perspective: Travel While Healthy [00:21:03]

Kelly treated patients across all age groups who said they had waited until retirement to travel but then could not due to knee replacements, back problems, or loss of a spouse. She argues that prioritizing travel earlier — even in small increments during long weekends or extended vacations — is the financially and physically prudent strategy. She also observed that newly retired patients who had never exercised often injured themselves trying to take up fitness suddenly, underlining the importance of maintaining health throughout one's career.

### Hobbies, Creative Outlets, and Retirement Mindset [00:25:17]

LJ and James highlight that many retirees become bored without hobbies or creative outlets and often return to work within months. LJ's advice: in retirement, you only need to find something you enjoy — profitability and skill level are irrelevant. Both emphasize an experimental mindset — treating pivots and changed plans as learning rather than failure — and ignoring social pressure about unconventional lifestyle choices such as having no permanent home base.
