# They Gave Up the ‘Traditional’ Retirement to Live the Life They Always Wanted

**Channel:** James Conole, CFP®
**Published:** 2025-08-23
**URL:** https://youtube.com/watch?v=97TmEEewul8
**Duration:** 38:40

## Summary

James Conole interviews John and Bev, the Retirement Travelers, who retired at 55 and have since visited 107 countries and all 50 US states. Their journey began during COVID when they purchased an Airstream and traveled the lower 48 states, discovering they could live comfortably in 220 square feet. Bev's MS diagnosis added urgency to their travel ambitions, and they made the financially grounded decision to sell their dream Florida home, eliminate all debt before retirement, and work with financial planners on a long-term strategy. They now run a YouTube channel and blog, emphasizing that meaningful retirement requires intentional planning, downsizing, and pursuing goals while health allows.

## Key Takeaways

- Retiring at 55 into full-time travel was enabled by being completely debt-free and having a long-term financial plan worked out with advisers.
- Living in a 220-square-foot Airstream revealed how little material possessions matter, catalyzing their decision to sell their home and downsize.
- Bev's MS diagnosis reinforced the 'go-go years' philosophy — the early years of retirement are the healthiest and should be used actively.
- Downsizing is presented as a gift to both the retiring couple (mental freedom, lower costs) and their children (no estate burden).
- Swedish death cleaning and digitizing all finances and documents is recommended as a way to reduce burden on surviving spouses and heirs.
- Lifestyle creep — buying bigger houses with each move — is cited as a major regret that would have been avoided with intentional frugality.
- Their 'live small so you can live big' philosophy encourages retirees to prioritize experiences over possessions and act on dreams while able.

## Detailed Notes

### Origin of the Retirement Travelers [00:00:41]

John retired at the end of 2019 and he and Bev initially planned modest blogging while doing a couple of months of travel per year. When COVID cancelled a planned Europe trip, Bev suggested buying an Airstream to see America. Within a week of COVID shutdowns, they purchased an Airstream and set out to visit all 50 states and national parks in the lower 48.

### Living With Less: The Airstream Revelation [00:04:22]

While traveling in a roughly 220-square-foot Airstream and still paying HOA fees back home, they realized they did not need a large house or many possessions. After the first year on the road, they began seriously considering selling everything and traveling the world for 10 years, noting they had retired at 55 while the average age in their community was 75.

### Urgency, MS, and the Go-Go Years [00:06:09]

Bev was diagnosed with multiple sclerosis around age 40, which instilled a sense of urgency to travel while physically able. Both frame their current period as the 'go-go years' of retirement — the healthiest phase — and argue that retirees should act on their dreams now rather than assuming future health or capability.

### Financial Foundations: Debt-Free and Planned [00:14:45]

They worked with financial advisers to build a long-term retirement plan before making major lifestyle decisions. Being completely debt-free before retirement was a prerequisite; they stress they could not have pursued full-time travel otherwise. When they sold their dream home, they set those proceeds aside as an invested safety net rather than spending them, preserving their financial security.

### Lessons Learned: Downsizing and Simplification [00:23:51]

Their biggest advice to younger selves is 'live small so you can live big' — avoid lifestyle creep and resist buying larger homes with each move. They undertook Swedish death cleaning, digitizing all documents and finances, which they describe as a gift to children who will not face the burden of sorting through an estate. They note that downsizing is often overlooked despite careful financial and legal planning.

### What's Next and Encouragement for Others [00:28:52]

They have shifted their YouTube content toward vlogging their real travel experiences, aiming to give viewers confidence to explore less-touristy destinations. They launched a Facebook community (Retirement Traveler Senior Travel Community) and are organizing a group trip to Victoria Falls. Their core message to those on the fence is to take the leap of faith, as the risk of inaction — regret — outweighs the risks of acting.
