# The Power of a One-Page Retirement Plan: Less Is More

**Channel:** Nick Davis, CFP®
**Published:** 2024-06-17
**URL:** https://youtube.com/watch?v=4q0sqPE2Cyw
**Duration:** 14:47

## Summary

Nick Davis introduces the one-page financial plan as a solution to the common problem of people being overwhelmed or distrustful of traditional multi-stage financial plans. Using a case study of Jim and Sandra—a couple planning retirement with salaries of $130,000 and $85,000, accounts split across taxable, IRA, and 401k—he walks through how to first build a financial narrative and then distill it into a single-page plan that includes a personal mission statement, goals, income distribution notes, key assumptions, and near-term action items. The goal is clarity, accountability, and actionability rather than comprehensive documentation.

## Key Takeaways

- A one-page financial plan is a distillation of the full financial narrative into the most actionable and important elements, not a replacement for comprehensive planning.
- Traditional financial plans often fail because they are confusing, untrusted, or lead to inaction due to their complexity.
- A personal money mission statement—answering what money is supposed to do for you—is the most important element of the one-page plan.
- Knowing which accounts to draw from and in what order (sequential vs. pro-rata distributions) can significantly affect taxes in retirement.
- Setting aside a cash or money market buffer for known near-term extras like home renovations prevents unplanned portfolio withdrawals.
- Tax-gain harvesting during the low-income window before Social Security may allow realization of capital gains at 0% federal rates.
- Key assumptions such as Social Security timing, 401k contribution levels, and state residency changes should be explicitly documented as plan commitments.

## Detailed Notes

### Problem with Traditional Plans and the One-Page Solution [00:00:59]

Traditional financial plans fail due to confusion, distrust, and inaction. Many planning software tools are designed to favor certain products, undermining objectivity. The one-page plan forces clarity by surfacing only the most critical assumptions and action items, keeping the client focused on what to do next.

### Building the Financial Narrative [00:04:21]

Jim (age not specified) and Sandra (57) build their narrative by tallying all savings, liabilities, income sources, and goals. Key observations: about two-thirds of assets are pre-tax, they plan to relocate from Colorado to Texas for tax efficiency, and Social Security timing and healthcare costs need to be modeled carefully. This narrative forms the foundation from which the one-page plan is derived.

### One-Page Plan: Goals, Mission, and Distribution [00:06:55]

The plan includes an at-a-glance summary of asset mix and net worth, a money mission statement (their 'due north'), specific goals (Jim reduces to part-time at 63, retires at 67; Sandra retires at 65; $125,000 lifestyle target; Colorado to Texas relocation), and detailed income distribution notes including a $40,000 cash buffer for extras in the first three years and a sequential distribution order starting with the taxable brokerage.

### Distribution Strategy and Guardrails [00:09:36]

The plan notes pro-rata distributions evaluated annually and paid monthly, with the distribution order guided by a guardrail income system. Tax-gain harvesting from Jim's brokerage during the low-income retirement years before Social Security may yield 0% capital gains rates. Sequential vs. pro-rata distribution strategies are tested annually to optimize outcomes.

### Key Assumptions and Future Tasks [00:11:34]

Documented assumptions include specific Social Security timing for each spouse, Roth IRA contribution eligibility near the income threshold, and the Secure 2.0 Act expanding Roth 401k access. Future tasks include evaluating an older life insurance policy, considering Roth conversions in Q3, and current tasks such as updating the will and verifying Social Security amounts directly from SSA records.
