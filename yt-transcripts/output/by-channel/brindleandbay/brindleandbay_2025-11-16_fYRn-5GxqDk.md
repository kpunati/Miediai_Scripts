# How to Spend Like the Top Retirees (3 Surprises Inside)

**Channel:** Nick Davis, CFP®
**Published:** 2025-11-16
**URL:** https://youtube.com/watch?v=fYRn-5GxqDk
**Duration:** 13:40

## Summary

Nick Davis describes the spending behaviors he observes among the happiest and wealthiest retirees, framing them as three distinct strategies. Backward budgeting means defining the desired retirement lifestyle first and then working backwards to determine the income required, removing guesswork and anxiety. Frontloading experiences means spending more on travel and experiences in the first five to ten years of retirement when health permits, treating those experiences as investments in life satisfaction. Diversifying spending sources means combining stable income floors such as Social Security and pensions with flexible portfolio withdrawals to simultaneously achieve predictability, growth potential, and tax optimization.

## Key Takeaways

- Top retirees start with the life they want and then calculate the required income, rather than forcing their lifestyle into an arbitrary budget.
- The go-go years of early retirement are the optimal time to frontload travel and meaningful experiences before health or energy declines.
- Diversifying income sources across stable and flexible streams provides both downside protection and upside flexibility.
- Mixing withdrawals from tax-deferred accounts, Roth accounts, and taxable brokerage accounts allows active management of the tax bracket throughout retirement.
- Combining all three strategies — backward budgeting, frontloading, and diversified income — creates an adaptive system rather than a static plan.

## Detailed Notes

### Surprise 1: Backward Budgeting [00:00:00]

Successful retirees begin by describing the specific lifestyle they want — travel destinations, time with grandchildren, car, home — and then calculate the income needed to support that life. This eliminates persistent low-level anxiety about whether savings are sufficient and replaces arbitrary savings targets with a personalized, values-driven income requirement.

### Surprise 2: Frontloading Experiences [00:03:10]

Top retirees deliberately spend more on travel and experiences in the first five to ten years of retirement, treating that spending as an investment in what Davis calls memory dividends. Waiting until later in retirement is risky because health and mobility often decline, making planned experiences difficult or impossible. Davis notes he has never heard a client regret a trip taken in their 60s but has heard many who wished they had gone sooner.

### Surprise 3: Diversified Spending Sources [00:06:33]

Rather than relying on a single income stream, successful retirees layer stable income — Social Security, pensions, rental income — as a foundation for essential expenses, then draw flexible withdrawals from investment portfolios for discretionary spending. This combination provides predictability when markets are volatile and growth potential when markets perform well. Strategic mixing of account types also minimizes taxes.

### The Integrated Framework [00:08:39]

Davis shows how the three strategies reinforce each other: the lifestyle plan sets the income target, the income strategy provides the confidence to spend, and the spending strategy is structured to execute the lifestyle plan. Frontloading is built into the plan based on the income picture rather than done impulsively, ensuring that early extra spending does not compromise future financial security.
