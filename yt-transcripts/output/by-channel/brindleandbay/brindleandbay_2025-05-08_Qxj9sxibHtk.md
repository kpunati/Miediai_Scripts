# Should I Gift Money Now or Wait Until Later?”

**Channel:** Nick Davis, CFP®
**Published:** 2025-05-08
**URL:** https://youtube.com/watch?v=Qxj9sxibHtk
**Duration:** 17:09

## Summary

The Brindle and Bay Money Master podcast panel discusses the financial and emotional dimensions of gifting wealth during one's lifetime versus at death. The hosts explore gift tax rules, estate tax thresholds, and practical gifting vehicles including QCDs, Roth conversions, TOD accounts, donor-advised funds, and trusts. Personal anecdotes—including a grandfather's $5,000 contribution toward a first home—illustrate the relational value of timely giving. The overarching message is that early gifting is more impactful when a retiree's plan is financially secure.

## Key Takeaways

- Gifting while alive allows recipients to use assets when they are most needed, often creating stronger relational bonds and shared memories.
- The annual gift tax exclusion (~$19,000/person) allows meaningful transfers without filing additional forms; married couples can double this amount.
- The lifetime exemption (~$13 million) may be reduced in the future, making timely gifting planning worthwhile for high-net-worth individuals.
- Assets transferred at death benefit from a step-up in basis, which can eliminate capital gains taxes, providing a reason to wait in some cases.
- Practical gifting vehicles include QCDs for those 70½+, Roth conversions for legacy growth, donor-advised funds for charitable giving, and TOD accounts for direct beneficiary designations.
- Confirming retirement plan security—accounting for healthcare costs, inflation, and custodial care—should precede any significant gifting decision.

## Detailed Notes

### The Case for Lifetime Giving [00:00:00]

The panel opens with personal stories of receiving timely financial gifts (including a $5,000 down-payment contribution for a first home at $57,000) to illustrate that money given when recipients need it most creates lasting memories and relational value. The book Die with Zero by Bill Perkins is recommended as a framework for thinking about lifetime giving and the overlap of health, wealth, and time.

### Financial Readiness and Gift Tax Rules [00:03:47]

Key prerequisites for gifting include stress-testing the retirement plan for inflation, healthcare costs, and longevity. The annual exclusion (~$19,000/person) allows tax-free transfers without triggering lifetime exemption tracking. The current ~$13 million lifetime exemption means estate taxes apply to relatively few households, though legislative changes could lower this threshold.

### Practical Gifting Strategies [00:11:20]

Strategies discussed include annual exclusion gifting over multiple years, QCDs to charities (available at 70½), Roth conversion of an unneeded IRA as an ultra-long legacy play, UTMA and TOD accounts for children and grandchildren, naming beneficiaries on labeled 'legacy IRA' accounts, donor-advised funds for current-year deductions, trusts to throttle distributions, and direct tuition payments which are handled separately from gift limits.

### When Waiting Makes Sense [00:14:54]

Step-up in basis on inherited appreciated assets can eliminate capital gains taxes that would apply if the same assets were gifted during the owner's lifetime. Waiting is also appropriate if the recipient is not financially mature, if the retirement plan remains uncertain, or if the donor wants to maintain control over how assets are eventually distributed.
