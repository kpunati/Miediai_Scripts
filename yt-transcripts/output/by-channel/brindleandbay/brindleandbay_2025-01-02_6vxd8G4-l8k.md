# The Ultimate New Year Checklist: Master Your Money in 2025

**Channel:** Nick Davis, CFP®
**Published:** 2025-01-02
**URL:** https://youtube.com/watch?v=6vxd8G4-l8k
**Duration:** 17:00

## Summary

Hosted by Nick Davis and newly credentialed CFP Tori Tenhagen, this podcast episode presents 10 essential year-end financial planning actions organized into four categories: income, taxes, budget/spending, and legal/estate. The discussion targets both pre-retirees building toward retirement and retirees already drawing down assets. Key themes include the importance of multi-bucket tax diversification for withdrawal flexibility, the need to regularly review actual spending rather than budgeting prospectively, and the often-neglected urgency of estate documents and beneficiary designations. The episode is conversational in tone with practical client anecdotes illustrating each point.

## Key Takeaways

- Review retirement income sources annually—whether in drawdown or approaching retirement—to ensure withdrawals align with tax strategy and spending needs.
- Having multiple tax buckets (pre-tax, Roth, brokerage) in retirement enables flexible withdrawal sequencing that can reduce total lifetime tax.
- RMDs must be tracked and taken at the correct amounts; failure incurs a steep IRS penalty.
- Roth conversions are not universally appropriate each year—the decision depends on current income, tax bracket, and projected future rates.
- Reviewing actual spending data from the prior period is more useful than forward budgeting and helps adjust behavior and planning assumptions.
- Beneficiary designations and estate documents should be reviewed annually, especially after major life changes, as errors can have lasting legal and financial consequences.
- Qualified charitable distributions and UTMA accounts are practical tools for tax-efficient giving to charities and teaching compound growth to younger generations.

## Detailed Notes

### Income Review and Withdrawal Strategy [00:02:04]

For retirees, the first step is identifying where income will come from—Social Security, pensions, portfolio withdrawals—and adjusting the withdrawal strategy to reflect tax efficiency. Pre-retirees should examine current Social Security projections and ensure their savings are spread across tax-status buckets to preserve future flexibility.

### RMD Planning and Tax Optimization [00:04:11]

RMDs apply at age 73 or 75 depending on birth year; if a retiree is already drawing primarily from a 401(k), the RMD may be automatically covered but should still be verified. Roth conversions deserve annual evaluation by comparing the current tax bracket to projected future bracket, though they are not appropriate for everyone in every year.

### Budget Reassessment and Spending Reality [00:07:22]

The hosts recommend looking backward at actual spending rather than creating abstract budgets. Understanding real spending patterns helps retirees calibrate whether they are under- or over-spending relative to their plan, with examples of clients adjusting materially in either direction after seeing their data.

### Estate Documents and Beneficiary Designations [00:11:39]

Wills, trusts, and beneficiary designations should be reviewed annually, not treated as one-time tasks. The hosts share a case where an unchanged beneficiary designation at death caused a nearly year-long legal dispute. They emphasize that the people executing these plans must also understand the decedent's wishes and know where assets are held.

### Gifting Strategies: QCDs and UTMAs [00:13:44]

Retirees over 70.5 can use qualified charitable distributions to send money directly from an IRA to charity, avoiding income tax and satisfying RMD requirements simultaneously. UTMA accounts are highlighted as an engaging way to transfer wealth to grandchildren while teaching ownership, compounding, and long-term investing habits.
