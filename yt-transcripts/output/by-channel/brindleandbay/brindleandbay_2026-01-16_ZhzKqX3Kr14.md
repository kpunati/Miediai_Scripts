# The Retirement Tax Window: Does This Apply to You?

**Channel:** Nick Davis, CFP®
**Published:** 2026-01-16
**URL:** https://youtube.com/watch?v=ZhzKqX3Kr14
**Duration:** 19:54

## Summary

Nick Davis and Andrew Bena (CFP/EA) of Brindle and Bay discuss the retirement tax window — the gap between leaving work and the onset of Social Security and RMDs — as the prime opportunity for proactive tax planning. They explain that failing to act during this window leaves income decisions to the IRS through forced RMDs, while strategic Roth conversions and capital gains harvesting during low-income years can meaningfully reduce lifetime taxes and IRMAA exposure. Andrew's background as an enrolled agent informs the technical depth around bracket management, IRMAA surcharges, and the new enhanced senior deduction available through 2028.

## Key Takeaways

- The retirement tax window opens at retirement and closes when Social Security and RMDs begin (ages 73-75), representing the last period of significant income control.
- Roth conversions and strategic IRA withdrawals during the window can reduce future RMD balances and limit permanent IRMAA surcharges.
- IRMAA surcharges can be temporary (during active conversion years) or permanent (if RMDs push income above thresholds indefinitely).
- The enhanced senior deduction adds $6,000 per person for taxpayers over 65 with AGI under $150,000 (married), available through 2028.
- Diversifying account types — traditional, Roth, and taxable brokerage — before retirement maximizes flexibility for tax management in retirement.
- Tax planning is described as a year-round, annual process that considers all income sources including dividends, capital gains, and future RMDs.

## Detailed Notes

### What is the Retirement Tax Window [00:03:25]

The retirement tax window is the period after leaving work but before Social Security and RMDs begin (ages 73-75). During this time, taxable income is typically at its lowest, creating an opportunity for Roth conversions and capital gains recognition at reduced rates. Andrew Bena emphasizes the 10-year window before and after the retirement transition as the most critical planning period.

### Strategic Actions During the Window [00:04:43]

Key moves include Roth conversions up to the top of the 22% bracket, strategic IRA withdrawals to pay taxes now at lower rates, resetting cost basis on appreciated assets, and sequencing withdrawals to manage bracket exposure. The advisors stress that without planning the IRS effectively decides income levels through forced RMDs.

### IRMAA and Medicare Surcharge Implications [00:09:09]

IRMAA surcharges based on income levels can become a permanent feature of retirement if RMDs consistently push income above thresholds. Conversions done early may cause temporary IRMAA exposure in conversion years but eliminate it later, while inaction can result in IRMAA for the remainder of retirement. The advisors use income projection graphs to visualize this tradeoff for clients.

### Enhanced Senior Deduction and New Tax Provisions [00:08:22]

A new enhanced senior deduction of $6,000 per person is available for taxpayers over 65 with married AGI under $150,000, effective through 2028. This was often mistaken for an elimination of Social Security taxation, but it is an additional standard deduction. The advisors note this is a useful amount to incorporate into conversion planning.

### How Advisors Identify the Window and Action Plan [00:13:36]

The planning process involves projecting future income sources (Social Security, RMDs, pensions, dividends) on a graph to identify low-income valleys and fill them with taxable conversions or withdrawals without breaching higher brackets. This is reviewed annually rather than set-and-forget, and is part of initial onboarding and ongoing client meetings.
