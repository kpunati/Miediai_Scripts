# Retiring Before 60? Sell These 5 Assets First...

**Channel:** Nick Davis, CFP®
**Published:** 2026-01-11
**URL:** https://youtube.com/watch?v=QjtIfIyZYF0
**Duration:** 17:10

## Summary

Nick Davis outlines a five-asset liquidation priority framework for pre-retirees, emphasizing that the sequence of selling determines after-tax wealth more than portfolio returns alone. The video uses a client case to show that coordinating withdrawals across taxable, tax-deferred, and tax-free buckets can meaningfully lower effective tax rates. Davis covers the tax treatment of long-term capital gains, REITs, concentrated stock, annuities, and early IRA/401(k) withdrawals. He concludes by showing a planning software comparison of multiple withdrawal orders for a single client.

## Key Takeaways

- Long-term capital gains in taxable brokerage accounts should often be liquidated first because they are taxed at 0–15% for most retirees, well below ordinary income rates.
- REITs, high-dividend stocks, and volatile concentrated positions generate inflexible ordinary income that can push retirees into higher brackets and trigger Medicare IRMAA surcharges.
- Concentrated company stock positions should be diversified before retirement to reduce sequence-of-returns risk and spread the tax impact over multiple years.
- Underperforming annuities and cash-value life insurance products may have better alternatives today, especially given the post-2022 interest rate environment.
- The early years of retirement (after work, before Social Security and RMDs) often represent the lowest tax window for voluntary IRA/401(k) withdrawals.
- Annual tax projections and coordinated multi-account withdrawals are essential for executing a consistent liquidation strategy over a multi-decade retirement.

## Detailed Notes

### Why Liquidation Order Matters [00:00:00]

Davis argues that the sequence of asset sales is as important as—sometimes more important than—investment returns. Selling without a strategy based on convenience can cost hundreds of thousands in avoidable taxes. The goal is to reduce effective tax rate by using lower brackets when available.

### Asset 1 & 2: Taxable Accounts and Income-Heavy Holdings [00:05:32]

Taxable brokerage accounts with long-term gains benefit from 0–15% capital gains rates, versus 22%+ ordinary income rates on IRA withdrawals. REITs and high-dividend stocks produce ordinary income that cannot be timed or deferred, making them harder to manage in retirement; eliminating or trimming these before retirement improves tax bracket control.

### Asset 3 & 4: Concentrated Positions and Underperforming Insurance Products [00:09:40]

Concentrated company stock creates catastrophic sequence-of-returns risk; gradual pre-retirement diversification spreads the tax cost over multiple years. Older annuities and cash-value life insurance often carry high internal fees and low returns relative to current alternatives, and reallocating can free capital for more flexible, tax-efficient income strategies.

### Asset 5 and Client Case Study [00:13:02]

Voluntary early withdrawals from traditional IRAs and 401(k)s in the low-income window before Social Security and RMDs begin can reduce the size of future forced distributions. Davis shows a planning tool comparison in which a taxable-first order produced an estimated lifetime tax bill of $187,000 at an 8.7% effective rate versus $289,000 at 12.43% for an IRA-first order for the same client.
