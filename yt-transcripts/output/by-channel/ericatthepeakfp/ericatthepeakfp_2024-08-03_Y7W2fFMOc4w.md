# Most Americans Will NEVER Retire: Here's How To Time Yours

**Channel:** Eric at The PeakFP
**Published:** 2024-08-03
**URL:** https://youtube.com/watch?v=Y7W2fFMOc4w
**Duration:** 17:33

## Summary

This video introduces the 'retirement risk zone,' defined as the 5-10 years before retirement or the early years of retirement itself, as a critical third phase of financial planning distinct from the accumulation and spending phases. CFP Eric Amzalag walks through a hypothetical couple's scenario using both a spreadsheet calculator and Right Capital financial planning software to illustrate how to refine savings targets and run stress tests. The video argues that financial planning tools alone are insufficient and should be paired with spreadsheet-based sanity checks. A key message is that retirees should proactively model forced early retirement scenarios, since data shows most Americans retire earlier than planned.

## Key Takeaways

- The retirement risk zone is a distinct third phase of retirement planning covering the 5-10 years before (or just after) leaving work.
- Financial planning tools like Right Capital provide probability-of-success estimates but do not tell you what specific actions to take to improve outcomes.
- Pairing a spreadsheet calculator with financial planning software helps triangulate required savings rates and starting portfolio sizes.
- Most Americans retire about 3 years earlier than planned, so stress-testing a forced early retirement scenario is prudent.
- A guardrail spending strategy can extend portfolio longevity when probability of success falls to marginal levels.
- Spending reductions in retirement are the least reliable lever because they depend on future behavioral changes that may not materialize.

## Detailed Notes

### Defining the Retirement Risk Zone [00:00:00]

The retirement risk zone is the 5-10 years before retirement (or early years of retirement if planning was neglected). During this phase, retirees are near peak wealth and peak savings capacity, and portfolio volatility begins to carry outsized consequences. The presenter argues every retiree passes through this zone, knowingly or not.

### Refining the Accumulation Plan [00:02:05]

The first task in the retirement risk zone is assessing whether current savings behaviors will produce sufficient assets by the retirement deadline. A downloadable spreadsheet calculator is demonstrated alongside Right Capital to illustrate that financial planning tools show probability of success but not what to do to improve it. A hypothetical couple needs $1,113,000 at retirement and must save approximately $2,345/month to reach it.

### Scenario Modeling and Contingency Planning [00:10:25]

The second task is constructing turn-by-turn directions for retirement, including if-then scenarios for early forced retirement. The video models Steve and Amanda being forced to retire at 67 (dropping success from 76% to 63%) and at 65 (dropping to 50%), then tests encore career income of $40,000-$75,000, additional savings, and $300/month spending cuts as remedies. The analogy used is that a car needs a runway to decelerate safely rather than crashing abruptly.

### Key Lessons and Call to Action [00:16:36]

The presenter urges viewers to begin retirement risk zone planning at least 5 years before their target retirement date, and recommends visiting Peak Financial Planning's website for personalized help navigating this phase.
