# An HONEST Review Of What Retirement Looks Like With $2.2M

**Channel:** Eric at The PeakFP
**Published:** 2025-06-21
**URL:** https://youtube.com/watch?v=gC2RxJLufEw
**Duration:** 13:48

## Summary

This case study follows William and Sherry Gallagher, a 60-year-old couple with $2.2M in mixed pre-tax and post-tax accounts, as Eric Amzalag uses Income Lab guardrail software to determine their maximum safe spending. Despite a 7.7% initial withdrawal rate, the plan becomes sustainable once Social Security income is layered in, reducing portfolio dependence to about 4% by 2032. Stress tests against the GFC and dot-com crash show only minor spending adjustments would have been required.

## Key Takeaways

- Clients with sufficient savings often feel paralyzed not from lack of money, but from lack of a clear spending framework.
- Guardrail planning calculates a spending capacity based on client-defined willingness to make future spending adjustments, not just a fixed withdrawal rate.
- A high early withdrawal rate (7.7%) can be sustainable if near-term Social Security income is expected to reduce portfolio dependence over time.
- The required portfolio return (4.8% net of inflation) is significantly below the S&P 500's 80-year average, meaning outsized market performance is not needed.
- Even in historically severe downturns like the GFC, a properly structured guardrail plan requires only small spending reductions.
- Knowing the most they can spend—even if they never spend up to it—gives clients confidence and permission to enjoy retirement.

## Detailed Notes

### Client Profile and Problem Statement [00:00:00]

William and Sherry Gallagher, both 60, have $2.2M across a traditional IRA ($1.35M), Sherry's IRA ($375K), a joint brokerage ($275K), cash ($28K at 3%), and a paid-off Texas home worth $425K. They plan $100K/year in spending and feel paralyzed despite having enough on paper.

### Determining Tolerance for Spending Adjustments [00:03:19]

Eric explains that the frequency of spending adjustments is inversely related to their size: a willingness to accept larger cuts means fewer cuts over a retirement. William and Sherry chose 80% confidence of never needing a spending adjustment, which sets the guardrail parameters in Income Lab.

### Calculating Spending Capacity [00:05:11]

With guardrail settings established, Income Lab generates a 2026 spending capacity of $154,000/year ($12,800/month gross). This results in a 7.7% initial withdrawal rate since Social Security has not yet started. Sherry's SS begins in 2028, William's in 2032, progressively reducing portfolio dependence.

### Stress Testing with Historic Market Downturns [00:11:06]

Simulations of retiring just before both the GFC and the dot-com crash show minimal impact: the GFC scenario triggers one 5% spending reduction in year two, while the dot-com scenario results in nearly no net change. Both cases confirm the plan's resilience to early-retirement market shocks.
