# One System That Makes TAX A Non-Issue For Retirees

**Channel:** Eric at The PeakFP
**Published:** 2026-02-28
**URL:** https://youtube.com/watch?v=UP8WD8wpz8o
**Duration:** 29:58

## Summary

Eric Amzalag, CFP and RICP at Peak Financial Planning, presents a structured, repeatable system for retirement tax planning built around four distinct tax seasons every retiree passes through: post-employer Cobra coverage, pre-Medicare private insurance, Medicare with IRMAA, and the RMD phase. Using a real spreadsheet walkthrough for a hypothetical couple, Jimmy and Janet (both 62, $4M in investable assets), he demonstrates why the system must evaluate constraints before opportunities and opportunities before execution sequence. The video shows that Jimmy and Janet cannot qualify for ACA subsidies without sacrificing their lifestyle, that Roth conversions of roughly $60,000 per year for 12 years are optimal given their spending surplus, and that simultaneous gain harvesting from a $400,000 concentrated stock position can be coordinated with conversions to reduce both tax and concentration risk. The total payoff: $896,000 in lifetime tax savings and a meaningfully larger tax-free Roth estate.

## Key Takeaways

- Tax planning in retirement follows four seasons (Cobra, pre-Medicare, Medicare/IRMAA, RMD phase), and opportunities available in an earlier season may disappear in a later one.
- Before running any Roth conversion math, determine the constraints first: identify MAGI cliffs, IRMAA brackets, and RMD start dates that limit or shape the opportunity set.
- For high-spending retirees with large after-tax accounts, qualifying for ACA subsidies often requires lifestyle sacrifices that outweigh the subsidy benefit — the math must be checked explicitly.
- Roth conversions should be reverse-engineered from spending surplus, not guessed; if a plan has no surplus, conversions will harm rather than help the retiree.
- Simultaneous gain harvesting from concentrated stock positions coordinates elegantly with Roth conversions because both occur in low-income early retirement years before Social Security and RMDs stack income.
- Without proactive planning, a $2M pre-tax IRA can grow to $5M by RMD age, generating $212,000+ in forced annual distributions at a 22% or higher bracket — a problem that is almost entirely preventable with 10-12 years of micro Roth conversions.

## Detailed Notes

### The Four Seasons Framework [00:03:29]

The system segments retirement into four tax seasons: (1) Cobra continuation coverage post-employment for up to 18 months; (2) pre-Medicare private insurance where MAGI must stay below 400% FPL (~$84,600 for couples) to access ACA subsidies; (3) Medicare, where IRMAA surcharges on Parts B and D create new income thresholds; and (4) the RMD phase beginning at age 73, where forced pre-tax distributions largely determine the tax bracket for the rest of life. The advisor's key insight is that the sequence matters — opportunities in Season 2 may be destroyed by inaction in Season 1.

### ACA Subsidy Evaluation for Jimmy and Janet [00:08:43]

Jimmy and Janet have $2M in after-tax brokerage assets, which might suggest they could limit income to stay below the $84,600 MAGI cliff. However, their $199,000 desired spending requires harvesting from those accounts, generating capital gains that push MAGI above the cliff regardless of account structure. The standard deduction reduces taxes but does not reduce MAGI for ACA purposes. The advisor concludes that qualifying for subsidies would require sacrificing $50,000-$100,000 of annual spending to save $15,000-$20,000 in premiums — a clearly unfavorable trade-off.

### Roth Conversion Strategy and RMD Mitigation [00:15:03]

With ACA subsidies off the table, the MAGI ceiling constraint disappears, opening the door for Roth conversions. Without conversions, Jimmy and Janet's $2M pre-tax account would grow to $5M by RMD age (75), generating $212,000 in forced distributions stacked on top of $84,000 in taxable Social Security benefits. The optimal strategy is $60,000/year in Roth conversions for 12 years, reverse-engineered using Holistiplan to fit within their $17,000 annual spending surplus. This reduces the age-75 RMD from $212,000 to $56,000 and grows the Roth account from $25,000 to $1.4M.

### Concentrated Stock Harvesting and Final Results [00:23:16]

The $400,000 concentrated single-stock position in Jimmy and Janet's taxable account presents both concentration risk and embedded capital gains. The advisor harvests gains annually up to the 0% or 15% long-term capital gains threshold, coordinated with Roth conversion income projections in Holistiplan. This reduces risk, resets cost basis, and uses tax-efficient proceeds to fund Roth conversion tax costs. The combined plan reduces their average lifetime tax rate from 20.8% to 17.1%, saves $896,000 in lifetime taxes, and improves net legacy — all through a 12-year sequence of coordinated annual decisions rather than a single large conversion.
