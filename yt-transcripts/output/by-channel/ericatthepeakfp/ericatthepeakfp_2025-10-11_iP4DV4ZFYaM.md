# Once I Show Retirees This, They Stop Worrying About Taxes

**Channel:** Eric at The PeakFP

## Summary

Eric Amzalag systematically builds a zero-federal-tax retirement income framework, starting with the 2025 standard deduction and 0% capital gains thresholds and layering in tax gain harvesting, tax lot selling, and tax loss harvesting. He highlights three traps that undermine simplistic zero-tax plans — IRMAA Medicare surcharges, ACA subsidy cliffs, and state-level capital gains taxes — then shows how strategic use of cash reserves and Roth withdrawals can neutralize all three while allowing up to $237,500 in annual spending. The video distinguishes between income as reported for tax purposes and actual spendable cash, a distinction the advisor identifies as the key insight separating adequate from masterful retirement tax planning.

## Key Takeaways

- The $47,500 standard deduction plus $96,700 in 0% capital gains together create $144,200 of income that is federally tax-free before any additional optimization.
- Tax lot selling allows a retiree to select which specific shares to sell, controlling exactly how much gain is realized and keeping total capital gains under the 0% threshold.
- Tax loss harvesting can offset additional gains beyond the 0% threshold, creating more spending money without increasing taxable income.
- Capital gains count as MAGI for both IRMAA and ACA subsidy calculations, meaning a technically tax-free strategy can still cost thousands annually in Medicare or insurance premiums.
- Roth withdrawals are invisible to IRMAA, ACA subsidy calculations, and most state income calculations, making them the preferred tool to fill spending gaps that would otherwise breach thresholds.
- Cash reserves allow timing control: retirees can avoid realizing gains in high-income years and instead harvest gains when income is low, then replenish cash in favorable years.
- Accessing $237,500 in spendable income with zero federal tax requires combining all five tools (pre-tax withdrawals, capital gain sales, loss harvesting, cash, and Roth), and the reported MAGI will be far lower than the cash actually received.

## Detailed Notes

### Foundation: Standard Deduction and 0% Capital Gains [00:00:32]

In 2025, a married couple aged 65 or older can shield up to $47,500 from federal income tax through the standard deduction, the over-65 additions, and the new senior bonus deduction under the One Big Beautiful Bill Act. Additionally, long-term capital gains up to $96,700 are taxed at 0% for married joint filers, creating $144,200 of combined tax-free income capacity before any advanced strategies.

### Three Core Harvesting Techniques [00:01:37]

Tax gain harvesting involves selling appreciated holdings up to the 0% capital gains threshold and optionally reinvesting to reset the cost basis, permanently eliminating future embedded gains. Tax lot selling uses specific identification accounting to sell only the lots that produce the desired gain amount, giving precise control over taxable income. Tax loss harvesting sells positions at a loss to offset other gains, with losses carrying forward to future years if unused, and can enable gains beyond the 0% threshold to be realized at zero net tax impact.

### Three Hidden Traps [00:07:30]

IRMAA Medicare surcharges are assessed two years later based on that year's MAGI; capital gains count toward MAGI even if federally untaxed, so a retiree could avoid income tax while still triggering Medicare premium increases of thousands of dollars annually. ACA subsidy cliffs can eliminate health insurance subsidies entirely if income crosses a threshold by even one dollar, making capital gains dangerous for pre-65 retirees. State taxes vary widely: California taxes capital gains as ordinary income at up to 13.3%, and many states provide no 0% bracket or lower standard deductions than the federal level.

### Advanced Strategies: Cash and Roth Layering [00:11:47]

Adding a cash reserve allows retirees to avoid realizing gains in years when MAGI is already close to a threshold, filling the spending gap from non-reportable cash instead. Roth withdrawals are fully invisible to IRMAA, ACA subsidies, and most state income taxes, making them the ideal marginal dollar when any threshold is at risk. The full $237,500 example combines a $47,500 traditional IRA withdrawal, $135,000 from selling appreciated brokerage positions, $30,000 from loss harvesting, and $25,000 from a Roth IRA — resulting in zero federal tax and a MAGI well below thresholds.
