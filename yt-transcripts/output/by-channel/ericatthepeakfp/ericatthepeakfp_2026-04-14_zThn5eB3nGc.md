# Retirement Expert Explains If You Can Retire TOMORROW

**Channel:** Eric at The PeakFP
**Published:** 2026-04-14
**URL:** https://youtube.com/watch?v=zThn5eB3nGc
**Duration:** 14:08

## Summary

Eric Amzalag reframes retirement readiness as five sequential milestones rather than a single savings number. He establishes that Roth conversions belong inside a larger strategy and not as standalone retirement enablers, then walks through calculating the spending gap the portfolio must cover, navigating the complex health insurance landscape (Medicare, Medigap, IRMAA, ACA marketplace), building a multi-year tax-efficient withdrawal roadmap, and finally documenting a guardrails-based contingency plan for market downturns. The video emphasizes that health insurance planning is the milestone that trips up the most retirees.

## Key Takeaways

- Roth conversions are a tax-optimization tool within a broader plan, not a lever that makes retirement possible on their own.
- Retirement readiness is a math problem: total spending minus guaranteed income equals the required portfolio income, which is often much less than people assume.
- Health insurance is the most complex milestone: Medicare has multiple interacting parts, IRMAA surcharges tie premium costs to income management, and pre-65 retirees face ACA marketplace learning curves.
- A tax-efficient withdrawal roadmap spanning 5-10 years can save hundreds of thousands by exploiting low-income windows before RMDs begin.
- A documented contingency plan with specific thresholds and spending adjustment rules is necessary to make market corrections manageable and to avoid fear-driven financial decisions.

## Detailed Notes

### Milestone 1: Roth Conversions as Optimization, Not Enablement [00:00:00]

Roth conversions move money between account types and trigger a tax bill; they do not create new money or increase the income a portfolio can generate. Their real benefit is reducing future RMDs and providing tax-free assets to heirs. Using them as a prerequisite to retirement is a conceptual error that distracts from the fundamentals.

### Milestones 2-3: Gap Calculation and Health Insurance [00:01:46]

Milestone 2 focuses on calculating the gap between desired spending and guaranteed income (Social Security, pension), which is all the portfolio must produce. Milestone 3 covers the health insurance landscape: Medicare's parts (A, B, D, Advantage, Medigap), IRMAA income surcharges, and the ACA private marketplace for pre-65 retirees where modified adjusted gross income drives premium costs and subsidy eligibility.

### Milestones 4-5: Withdrawal Roadmap and Contingency Plan [00:07:55]

Milestone 4 is a multi-year withdrawal roadmap that identifies low-income windows before RMDs to maximize after-tax outcomes, manages MAGI for IRMAA and ACA subsidies, and integrates Roth conversions strategically. Milestone 5 is a documented guardrails plan specifying trigger thresholds, spending adjustment percentages, and recovery conditions, which eliminates the anxiety that makes retirees vulnerable to fear-based financial products.
