# Once Pre-Tax Millionaires Learn This, They Stop Worrying About Tax

**Channel:** Eric at The PeakFP
**Published:** 2026-05-22
**URL:** https://youtube.com/watch?v=fspZHKFuzQc
**Duration:** 14:23

## Summary

Eric Amzalag debunks blanket 'tax time bomb' retirement narratives by modeling the actual dollar-level impact of RMDs at three pre-tax wealth tiers: $5M, $3M, and $1M. Using JP Morgan household spending data and 2026 federal tax brackets, he shows that at $5M the lifetime excess tax burden approaches $1.3M including IRMAA, while at $1M the incremental tax drag is relatively minor. The video argues that most retirement media warns about a problem that statistically affects very few households.

## Key Takeaways

- The RMD 'tax time bomb' narrative is only materially accurate at very high pre-tax balances; at $1M the extra lifetime tax is modest.
- At $5M pre-tax, an estimated $1.1M in extra federal tax and $200K in IRMAA surcharges can result from RMDs over a 21-year retirement.
- The widow/widower scenario worsens tax outcomes significantly because the survivor files as a single filer in narrower brackets.
- Only 4.6% of US households have over $1M in retirement accounts, so most consumers face a problem the media overstates.
- Running the math at your actual balance rather than reacting to fear-based headlines is the key practical takeaway.

## Detailed Notes

### Media Fear vs. Mathematical Reality [00:00:00]

CNBC, Kiplinger, Schwab, and Experian all use alarming language about RMDs. The host argues these warnings are real in principle but skip the key variable: how much money is actually in pre-tax accounts. The video commits to running the math at three specific balance levels.

### $5M Pre-Tax Scenario [00:00:48]

At $5M, the age-75 RMD is ~$203K vs. a ~$135K spending need (based on JP Morgan data), forcing ~$68K of unwanted taxable income. The extra federal tax is ~$16K/year, plus $5,800/year in IRMAA for the couple. Over 21 years this accumulates to ~$1.1M in extra federal tax and ~$200K in IRMAA. A surviving widow could add another ~$375K in taxes.

### $3M Pre-Tax Scenario [00:05:55]

At $3M the RMD picture is similar in structure but smaller in magnitude. The extra annual tax burden is ~$18K, with no IRMAA as a married couple. The lifetime extra tax is estimated between $500K and close to $1M once the survivor scenario is included. The host cautions against treating the $3M case identically to the $5M case.

### $1M Pre-Tax Scenario and Broader Context [00:09:07]

At $1M the forced RMD excess over spending needs is small, the marginal extra tax is only ~$5K/year, and IRMAA is not triggered at age 75. The 21-year lifetime extra tax estimate is up to $250K, but heavily back-loaded. The Congressional Research Service reports only 4.6% of US households exceed $1M in retirement accounts, meaning most people face a problem the media dramatizes disproportionately.
