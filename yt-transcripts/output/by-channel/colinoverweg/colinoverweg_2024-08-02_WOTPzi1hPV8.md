  A non-qualified stock option (NSO) is a type of stock option that allows an employee or other service provider to purchase a company's shares at a predetermined price, called the exercise price or strike price, within a specific time period. NSOs are considered non-qualified because they don't meet all the requirements of the IRS to qualify for the same tax-advantaged treatment as incentive stock options (ISOs). When an NSO is exercised, the recipient is taxed on the difference between the exercise price and the fair market value of the shares on the exercise date. This tax includes ordinary income tax, Social Security tax, and Medicare taxes. The recipient may also owe taxes when they sell the shares.
