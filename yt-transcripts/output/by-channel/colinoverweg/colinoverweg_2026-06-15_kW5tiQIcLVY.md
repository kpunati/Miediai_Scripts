## Summary

Colin Overweg analyzes SpaceX's recent IPO valuation and market performance. He examines the company's three core businesses (rockets, Starlink, and xAI), discusses Morgan Stanley's revenue projections of $330 billion by 2030 against Elon Musk's $1 trillion prediction, and evaluates whether the stock is a good investment given its rapid 35% surge from IPO price. The video emphasizes historical context showing that large tech IPOs typically experience significant drawdowns of around 53% median within 12 months, suggesting investors should consider waiting for better entry points.

## Key Takeaways

- SpaceX's $2.4 trillion valuation makes it the sixth largest US company, but historically large tech IPOs experience significant pullbacks after launch.
- The company operates three revenue streams: rocket launches, Starlink satellite connectivity, and xAI artificial intelligence operations.
- Morgan Stanley projects SpaceX revenues to grow from $18 billion to $330 billion by 2030, while Elon Musk claims it could reach $1 trillion.
- Investors should weigh the long-term opportunity against historical patterns showing 53% median drawdowns in major tech IPOs within 12 months post-IPO.
- The video recommends caution and waiting for better entry points rather than chasing the initial IPO surge.

## Detailed Notes

### SpaceX IPO Valuation [00:00:00]

SpaceX IPO'd at $135 per share with an implied valuation of $1.77 trillion. Stock surged 35% in first two trading days, pushing market cap to $2.4 trillion and making it the sixth largest US company by market capitalization.

### Business Segments [00:00:00]

SpaceX operates three core segments: rocket and aerospace launch services, Starlink providing satellite connectivity and Wi-Fi, and xAI artificial intelligence operations. There is also speculation about potential acquisition or integration with Tesla.

### Revenue Projections and Valuation [00:00:00]

Morgan Stanley estimates SpaceX revenue growing from current $18 billion to $160 billion in 2030 and potentially $330 billion. Elon Musk tweeted the company could reach approximately $1 trillion in revenue by 2030, significantly higher than analyst projections.

### Historical IPO Performance Analysis [00:00:00]

Historical data from Peter Maluke, CEO of a major RAAS provider, shows that major tech IPOs over the past 15 years have experienced median drawdowns of 53% within 12 months after IPO pricing. SpaceX would be unusual if it maintained positive returns throughout the period.
