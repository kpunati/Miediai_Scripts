# 7 Things Retirees Are Told to Buy (That You DON'T Need)

**Channel:** Kevin Lum, CFP®
**Published:** 2025-11-15
**URL:** https://youtube.com/watch?v=axIgg9Yvrhw
**Duration:** 15:51

## Summary

Kevin Lum walks through seven common retirement purchases that CFPs often find unnecessary for most retirees, opening with life insurance after dependents no longer rely on the insured's income. He dedicates substantial time to variable annuities, explaining their high fee structures and poor net returns relative to index funds. He also covers vacation homes, high-expense-ratio active funds, extended warranties, direct investment real estate, and complex structured products. A recurring theme is that fear-based sales pitches exploit retirees who lack a concrete financial plan, and that simplicity and planning are the best defenses.

## Key Takeaways

- Life insurance for retirees whose dependents are self-sufficient and debts are paid is typically paying for peace of mind that is no longer necessary.
- Variable annuities combine high fee structures (often 3-4% annually) with market risk, making them rarely appropriate despite their widespread promotion.
- Vacation homes are frequently underused; the all-in annual cost often exceeds the cost of renting comparable accommodations.
- Over 15 years, 88% of active managers underperform their benchmark, making high-expense-ratio funds difficult to justify.
- Direct rental real estate is rarely passive income; the combination of maintenance, vacancies, and management often yields far less than projected.
- Complex financial products (structured notes, buffered ETFs, market-linked CDs) tend to benefit the seller more than the buyer; if you cannot explain how the product makes money, avoid it.
- A clear, stress-tested retirement plan reduces the emotional power of fear-based product pitches.

## Detailed Notes

### Life Insurance: When the Need Disappears [00:00:00]

Life insurance exists to replace income for dependents. If a spouse has independent retirement assets, children are self-sufficient, and mortgages are paid off, the insurable interest is gone. Exceptions include special-needs dependents, estate tax situations, and certain business arrangements, which apply to roughly 10% of retirees.

### Variable Annuities: High Fees and Hidden Risk [00:02:06]

Variable annuities are sold, not bought — often at free dinner seminars. Annual fees of 3-4% are common, yet the investor still bears market risk. A reviewed client had a $500,000 balance barely break even over 7 years, and a Wall Street Journal-cited case showed a $28.3 million variable annuity shrink to $26.3 million over 6 years while the market doubled. Fixed or immediate annuities providing guaranteed income are a more defensible alternative.

### Vacation Homes and Direct Real Estate [00:04:14]

Vacation homes often see only 3-4 weeks of actual use, while fixed costs (property tax, HOA, maintenance, insurance) accumulate year-round. Renting accommodations on a trip-by-trip basis is typically cheaper and eliminates ownership stress. For investment real estate, retirees often project $1,000/month in net income but net far less after expenses; one example netted $200/month after all costs.

### Active Funds, Extended Warranties, and Complex Products [00:06:38]

Morningstar data shows 88% of active managers underperform over 15 years, making high expense ratios hard to justify. Extended warranties are profit centers for retailers; self-insuring via an emergency fund is usually superior. Structured notes, buffered ETFs, and market-linked CDs sacrifice transparency and liquidity, and gold sold by third parties often charges excessive fees and storage costs.

### The Common Thread: Fear and the Role of a Plan [00:14:02]

Most unnecessary purchases are driven by fear — of market crashes, of dying without leaving anything, of missing out. Retirees with a concrete, stress-tested financial plan find that fear-based pitches lose their power. The video offers free access to Right Capital planning software and encourages building a plan before evaluating any product.
