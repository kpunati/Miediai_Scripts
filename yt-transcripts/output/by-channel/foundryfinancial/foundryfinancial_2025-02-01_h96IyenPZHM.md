# 6 Essential Steps Before You Retire - Don't Miss These!

**Channel:** Kevin Lum, CFP®
**Published:** 2025-02-01

**Duration:** 19:39

## Summary

Kevin Lum presents a practical six-step framework for retirement preparation, using RightCapital financial planning software to illustrate each concept. The video covers envisioning retirement lifestyle, building a dual-bucket budget (essential vs. non-essential expenses), mapping income sources and investment accounts, reducing debt and building cash reserves, planning for healthcare costs including Medicare options, and implementing tax strategies such as Roth conversions. A live software walkthrough using a hypothetical couple (Phil and Claire, each with $600,000 in 401k accounts) demonstrates how to aggregate assets and run retirement projections.

## Key Takeaways

- Create two budget categories before retiring: essential bare-bones expenses and non-essential nice-to-haves, to enable confident spending in early retirement.
- Know your guaranteed income sources (Social Security, pension, annuity) and whether they include inflation riders, since most pensions and annuities do not adjust for inflation.
- Entering retirement debt-free with a cash reserve of at least six months of expenses improves both plan stability and psychological confidence.
- Healthcare planning is especially critical for early retirees (before age 65) who do not yet qualify for Medicare and must navigate COBRA or exchange plans.
- Starting Roth conversions years before retirement, rather than waiting, can significantly increase long-term tax efficiency.
- Use financial planning software like RightCapital or Boldin to run scenarios comparing Social Security timing, Roth conversion amounts, and asset allocation impacts.

## Detailed Notes

### Envisioning Your Retirement and Budgeting [00:00:00]

Retirees need to know what they are retiring to, not just what they are retiring from; depression rises when people retire without a plan. Two-bucket budgeting (essential and non-essential) helps people confidently spend money early in retirement when spending tends to be higher.

### Assessing Income Sources and Investment Accounts [00:04:09]

Key numbers to gather include guaranteed income (Social Security, pension, annuity), whether those income sources have inflation riders, and the split between tax-deferred (traditional IRA, 401k), tax-free (Roth IRA), and non-qualified brokerage accounts. Asset allocation across cash, stocks, and bonds should also be documented.

### RightCapital Software Walkthrough [00:05:12]

Kevin walks through setting up a hypothetical couple in RightCapital, entering income, Social Security estimates, 401k balances, and a taxable brokerage account. The dashboard shows the breakdown between qualified (tax-deferred) and non-qualified assets and the overall stock/bond allocation, forming the foundation of a retirement plan.

### Debt Reduction and Cash Reserves [00:12:32]

Entering retirement with minimal high-interest debt and a cash reserve of at least six months of expenses is recommended. Having a war chest of cash is especially important for those planning Roth conversions, as it avoids the need to sell investments in a down market to cover living expenses.

### Healthcare Planning [00:13:35]

Early retirees must plan how to cover healthcare before Medicare eligibility at age 65, with options including COBRA, exchange plans, and employer plans. For those going on Medicare, the choice between traditional Medicare with a Medigap policy versus Medicare Advantage is presented, with a preference expressed for traditional Medicare as it functions more like a PPO.

### Tax Strategy and Roth Conversions [00:16:42]

Early tax planning and Roth conversions before retirement can substantially reduce lifetime tax liability. RightCapital can model the impact of delaying Roth conversions by two to three years, showing the compounding effect. Delaying Social Security creates more room for Roth conversions in the lower tax brackets before Social Security income begins adding to taxable income.
