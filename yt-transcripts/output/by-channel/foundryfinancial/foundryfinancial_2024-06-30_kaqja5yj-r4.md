# The BEST DIY Retirement Software? A CFP® Reviews New Retirement.

**Channel:** Kevin Lum, CFP®
**Published:** 2024-06-30
**URL:** https://youtube.com/watch?v=kaqja5yj-r4
**Duration:** 20:14

## Summary

Kevin Lum, a CFP at Foundry Financial, builds a live retirement plan in New Retirement software for a hypothetical couple (Phil and Claire, both 60, planning to retire at 62) and compares the experience to RightCapital, the advisor-grade software he uses with clients. The walkthrough covers assumptions, expenses, Social Security income, investment accounts, and Roth conversion scenarios. Kevin concludes that New Retirement is the best DIY option due to its educational prompts and support, but prefers RightCapital's tax center, investment return modeling, and Roth conversion flexibility for more sophisticated analysis.

## Key Takeaways

- New Retirement is recommended for DIY investors who want robust planning tools with educational guidance and customer support.
- RightCapital is better suited for financial advisors and clients with complex tax situations due to more granular control over return assumptions and withdrawal sequences.
- New Retirement's default inflation assumptions (2-3%) are lower than historical averages and may cause plans to appear more optimistic than warranted.
- The same retirement scenario can show very different probability-of-success figures (75% vs. 89%) depending on which software is used and how assumptions are configured.
- Roth conversion estimates can vary dramatically between platforms — the ability to set heirs' estimated tax rates in RightCapital captures additional value that New Retirement misses.
- New Retirement's strength lies in tax visualization, estate projections, and Social Security timing exploration for non-advisors.

## Detailed Notes

### Background: Why New Retirement Over RightCapital for DIY [00:00:00]

Kevin previously gave viewers access to RightCapital, but over 5,000 signups revealed that the advisor-designed software was too complex for DIY users. New Retirement emerged as his recommendation for self-directed planners due to its guided interface, learning tools, and customer support.

### Setting Assumptions and Inputs [00:01:02]

The demo uses Phil and Claire (both 60, retiring at 62) with $8,000/month in expenses, $1,000/month pre-Medicare healthcare costs, $5,200/month combined Social Security at full retirement age, $1.2 million in retirement accounts, and $300,000 in cash at 5% return. Kevin flags New Retirement's inflation range (2-3%) as below historical averages and notes that user-entered return assumptions introduce significant subjectivity.

### Plan Results and Probability of Success [00:07:20]

New Retirement shows a 75% chance of success with $3 million remaining in optimistic scenarios and a 90% chance of running out of money in pessimistic scenarios by 2054. Kevin argues this pessimistic view may make people unnecessarily nervous, noting RightCapital gives the same couple an 89% success probability even after deducting a 1% advisor fee.

### Roth Conversion Analysis and Platform Comparison [00:12:36]

New Retirement's Roth optimizer suggests three conversions adding approximately $719,000 in estate value and saving $221,000 in taxes. RightCapital, with more aggressive conversion modeling and the ability to set heirs' tax rates, projects savings of nearly $1 million. Kevin identifies the Tax Center as New Retirement's main weakness and praises RightCapital's withdrawal-sequence controls and historical return modeling based on actual portfolio data.
