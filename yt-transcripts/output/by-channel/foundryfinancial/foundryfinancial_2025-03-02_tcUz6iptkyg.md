# 87% Of People Get These 3 Retirement Topics WRONG — Test Yourself!

**Channel:** Kevin Lum, CFP®

## Summary

Kevin Lum, CFP, addresses three retirement topics that financial advisors and individuals frequently misunderstand. He clarifies that the annual gift tax exclusion ($18,000 in 2024, $19,000 at time of recording) is a reporting threshold, not a hard tax trigger, and that gifts exceeding it simply require IRS Form 709 filing against the lifetime estate exemption. He then distinguishes the two Roth IRA five-year rules: one governs earnings withdrawals and requires only that any Roth was opened five or more years ago, while the other applies per-conversion and can be bypassed if the account holder is over 59.5. Finally, he explains the Social Security earnings limit before full retirement age and notes that withheld benefits are recalculated and restored later.

## Key Takeaways

- Exceeding the annual gift exclusion requires filing Form 709 but does not produce an immediate tax liability unless the lifetime exemption (over $13 million in 2024) is also exceeded.
- There are two separate Roth IRA five-year rules: the first applies to earnings and requires any Roth to have been open five or more years; the second applies per-conversion and is waived after age 59.5.
- Roth contributions can always be withdrawn tax- and penalty-free at any time regardless of account age.
- The Social Security earnings limit before full retirement age causes temporary benefit withholding, not permanent loss; benefits are recalculated upward once the recipient reaches full retirement age.
- The annual per-person gift exclusion is indexed to inflation and was $19,000 per individual or $38,000 per couple as of early 2025.

## Detailed Notes

### Gift Tax Misconceptions [00:01:04]

Most people believe exceeding the annual gift limit triggers an immediate tax bill. In reality, gifts above the annual exclusion require filing IRS Form 709, which tallies cumulative lifetime gifts against the estate tax exemption ($13.6 million for single filers in 2024). No tax is owed unless total lifetime gifts plus estate value exceed that threshold.

### Annual Exclusion Amounts [00:04:12]

The 2024 annual exclusion was $18,000 per person; at the time of recording (February 2025) it had risen to $19,000 for a single filer and $38,000 for a married couple. The exclusion is per recipient, so a couple with ten children could give each child $38,000 without any lifetime-limit impact.

### Roth IRA Five-Year Rules [00:05:14]

Rule one: earnings cannot be withdrawn tax-free until the Roth has been open at least five years and the owner is 59.5 or older; only one five-year clock runs across all Roth accounts. Rule two: each individual Roth conversion starts its own five-year clock; early withdrawal of converted amounts before five years or age 59.5 may incur penalties, but this rule is irrelevant for those already over 59.5.

### Social Security Earnings Limit [00:09:26]

Collecting Social Security before full retirement age while still working triggers a $1 withholding for every $2 earned above the annual threshold ($22,320 in 2024). Once the recipient reaches full retirement age the earnings limit disappears entirely, and any previously withheld benefits are added back through a recalculated higher monthly payment.
