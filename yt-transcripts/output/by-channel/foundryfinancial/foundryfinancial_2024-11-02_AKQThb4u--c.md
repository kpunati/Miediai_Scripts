# If you’re in your 50s or 60s — 5 CRITICAL financial moves.

**Channel:** Kevin Lum, CFP®
**Published:** 2024-11-02
**URL:** https://youtube.com/watch?v=AKQThb4u--c
**Duration:** 13:29

## Summary

This video from Kevin Lum of Foundry Financial addresses five action-oriented financial moves for people in their 50s and 60s. The moves cover building flexibility through non-retirement savings, eliminating debt, optimizing portfolios to reflect actual risk tolerance, establishing proper estate planning documents, and running Roth conversion calculations even while still working. Lum argues that starting Roth conversions before retirement can have a significantly larger long-term impact than waiting until income drops in retirement.

## Key Takeaways

- Prioritize building taxable brokerage accounts and savings outside qualified retirement accounts to create flexibility in early retirement.
- Pay down all high-interest debt and ideally the mortgage before retiring to reduce fixed costs and stress.
- Evaluate portfolio risk based on whether a market downturn would force a delay to retirement, not simply based on age.
- Set up a will, power of attorney, healthcare directive, and review beneficiaries; consider a revocable living trust.
- Run Roth conversion calculations now rather than waiting for retirement — converting to the top of the 22% or 24% bracket while still employed may be advantageous.
- Paying Roth conversion taxes from outside qualified accounts significantly amplifies the long-term benefit.

## Detailed Notes

### Move 1: Save Outside Retirement Accounts [00:00:00]

Lum recommends building cash reserves and taxable brokerage accounts alongside retirement contributions. Non-qualified accounts provide flexibility for early retirement spending, Roth conversions, and emergency needs that retirement accounts cannot easily supply.

### Move 2: Pay Down Debt [00:03:08]

Eliminating high-interest debt first and then working down the interest ladder is recommended. Lum argues that entering retirement debt-free (or at minimum mortgage-free) dramatically reduces financial stress and simplifies cash flow management.

### Move 3: Optimize the Portfolio [00:04:13]

Portfolio optimization near retirement means evaluating risk tolerance and diversification, not automatically shifting into bonds based on age. The key question is whether you could tolerate delaying retirement if markets fell sharply. Rolling a 401k to an IRA rollover account may give more control as you approach retirement.

### Move 4: Set Up an Estate Plan [00:07:21]

An estate plan should include a will, limited power of attorney, healthcare directive, and updated beneficiaries on all accounts. Lum recommends exploring a revocable living trust as a way to protect heirs and simplify estate settlement, noting it is not just for the wealthy.

### Move 5: Consider Early Roth Conversions [00:09:28]

Using a case study of a couple aged 54 with $1.5 million in qualified accounts, Lum illustrates that converting to the top of the 24% tax bracket before retirement could produce a significantly larger tax-adjusted portfolio than waiting until retirement. Conversions funded from outside the qualified accounts amplify long-term savings.
