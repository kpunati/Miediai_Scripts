# Technology is Separating Advisors. It Could IMPACT You.

**Channel:** Kevin Lum, CFP®
**Published:** 2024-08-31
**URL:** https://youtube.com/watch?v=83gqTMFwh44
**Duration:** 16:28

## Summary

Kevin Lum explains why technology is separating financial advisors into two tiers: those still doing manual or minimal planning and those using purpose-built fintech to deliver optimized retirement, tax, and income outcomes. He provides live demonstrations of five tools he uses — Right Capital for holistic retirement modeling, Holistiplan for side-by-side tax scenario analysis, Income Laboratory for dynamic spending planning, Riskalyze for portfolio risk scoring and stress-testing, and Wealth.com for AI-assisted estate planning. The episode positions this technological shift as democratizing sophisticated advice previously available only to the ultra-wealthy.

## Key Takeaways

- Technology is enabling smaller advisory firms to compete with large institutions by automating complex multi-variable optimizations.
- Right Capital can model Social Security optimization and portfolio adjustments simultaneously to improve a retirement plan's probability of success.
- Holistiplan enables side-by-side tax scenario modeling to reveal how income source mix (ordinary vs. capital gains) dramatically affects effective tax rates.
- Income Laboratory uses historical economic data rather than pure Monte Carlo simulation to set dynamic spending limits tied to actual portfolio performance.
- Riskalyze quantifies portfolio risk with a score and stress-tests against historical market events like 2008.
- Consumers should ask advisors: 'What is one piece of new technology you've implemented to better serve clients?' to assess whether their advisor is keeping pace.
- Advisors who only manage assets without tax and income planning are delivering suboptimal value, particularly for those approaching or in retirement.

## Detailed Notes

### The Case for Technology in Financial Planning [00:00:00]

Lum argues that technology is separating advisors because retirement planning involves too many intersecting variables to optimize via spreadsheet. The Future Proof conference in California surfaces emerging fintech tools. Democratization of this technology is also enabling DIY investors to access better planning tools (e.g., New Retirement) than were previously available.

### Right Capital and Holistiplan Demonstrations [00:03:10]

Right Capital demonstration shows Social Security and portfolio optimization raising a sample couple's success probability from 65% to 92%. Holistiplan compares two scenarios with $125,000 total income: a Roth conversion plus long-term capital gains mix versus an IRA-only distribution. The capital-gains-optimized scenario produces a 5.1% effective tax rate versus 11.5% — illustrating the dollar value of income layering strategies.

### Income Laboratory, Riskalyze, and Wealth.com [00:09:30]

Income Laboratory replaces Monte Carlo simulation with historically-calibrated algorithms to set personalized spending guardrails; a demo account with $2.4M shows monthly spending capacity of ~$13,800, declining modestly to ~$13,150 in a 28% market drawdown. Riskalyze assigns a 0-100 risk score and stress-tests portfolios against historical scenarios including 2008 (down ~17% vs. S&P -37%) and 2013 bull market. Wealth.com provides AI-assisted estate planning document generation.
