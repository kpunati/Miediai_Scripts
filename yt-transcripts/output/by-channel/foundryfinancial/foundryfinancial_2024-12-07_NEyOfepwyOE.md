# The 7 MOST IMPORTANT Things to Own in Retirement

**Channel:** Kevin Lum, CFP®
**Published:** 2024-12-07
**URL:** https://youtube.com/watch?v=NEyOfepwyOE
**Duration:** 16:16

## Summary

This video is a lifestyle-oriented counterpart to purely financial retirement content, listing seven investments that clients and the advisor personally report adding value in retirement. The presenter argues that retirement planning must extend beyond savings and income to include physical health, comfort, mobility, and having reliable professional guidance. Each recommendation is grounded in practical reasoning rather than strict financial calculation, though the emergency fund and financial advisor items do address core financial security.

## Key Takeaways

- A home gym removes friction for staying active and can be roughly cost-neutral compared to a gym membership while supporting long-term health.
- Investing in sleep quality through beds, supplements, or environment adjustments pays dividends in both daily wellbeing and long-term health outcomes.
- A reliable car with good safety features and a long warranty reduces stress and costly repairs; retirement may also be the time for a once-in-a-lifetime 'irresponsible' car purchase for those who can afford it.
- Quality luggage is a practical investment for retirees who plan to travel frequently.
- Home improvements that address long-standing functional complaints deliver ongoing enjoyment and are often best made early in retirement.
- An emergency fund of at least six months of expenses in liquid accounts prevents unexpected costs from derailing the retirement budget.
- A fiduciary financial advisor focused on retirement can add value through tax optimization, portfolio management, and peace of mind that may outweigh their fee.

## Detailed Notes

### Home Gym for Consistent Fitness [00:00:00]

The presenter argues that for most people a home gym eliminates the barriers to consistent exercise. He uses a Tonal system and free weights as examples, noting it is roughly cost-neutral versus a gym membership while improving consistency.

### Sleep Quality as a Health Investment [00:03:06]

Quality sleep is described as priceless, with options including a new mattress, supplements, or environmental changes. Research linking sleep quality to long-term health outcomes is cited as justification for this investment.

### Reliable Car and the Bucket-List Car [00:04:08]

A reliable vehicle with good safety ratings and accessibility features is recommended for functional reasons. However, the presenter acknowledges that early retirement may also be the one appropriate time to make an 'irresponsible' purchase of a dream car, suggesting renting as a lower-cost way to test whether ownership is truly desired.

### Luggage, Home Improvements, and Emergency Fund [00:08:17]

Quality luggage (citing Away as an example brand) eases travel. Home upgrades addressing long-standing pain points, such as a kitchen remodel or backyard improvement, are described as high-enjoyment investments made best early in retirement. An emergency fund of at least six months of expenses in liquid, accessible accounts protects the retirement plan from unexpected shocks.

### Financial Advisor as a Key Investment [00:13:30]

The presenter argues a fiduciary retirement specialist adds value through tax-efficient withdrawal planning, investment rebalancing, and proactive guidance. Vanguard research is cited suggesting advisor value can exceed advisor fees. The presenter acknowledges the self-serving nature of this recommendation while maintaining the substance.
