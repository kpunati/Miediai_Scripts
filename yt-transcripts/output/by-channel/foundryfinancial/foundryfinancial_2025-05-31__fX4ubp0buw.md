# Retirees Spend 80% of Their Income in These 5 Areas! How do you compare?

**Channel:** Kevin Lum, CFP®

## Summary

This video by CFP Kevin Lum uses Bureau of Labor Statistics data to benchmark average retiree spending at approximately $52,000 per year, representing about 80% of pre-retirement expenses. Lum identifies the five largest spending categories — entertainment, food, healthcare, transportation, and housing — which together account for roughly 80% of all retiree expenditures. For each category, he provides practical, low-effort cost reduction strategies that do not require dramatic lifestyle changes. The video serves as a re-release of an earlier episode the presenter deemed important enough to revisit.

## Key Takeaways

- Average retirees spend about $52,141 per year, leaving only about $1,000 surplus, making thin margins vulnerable to inflation.
- Entertainment costs can be dramatically reduced using free senior programs, library apps like Libby, and university audit opportunities.
- Buying store brands (especially Aldi and Whole Foods 365) and non-perishables in bulk at Costco can cut food costs by 20-25%.
- Choosing the right Medicare supplement plan and using generic drugs are the primary levers to reduce healthcare costs.
- Transportation savings include using the Gas Buddy app, switching to per-mile insurance like Metro Mile, and eliminating a second vehicle.
- Housing is the largest category at 36% of spending; renting part of your home on Airbnb, downsizing, or relocating can significantly reduce costs.
- Retirees who reduce spending without reducing lifestyle may be able to retire earlier or more comfortably than originally planned.

## Detailed Notes

### Retiree Spending Benchmarks [00:01:28]

The BLS reports average spending of $52,141 per year for those 65+, about $14,000 less than the general population. With average after-tax income of $53,149, margins are very thin at roughly $1,000 surplus. Retirement spending typically runs at 75-80% of pre-retirement levels, consistent with the national data showing $52,000 vs. $66,928 pre-retirement.

### Entertainment and Food Cost Reduction [00:03:08]

Entertainment averages $240/month (6% of budget). Strategies include senior discounts, auditing university classes, free museum days, Silver Sneakers, and the Libby library app for free ebooks and audiobooks. Food is 12% of budget (~$500/month); using shopping lists, store brands, bulk buying at Costco for non-perishables, and avoiding impulse purchases via grocery delivery apps like Instacart can reduce waste and costs.

### Healthcare and Transportation Savings [00:11:41]

Healthcare averages about $585/month (13% of budget); the key lever is selecting the optimal Medicare supplement plan that covers preferred doctors and medications, using generic drugs, and advocating against billing errors. Transportation (~$600/month) is surprisingly larger than healthcare; strategies include using Gas Buddy to find cheaper gas, shopping auto insurance, using per-mile insurance like Metro Mile, evaluating whether a second vehicle is needed, and renting a car out on Turo.

### Housing: The Largest Category [00:18:33]

Housing is 36% of expenses at ~$1,600/month. Strategies include renting a room or back house on Airbnb (one client earns $3,000/month), downsizing to reduce property taxes and maintenance, relocating to lower cost-of-living states or internationally, moving to a 55+ planned community with HOA-covered maintenance, improving home energy efficiency with potential tax credits, and asking utility companies about senior discount programs.
