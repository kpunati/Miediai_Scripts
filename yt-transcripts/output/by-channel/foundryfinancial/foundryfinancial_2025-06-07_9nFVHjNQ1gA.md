# If I Wanted to Retire Well, This is What I'd Do in 2025

**Channel:** Kevin Lum, CFP®
**Published:** 2025-06-07
**URL:** https://youtube.com/watch?v=9nFVHjNQ1gA
**Duration:** 16:06

## Summary

Kevin Lum argues that the five years before retirement are uniquely powerful for course correction because they typically combine peak earnings with declining household expenses. He walks through five concrete moves: tracking spending precisely, eliminating high-rate debt, maximizing tax-advantaged savings (including HSA and mega backdoor Roth where available), avoiding premature portfolio conservatism, and creating a formal tax map. A case study with Phil and Claire demonstrates how two common emotional mistakes—going too conservative and claiming Social Security early—can devastate a plan, and how reversing them transforms outcomes.

## Key Takeaways

- The five years before retirement are often peak earning years with declining expenses, making them the most impactful window for increasing savings and eliminating debt.
- Tracking actual spending is a prerequisite to retirement planning; many people cannot accurately state their monthly expenses without a budget tool.
- High-interest debt must be eliminated before retirement; every interest dollar paid is a dollar that cannot compound.
- Avoiding premature conservatism is critical: shifting from 35% to 60% stocks more than quadrupled one couple's probability of success before Social Security timing was even addressed.
- Delaying Social Security to maximize benefits, combined with a growth-oriented portfolio, can turn a failing retirement plan into a robust one.
- Tax planning—covering Roth conversions, RMDs, IRMAA, and ACA subsidies—can save hundreds of thousands or over a million dollars over retirement.

## Detailed Notes

### Strategic Move 1: Clarify Living Expenses [00:02:26]

Lum argues that most people approaching retirement cannot accurately state what they spend. Getting precise on spending enables identification of budget leaks, confirmation that the retirement income target is realistic, and redirection of surplus cash toward debt payoff and savings. He recommends Monarch Money for tracking.

### Strategic Move 2: Debt Elimination Sprint [00:04:33]

High-interest debt—credit cards, car loans, HELOCs—should be eliminated before retiring. Low-rate mortgages may be an exception. Carrying debt into retirement adds financial stress and destroys compounding. The debt snowball and avalanche methods are offered as structured payoff frameworks.

### Strategic Move 3: Maximize Tax-Advantaged Savings [00:05:09]

Catch-up contributions are available at 50 or older for 401k plans. Lum cautions against defaulting to traditional pre-tax contributions without considering tax bracket implications. He highlights the HSA as universally beneficial for eligible participants and the mega backdoor Roth as a powerful option for those whose employer plans allow after-tax contributions, potentially exceeding $70,000 per year and adding roughly $500,000 to a Roth account over five years.

### Strategic Moves 4 and 5: Asset Allocation and Tax Map [00:09:14]

Using Phil and Claire (both 60, retiring in 5 years, $1 million 401k, $9,500/month spending goal), Lum shows that going from 35% to 60% stocks raises success probability from 12% to 50%, and adding optimal Social Security timing (age 70) pushes it to 79%. He also identifies tax planning—Roth conversions, RMD projections, IRMAA, ACA subsidies—as potentially saving hundreds of thousands to over a million dollars for those who address it proactively.
