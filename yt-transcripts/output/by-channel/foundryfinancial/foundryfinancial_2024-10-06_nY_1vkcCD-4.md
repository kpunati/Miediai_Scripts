# 7 Essential Accounts You Can't Afford to Ignore

**Channel:** Kevin Lum, CFP®
**Published:** 2024-10-06
**URL:** https://youtube.com/watch?v=nY_1vkcCD-4
**Duration:** 19:45

## Summary

Kevin Lum presents a foundational overview of seven financial accounts everyone should consider, progressing from everyday checking to tax-advantaged retirement vehicles and taxable brokerage accounts. The video pairs each account type with a specific optimization strategy, including using short-term government treasuries or the BOXX ETF to shelter cash interest from state tax, maximizing HSA compounding by paying medical expenses out of pocket and reimbursing years later, and using backdoor and mega backdoor Roth strategies for high earners. Asset location — placing tax-inefficient assets in tax-deferred accounts and growth-oriented assets in taxable accounts — is highlighted as a key but underutilized technique.

## Key Takeaways

- Keep one to two months of expenses in checking and one to two years in a high-yield savings or cash management account for tiered liquidity.
- HSA accounts offer triple tax advantages and the ability to defer reimbursement for qualified expenses indefinitely, enabling tax-free compounding for decades.
- Over-reliance on traditional 401k savings creates concentrated pre-tax balances that can generate large taxable RMDs in retirement.
- High earners above Roth IRA income limits can still contribute via the backdoor Roth — making a non-deductible traditional IRA contribution and immediately converting it.
- Asset location — placing bonds in tax-deferred accounts and stocks in taxable accounts — reduces overall tax drag on a portfolio.
- Government treasury interest is exempt from state income tax, offering a meaningful yield advantage in high-tax states over standard high-yield savings.

## Detailed Notes

### Checking and Cash Management Accounts [00:01:02]

Checking accounts serve as the financial hub for daily transactions; the recommendation is to keep only one to two months of living expenses there. Cash management accounts at brokerages like Schwab or Fidelity can hold government treasuries, money market funds, and brokered CDs, often offering higher yields and state-tax-exempt interest compared to traditional high-yield savings.

### Health Savings Account Strategy [00:04:10]

The HSA offers triple tax advantages: deductible contributions, tax-free growth, and tax-free qualified withdrawals. The advanced strategy advocated by Michael Kitces and highlighted here is to pay all medical expenses out of pocket, invest the HSA fully, and then reimburse yourself years or decades later, effectively converting the account into a tax-free lump-sum for retirement. Lum warns the account is fully taxable to heirs and should be spent down during the owner's lifetime.

### Work Retirement and IRA Accounts [00:06:14]

401k and 403b plans are the primary accumulation vehicle for most workers due to automatic payroll deductions and employer matches, but over-reliance on pre-tax accounts can create substantial RMD-driven tax bills in retirement. The Roth 401k option and Roth IRA should be prioritized when income is below the 22-24% federal bracket. For high earners, the backdoor Roth — contributing to a non-deductible traditional IRA and immediately converting — remains an accessible path to Roth savings.

### Taxable Brokerage Account and Asset Location [00:10:27]

Taxable brokerage accounts are flexible, penalty-free, and can support securities-backed loans as an alternative to HELOCs. The primary mistake is holding tax-inefficient assets such as bonds, dividend-heavy equities, MLPs, or actively managed mutual funds inside taxable accounts. Correct asset location — tax-inefficient assets in tax-deferred accounts and growth equities in taxable accounts — materially reduces long-term tax drag.
