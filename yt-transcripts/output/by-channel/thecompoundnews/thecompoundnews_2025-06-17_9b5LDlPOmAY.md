# Why Amazon is Josh's Favorite Mag 7 | WAYT?

**Channel:** The Compound
**Published:** 2025-06-17
**URL:** https://youtube.com/watch?v=9b5LDlPOmAY
**Duration:** 54:51

## Summary

In this episode of 'What Are Your Thoughts?', Josh Brown and Michael Batnick cover the market's surprisingly muted reaction to escalating Israel-Iran tensions and Strait of Hormuz concerns, attributing it to the diminished weight of energy stocks in the S&P 500 and normalized geopolitical noise. The hosts debate congressional stock trading ethics, analyze Jensen Huang's European sovereign AI push, and examine Nvidia's outsized influence on S&P returns. Josh makes a detailed bull case for Amazon as his top Mag 7 pick based on AWS cloud dominance and accelerating earnings power, while the episode closes with a mystery chart reveal on Oracle's cloud-driven resurgence.

## Key Takeaways

- The energy sector now represents roughly 3% of the S&P 500 versus 12-13% historically, explaining why Middle East geopolitical events no longer move markets as they once did.
- Nvidia contributes approximately 26% of the S&P 500's year-to-date total return and is expected to drive 17% of the index's total earnings growth in 2025.
- Bank of America's global fund manager survey shows investor sentiment has fully recovered to pre-Liberation Day levels, with international stocks now seen as the consensus top performer over the next 5 years — making Mag 7 the contrarian trade.
- Josh Brown makes the bull case for Amazon at $217, arguing AWS is worth more than Amazon's entire $2.3 trillion market cap and that Amazon's earnings power has tripled over five years to $66 billion.
- The OpenAI-Microsoft rift — centered on data sharing from a $3 billion acquisition — could benefit competitors like Anthropic and complicates Microsoft's investment thesis.
- Jensen Huang's European tour promoting 'sovereign AI' signals a new wave of government-level AI infrastructure investment that could benefit Nvidia, Palantir, and defense-tech companies.
- Oracle emerged as the mystery chart winner, with Barron's projecting it will lead in cloud database, cloud applications, and cloud infrastructure — despite being late to the cloud era.

## Detailed Notes

### Geopolitics and the Energy Market's Muted Reaction [00:02:24]

Josh and Michael discuss how oil markets barely responded to Israel-Iran escalation, with WTI crude futures spiking 8% Sunday night before giving it all back within two days. They attribute this to domestic US oil production growth and the energy sector's collapse in S&P weighting from 12-13% two decades ago to roughly 3% today. Josh holds IEO and Chevron as permanent oil-shock hedges in his portfolio but acknowledges both have returned almost nothing over three years.

### Market Sentiment and Congressional Trading Controversy [00:13:50]

The VIX fell 8%, NASDAQ recovered above pre-conflict levels, and Bank of America's fund manager survey shows sentiment back to pre-Liberation Day levels. The hosts note a Wall Street Journal report that more than a dozen House lawmakers and family members made over 700 stock trades in the six days surrounding the April tariff announcement and pause, sparking debate about whether congressional trading restrictions should mirror those imposed on bank employees. Nancy Pelosi's TEM (Tempus AI) call option trade — up 133% — was cited as an example.

### Jensen Huang, Sovereign AI, and Nvidia's Market Dominance [00:22:33]

Jensen Huang's European tour, including meetings with French President Macron and UK PM Starmer at Viva Tech, is framed around 'sovereign AI' — the concept that countries should build domestic AI infrastructure reflecting local culture and data. Unlike previous US tech waves, Europe is not resisting Nvidia. Josh notes Nvidia is up 50% from April lows and contributes 26% of the S&P's YTD total return; the SMH semiconductor ETF is up 47% from its April 8th low with 100% of components above their 50-day moving averages.

### OpenAI vs. Microsoft: A Rift with Broad Implications [00:38:25]

Microsoft owns 49% of OpenAI but the relationship is fracturing over a $3 billion acquisition whose proprietary data OpenAI refuses to share. OpenAI must complete its conversion from nonprofit to full for-profit entity by year-end to access $20 billion in SoftBank financing. OpenAI's annualized revenue run rate doubled from $5.5 billion to $10 billion in six months, with 500 million weekly active ChatGPT users. The hosts suggest a potential OpenAI IPO could be the largest in history, surpassing even Alibaba and Facebook, and that the rift benefits Anthropic and other competitors.

### Make the Case: Amazon as Top Mag 7 Pick [00:45:17]

Josh presents a technical and fundamental bull case for Amazon at $217 targeting $250, noting a clean low-volume retest of the 200-day moving average post-earnings gap and RSI at 64-65. He argues AWS — with 29% global cloud share — could be worth $3 trillion as a standalone company versus Amazon's $2.3 trillion total market cap. Amazon's earnings have tripled over five years to $66 billion, but the stock has underperformed the S&P 500 by 41 percentage points over five years, making it the 'fat pitch' of the Mag 7.

### Mystery Chart Reveal: Oracle's Cloud Resurgence [00:50:42]

The mystery chart revealed Oracle, whose stock has sprung from lows on the back of a Barron's report projecting Oracle will lead in cloud database, cloud applications, and cloud infrastructure. Q4 capital spending reached $9.1 billion, nearly 60% of total revenue, which shareholders are rewarding rather than penalizing. The hosts note CEO Safra Catz is largely unknown to casual tech followers while Larry Ellison, the 80-year-old founder and largest shareholder, still defines the company's identity.
