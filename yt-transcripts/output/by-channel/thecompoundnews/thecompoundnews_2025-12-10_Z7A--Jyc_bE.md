# Netflix vs. Paramount | Animal Spirits 442

**Channel:** The Compound
**Published:** 2025-12-10
**URL:** https://youtube.com/watch?v=Z7A--Jyc_bE
**Duration:** 69:54

## Summary

In episode 442 of Animal Spirits, Michael Batnick and Ben Carlson conduct an end-of-year review covering Batnick's 2025 predictions scorecard, historical Nasdaq 100 return patterns, and the notable outperformance of international stocks over the US market in 2025. The hosts discuss OpenAI's projected multi-year cash burn relative to prior tech giants, the labor market's gradual softening versus still-resilient consumer spending data from Visa and MasterCard CEOs, and the major developing story of Netflix's $72 billion bid for Warner Brothers Discovery competing against a hostile offer from Paramount. Additional topics include Goldman Sachs's acquisition of Innovator ETFs, baby boomer wealth concentration and eventual intergenerational transfer, housing market normalization, and a discussion of wealth without meaning drawing on cultural commentary.

## Key Takeaways

- The Nasdaq 100's five down years since 1995 have all been losses of 30% or more, an often-overlooked historical fact that underscores the index's binary return profile.
- International stocks (ACWI ex-US) have outperformed the US Russell 3000 by roughly 13 percentage points in 2025, a divergence the hosts expect will finally attract mainstream media attention.
- Netflix's bid for Warner Brothers Discovery is framed as a defensive land-grab against YouTube rather than a traditional acquisition, given Netflix's flat subscriber growth and YouTube's rising share of US TV viewing time.
- Consumer hard data from Visa and MasterCard CEOs shows spending remains stable despite increasingly gloomy sentiment surveys, continuing the soft-data vs. hard-data divergence theme.
- Goldman Sachs's acquisition of Innovator ETFs for $2 billion on $28 billion AUM is seen as a validation of the psychological value of options-based buffer strategies over purely mathematical optimization.
- Baby boomers hold $85 trillion in assets and wealth is likely to flow to millennials over coming decades through inheritance rather than being spent down.
- Entertainment industry consolidation is accelerating: a Netflix-Warner deal would likely push Paramount toward merging with Peacock, reshaping the streaming landscape.

## Detailed Notes

### Nasdaq 100 Historical Return Analysis [00:00:05]

Batnick presents a chart showing tech and communication services account for nearly 80% of S&P 500 total return in the 2020s. An analysis of Nasdaq 100 annual returns since 1995 reveals 26 of 31 years were positive, but every single down year was a loss of 30% or worse. The index has still compounded at 15% annually over that span, versus 11% for the S&P 500, inclusive of an 83% peak-to-trough decline during the dot-com bust.

### 2025 Prediction Scorecard [00:00:05]

Batnick reviews his 2025 predictions made with probability-style odds. Successful calls included private investment surge (Blackstone record flows), money staying in money market funds (approaching $8 trillion), and mortgage rates keeping the housing market frozen. Notable misses included equal-weight outperforming cap-weight (failed, market-cap won by a large margin) and a VIX spike to 50 (debated, reached approximately 37-40 intraday on Liberation Day). The MicroStrategy levered ETF blowup was a 3% probability miss — it declined sharply but did not unravel catastrophically.

### International Stocks and 2026 Outlook [00:00:05]

Fidelity's outlook segment highlights international stocks reigniting, with ACWI ex-US up approximately 30% versus the Russell 3000 up 17% in 2025. The dollar fell roughly 10% earlier in the year then stabilized, but valuations have re-rated upward and investor interest in international markets has returned. Carlson flags international outperformance as a top 2026 prediction and expects it to finally become a mainstream media story if the trend continues.

### OpenAI and AI Capital Expenditure Debate [00:00:05]

A chart circulating from Jim Reid via Joe Weisenthal shows OpenAI's projected cash burn to 2029 as far exceeding Amazon's or Spotify's path to profitability. IBM's CEO is quoted saying a 1-gigawatt data center costs approximately $80 billion to fill, and total global AI CapEx commitments may reach $8 trillion — requiring roughly $800 billion in annual profits just to cover interest. Both hosts express skepticism that these projections will materialize, noting Microsoft would likely cut funding well before then.

### Consumer Spending vs. Labor Market Softening [00:00:05]

Kevin O'Leary highlighted that businesses with 20-49 employees shed 74,000 payrolls in November, the sixth decline in seven months and largest since October 2020. However, MasterCard and Visa CEOs both described spending as stable and strong through October and early November, illustrating the ongoing gap between soft sentiment data and hard transaction data. The hosts note unemployment remains below 5% and the economy would need materially worse job losses before consumer spending turns.

### Netflix vs. Paramount Bid for Warner Brothers Discovery [00:00:05]

Netflix had an offer accepted by Warner Brothers Discovery for approximately $72 billion in equity and $10 billion in debt, but Paramount subsequently submitted a higher hostile bid. Polymarket showed Netflix at 38% and Paramount at 43% probability of winning. The hosts debate motivations: Ben Thompson at Stratechery frames this as Netflix defending against YouTube's rising TV viewing share. Ben Carlson worries about HBO's handling, while Batnick sees the deal as potentially bad for movie theaters given Netflix's stated intent to shorten theatrical exclusivity windows. Both note that large entertainment mergers have rarely worked historically.

### Goldman Sachs Acquires Innovator ETFs and Other Market Notes [00:00:05]

Goldman Sachs announced an agreement to buy Innovator ETFs for approximately $2 billion; Innovator managed $28 billion in assets. The hosts, who had covered Innovator founder Bruce Bond since 2018, view the deal as validation of the psychological value of buffer ETF strategies beyond what pure quantitative optimization can capture. Vanguard announced it would allow customers to buy Bitcoin ETFs, which the hosts believe mattered more for sentiment than actual flows. Fidelity reports 654,000 401(k) millionaires, representing 3.2% of account balances.
