# Yes, You May Sell Some Nvidia Now | WAYT?

**Channel:** The Compound
**Published:** 2025-07-15
**URL:** https://youtube.com/watch?v=NZufonUBKo8
**Duration:** 63:42

## Summary

In this episode of 'What Are Your Thoughts,' Josh Brown and Michael Batnick debate whether retail investors heavily concentrated in Nvidia should consider trimming their positions at the $4 trillion market cap level. Brown argues that if someone is nervously asking whether to sell, they probably should, while both hosts acknowledge the fundamental AI spending tailwinds remain intact. The episode also covers bull market technical indicators, Gen X wealth transfer dynamics, Elon Musk's multi-company maneuvers, a Netflix earnings preview, and bullish technical and fundamental setups in Solana and CME Group.

## Key Takeaways

- Brown's updated advice is that investors anxiously asking whether to sell Nvidia should probably sell some, as the question itself signals over-concentration rather than a view on fundamentals.
- The Trump administration's reversal allowing Nvidia to sell H20 chips in China removes a key bearish overhang and opens a market where Chinese AI spending is estimated at $98 billion for 2025.
- Despite Nvidia's rally, Schwab data shows retail clients were net sellers every week in June, suggesting institutional buyers are driving the stock higher while retail reduces exposure.
- The risk-on/risk-off ratio is at its highest level since early 2024, and leveraged long ETF assets under management are near all-time highs at $128 billion, reflecting broad bull market conditions.
- Gen X stands to inherit $1.4 trillion annually over the next decade but remains underserved by financial advisers, representing a significant near-term opportunity for wealth managers.
- Brown makes a bullish technical case for Solana, citing a trend breakout, inverse head-and-shoulders pattern, cup-and-handle formation, and a potential Solana spot ETF as a catalyst.
- CME Group is presented as a lower-risk proxy for the 'gamblification' of finance compared to Robinhood, while Interactive Brokers is highlighted as a breakout stock with 30% quarterly account growth.

## Detailed Notes

### Should Investors Sell Nvidia? [00:00:18]

Brown explains that the most common question he receives is whether to sell Nvidia, which has surged from below $100 to around $170 in two months and now holds a $4 trillion market cap. His new answer is that if someone is nervously asking, they should sell some, framing it as regret minimization — the emotional pain of not selling during a drawdown far outweighs the regret of selling some before a further rally. He notes he remains personally long, believing the stock could eventually reach the 200s.

### China Sales Reversal and Competitive Landscape [00:00:18]

A major catalyst discussed is the Trump administration allowing Nvidia to resume selling H20 AI chips in China, removing a significant bearish overhang. Batnick adds that Goldman Sachs projects hyperscaler capex will exceed $1.15 trillion from 2025-2027, more than double the prior three years. Brown also enumerates growing chip competitors including AMD's MI 355X, Intel's Gaudi 3, Broadcom's Tomahawk Ultra, Amazon's Trainium 2, Google's TPUs, and in-house chips from Meta and Huawei, warning these could eventually pressure Nvidia's valuation multiple even without affecting earnings.

### Bull Market Signals and Sentiment [00:00:18]

The hosts review a risk-on/risk-off ratio — constructed from copper, high-yield bonds, the Australian dollar, semiconductors, and high-beta stocks versus gold, Treasuries, the yen, utilities, and staples — which is at its highest since early 2024. Leveraged long ETF AUM stands at $128 billion near all-time highs. Despite these signs, the equal-weight S&P 500 has not broken to a new all-time high, and the only meaningful investing theme driving returns is AI, with bank earnings strength attributed entirely to booming asset values rather than organic lending growth.

### Gen X Wealth Transfer Opportunity [00:00:18]

Citing a Cerulli Associates report, Batnick notes Gen X (born 1965-1980) will inherit $1.4 trillion annually over the next decade as baby boomers pass wealth to their children. Gen X currently represents only 25% of adviser clients despite being in peak earning years, and two-thirds of people in their 40s lack a financial adviser. The hosts call this an underserved 'fat pitch' for wealth management firms, especially as adviser-reliance rates jump significantly when clients move from their 40s to their 50s.

### Elon Musk's Interconnected Business Moves [00:00:18]

The hosts discuss SpaceX's $400 billion valuation from an insider round, Tesla's reported $2 billion investment into xAI, and the merger of xAI with X (formerly Twitter) valuing xAI at $80 billion and X at $33 billion. Brown frames Musk's strategy as a 'spinning plates' approach where strong businesses prop up weaker ones, citing the Solar City precedent, and argues regulators have little power to intervene given Musk's political position.

### Netflix Earnings Preview and Brokerage Stocks [00:00:18]

Netflix enters earnings week in a roughly 7-8% drawdown from its June high, with expectations of 15.7% revenue growth to $11.1 billion and 45% EPS growth year-over-year. Both hosts advise waiting for the print rather than buying ahead of it, given the stock is already up 40% year-to-date. The discussion pivots to comparing Robinhood ($90B market cap) and CME Group ($98B), with Brown making the case for CME as a lower-risk proxy on market gamification; Batnick counters that Interactive Brokers (IBKR) is the better pick, noting a technical breakout and 30% quarterly account growth.

### Solana Mystery Chart and Crypto Outlook [00:00:18]

Brown presents Solana as a mystery chart and makes a technical case: a trend channel breakout, an inverse head-and-shoulders pattern forming, and a cup-and-handle with a trigger at $180-185. Fundamentally, he views Solana as the leading layer-1 blockchain for asset tokenization given its throughput capacity. A pending spot Solana ETF is cited as a major pending catalyst, drawing parallels to the Bitcoin and Ethereum ETF approvals. Both hosts say they are dollar-cost averaging into Solana.
