# Animal Spirits: Live from Miami | Animal Spirits 404

**Channel:** The Compound
**Published:** 2025-03-19
**URL:** https://youtube.com/watch?v=sllplG2glCI
**Duration:** 37:27

## Summary

Recorded live at Future Proof Citywide in Miami on March 19, 2025, Michael Batnick and Ben Carlson discuss a rapid market correction in Mag 7 stocks and broader equities, noting that corrections of this speed have historically been followed by recoveries. The hosts debate whether the current environment constitutes a 'vibecession'—where soft sentiment data leads economic activity—and examine diverging signals between consumer surveys and actual spending behavior. They also address the Wall Street Journal's 'buy and hold is dead' narrative, the IPO drought, the coming great wealth transfer to women, and the role of political partisanship in distorting economic sentiment surveys.

## Key Takeaways

- The Mag 7 correction was among the fastest on record, but historical data shows markets recover 100% of the time at 3 and 6 months post such corrections.
- Both hosts lean against a full bear market, with Batnick assigning a 74% probability of no bear market, while acknowledging a retest of lows is common after sharp bounces.
- Consumer sentiment has deteriorated sharply but prior episodes (2022) show consumers continue spending even when sentiment collapses, making the data hard to act on.
- Political partisanship has made economic sentiment surveys nearly useless as a signal—readings swing dramatically based solely on which party is in office.
- European equities, long shunned after years of outflows, are now attracting capital in a reversal that almost no one predicted after the 2024 election.
- The IPO market remains effectively closed, falling from 260 offerings in 2021 to roughly 50 in the prior year and a dozen year-to-date.
- A $124 trillion wealth transfer by 2048 is expected to disproportionately benefit women, with potential implications for consumer and investment trends.

## Detailed Notes

### Market Correction Speed and Context [00:00:05]

The hosts discuss one of the fastest corrections on record, citing Ryan Dietri's data showing markets are higher 3 and 6 months after such events 100% of the time and 12 months higher 83% of the time across 5 historical occurrences. Batnick notes he does not predict a bear market but acknowledges a retest of lows is a common pattern after a sharp snap-back rally.

### Buy-and-Hold Dead? WSJ Article and Investor Behavior [00:00:05]

The hosts mock a Wall Street Journal article claiming buy-and-hold investing has ended, citing anecdotes from two individual investors who exited the market. They argue this narrative recurs every correction and that investors who exit without a re-entry plan typically suffer by missing recoveries. The 60/40 portfolio is described as alive because bonds are currently performing well.

### Vibecession and Sentiment vs. Hard Data [00:00:05]

Batnick introduces the idea of a 'double dip vibecession'—deteriorating sentiment without confirmed economic contraction in hard data. Charts from Torson Slack show sharp declines in corporate capex plans and rising consumer job concerns. The hosts caution that in 2022, sentiment was equally dire yet consumers kept spending, making sentiment data an unreliable leading indicator until confirmed by actual retail and employment data.

### Partisan Sentiment Distortion and Investment Implications [00:00:05]

A CNBC poll shows economic sentiment flipped dramatically along party lines following the 2024 election—Democrats' 'good or excellent' rating dropped from 50% to 10%, Republicans rose from 3% to 25%. Carlson argues the independent reading (20% to 10%) is the only meaningful signal, while the hosts agree algorithms rewarding negativity make these surveys structurally biased and unlikely to ever reflect genuine optimism.

### IPO Market, International Equities, and Wealth Transfer [00:00:05]

IPO volume collapsed from 260 in 2021 to around 50 the prior year and roughly 12 year-to-date, raising questions for private fund investors about liquidity timelines. European equities are seeing inflows for the first time after three years of relentless outflows, a reversal almost no market observer anticipated. A Bank of America report projects $124 trillion in wealth transfers by 2048 will disproportionately flow to women.
