# This Won't End Well | Animal Spirits 464

**Channel:** The Compound
**Published:** 2026-05-13
**URL:** https://youtube.com/watch?v=mbkDuV9BW6s
**Duration:** 72:08

## Summary

Animal Spirits episode 464 features Michael Batnick and Ben Carlson discussing a historic stock market melt-up in which semiconductor names like Micron and SanDisk have delivered triple-digit to quadruple-digit gains while still trading at single-digit forward P/E multiples. The hosts argue this is the most fundamentally justified melt-up in history because earnings growth has outpaced price appreciation, causing the S&P forward P/E to fall even as the index rose. The episode also covers Ben Carlson's new book 'Risk & Reward', the Ritholtz Porter House momentum strategy launching in June, and a wide-ranging discussion of AI's impact on workforce training, financial advising, GLP-1 adoption, crypto margin loan misadventures, and retirement longevity planning.

## Key Takeaways

- The NASDAQ 100 is up nearly 650% over the past 10 years, surpassing all prior historic bull markets including Japan in the 1980s and the 1990s dot-com era.
- Semiconductor stocks now represent 22% of S&P 500 market cap, up from just 6% in April 2025, driven by insatiable AI compute demand.
- Despite dramatic price appreciation, Micron and SanDisk trade at roughly 10 times forward earnings, making a traditional bubble diagnosis difficult to sustain.
- AI is dismantling the apprenticeship model for white-collar professions, with entry-level tech hiring down more than 50% below pre-pandemic levels.
- Ritholtz Wealth is launching a rules-based momentum strategy ('Porter House') in partnership with Franklin Templeton that moves to cash when no stocks meet quality criteria.
- GLP-1 drug usage has roughly doubled in a year, and alcohol/beverage company market caps have shed over $830 billion since 2021.
- The great wealth transfer to Gen X and millennials will be delayed for many until the 2030s-2040s as parents live longer, reshaping retirement planning assumptions.

## Detailed Notes

### Ben Carlson's New Book: Risk & Reward [00:00:04]

Ben Carlson's new book 'Risk & Reward' is structured as alternating chapters of risks and rewards, covering 100-plus years of financial history, behavioral psychology, and math. The book is aimed at both overly conservative investors from the 2010s and overly aggressive investors in the current cycle. Carlson read the audio book himself and the book contains over 50 charts.

### Stock Market Melt-Up and Semiconductor Surge [00:00:04]

The S&P 500 gained 16% in 6 weeks, the most since the GFC, and semiconductor stocks (Micron, SanDisk, Western Digital, South Korean names) have led the rally dramatically. The SOXX index weighting in the S&P rose from 6% to 22% in roughly a year. Despite Micron being up 800% over one year, it trades at under 10 times 2026 earnings, and forward P/E for the broad S&P has actually declined 4% year-to-date even as the index rose 8%, which the hosts argue makes this melt-up more fundamentally justified than prior ones.

### South Korea and Emerging Markets [00:00:04]

South Korea's stock market (EWY) has surged to become the world's seventh-largest equity market, surpassing Canada and the UK, with SK Hynix and Samsung (both AI plays) comprising ~50% of the index. Emerging markets overall are up 23% in six weeks, their best rally in 17 years, as they have transitioned from energy-heavy to technology-heavy compositions.

### AI's Impact on Workforce and Financial Advice [00:00:04]

Drawing on a Baillie Gifford article, the hosts discuss how AI is eliminating the apprenticeship model for junior professionals, with entry-level tech hiring down 50%-plus from pre-pandemic. The Uber CFO noted they underestimated AI's labor-substitution impact for 2026. Clients are arriving at financial advisers more AI-informed, rewarding advisers who can engage at a higher level while commoditizing basic asset allocation.

### Porter House Momentum Strategy Launch [00:00:04]

Ritholtz Wealth Management partnered with Franklin Templeton to build an in-house rules-based momentum strategy called Porter House, launching in June for Ritholtz clients only. Unlike most momentum ETFs, it will hold cash rather than switch to defensive names when no quality stocks meet the buy criteria, a discipline inspired by investor John Borman.

### GLP-1 Drugs and Crypto Margin Mishaps [00:00:04]

GLP-1 usage in the US has risen from 5.8% (Q1 2024) to roughly 12.4% a year later, contributing to a decline in alcohol and beverage company market caps totaling over $830 billion since 2021. In the crypto segment, 'soccer dads' described borrowing against Bitcoin at 100K to buy MicroStrategy call option ETFs, a leveraged bet that forced them to continuously fund margin calls over six months.

### Retirement, Longevity, and the Great Wealth Transfer [00:00:04]

A 65-year-old couple has roughly a 64% chance that at least one partner lives beyond 90, making longevity risk central to retirement planning. The great wealth transfer from baby boomers will be delayed for millennials until the 2030s-2040s, and the first transfer will predominantly go to surviving spouses (mostly women), a demographic shift the financial industry is not yet prepared for.
