# Panic at the Disco! | TCAF 186

**Channel:** The Compound
**Published:** 2025-04-04
**URL:** https://youtube.com/watch?v=4ycVYrGjNLE
**Duration:** 74:18

## Summary

Recorded on April 4, 2025 — the largest single-day U.S. equity selloff since June 2020 — this episode of The Compound and Friends brings in Steve Quirk, Chief Brokerage Officer at Robinhood, to assess the tariff shock and explain how Robinhood's platform is evolving. The conversation covers behavioral patterns of Robinhood's young customer base during market stress, the strategic acquisition of TradePMR to enter RIA custody, and the launch of 24-hour equity trading. Quirk also addresses Vlad Tenev's controversial remarks about financial advisor fees and positions Robinhood's prediction markets expansion as a natural adjacency to its event-contract trading infrastructure.

## Key Takeaways

- Trump's tariff announcement on April 3 triggered one of the worst one-day market drops in a decade; the S&P fell ~4.5% and the Dow shed ~1,700 points by close.
- Robinhood's ~25 million customers, averaging age 30 and roughly half first-time investors, broadly held dry powder and rotated into broad-based ETFs (QQQ, SPY, IWM) on down days rather than chasing individual names.
- Robinhood acquired TradePMR to enter the RIA custody space rather than build from scratch, leveraging TradePMR's customer-service reputation and existing advisor relationships.
- The long-to-inverse levered ETF ratio compressed from 12:1 to 7:1 during the selloff, reflecting some repositioning but still dominated by long-side speculative demand.
- Robinhood's 24/5 trading (Sunday 8 PM through Friday close) is growing; Sunday nights are the busiest non-earnings sessions, driven by weekend news flow and crypto-native user expectations.
- Vlad Tenev's widely criticized comment capping advisor fees at $250/year was clarified by Quirk as referring to automated asset management, not human wealth management services.
- Prediction and event contracts (elections, Fed decisions) are an expanding Robinhood product line; the 2024 presidential election contract saw 810 million contracts and 600,000 participating accounts in one week.

## Detailed Notes

### Market Reaction to Tariff Announcement [00:08:42]

Goldman Sachs estimated the effective tariff rate increase at 12.6% net of exemptions, lower than headline figures. Josh Brown and Michael Batnick argued the market was likely still underpricing the literalness of the tariffs, with Neil Dutta quoted saying 'Trump shot the hostage.' Nike was highlighted as a particularly damaged company given its Vietnam and Indonesia manufacturing concentration; the stock was down 14% pre-market and had already fallen 68% from 2015 highs.

### Robinhood Customer Behavior During Selloffs [00:09:59]

Quirk explained that on severe down days Robinhood customers shift volume away from single names toward broad-based index ETFs like QQQ, IWM, and SPY — the opposite of what hosts expected. He noted that most customers are net buyers who carry dry powder and rotate winners into depressed names rather than capitulating entirely. The cohort's inexperience with severe drawdowns was acknowledged; Quirk framed market shocks as necessary education that cannot be simulated.

### Levered ETF Dynamics and Retail Speculation [00:39:17]

Todd Sohn's data showed the long-to-inverse levered ETF ratio peaked at 12:1 and had compressed to 7:1. Inverse ETFs like SOXS (triple-leveraged bearish semis) were up 28% on the day. Quirk acknowledged that the vast majority of levered ETF use at Robinhood is speculative, not hedging, noting that the daily reset mechanism makes multi-day inverse positions effectively useless as portfolio hedges.

### Robinhood Growing Up: TradePMR and Wealth Management [00:47:30]

Robinhood acquired TradePMR to enter the RIA custody space with an established platform rather than building from scratch. Quirk highlighted TradePMR's best-in-class customer-service reputation and the strategic opportunity to connect Robinhood's 25–30 million predominantly young accounts to the wealth management industry, which is three times the size of self-directed brokerage. A planned referral program will allow advisors to onboard Robinhood clients who already have accounts, reducing friction and enabling lower account minimums through technology efficiency.

### 24-Hour Trading and Platform Expansion [01:02:27]

Robinhood offers 24/5 trading on approximately 1,000 equity symbols, beginning Sunday at 8 PM. Volumes have grown consistently, with Sunday nights the largest non-episodic sessions due to weekend news accumulation. Quirk noted the SEC's primary concern was liquidity, addressed by requiring limit orders only. International brokers from Asia and Europe also participate, adding depth during off-hours. Quirk acknowledged initial personal skepticism about the format given the implications for round-the-clock client management responsibilities.

### Prediction Markets and Event Contracts [01:07:23]

Robinhood has launched event contracts on topics including presidential elections and Fed rate decisions. The 2024 presidential election contract drew 810 million contracts and 600,000 accounts in its first week. Quirk noted the contracts provided real-time price discovery that ratified the election outcome hours before television networks called swing states. The hosts raised the question of whether conflating sports betting, retirement savings, and prediction markets in one app creates behavioral risk, which Quirk addressed by framing sports as an investable business sector.
