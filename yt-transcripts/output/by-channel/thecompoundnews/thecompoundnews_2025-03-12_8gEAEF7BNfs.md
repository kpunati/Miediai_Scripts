# The Tariff Tantrum | Animal Spirits 403

**Channel:** The Compound
**Published:** 2025-03-12
**URL:** https://youtube.com/watch?v=8gEAEF7BNfs
**Duration:** 64:06

## Summary

In episode 403 of Animal Spirits, Michael Batnick and Ben Carlson react to a sharp early-2025 stock market correction — dubbed the 'Tariff Tantrum' — triggered by aggressive trade policy and macro uncertainty under the second Trump administration. The hosts argue the pullback is a healthy reintroduction of risk after back-to-back 20%-plus years, not the start of a deep recession, while acknowledging a meaningful slowdown is underway. They also discuss labor market softening, international equities outperforming the US, the underwhelming US strategic Bitcoin reserve announcement, and why AI remains the defining macro force of the decade.

## Key Takeaways

- The S&P 500 correction is fast and painful but the hosts frame it as a healthy reset after two consecutive 20%-plus years rather than the start of a structural bear market.
- Bonds and international equities are finally acting as diversifiers, with European stocks benefiting from Germany's fiscal pivot and NATO re-commitment spending.
- Individual growth stocks have suffered severe drawdowns — Tesla -54%, Coinbase -50%, the 2x MicroStrategy ETF -89% — illustrating why timing concentrated positions is nearly impossible.
- The labor market is cooling: recent graduates report scarce entry-level opportunities, and the share of consumers expecting fewer jobs in six months is the highest since 2013.
- The strategic Bitcoin reserve executive order was a procedural non-event — the government simply committed not to sell roughly 200,000 Bitcoin it already held via forfeitures.
- Over 85-90% of professional active managers underperform their benchmarks over 10- and 15-year periods per SPIVA data, and this period likely saw the widest retail-vs-professional gap in history.
- AI is identified as the swing factor for the rest of the decade: a bust would likely stem from AI underdelivering, while a boom would follow if AGI arrives and boosts productivity.

## Detailed Notes

### Market Context and the Correction in Perspective [00:00:49]

As of Monday's close, the S&P 500 was down 9% from its February 19 all-time high, with the Nasdaq 100 off nearly 13% and the Russell 2000 nearly 17% — mirroring the speed of the February 2020 COVID drawdown. The hosts argue that after consecutive 20%-plus gains in 2023 and 2024, a correction is a welcome reintroduction of risk rather than a systemic threat. Bonds (AGG) posted their best relative day versus the S&P in 25 years, validating the diversification case.

### Growth Stock Carnage and the Limits of Market Timing [00:10:24]

Individual high-flyers have collapsed: Tesla -54%, Coinbase -50%, Robinhood -45%, Nvidia -30%, DraftKings -30%, and the 2x leveraged MicroStrategy ETF down 89% since late December. The hosts use these examples to argue that concentration risk is effectively untimable — stocks that go up the stairs come down in the elevator, and by the time the drop is obvious it feels too late to sell.

### Recession Risk, Labor Market, and Macro Signals [00:22:12]

A listener email vividly illustrates anecdotal labor market softening: scarce entry-level openings, mass layoffs, and four times more applicants for junior roles than previously. Conference Board data shows the share of consumers expecting fewer jobs in six months is the highest since 2013. Delta CEO Ed Bastian noted revenue growth of 4% — still positive but decelerating — and distinguished between normalization and contraction. The hosts put recession probability at around 65% over the next three years.

### International Outperformance, Dollar Weakness, and SPIVA [00:13:10]

HSBC upgraded European equities to overweight (ex-UK) and downgraded the US to neutral, citing Germany's fiscal pivot and NATO spending as a genuine structural catalyst. Fund flows into the largest US-listed European ETF reached their highest levels since 2018. The hosts note that a weakening dollar adds a currency tailwind for US investors holding international stocks. Separately, SPIVA data confirms 85-90% of active managers underperform benchmarks over 10-15 year periods.

### Bitcoin Reserve and Crypto Commentary [00:38:48]

President Trump signed an executive order establishing a strategic Bitcoin reserve, but the order only codifies that the government will not sell the roughly 200,000 Bitcoin acquired through criminal forfeitures. No new purchasing is authorized. Cliff Asness critiqued the concept as counterproductive, arguing that promoting Bitcoin as an alternative store of value undermines the US dollar's status as the world's reserve currency — the source of America's 'exorbitant privilege'.

### Wealth Transfer, Longevity, and AI Outlook [00:34:56]

JP Morgan's Guide to Retirements shows a 43% probability that at least one member of a healthy married couple survives to age 95, meaning millennials banking on inheritance will likely receive it at 60-65 — too late for it to transform early financial planning. On AI, the hosts cite New York Times reporting that AGI may be imminent, and argue AI remains the decade's decisive macro variable: a recession without AI follow-through could permanently displace workers, while AGI delivery could power the next boom.
