# The Best Performing Stocks of 2025 | Animal Spirits 445

**Channel:** The Compound
**Published:** 2025-12-31
**URL:** https://youtube.com/watch?v=wuMtwvqtIBY
**Duration:** 55:59

## Summary

In the final Animal Spirits episode of 2025, Michael Batnick and Ben Carlson review the year's top and bottom S&P 500 performers, with storage/semiconductor companies dominating the winners and retailers like Lululemon and Decker's among the losers. They highlight the dramatic outperformance of international small caps over US small caps, rising concerns about private credit expanding into riskier consumer debt, and the explosive growth of family offices. The hosts also discuss retirement spending strategies, the ongoing validity of 401k plans, crypto sentiment post-ETF, and why consumer sentiment data has become unreliable.

## Key Takeaways

- International small cap value outperformed US small cap value by nearly 40 percentage points in 2025, the widest spread since at least 2008.
- Private credit firms purchased 136 billion in consumer loans in 2025, roughly 14 times the 2024 figure, with KKR and others expanding into credit cards and buy-now-pay-later debt.
- Family offices now oversee 5.5 trillion in assets, a 67% increase in five years, and are projected to exceed hedge fund industry AUM by 2030.
- Silver surged approximately 170% in 2025, significantly outpacing gold's approximately 70% gain, driven partly by technology and data center demand.
- Consumer sentiment is at its lowest since early 1980, though the hosts argue survey quality has deteriorated and the data no longer reflects real economic conditions.
- 401k plans have succeeded: active defined contribution participants have grown to nearly 90 million from 30 million pension participants at the 1980 peak.
- Retirement planning advice is shifting toward encouraging higher spending in the first decade of retirement, when health is better, rather than over-saving.

## Detailed Notes

### 2025 Stock Market Review: Best and Worst Performers [00:00:05]

The best-performing S&P 500 stocks in 2025 were dominated by storage and semiconductor names—SanDisk, Western Digital, Micron, and Seagate—described as picks-and-shovels AI plays. Newmont, the only gold miner in the S&P 500, also made the list. The worst performers fell 40-70% and included well-known consumer names like The Trade Desk, Decker's Outdoor, Lululemon, and Chipotle.

### International Small Caps vs. US Small Caps [00:00:05]

The S&P 600 (US small caps) suffered its worst 12-month relative performance versus developed ex-US stocks since at least 2008, defying the 'Main Street's turn' narrative from Liberation Day. DFA International Small Cap Value ETF was up 48% while US small cap value gained only 10%, a spread of nearly 38 percentage points. The S&P 100's profit margin of 15.7% compared to the S&P 600's 5.9% is near an all-time high spread, which the hosts see as a potential catalyst for small cap mean reversion.

### AI, Power Infrastructure, and Electricity Demand [00:00:05]

Fidelity's 2026 outlook highlighted AI-driven electricity demand growth. GE Vernova, the energy turbine company spun off in 2024, was cited as a standout performer, reportedly up over 1000% year to date. The hosts discussed how AI investment may play out as a commodities and power infrastructure trade rather than a winner-take-all technology bet.

### Family Offices and Private Credit Growth [00:00:05]

Family offices now oversee an estimated 5.5 trillion in assets globally, a 67% jump over five years, with Deloitte projecting growth to 9 trillion by 2030—exceeding hedge funds. The hosts noted status and privacy as key drivers. On the private credit side, firms including KKR and Sixth Street purchased 136 billion in consumer loans in 2025, nearly 14 times the 2024 volume, raising concerns about credit quality in a future recession despite strong current performance.

### Crypto, Sentiment, and the Economy [00:00:05]

Bitcoin catalysts (ETF approvals, deregulation) have largely played out and the hosts sense waning enthusiasm. US consumer sentiment reached its lowest level since early 1980, though both hosts argued survey methodology has deteriorated significantly since the pandemic and the data should be treated with skepticism.

### Retirement Planning and 401k Success [00:00:05]

JP Morgan retirement data shows spending decreases roughly linearly every five years after age 60, supporting the 'first decade' strategy of front-loading retirement spending when health is better. Active defined contribution plan participants have grown from around 30 million in the late 1970s to nearly 90 million today, with the hosts arguing 401ks have been a policy success despite longstanding criticism.
