# Why Global Stocks are Crushing the US Market | WAYT?

**Channel:** The Compound
**Published:** 2025-02-18
**URL:** https://youtube.com/watch?v=SkxVZbory_U
**Duration:** 51:56

## Summary

Josh Brown and Michael Batnick host a wide-ranging episode covering the notable outperformance of international equities — particularly European and Chinese stocks — relative to the US in early 2025, supported by charts from multiple technical and macro analysts. They analyze the potential economic impact of large-scale federal workforce reductions under the DOGE initiative, examine Jamie Dimon's leaked return-to-office tirade, and explore post-ZIRP investing lessons via gig economy platforms like DoorDash and Instacart. The episode closes with Brown making a bull case for CME Group and a mystery chart revealing ARK ETF's surprising recent resurgence.

## Key Takeaways

- International equities, especially European and Chinese stocks, have been outperforming US markets since late 2024, driven by Chinese fiscal stimulus, DeepSeek enthusiasm, and Europe's AI investment ambitions.
- The ACWI ex-US index is in its longest sustained uptrend since before the 2008 financial crisis, at 98 weeks, though a decisive breakout above pre-crisis resistance has not yet occurred.
- Federal layoffs under DOGE — potentially 275,000 workers — represent a historically large single-period job cut, but market and spending impacts may be muted near-term as workers remain on paid leave through September.
- Jamie Dimon's leaked audio underscores a broader executive push for in-person work, with particular concern about the long-term career development costs for young workers in remote environments.
- Post-ZIRP, value creation in tech shifted from growth-at-any-cost to capital efficiency; DoorDash and Instacart illustrate how network consolidation, not just cheap capital, drives durable moats.
- CME Group is presented as a high-conviction breakout opportunity given record highs, strong earnings, and a 4.3% dividend yield.
- ARK ETF's recent outperformance is largely driven by holdings like Palantir, Coinbase, and Robinhood, though hosts are cautious about reading too much into a momentum-driven rotation.

## Detailed Notes

### Global Stocks Outperforming the US [00:02:27]

Brown and Batnick walk through technical and macro evidence — from Willie DeWit and Ed Yardeni — that international markets, particularly the ACWI ex-US, European EMU, and Chinese indices, are outperforming the US. They attribute European strength partly to China trade linkages and EU AI investment commitments, noting the ACWI ex-US is at a 98-week uptrend but has not broken the critical pre-2008 resistance level.

### China and the DeepSeek Catalyst [00:09:47]

The hosts discuss Chinese equity momentum via FXI and KWEB, crediting last September's Chinese fiscal bazooka and the DeepSeek release as catalysts. Analyst Jeff de Graaf compares China's potential inflection to the US S&P bottoming in 2008-2009. Brown bought FXI and acknowledges past failed attempts to call a Chinese market bottom.

### US Tech and Mag 7 Dynamics [00:16:04]

Despite international rotation, US tech ETF flows continue rising and the S&P and NASDAQ 100 hit new all-time weekly closing highs. The hosts note significant dispersion within the Mag 7 — Meta is up while Tesla and Microsoft are in drawdowns — and suggest the large-cap tech thesis may shift to new leaders like Salesforce or Uber as older names stumble.

### Federal Layoffs and Economic Impact [00:20:21]

Citing Ritholtz CIS Callie Cox and economist David Kelly, the hosts examine the DOGE-driven federal workforce cuts. Up to 275,000 workers may be affected, but because laid-off employees remain on paid leave through September, near-term consumer spending impact is likely minimal. Government jobs historically serve as a countercyclical buffer, and the loss of this stabilizer is flagged as a longer-term concern.

### Jamie Dimon on Return to Office [00:31:50]

A leaked audio clip of Jamie Dimon berating employees over remote work practices is played and discussed. The hosts broadly agree with his points about remote work's impact on young workers' development and collaboration, but push back on Dimon's implication that working seven days a week is a universal expectation.

### Post-ZIRP Investing and Gig Economy Platforms [00:38:02]

The hosts discuss DoorDash and Instacart as case studies in post-ZIRP investing: the durability of their three-sided networks came from consolidation (from six or seven players to two), not just cheap capital. Uber CEO Dara Khosrowshahi's observation that value creation has shifted from fundraising to capital efficiency is cited. Schwab's move to 24/5 trading is framed as a defensive competitive response rather than a strategic ambition.

### Make the Case: CME Group and ARK Mystery Chart [00:46:30]

Brown makes a bull case for CME Group (CME), noting a breakout attempt at 3-year highs, strong earnings beat, 10% annual revenue growth, and a 4.3% dividend yield. The episode closes with Batnick's mystery chart revealed as ARK Innovation ETF, which has outperformed the S&P by roughly 10 percentage points over the past year, driven by Palantir, Coinbase, and Robinhood.
