# These are Cheap Stocks with Catalysts | TCAF 235

**Channel:** The Compound
**Published:** 2026-03-27
**URL:** https://youtube.com/watch?v=jOp6APG9wNo
**Duration:** 72:54

## Summary

Jonathan Boyar, principal at Boyar Value Group, joins Josh Brown and Michael Batnick to discuss special situation value investing in a volatile 2026 market. The conversation covers the dramatic multiple compression in Mag 7 names, arguing these are now attractively valued rather than bubble-like, while distinguishing between companies with durable moats (Microsoft, Salesforce) versus those facing structural questions (Meta). Boyar presents detailed bull cases for Uber as the logistics network that will aggregate autonomous vehicle supply, MSG Sports as a sports franchise trading at a steep discount to private market value, Scotts Miracle-Gro as a depressed consumer staples business with M&A potential, and Atlanta Braves as a Malone simplification play. Throughout, the discussion emphasizes catalyst identification as essential to avoid value traps.

## Key Takeaways

- Multiple compression in Mag 7 names (Nvidia ~20x vs. 5-yr avg 48x, Meta at ~17x) creates opportunity for investors with a 2-3 year horizon, but catalysts—either CapEx restraint or demonstrated AI revenue—are needed for re-rating.
- Uber's autonomous vehicle risk is misunderstood: as a logistics aggregator, Uber benefits from more AV players (Waymo, Rivian, Lucid partnerships), not fewer, and its delivery and Uber One subscription segments add durable revenue.
- MSG Sports trades at roughly half its private market value with concrete near-term catalysts: a planned spin into separate Knicks (MSGK) and Rangers (MSGR) entities, and a 2027 tax law change forcing publicly traded sports teams to go private.
- Identifying a catalyst beyond cheapness is essential to avoid value traps; Boyar's process involves writing full research reports before buying and publishing them to major hedge fund clients to stress-test the thesis.
- Scotts Miracle-Gro and Atlanta Braves are highlighted as family-controlled businesses with aging leadership likely to sell, offering M&A optionality at depressed valuations.
- The current market's internal logic—selling the most expensive deciles hardest—creates a stock-picker's environment, with equal-weight S&P 500 and Russell 2000 expected to mean-revert.
- Adobe was a pass for Boyar because it lacks the data lock-in of Salesforce and faces the risk that professionals eventually bypass the creative software layer entirely when interacting with AI.

## Detailed Notes

### Market Environment and Mag 7 Valuation Compression [00:07:05]

Boyar argues the volatile market is actually a normal environment for value investors after the aberration of 2023-2025. Mag 7 average forward PE has compressed to 21.5x; Nvidia is now under 20x vs. a 5-year average of 48x, and Meta is at 16-17x. The discussion identifies sellers as mostly short-term oriented pod shops de-risking, not investors making fundamental judgments about 3-year earnings power. The key debate is whether CapEx-heavy AI investment destroys or creates value; Boyar sees a coming 'year of efficiency' moment similar to Meta's 2022 pivot.

### Value Investing Process: Catalysts, Write-Ups, and Avoiding Value Traps [00:32:22]

Boyar requires a full written research report before any purchase, distributed to large hedge fund and mutual fund clients for adversarial feedback. Finding a great business at a cheap price is only two-thirds of the job; a catalyst within a 2-3 year timeframe is required to avoid value traps. Salesforce (CRM) is cited as a position being built slowly, supported by its role as system of record in regulated industries, Starboard's activist re-entry, and $25 billion in buybacks by Benioff.

### Uber: Autonomous Vehicle Bull Case [00:44:55]

Boyar and Brown argue Uber is best understood as a logistics network, not a tech company vulnerable to a single autonomous winner. The thesis requires multiple AV manufacturers (Waymo, Rivian, Lucid, OEMs enabled by Nvidia's autonomy stack); Uber is actively seeding this ecosystem with capital commitments to prevent Tesla/Waymo duopoly. Uber generates roughly $10 billion in free cash flow, is buying back stock, growing 20% annually, and benefits from a cross-platform Eats/rides flywheel that Waymo cannot replicate.

### MSG Sports and Sports Franchise Arbitrage [00:38:14]

MSGS trades at an $8 billion enterprise value against a Forbes private market value of $14.75 billion for the Knicks and Rangers combined. Near-term catalysts include a planned split into separate publicly traded Knicks (MSGK) and Rangers (MSGR) entities, a minority strategic investor likely at a premium, and a 2027 tax law change (top-5 employee salary deductibility eliminated for public companies) that effectively forces a going-private or sale. Boyar previously wrote a public letter to Dolan outlining these steps.

### Additional Stock Ideas: SMG, MGM, Atlanta Braves [01:00:29]

Scotts Miracle-Gro (SMG) trades at 9.5x EBITDA vs. a fair 12-13x, weighed down by its failed cannabis infrastructure bet; the aging Hagedorn family ownership makes a sale plausible. MGM Resorts has retired 45% of shares since 2021 and has an underappreciated 2030 Japan casino catalyst projecting ~$6 billion in revenue from a sole licensed operation. Atlanta Braves Holdings (BATRA) is trading around $42 against Boyar's ~$60 sale estimate, with John Malone simplifying his empire and the team plus Battery Atlanta real estate offering asset-backed downside protection.
