# One of the Greatest Bull Market Years in History | WAYT?

**Channel:** The Compound
**Published:** 2024-12-31
**URL:** https://youtube.com/watch?v=eRvzi18L8kc
**Duration:** 70:06

## Summary

In this year-end episode of 'What Are Your Thoughts,' Josh Brown and Michael Batnick of Ritholtz Wealth Management conduct a comprehensive review of 2024, which they characterize as one of the greatest bull market years in history. The Nasdaq gained 32% and S&P 500 gained 27%, driven by large-cap technology and momentum factors, while value strategies and healthcare significantly underperformed. The episode covers major market events including record ETF AUM, the explosive rise of private market investments flowing into wealth management, shifts in consumer behavior driven by e-commerce and GLP-1 obesity drugs, and a winners/losers analysis across finance and investing.

## Key Takeaways

- The Nasdaq gained 32% and S&P 500 gained 27% in 2024, with momentum factor up 48.4% — the strongest five-year run for any factor in living memory.
- Vistra (VST) was the best full-year S&P 500 performer at +266%, marking the first time a utility topped the index since approximately 2001.
- US ETF AUM reached $10 trillion, with the BlackRock iShares Bitcoin ETF breaking all records for fastest asset accumulation on launch.
- Private market investments are entering mainstream wealth management, with key concerns around fees, liquidity, and due diligence quality despite structural tailwinds.
- Healthcare significantly underperformed during the bull market; GLP-1 drugs dominated investor attention and crowded out interest in other pharmaceutical categories.
- MicroStrategy (up 422.5%), Palantir, and Tesla were among the biggest market cap gainers; Carl Icahn's IEP fell 36% and is now 87% below all-time highs.
- E-commerce accounted for a record share of US retail sales, crossing $1.16 trillion over 12 months, with TikTok Shop now larger than Shein and Sephora combined.

## Detailed Notes

### 2024 Market Returns and Factor Performance [00:00:22]

The Nasdaq returned 32%, S&P 500 27%, Russell 2000 12%, and emerging markets 9% in 2024, with large-cap tech continuing to dominate for the second consecutive year. The momentum factor delivered 48.4% and has posted a 20% annualized return over five years — the best run for any factor in modern history. Value and dividend-focused strategies significantly lagged, with Dogs of the Dow trailing the Dow by 14 percentage points for the fifth time in six years.

### Sector Performance and Top/Worst Stocks [00:00:22]

Seven of eleven S&P 500 sectors posted double-digit returns; communications (Meta, Alphabet, Netflix) led at 36.6%, followed by financials at 32% and consumer discretionary at 29.5%. Healthcare was the standout underperformer, its weakest bull market relative performance on record per Ned Davis Research, largely because GLP-1 drugs consumed all investor attention. Top individual stocks included Vistra (+266%), Nvidia (+176%), GE Vernova, and United Airlines; worst performers included Adobe (-25%), AMD, Pepsi, and large-cap pharma names.

### ETFs, IPOs, and M&A Milestones [00:00:22]

US ETF AUM crossed $10 trillion in 2024, with passive dwarfing active by roughly 10-to-1. The BlackRock iShares Bitcoin ETF set records as the fastest asset-accumulating ETF launch ever. IPO volumes rose 50% year-over-year to 146 deals worth $29.6 billion, though still well below 2021's SPAC-era peak of 400 deals at $142 billion. North American M&A remained roughly flat at $1.9 trillion, with hosts predicting deregulation under Trump 2.0 would significantly increase M&A activity in 2025, especially in media and tech.

### Consumer Trends: E-commerce and GLP-1 Impact [00:00:22]

E-commerce reached a secular high as a share of US retail sales, crossing $1.16 trillion in the trailing 12 months — more than doubling in five years. TikTok Shop emerged as larger than Shein, Sephora, and QVC combined. The obesity rate among US adults dipped below 40% for the first time in years, attributed to GLP-1 drugs; Viking Therapeutics (VKTX) was highlighted as the key stock to watch for the pill-based next wave. Despite strong asset prices, 60% of Americans still believed the US was in a recession.

### Rise of Private Markets in Wealth Management [00:00:22]

The hosts debated the secular shift of private market investments into retail and RIA portfolios, with Harvey Schwartz of Carlyle as backdrop. Josh Brown argued returns would be lower going forward due to capital inflows and compressed illiquidity premiums, but professionalization and fee compression would benefit retail investors over time. Michael Batnick highlighted that 88% of US companies generating $100M+ in revenue are privately held, leaving the public markets covering only 12% of available investing opportunity. Jason Zweig's skeptical Wall Street Journal op-ed warning about higher fees, conflicts of interest, and illiquidity was cited as a necessary counterpoint.

### Winners and Losers of 2024 [00:00:22]

Major winners: Elon Musk (political influence, Tesla +73%, SpaceX); Michael Saylor (MicroStrategy +422.5%, added $56B market cap); Sam Altman (OpenAI raised $6.6B, now valued at $157B); Peter Thiel (Palantir +139B market cap); Jamie Dimon (JPMorgan +45%); Apollo/KKR/Goldman leaders as private equity and banking surged. Major losers: Gary Gensler (SEC losses in crypto cases, forced out post-election); Carl Icahn (IEP -36%, now 87% below all-time highs, lost $14.8B in market cap); value investors (Russell 1000 Value +13.9% vs growth); AMD shareholders (-vs Nvidia's +176%). IBM was a surprise quiet all-time high, up 35%.
