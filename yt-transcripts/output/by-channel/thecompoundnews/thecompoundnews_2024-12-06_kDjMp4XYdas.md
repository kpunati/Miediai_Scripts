# Three Contrarian Trades for 2025 | TCAF 168

**Channel:** The Compound
**Published:** 2024-12-06
**URL:** https://youtube.com/watch?v=kDjMp4XYdas
**Duration:** 87:22

## Summary

In TCAF episode 168, Peter Boockvar of Bleakley Financial Group presents three contrarian trade ideas for 2025: platinum (historically priced above gold but now far below at ~$950), Chinese consumer equities (via internet stocks, Macau casino names, and AIA Group), and bombed-out international clean energy ETFs. The panel also pays tribute to Art Cashin, debates Bitcoin's narrative-driven valuation versus gold, and examines whether US exceptionalism and AI-driven multiple expansion can persist. A significant portion addresses Amazon's entry into foundational AI with Nova LLMs and Trainium chips, which the hosts argue poses real competitive risk to Nvidia and Microsoft.

## Key Takeaways

- Platinum's collapse relative to gold is a contrarian opportunity because EV adoption fears overstated the decline in catalytic converter demand, and hybrids—which are winning the auto transition—use platinum too.
- Chinese consumer exposure via internet stocks, Macau casino operators, and AIA Group may benefit as China's middle class grows from 300 million to a projected 600 million over the next decade.
- Trump tariff risk is underpriced by markets if tariffs are used as revenue-raising 'pay-fors' for extending tax cuts rather than as strategic policy tools.
- Amazon's launch of Nova foundational LLMs and Trainium 2 chips introduces real competition for both Nvidia (hardware) and OpenAI/Microsoft (models), potentially compressing AI-related multiples.
- The next leg of the AI trade may be margin expansion at the user level—companies like small caps and multinationals—rather than continued multiple expansion at the hyperscaler level.
- Money market outflows in a rate-cutting cycle could inject $626 billion to $2 trillion into equities in 2025, though the mechanics of 'cash on the sidelines' are nuanced.
- Art Cashin's legacy is discussed as exemplifying pattern recognition, intellectual honesty, and the value of reading non-confirming signals across asset classes.

## Detailed Notes

### Art Cashin Tribute [00:20:18]

The hosts and Boockvar reflect on Art Cashin, director of floor operations at UBS who passed away at 83. Cashin's signature approach was cross-asset detective work—if the stated reason for a market move did not fit what bonds or the dollar were doing, it was the wrong reason. His Cuban Missile Crisis anecdote about buying when missiles are 'in the air' because sellers would be sorry almost every time is highlighted as foundational investing wisdom.

### Three Contrarian Trades for 2025 [00:31:55]

Boockvar identifies platinum (trading at ~$950 vs gold at ~$2,600, historically inverted), Chinese consumer stocks and Macau casino names (AIA Group, internet stocks), and international clean energy ETFs as extreme contrarian setups. He argues EV penetration fears were the core reason for platinum's collapse and that hybrid vehicle growth actually requires more platinum than ICE engines. The iShares Global Clean Energy ETF (ICLN) is cited as down roughly 44% over five years.

### Bitcoin, Gold, and Narrative Assets [00:03:10]

The pre-show cold open features an extended debate on whether Bitcoin's first-mover network effect creates a permanent moat or whether substitutes can emerge (as with Myspace/Facebook). Boockvar argues Bitcoin should trade like gold if the Satoshi thesis holds, noting Powell said Bitcoin competes with gold not the dollar. The panel discusses Michael Saylor's strategy of pitching Microsoft to hold Bitcoin as a treasury reserve asset.

### Trump Tariffs and the Orange Swan Risk [00:52:03]

Boockvar warns that if tariffs are deployed primarily as revenue to offset extending the Trump tax cuts—rather than for strategic or reshoring goals—markets are unprepared for the inflationary and disruptive consequences. He labels this the 'orange swan' for 2025. Peter Navarro's rehire and the appointment of Jameson Greer as Lighthizer 2.0 are cited as signals that real tariff implementation is coming.

### AI Competition: Amazon's Entry and Nvidia Risk [01:04:34]

Amazon's AWS re:Invent announcements—Nova foundational LLMs and Trainium 2 chips competitive with Nvidia H100s—are discussed as a meaningful rerating of competitive dynamics. The hosts argue Amazon already has the customer relationships through AWS, so Nova's competitive benchmarks could erode OpenAI/Microsoft and Nvidia moats. Trainium 3 is expected to be 30-40% more cost-effective than Trainium 2, making Nvidia's dominance timeline uncertain.

### US Exceptionalism and International Opportunity [00:56:15]

The panel debates whether US outperformance relative to international markets can persist given 70% global equity market cap concentration in the US. Boockvar argues a pendulum shift toward Asia is more likely than Europe. The JP Morgan observation that technology benefits historically broaden earnings across industries—as in 1950s and late-1970s—is cited as a potential template for AI benefits accruing beyond the Mag 7.
