# JC's Revenge | TCAF 174

**Channel:** The Compound
**Published:** 2025-01-17
**URL:** https://youtube.com/watch?v=aWWrla7vC3U
**Duration:** 91:25

## Summary

Episode 174 of The Compound and Friends brings together hosts Michael Batnick and Josh Brown with returning guest JC Parets, chief strategist at All-Star Charts, for a wide-ranging technical and macro discussion. The conversation opens with a debate on skill versus luck in long-term equity investing, using Nvidia's 100x return as the central example, before pivoting to a detailed review of JC's current chart book. Sentiment indicators show elevated retail and newsletter-writer bearishness despite the S&P 500 sitting near all-time highs, which the hosts interpret as a bullish contrarian signal. Key trade ideas include a dollar peak thesis, long bank stocks and gold, long China via FXI, and a short on Eli Lilly below the $710-730 breakdown level.

## Key Takeaways

- JC Parets views the spike in bearishness among individual investors and newsletter writers as a bullish contrarian signal in an ongoing bull market.
- Consumer Staples underperforming on a relative basis is interpreted as a risk-on confirmation, not a warning sign.
- Credit spreads at cycle-tight levels and a non-expanding new-lows list are cited as evidence there is no imminent systemic stress.
- The dollar may be peaking based on extreme small-speculator long positioning in CFTC data, which would be a catalyst for international stocks, commodities, and duration.
- Eli Lilly and Novo Nordisk are flagged as potential short setups, with LLY described as a 'massive rounding top' and NVO already breaking to new 52-week lows.
- Gold is highlighted as the best-performing asset of the 21st century, with a target of $5,000 and silver seen as a leveraged follow-on trade.
- Bitcoin consolidating just below its record high at the 161.8% Fibonacci extension is considered normal bull-market behavior; a faster breakout would be more bullish.

## Detailed Notes

### Skill vs. Luck Debate in Long-Term Investing [00:13:38]

The hosts debate whether holding Nvidia through multiple 50%-plus drawdowns over eight years constitutes skill or luck. JC argues relative strength was the signal; Brown says it requires emotional temperance that is its own form of skill. Batnick counters that without a repeatable framework, surviving catastrophic drawdowns is luck given that over 40% of Russell 3000 stocks never recover from 70%-plus declines.

### Sentiment Indicators and Money Market Flows [00:34:36]

AAII individual investors registered their most bearish reading in over a year, and a composite combining AAII with newsletter-writers sentiment sits at the most bearish since November 2023. A composite sentiment index blending surveys with actual positioning data (VIX, put/call ratios) sits in neutral territory, suggesting sentiment is not a headwind. Weekly money-market fund inflows of $66 billion were the largest since April 2020, which JC frames as a fear signal inside a bull market.

### Sector Rotation and Risk-On Confirmation [00:42:57]

Consumer Staples are making all-time lows on a relative basis versus the broad market, which JC characterizes as confirmation that investors are not seeking defensives. The equally-weighted Consumer Discretionary vs. Staples ratio is hitting new all-time highs, and an equally-weighted index of the six largest US banks has broken out above its Great Financial Crisis high. Credit spreads (OAS and high-yield) are at the tightest levels of the current cycle.

### Dollar Peak Thesis and International Implications [00:51:34]

CFTC commitment-of-traders data shows small speculators at their most net-long the dollar and most net-short the Euro in three years, which JC reads as a contrarian signal. The Euro has retraced 61.8% of its entire COVID-era rally. A dollar rollover would likely benefit small-cap US stocks, European equities, emerging markets, China, and support Brown's long-duration bond trade.

### Short Ideas: Eli Lilly and Novo Nordisk [01:15:36]

JC describes Eli Lilly's chart as 'one of the worst rounding tops I have seen in a long time,' already in a 30% drawdown from its near-$900 billion peak market cap. The short trigger is a break below $710-730. Novo Nordisk has already broken to new 52-week lows. The fundamental backdrop cited is increasing competition for GLP-1 obesity drugs from dozens of manufacturers.

### China, Gold, and Bitcoin Setups [01:03:07]

Gold is identified as the best-performing asset since 2000, with JC targeting $5,000 and describing it as a secular bull market; copper is expected to follow. China saw its biggest-ever momentum thrust in late September 2024, and JC argues these thrusts historically mark bull market beginnings rather than ends; FXI call options at multiple price points are the recommended vehicle. Bitcoin is consolidating at the 161.8% Fibonacci extension just below its record high, and JC frames a faster breakout as more bullish than a prolonged base.
