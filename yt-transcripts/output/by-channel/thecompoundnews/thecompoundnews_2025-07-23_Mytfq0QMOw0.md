# What Is Upper Middle Class? | Animal Spirits 422

**Channel:** The Compound
**Published:** 2025-07-23
**URL:** https://youtube.com/watch?v=Mytfq0QMOw0
**Duration:** 74:39

## Summary

Animal Spirits episode 422 features Michael Batnick and Ben Carlson discussing whether current stock market return rates of 13-14% annually can persist, given historical precedent and economic constraints. They cover the extreme concentration of the Mag 7 relative to the rest of the S&P 500, the ongoing underperformance of small caps, and the explosion of retail trading activity and meme stock speculation exemplified by Opendoor's wild price swings. The episode also addresses Bitcoin's maturation as an asset, Midwest housing resilience, personal finance questions about concentrated positions and upper-middle-class income thresholds, and various entertainment recommendations.

## Key Takeaways

- Stock market returns of 12-14% annually since 1980 are historically exceptional and cannot persist indefinitely due to the mathematical impossibility of the market growing faster than the economy over many decades.
- The Mag 7's market cap concentration has grown dramatically: in 2018 the top 5 equaled the bottom 282 S&P 500 companies; by 2025 the Mag 7 equals the bottom 432.
- Small caps have significantly underperformed large caps since 2014 (7.2% vs 13.2% annually), but low valuations and low sentiment may set them up for a catch-up rally similar to what happened with international stocks.
- Retail trading has reached record levels in 2025, with Q2 being the biggest trading quarter in history and meme stock activity in Opendoor highlighting retail investors' power to drive price action independent of fundamentals.
- Bitcoin's volatility has declined as it has grown to a ~$2.5 trillion asset, and new ETF inflows are purchasing Bitcoin at more than a 2-to-1 ratio relative to new supply.
- Upper-middle-class income ranges from $117,000 to $150,000 nationally per Census Bureau data, but varies widely by state and is insufficient without knowing local cost of living.
- Holding a single stock at 50% of a Roth IRA is a high-risk approach that can produce outsized gains or devastating losses, and most individual stocks eventually suffer catastrophic declines from which they never recover.

## Detailed Notes

### Stock Market Returns and Sustainability [00:02:09]

Ben argues the current period resembles a mini 1980s-90s bull market, citing annualized returns of 17-18% in those decades. Since 1980, the S&P has returned 12.1% annually. However, the hosts agree 13-15% annual returns cannot persist for 25-50 more years because the stock market would eventually dwarf the broader economy, which only grows at 2-3% real annually.

### Mag 7 Concentration and Small Caps [00:07:22]

The Mag 7's combined market cap now equals the bottom 432 S&P 500 companies, up from the top 5 equaling the bottom 282 in 2018. Meanwhile, the Russell 2000 has lagged the S&P by over 6 percentage points annually since 2014 and investors have pulled $12 billion from small-cap ETFs while adding $150 billion to large-cap funds. Nvidia alone is 65% more valuable than the entire Russell 2000. The hosts see a potential small-cap catch-up when sentiment and valuations are this depressed, analogizing to international stocks' 2025 rally.

### Retail Trading and Meme Stocks [00:17:22]

Q2 2025 was the biggest trading quarter in history, with retail investors buying more stocks on a net basis than any other six-month period in the past decade. Opendoor (OPEN) surged roughly 800% in a month driven by Reddit communities amplifying analyst Eric Jackson's bullish thesis, before crashing 40% intraday. The hosts note that retail's ability to swarm stocks has permanently changed market dynamics, unlike the institutional short-seller dominated era post-2008.

### Bitcoin and Crypto [00:38:36]

Bitcoin has reached ~$120,000 per coin and roughly $2.5 trillion in market cap, with declining volatility as the asset matures. ETF inflows are purchasing Bitcoin at more than a 2-to-1 ratio versus new supply. Ben frames his Bitcoin thesis purely on supply and demand: more buyers than sellers. The hosts note that Bitcoin is increasingly being treated as a tradable asset class rather than an ideological movement, explaining the muted emotional response compared to prior all-time highs.

### Real Estate and Housing Markets [00:44:57]

Housing prices are falling primarily in Florida, Texas, and the West Coast — markets that saw the largest migration-driven runups — while Midwest metros show no meaningful price declines. A Grand Rapids home that sold for $200,000 in 2016 is now listed at $425,000, up 85% in a decade, illustrating affordability challenges for first-time buyers. Ben maintains his long-term thesis that Great Lakes cities are a strong hedge given freshwater access and lower price appreciation.

### Personal Finance and Upper-Middle-Class Definition [00:48:23]

The hosts discuss a listener holding Robin Hood (HOOD) at 50% of their Roth IRA after a 180% gain, highlighting the risks of concentrated single-stock positions given that 40% of Russell 3000 stocks have suffered 70%+ declines from which they never recovered. CNBC data shows upper-middle-class income ranges from $117,000 to $150,000 nationally, with significant state-by-state variation. Retirement trends show more Americans working into their late 60s, with entitlement spending rising as a share of GDP while non-defense discretionary spending is flat.
