# Preparing for the Next Capital Loss Cycle | TCAF

## Summary

In this episode of The Compound and Friends, Dan Ivascyn, PIMCO's Group CIO, presents PIMCO's 2026 secular outlook 'Rupture and Resilience,' arguing that geopolitical disruption, tariffs, and deglobalization represent structural rather than cyclical shifts. Ivascyn makes the case for resilient fixed income positioning over defensive cash-hoarding, citing attractive starting yields and the rise of structured AI infrastructure financing as a new source of investment-grade-quality returns. He warns that AI is now disrupting old-economy business models in ways that will generate credit losses independent of the broader economic cycle, and that equity valuations measured by traditional metrics like the Shiller CAPE leave little room for error over the next 5-10 years.

## Key Takeaways

- PIMCO's secular thesis 'Rupture and Resilience' argues that geopolitical and economic disruptions are structural, not cyclical, and investors should position for ongoing volatility.
- Now is not the time for wildly defensive positioning; Ivascyn favors high-quality risk assets that offer downside protection while capturing growth upside.
- Structured AI infrastructure financing (e.g., data center deals) allows fixed income investors to achieve investment-grade risk at yields similar to high-yield bonds, representing a compelling new opportunity set.
- A new credit loss cycle is beginning, driven less by macroeconomic recession and more by AI's disruption of old-economy businesses with legacy debt loads.
- US equity valuations are near historically stretched levels by Shiller CAPE and equity risk premium metrics, suggesting subdued 5-10 year forward returns.
- AI is likely to be disinflationary over the medium-to-long term through productivity gains, but could cause near-term inflation through materials demand and create significant white-collar worker displacement.
- The starting yield on a bond portfolio is a near-perfect predictor of 5-year forward returns, making today's 5-8% bond yields much more attractive than the 1-2% environment of the prior decade.

## Detailed Notes

### PIMCO Background and Leadership Transition [00:08:15]

Ivascyn describes his 2017 succession as less of a philosophical shift and more of a change in communication style and media presence. Under Bill Gross, big firmwide macro calls drove returns, but post-GFC the era of fat pitches largely ended, requiring more diversified smaller-trade generation. Ivascyn emphasizes the firm's team-based investment process as a core constant.

### The Secular Forum and 'Rupture and Resilience' Outlook [00:15:18]

PIMCO's secular forum is a 40-year tradition of stepping back from daily noise to identify 3-5 year macro themes. This year's outlook, 'Rupture and Resilience,' escalates from prior year's 'Fragmentation Era' language. Key rupture forces include geopolitical conflict (Ukraine, Middle East), US political polarization, China trade tensions, tariffs as political tools, and massive AI-driven economic uncertainty.

### Investment Positioning: Resilience Over Defense [00:29:49]

Despite uncertainty, Ivascyn argues against wildly defensive positioning given resilient US economic growth, strong middle-to-upper income consumer balance sheets, and rising productivity from AI. The four major capital investment waves (AI infrastructure at $7.6T, energy grid at $2.6T, defense at $2.4T, reshoring at $1.4T) create significant economic stimulus and bond issuance opportunities.

### AI Infrastructure as a Fixed Income Opportunity [00:37:14]

For the first time in years, major tech hyperscalers (Nvidia, Google, Oracle, Meta) are coming to bond markets for financing, including mega data center deals in the $20-40 billion range. PIMCO can structure investment-grade quality exposure to Meta-equivalent credit risk at spreads 200 basis points above their public bonds, or target riskier tranches at 9-15% yields as equity alternatives. Key risk: ensuring strong documentation and collateral rights, since complex structures can mask true credit exposure.

### The Coming Credit Loss Cycle [00:47:35]

Ivascyn explains that the post-GFC period featured almost no credit losses due to low rates, COVID stimulus, and lenders extending maturities rather than forcing defaults. AI now threatens old-economy businesses with real cash flows, creating a loss cycle that is independent of macroeconomic growth. PIMCO expects losses to migrate from near-zero to mid-single digits in below-investment-grade credit, reducing historical 8% returns to roughly 4-5% in many private credit segments — much less attractive at today's risk-free rates.

### Equity Valuations and Fixed Income as an Alternative [00:42:17]

US equity risk premium is near zero and Shiller CAPE is near historical peaks last seen before the internet bubble. While earnings growth from tech giants could justify current valuations, Ivascyn notes that high starting valuations historically correlate with poor 5-10 year forward returns. Meanwhile, the 95%+ correlation between starting bond yield and 5-year forward bond returns makes fixed income more attractive today than at any point in the past 15 years.

### New Fed Chair and AI's Long-Term Impact [00:53:51]

Ivascyn views the new Fed chair (Kevin Walsh) as likely to be sufficiently independent on rates and balance sheet, preferring rules-based, minimal communication over the current transparency regime. On AI, Ivascyn is constructively optimistic about long-run productivity and disinflation, but warns of near-term inflationary pressure from materials demand, plus meaningful white-collar worker displacement — particularly for middle-income professional cohorts — that could trigger political backlash and regulatory friction.
