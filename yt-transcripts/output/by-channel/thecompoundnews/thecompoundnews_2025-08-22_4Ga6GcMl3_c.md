# Tom Lee Says It's Still Early | TCAF 205

**Channel:** The Compound
**Published:** 2025-08-22
**URL:** https://youtube.com/watch?v=4Ga6GcMl3_c
**Duration:** 80:06

## Summary

In TCAF episode 205, Tom Lee makes the case that the U.S. equity market remains early in a secular bull run, pointing to the equal-weight S&P PE still near its six-year average despite multiple crisis events any one of which should have ended the cycle. He argues AI is the singular but historically precedented driver of markets, comparable to past boom cycles, and that current market breadth and earnings quality undermine bear arguments about narrow participation. Lee also outlines a detailed thesis for Ethereum via his role as chairman of Bitmine Immersion Technology, describing it as a leverage-free Ethereum treasury with a $6.6 billion ETH position backed by equity capital from investors like Druckenmiller and Kathy Wood. He closes by discussing his Granny Shots ETF, multi-theme stock selection, the Fed's path toward rate cuts, and labor market distortions.

## Key Takeaways

- V-shaped recoveries are statistically normal after waterfall declines — 12 such events since 1929 all showed symmetric bounces without sustained retests.
- The equal-weight S&P PE has averaged 16.3 over six years and sits near that level today despite six major shocks, suggesting the market is not expensively valued on a broad basis.
- AI is the dominant, cross-sector market driver in 2025, affecting utilities (power), industrials (data centers), financials (capital markets activity), and communications — but Lee argues thematic single-driver bull markets are historically normal.
- The ISM manufacturing index has been below 50 for the longest stretch since 1950, which Lee reads as bullish — it signals businesses are underinvested and represent a coiled spring when Fed policy becomes clearer.
- Bitmine's Ethereum treasury is fully equity-financed with no leverage, distinguishing it from leveraged crypto schemes, and benefits from a 3% native ETH staking yield.
- The Granny Shots ETF (GRNY) uses multi-theme stock screening and reached $2.28 billion AUM within nine months of launch, ranking in the top second percentile year-to-date.
- Lee believes the U.S. financial system is being rearchitected onto blockchain (especially Ethereum), potentially re-rating banks as tech-multiple businesses.

## Detailed Notes

### Bull Market Context and V-Shaped Recoveries [00:12:15]

Tom Lee argues that the speed of the 2025 market recovery from April tariff-driven selloff is consistent with historical V-shaped patterns. He cites research showing that after waterfall declines — rapid drops to 10-20% — the recovery is symmetric at a 1.4-to-1.7x ratio of decline time. Lee lists 12 such events since 1929, including 1982, 1987, 2009, 2020, and 2025. He notes that retests rarely happen at the index level post-financial-crisis.

### Market Valuation and AI as the Driving Theme [00:19:00]

Lee makes the case that the equal-weight S&P 500 PE has stayed near its six-year average of 16.3x despite six 'kill shots' — the COVID crash, fastest inflation in history, fastest Fed hikes, tariff shock, supply chain crisis, and U.S. bombing of Iranian nuclear facilities. He and Josh Brown agree that AI capex is the singular driver but Lee argues single-theme bull markets are historically normal, pointing to boomer demographics driving retail stocks in the 1980s and internet infrastructure in the 1990s. He adds that margins expanding and no leverage cycle means this is not late-cycle for AI.

### Labor Market, ISM, and Fed Policy [00:35:42]

Lee views the 28-month stretch of sub-50 ISM as the longest since 1950, reading it as a sign of deeply suppressed business confidence rather than structural damage — once confidence turns, he expects a multi-year expansion above 50. He discusses labor market data quality issues with BLS versus ADP, the difficulty recent college graduates face due to AI disruption and a mismatch between liberal-arts-heavy graduate supply and trade/tech demand, and the unusual spread between Fed funds and 30-year mortgage rates. He believes two Fed cuts by year-end are likely, which combined with falling mortgage spreads could trigger a large refinance and housing activity boom.

### Ethereum and Bitmine Immersion Technology [00:55:29]

Lee details Bitmine's transformation from a Bitcoin miner into an Ethereum treasury company after he joined as chairman in June 2025. As of mid-August 2025, Bitmine held approximately 1.5 million ETH (~$6.6 billion) with zero leverage — entirely equity financed via institutional investors including Druckenmiller and Kathy Wood. Lee explains that Ethereum's 3% proof-of-stake yield means a $200 million annual income stream once staking is activated. He sees Ethereum as uniquely positioned because 60% of the stablecoin market runs on it, and the Genius Act is expected to expand stable coins from $250 billion to $4 trillion, creating exponential demand for the Ethereum network.

### Granny Shots ETF and Looking Ahead [01:09:45]

Granny Shots ETF (GRNY) reached $2.28 billion in AUM within nine months of its November 2024 launch, ranking in the second percentile year-to-date among 1,392 peers. The strategy uses equal-weighting and multi-theme screening — stocks must be relevant to at least two of Lee's long-term themes (AI, millennials, financials rearchitecture, seasonality patterns) to be included. Holdings include Meta, Goldman Sachs, Caterpillar, PayPal, Progressive, and Monster Beverage. Lee expects to gain access to major wirehouse platforms after the one-year mark, which would significantly expand AUM.
