# So Bearish It's Bullish | Animal Spirits 409

**Channel:** The Compound
**Published:** 2025-04-23
**URL:** https://youtube.com/watch?v=dSc4x7j0uoY
**Duration:** 65:07

## Summary

In episode 409 of Animal Spirits, Michael Batnick and Ben Carlson discuss the tariff-driven economic uncertainty gripping markets in April 2025, arguing the situation feels so bearish it becomes contrarily bullish. They examine supply chain risks for small businesses, the dollar's decline, and whether Bitcoin is genuinely decoupling from equities. Despite recession warnings from prominent economists, the hosts note corporate earnings remain resilient, retail investors are aggressively buying dips, and the overall correction remains relatively mild given the prior two years of 20%-plus gains.

## Key Takeaways

- Trump advisers Bessent and Lutnik engineered a tariff pause by approaching Trump without Navarro present, illustrating the chaotic and unpredictable nature of current trade policy.
- Small businesses face existential risk from extended tariffs while large corporations have sufficient margin buffers to weather the disruption.
- Bitcoin appears to be decoupling from the NASDAQ 100 and moving in line with gold, potentially reflecting dollar weakness rather than pure risk-on sentiment.
- Retail investors poured a record $6.6 billion into leveraged long ETFs in a single week, showing aggressive dip-buying behavior that historically has been rewarded.
- Private equity, particularly Blackstone, is pivoting hard toward the wealth management channel as institutional deal flow and exits remain frozen.
- The current equity correction, while painful, is still relatively mild versus recent history given the S&P remains in positive territory over the trailing one-year period.
- Consumer spending data from American Express suggests the wealth effect may be overstated, as cardholders continue spending at 2024 pace despite declining sentiment.

## Detailed Notes

### Tariff Policy Chaos and White House Dynamics [00:00:04]

WSJ reporting revealed Bessent and Lutnik rushed to the Oval Office during Navarro's absence to pitch a tariff pause, which Trump immediately posted on Truth Social. Both hosts interpret this episode as evidence of chaotic, personalistic governance. Ben firmly rejects the 'end of empire' framing, viewing tariffs as a negotiating tactic rather than intentional economic destruction.

### Supply Chain and Small Business Risks [00:00:04]

Supply chain commentators Ryan Peterson and Craig Fuller warn that container volumes from China have collapsed, with California port freight expected to nearly disappear starting in May. The hosts argue large corporations can reroute supply chains through Mexico, Singapore, and Vietnam, but small businesses lack the capital to do so and may face bankruptcy. The hosts contrast the resilience of corporate stocks against acute pain likely concentrated in Main Street businesses.

### Recession Signals and Consumer Spending [00:00:04]

Economist Neil Dutta stated on CNBC that the US is already in a recession. However, American Express earnings data showed consumer spending in early Q2 2025 was consistent with or better than 2024 levels across all customer segments, with no notable pull-forward activity. The hosts argue employment, not equity wealth, is the key driver of consumer spending, citing 2022 as evidence that stock market declines alone do not curtail consumption.

### Market Conditions: Dollar, Bitcoin, and Equities [00:00:04]

As of the recording, the S&P 500 was down roughly 16% from its highs, NASDAQ 100 down nearly 20%, and Russell 2000 down 25%; however, over the trailing twelve months the S&P remained up, making the correction appear less severe in context. Bitcoin at approximately $90,000 was outperforming tech stocks by 10 percentage points over five days, prompting discussion of whether it is tracking the dollar-weakness narrative like gold rather than acting as a risk-on asset. The US dollar's slide has simultaneously driven the strongest period of US equity underperformance vs. MSCI World ex-US in 32 years.

### Private Equity and Fixed Income Dynamics [00:00:04]

Batnick remains bullish on Blackstone despite sector selloffs, citing $270 billion in wealth management channel assets and record Q1 growth. Both hosts see private credit displacing high yield bonds in adviser portfolios, as evidenced by flat or declining AUM in HYG and JNK while Treasury ETF assets continue growing. The liquidity trade-off of private markets versus publicly traded credit is acknowledged as a potential friction point if a prolonged slowdown materializes.

### Housing and Real Estate [00:00:04]

Zillow forecast a 1.7% decline in home prices over the next year, which the hosts consider a mild base case rather than a true bear scenario. Mortgage refinance rejection rates hit their highest level since 2013, though Logan Mosambi's framing is cited: people refinancing from a 3% mortgage to 7% are already in financial distress, so the data is less alarming than headline numbers suggest.
