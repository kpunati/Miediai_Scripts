# Buy the Porsche | Animal Spirits 426

**Channel:** The Compound
**Published:** 2025-08-20
**URL:** https://youtube.com/watch?v=ZC8VXxw39io
**Duration:** 61:06

## Summary

Animal Spirits episode 426 features Michael Batnick and Ben Carlson discussing a broadly uneventful summer for equities despite all-time highs, with the market grinding sideways rather than surging. They debate whether the AI trade is over-extended, compare the Mag 7 to the broader S&P 493, and discuss healthcare as a contrarian value play. The episode also covers real-world economic topics including K-shaped consumer spending, tariff cost absorption by businesses, and the stubbornly expensive US housing market where roughly one in ten homes now exceeds $1 million in value.

## Key Takeaways

- The stock market reached all-time highs but moved largely sideways through summer 2025, with earnings beats driven by strong revenue rather than managed expectations.
- The Mag 7 recovered from a ~25% drawdown through April and has since outperformed, while small caps and international stocks rebounded roughly equally from the Liberation Day lows.
- Healthcare stocks are considered cheap on a valuation basis after underperforming technology significantly since 2020, representing a potential contrarian opportunity.
- US corporate buybacks are on pace to top $1.1 trillion in 2025, a record, with large-cap tech companies like Apple leading the activity.
- Goldman Sachs estimates businesses absorbed over half of tariff costs, but consumers should expect more pass-through in coming months.
- AI is already displacing entry-level analyst roles in financial services, raising concerns about the career ladder for young professionals.
- About 9-10% of US homes are now valued at $1 million or more, and Americans are increasingly renting rather than moving to cheaper markets.

## Detailed Notes

### Stock Market Conditions and Earnings [00:00:04]

Hosts describe the market as 'boring' — at all-time highs but moving sideways rather than rallying aggressively. Q2 S&P 500 earnings beat rates were high and revenue beats came in near 80%, with analysts having been too bearish. Retail investor flows into NASDAQ 100 stocks quintupled in relative terms during the year, though the absolute level remains around 0.25% of market cap.

### Mag 7 vs. S&P 493 and AI Debate [00:01:42]

Nvidia and Microsoft alone approach the combined market cap of all healthcare, utilities, staples, and energy stocks. The Mag 7 was down ~25% through April before recovering to outperform. Both hosts agree AI is likely overexcited but potentially transformative, referencing Howard Marks' memo that outlines responses ranging from defensive positioning to outright shorting — Marks explicitly warns against shorting a bull market.

### Small Caps, Contrarian Trades, and Healthcare [00:12:39]

Historical cycles show small caps have had long outperformance windows and are not necessarily dead. Contrarian investing is harder today because repeated underperformance has burned most contrarians out of pounding the table. A JP Morgan 30-page report on healthcare stocks notes they priced identically to tech from 1990 through COVID with lower volatility, but have underperformed significantly since 2020 and now price in a lot of bad news.

### AI Job Displacement [00:24:39]

A listener email describes being laid off six months after being hired as a firm's first analyst, with management citing AI tools enabling advisors to do more with less. Hosts acknowledge this as a real trend, particularly at the entry-level, while remaining broadly optimistic about economic adaptation. The advice given to young people: avoid roles defined by grunt work that computers can already do.

### Crypto Rebalancing and MicroStrategy [00:29:19]

Ben sold a third of his crypto position after it grew too large as a share of net worth, buying started in mid-2020 at roughly $10,000 per Bitcoin. Since the 2021 peak, Bitcoin has approximately doubled while Ethereum has only recently recovered to prior highs. MicroStrategy has purchased 629,000 Bitcoin for approximately $46 billion in total capital deployed, backed by notable investors including Peter Thiel and Stanley Druckenmiller.

### K-Shaped Economy and Tariffs [00:18:25]

Fast food chains are seeing significant traffic declines (some double-digit year-over-year) while sit-down restaurants like Olive Garden and Texas Roadhouse post strong growth, reflecting the spending divide between income cohorts. Goldman Sachs estimates businesses absorbed over half of tariff costs so far, but expects the consumer share to rise significantly in coming months as trade deal uncertainty clarifies.

### Housing Market and IRA Assets [00:34:46]

Approximately 9-10% of US homes are now worth $1 million or more. Renter households have surged by 1.6 million since 2023, yet the percentage of Americans who move annually has fallen from 17-18% in the mid-1990s to just 8% today — people are choosing to rent rather than relocate. IRA assets stand at $17 trillion, up from roughly $11 trillion pre-pandemic, with deregulation expected to open these assets to broader investment products.
