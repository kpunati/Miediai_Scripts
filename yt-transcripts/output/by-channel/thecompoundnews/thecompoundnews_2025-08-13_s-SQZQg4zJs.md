# Measuring the Wealth Effect | Animal Spirits 425

**Channel:** The Compound
**Published:** 2025-08-13
**URL:** https://youtube.com/watch?v=s-SQZQg4zJs
**Duration:** 63:42

## Summary

Animal Spirits episode 425 features Michael Batnick and Ben Carlson examining how the stock market's growing share of household wealth—now at a record 36% of assets—is reshaping consumer spending and the K-shaped economy. The hosts debate whether the Mag 7's concentration resembles the Nifty Fifty bubble of the 1970s, and whether crypto treasury strategies pioneered by MicroStrategy and Tom Lee's Bitmine can be replicated by copycat companies. Additional topics include the failure rate of alternative mutual funds as a cautionary tale for private markets entering 401ks, ESPN's standalone streaming launch, and listener recommendations for movies and TV.

## Key Takeaways

- Stocks now account for a record 36% of U.S. household assets, making the wealth effect a more powerful driver of consumer spending than in prior decades.
- The top 10-20% of earners now account for roughly half of consumer spending, meaning recession risk depends heavily on whether affluent households continue to spend.
- The Mag 7's premium valuations are more comparable to the Nifty Fifty of the early 1970s than to the dot-com bubble, since 2022 already delivered a significant washout.
- Crypto treasury strategies may work for Tom Lee and MicroStrategy but are unlikely to succeed for the 154+ copycat public companies borrowing to buy Bitcoin or Ethereum.
- A 75% mortality rate among alternative mutual funds launched before 2015 is cited as a warning sign for the coming push to include private market products in 401ks.
- Major consumer-facing companies—Mastercard, Visa, Amazon, SoFi—are all reporting healthy consumer spending, contradicting broader pessimistic sentiment.
- Money market assets have reached $7.2 trillion and may only rotate out if the Fed cuts rates substantially below current levels.

## Detailed Notes

### Wealth Effect and Stock Market Concentration [00:00:04]

Chartist Matt Kadnar's data shows household net worth is more tied to stock market performance than at any prior point in history. Stocks now represent 36% of household assets—a record going back to the 1950s—and 60% of households now own stocks versus 19% in 1983. Individual options activity recently reached 20% of total market activity, exceeding the 2021 meme stock peak.

### Mag 7 vs. Nifty Fifty and Bubble Debate [00:00:04]

The hosts debate whether current large-cap concentration constitutes a bubble. JP Morgan data shows the top 10 stocks trading at 29x earnings versus 21x for the rest of the S&P. Batnick argues the Nifty Fifty—which traded at 42x in the early 1970s versus the S&P at 19x—is a better historical comp than the dot-com bubble, with stocks like McDonald's and Disney falling 60-70% in that era. The 2022 drawdown (Nvidia -70%, Netflix -70%, Meta -70%) is noted as a recent precedent.

### K-Shaped Economy and Consumer Spending [00:00:04]

The top 10% of earners now drive approximately half of all U.S. consumer spending, up from 36% three decades ago. The bottom 80% of households have reverted to spending in line with inflation after the post-pandemic revenge spending surge. Carlson cites earnings call comments from Mastercard, Visa, Amazon, and SoFi all indicating continued consumer health despite macro uncertainty.

### Crypto Treasury Strategies: Tom Lee and Bitmine [00:00:04]

Harvard Management Company disclosed a $116 million position in BlackRock's iShares Bitcoin Trust. ETH outperformed Bitcoin for 14 consecutive days by trading volume. Tom Lee's Bitmine Technologies announced a $20 billion equity offering targeting 5% of all ETH supply, following MicroStrategy's blueprint. However, the FT reported 154 public companies have committed a combined $98 billion to crypto purchases, and the hosts warn that copycat strategies are unlikely to work for most participants.

### Private Markets in 401ks and Alternative Fund Mortality [00:00:04]

Jeffrey Ptak's Morningstar analysis found 75% of alternative mutual funds that existed in January 2015 have since been liquidated or merged—1,004 out of 1,345. Legislation to allow private assets into 401ks is advancing. The hosts disagree on severity: Carlson worries about illiquidity mismatches and fee drag, while Batnick does not expect a retirement crisis, predicting investors will simply underperform index funds.

### Streaming and Entertainment [00:00:04]

ESPN launched a standalone streaming service at $25/month. Ben is skeptical given competition from YouTube TV at $80 for broader content. Paramount Plus acquired UFC rights for $1.1 billion over seven years, ending the pay-per-view model. Hosts recommend the films Weapons and Together, and the Apple TV+ miniseries Lessons in Chemistry.
