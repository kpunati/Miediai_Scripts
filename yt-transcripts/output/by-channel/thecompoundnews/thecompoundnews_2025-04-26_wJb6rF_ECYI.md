# Derek Thompson Feels Like He's Taking Crazy Pills

**Channel:** The Compound
**Published:** 2025-04-26
**URL:** https://youtube.com/watch?v=wJb6rF_ECYI
**Duration:** 33:32

## Summary

Derek Thompson joins The Compound's Animal Spirits to discuss Abundance, the book he co-authored with Ezra Klein arguing America must build more housing, clean energy, and infrastructure to solve widespread scarcity. The interview spans the political reception of the book under Trump, why third parties remain structurally impossible despite soaring independent identification, the identity-consuming nature of MAGA, and Thompson's now-viral CNBC breakdown reacting to hedge fund manager Kyle Bass's claim that a crashing economy would be good for 25-year-olds. The discussion closes on the concrete supply chain consequences of US-China tariffs, where Thompson cites data showing extreme import concentration in consumer goods like child safety seats and toys, and warns that small businesses — unlike large retailers — have no ability to lobby for exemptions.

## Key Takeaways

- The Abundance agenda argues the US must shift from demand-side to supply-side solutions — building housing, energy, and infrastructure rather than just subsidizing consumption.
- Despite the Democratic Party's brand reaching historic lows, Thompson sees an opening for moderates to adopt abundance as a reform message that runs against the system without wanting to destroy it.
- MAGA functions as a personality-transforming ideology unlike most political movements, which Thompson uses to explain why even indefensible policies retain strong partisan support.
- Thompson's CNBC viral moment illustrated the disconnect between insider financial narratives framing market crashes as constructive resets and the lived harm to ordinary Americans.
- Supply chain data shows the US is extremely dependent on China for everyday consumer goods including 99% of certain child safety seats and 70-80% of children's toys, making tariff impacts broadly felt.
- Small businesses face a compounded disadvantage: they cannot calculate rapidly shifting tariff rates on their supply chains and they lack the lobbying access that large retailers like Target enjoy.
- Thompson predicts shipping volumes, manufacturing orders, layoffs, and retail shortages will materialize within six months as the real-economy lagged effect of the US-China trade war.

## Detailed Notes

### What Is Abundance and Why Does It Matter Now [00:01:07]

Thompson explains Abundance as a synthesis of his abundance agenda concept (building more vaccines, houses, clean energy, doctors) and Ezra Klein's supply-side progressivism thesis. He notes the book was written in 2023-2024 for a potential Kamala Harris administration. In the actual Trump era, the book has found an unexpected audience among center-right Romney-type Republicans who see it as a pro-growth, pro-science, pro-free-trade agenda. The Democratic Party's approval rating has collapsed to 27%, creating a clearing in the political forest that Thompson believes abundance-style reformism could fill.

### Third Parties, MAGA Identity, and Political Realignment [00:04:54]

Thompson argues that while independent identification is at record highs, structural barriers — party infrastructure and first-past-the-post voting — make a successful third party nearly impossible. He reframes the landscape as having been four parties in 2015 (MAGA, Romney Republicans, Obama-Biden Democrats, Bernie Democrats), with Trump effectively eating the Romney Republican wing. He contrasts ideologies that give people new thoughts with ideologies that give people an entire personality, arguing Trumpism is unusually effective at the latter, changing how adherents process economic data and conflicting information.

### The CNBC Crazy Pills Moment [00:13:21]

Thompson recounts returning exhausted from book tour to appear on CNBC and listening in the green room to hedge fund manager Kyle Bass calmly argue that a falling stock market, falling housing prices, and a recession would all be net positives. The contrast between Bass's soothing delivery and what Thompson found to be economically incoherent reasoning produced his visible on-air breakdown. He clarifies the key distinction: Abundance argues for building more housing to reduce prices through supply, not for crashing the economy to produce forced corrections.

### Trade War Consequences: Supply Chains and Small Business [00:23:46]

Thompson cites supply chain researcher Jason Miller's data showing extreme US import concentration from China in everyday consumer goods: 99% of certain child safety seats, 93-94% of children's coloring books, and 70-80% of toys for children up to age 12. He predicts a visible wave of retail shortages and price increases in the next six months that will matter more politically than stock market movements. He distinguishes large retailers like Target and Walmart, whose CEOs can lobby the White House for tariff exemptions, from small businesses that have no such access and cannot operationally manage week-to-week tariff rate changes in their supply chains.
