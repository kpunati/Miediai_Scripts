# Hear Me Out - What if We Rally Furiously?

**Channel:** The Compound
**Published:** 2026-04-21
**URL:** https://youtube.com/watch?v=2nY5-rKujrs
**Duration:** 60:29

## Summary

In this episode of What Are Your Thoughts, Josh Brown and Michael Batnick cover Tim Cook's retirement from Apple after 15 years and his handoff to hardware engineering chief John Ternus. They discuss Tom Lee's bullish call that simultaneous earnings growth and multiple expansion could make the next 18-24 months one of history's best market periods, contextualized against the current post-tariff-war rally happening without corporate buybacks and with limited retail participation. Earnings from Netflix, Morgan Stanley, and Schwab are dissected, with Morgan Stanley's wealth management engine drawing particular praise and Schwab facing investor concern over AI competitive threats to its cash-sweep revenue model.

## Key Takeaways

- Tim Cook grew Apple's market cap 11x from ~$377 billion to over $4 trillion in 15 years, and successor John Ternus is described as a more decisive, detail-obsessed engineer likely to represent continuity.
- Tom Lee argues US equity multiples should expand, not contract, and that retail under-investment plus international demand could fuel a furious multi-year rally toward S&P 10,000.
- The recent strong market rally occurred during a corporate buyback blackout and a seasonally weak retail buying period, making the move technically unusual and notable.
- Morgan Stanley's wealth management channel took in $118 billion in net new assets in Q1, with wealth management at ~45% of total revenue and regarded as the best-built funnel in the industry.
- Schwab stock fell ~8% after Q1 earnings despite a record quarter, driven largely by investor fear that JPMorgan's forthcoming AI cash management tool could erode Schwab's sweep-cash revenue.
- Small caps (Russell 2000) and micro caps (Russell Microcap up ~75% year-over-year) are seeing strong relative performance, viewed as beneficiaries of AI spending by smaller companies and anticipation of rate cuts.
- Caterpillar has been on The Compound's best-stocks list since July and has outperformed; the hosts highlight it as an example of a clean technical uptrend in an industrial Dow component.

## Detailed Notes

### Tim Cook's Departure and Apple Leadership Transition [00:00:18]

Tim Cook became CEO in 2011 when Steve Jobs passed and is credited with 11x-ing Apple's market cap. His successor John Ternus is a 25-year Apple veteran and VP of hardware engineering who oversaw iPad, AirPods, MacBook Pro, iPhone 12, and Apple Watch. Bloomberg describes Ternus as more decisive than Cook's consensus-oriented style. The hosts see this as continuity rather than a sharp strategic pivot.

### Trump on Iran, the Dow, and Anthropic [00:00:18]

Trump told Squawk Box the US would reach a deal with Iran and expressed surprise oil remained under $100 despite the Strait of Hormuz conflict. He referred to the Dow as personal ('when I get back to 50,000'), reinforcing the view that he equates the index with his own performance. On Anthropic, he signaled a thaw after recent White House meetings, easing concerns about regulatory pressure on the AI company.

### Tom Lee Bull Case and Market Rally Dynamics [00:00:18]

Tom Lee argues US P/E multiples should expand as the war premium fades, retail investors are under-invested, and international capital has limited alternatives to US growth equities. The hosts note the recent rally achieved 12% in 13 days—historically a rare event usually seen in bear markets at much deeper drawdowns—while the current selloff was only ~9%. The FOMO dynamic is seen as the dominant force, with every selloff historically looking like a buying opportunity.

### Netflix Earnings: Record Revenue vs. Soft Guidance [00:00:18]

Netflix reported its biggest quarter ever by revenue but saw the stock fall ~9% after earnings due to weaker Q2 and cautious full-year guidance. Analysts cited the lack of a full-year raise as the problem while maintaining ratings. The hosts highlight the intensifying YouTube vs. Netflix competition as the defining streaming battle, and note Netflix's FY2026 EBIT of $16.2 billion now exceeds all of Disney's adjusted operating income.

### Morgan Stanley and Schwab Earnings [00:00:18]

Morgan Stanley's wealth management model—described as the best funnel in the industry—generated $118 billion in Q1 net new assets with wealth at ~45% of revenue. Schwab reported record total client assets of $11.77 trillion and 1.3 million new brokerage accounts but saw its stock fall ~8% as investors feared JPMorgan's AI cash management tool could disrupt Schwab's sweep-cash business, which accounts for roughly half its revenue. The hosts are skeptical the JPMorgan tool represents a near-term threat.

### Small Cap and Micro Cap Rally [00:00:18]

The Russell 2000 appears to be snapping a multi-year downtrend vs. the S&P 500, and the hosts frame the rotation as the 'handoff' from AI hyperscaler capex to smaller beneficiary companies. The Russell Microcap Index is up ~75% year-over-year vs. S&P 500's 36%, though the hosts note several top IWC holdings are crypto miners of questionable quality.

### Caterpillar Mystery Chart [00:00:18]

Caterpillar was revealed as the mystery Dow component, highlighted as having been on The Compound's best-stocks list since July without falling off. The hosts point to its clean 50-day and 200-day technical structure as a textbook uptrend, though caution that buying at current parabolic levels without a stop would be reckless.
