# Winners & Losers From the Stock Market Rally | Animal Spirits 412

**Channel:** The Compound
**Published:** 2025-05-14
**URL:** https://youtube.com/watch?v=-A3SCs0sJ7k
**Duration:** 67:22

## Summary

In episode 412 of Animal Spirits, Michael Batnick and Ben Carlson examine the market's rapid V-shaped recovery after the US-China tariff pause, declaring retail investors the clear winners while Wall Street bulls who had turned bearish were exposed. They highlight record corporate buyback announcements as evidence that corporations themselves were not pricing in a catastrophic trade war scenario. The episode also covers growing concerns about private equity being marketed into 401(k) vehicles and the potential for retail savers to become the ultimate bag holders, as well as housing market divergence between the supply-flooded South and inventory-starved Northeast.

## Key Takeaways

- Retail investors who bought the dip during April 2025 tariff volatility were vindicated as the S&P 500 recovered nearly all losses within weeks.
- Corporate buyback announcements hit record highs during the tariff crisis, signaling that large companies were not expecting a prolonged economic catastrophe.
- The hosts argue the tariff episode may qualify as 'the most unnecessary bear market of all time' since the root policy was reversed within roughly a month.
- Proposed 4-to-1 leverage products for 401(k) savers are viewed skeptically, with concern that private credit yield assumptions are fragile and borrowing costs may not remain attractive.
- Private equity firms like KKR and Apollo are actively lobbying to get allocations into target date funds, and the hosts worry that retail investors will face compressed returns as too much capital chases a limited deal pipeline.
- Housing inventory in Florida and the broader US South is surging to multi-year highs while the Northeast faces near-historic shortages, reflecting a reversal of pandemic-era migration demand.
- Coinbase joining the S&P 500 and ETH rising roughly 65% in a month illustrate continued mainstreaming of crypto, though the hosts note no one could clearly explain ETH's catalyst.

## Detailed Notes

### Tariff Backtrack and the Stock Market Rally [00:03:16]

Batnick and Carlson credit the market's rapid recovery to Trump reversing extreme tariff levels after pressure from major CEOs including Jamie Dimon, Tim Cook, and Walmart executives. The S&P 500 gained 3% in a single session after the pause announcement and recovered to within 5% of all-time highs. They emphasize that both 'doom scenario' pessimists and 'brilliant strategic chess move' proponents were wrong, leaving a muddle-through outcome with tariffs still elevated but far below Liberation Day peaks.

### Retail Investors vs. Wall Street Sentiment [00:06:39]

Retail investors purchased a record $4.7 billion in equities on April 4th despite widespread panic, while Wall Street strategists and hedge fund managers like Paul Tudor Jones predicted new lows. The hosts note that contrarian data signals such as 9-to-1 NYSE advancing volume on April 9th had historically perfect forward-return track records. Missing even a single best day — such as the 10% up-day on April 9th — would have left investors down 9% versus flat for those who stayed invested.

### Corporate Buybacks and Recession Odds [00:19:14]

S&P 500 companies announced record buyback plans of $192 billion in a week and $518 billion over three months, suggesting large corporations were not pricing in a severe downturn. Kalshi recession probability estimates fell from roughly 75% to 41% following the tariff pause, and the hosts argue the market may have actively prevented a recession by rebounding strongly enough to restore confidence before the slowdown became self-fulfilling.

### Leverage in 401(k)s and Private Equity Risk [00:23:26]

Startup Basic Capital is pitching 4-to-1 leverage for retirement savers, borrowing at 6.25% to invest in private credit targeting 9% yields. Batnick and Carlson are skeptical of the yield spread and concerned about what happens if private credit yields compress. They also discuss KKR and Apollo pushing to place private equity into target date funds, worrying that the flood of capital from evergreen and interval funds will result in lower future returns as money must be deployed immediately rather than waiting for attractive deals.

### Crypto: Coinbase S&P Inclusion and ETH Surge [00:31:12]

Coinbase is entering the S&P 500 and acquiring Derivet for $2.9 billion while Robin Hood announced a $250 million acquisition of a similar Canadian options platform. Ethereum surged approximately 65% in a month with no clear data-driven explanation identified on crypto Twitter. Coinbase stock has underperformed Bitcoin significantly since its 2021 IPO, which the hosts find surprising given the expected leveraged-beta relationship.

### Housing Market Divergence [00:34:56]

Active housing listings in the US South, particularly Florida, are at decade highs as pandemic-era migration demand has been exhausted and inventory surges. The Northeast by contrast faces near-historic supply shortages. Batnick shares a personal anecdote about selling a Long Beach rental property and accepting that easy money has already been made in post-pandemic real estate markets.

### Jonathan Clements Legacy and Recommendations [00:45:30]

The hosts highlight Jonathan Clements's initiative to grant young people aged 18-24 a $1,000 grant to open Roth IRAs after completing a personal finance course, organized through the Bogle Center. Both hosts announced personal contributions to the program. The recommendations segment covers TV shows including The Studio on Apple TV+, Four Seasons on Netflix, and The Last of Us Season 2, along with NBA playoffs discussion centered on the New York Knicks.
