# How Coinbase Became the Godfather of Crypto | LFTC

**Channel:** The Compound
**Published:** 2025-08-18
**URL:** https://youtube.com/watch?v=-zD5WFeklaY
**Duration:** 42:33

## Summary

Josh Brown and Michael Batnick interview Coinbase CFO Alesia Haas and CLO Paul Grewal on the company's strategic vision and recent milestones. The discussion covers Coinbase's evolution from a crypto exchange to an infrastructure provider for the entire digital asset ecosystem, including ETF custody, stablecoin distribution through USDC and Circle, and the legal battles with the SEC. The executives outline plans to expand into derivatives, tokenized equities, and become an everything exchange, while reflecting on the regulatory landscape shift under the Trump administration.

## Key Takeaways

- Coinbase positions itself as the infrastructure provider of choice for institutional crypto adoption, securing over 80% of Bitcoin and ETH ETF assets.
- The company views crypto's evolution in three stages: asset class, financial system, and app platform, with Base as a layer-two example.
- Paul Grewal argues that suing the SEC in 2023 was ultimately necessary for the industry's survival and a fight for all crypto companies, not just Coinbase.
- Alesia Haas explains Bitcoin treasury companies are using equity wrappers to gain crypto exposure because many funds cannot hold spot commodities directly, introducing a new but opaque form of leverage.
- Coinbase plans to become an everything exchange by tokenizing equities, commodities, real estate, and prediction markets, bringing them onchain.
- The Genius Act on stablecoins has passed, but market structure legislation clarifying the SEC/CFTC boundary over digital assets remains pending in the Senate.
- Spreads on spot digital assets remain wide because the market is not yet fully commoditized, but compression is expected as more platforms offer the same products.

## Detailed Notes

### Company Scale and Crypto as an Asset Class [00:00:16]

Coinbase is a top-25 market cap company in the XLF, larger than Nasdaq, MEI, and CBOE. CFO Haas corrects the intro noting $400+ billion in assets on platform. The hosts note that spot Bitcoin and Ether ETFs did about $40 billion in weekly volume, comparable to VOO and QQQ.

### FTX Collapse and Industry Recovery [00:09:57]

Haas was unsurprised by both the crypto crash following FTX's failure and the subsequent recovery, citing leverage unwinding as the driver of the decline and institutional buying as a tailwind during the trough. Coinbase had to undergo a re-education process with banks and partners to rebuild trust in its controls and infrastructure.

### Regulatory Battles and Political Landscape [00:11:14]

Grewal credits the Trump administration as the most pro-crypto in history while describing the Biden administration as actively hostile, including pressuring companies to leave the US via regulation by enforcement. Coinbase sued the SEC in 2023 after years of failed engagement, ultimately winning clarity that non-custodial wallets fall outside federal securities law. The company still faces a lawsuit from Oregon and works on international regulatory issues.

### ETF Custody and Institutional Partnerships [00:26:55]

Coinbase custody for ETFs cements its status as institutional infrastructure of choice after competing against traditional and crypto-native players. PNC Bank has partnered with Coinbase to embed crypto trading and storage under its platform. JP Morgan is enabling credit card points redemption into Coinbase, moving from prior hostility to active partnership.

### Stablecoins and USDC Strategy [00:25:18]

Coinbase partnered with Circle in 2018 to build USDC as a multi-party ecosystem rather than launching a proprietary stablecoin, believing network effects and broad distribution were more valuable than ownership. At Q2 end, Coinbase held $14 billion of the $60 billion in circulating USDC. The Genius Act has passed, providing the first legislative framework for stablecoins.

### Future Vision and Everything Exchange [00:30:58]

Coinbase's strategic roadmap is to become an everything exchange by tokenizing all asset classes including equities, commodities, real estate, and prediction markets. The company recently launched 24/7 futures and closed its acquisition of Deribit to add options, with tokenized equities as the next frontier. This puts Coinbase on a collision course with traditional brokerages such as Vanguard, Schwab, and Fidelity.

### Bitcoin Treasury Companies and Leverage Risk [00:19:28]

Strategy holds 629,000 Bitcoin; 154 public companies have raised or committed $98 billion to buy crypto. Haas explains these companies use equity wrappers because fund mandates prohibit holding spot commodities directly. The key unresolved risk is that total leverage in the system is opaque since many crypto holdings sit outside the US without financial reporting obligations.
