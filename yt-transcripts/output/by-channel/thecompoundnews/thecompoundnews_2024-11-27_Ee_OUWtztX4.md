# The Leverage Mania | Animal Spirits 388

**Channel:** The Compound
**Published:** 2024-11-27
**URL:** https://youtube.com/watch?v=Ee_OUWtztX4
**Duration:** 64:38

## Summary

Animal Spirits episode 388 covers a wide range of financial and cultural topics recorded just before Thanksgiving 2024. Michael Batnick and Ben Carlson examine the historic stock market run, record S&P 500 concentration in top ten names, and MicroStrategy's unprecedented leveraged Bitcoin treasury scheme. The hosts also discuss tariff threats from the incoming Trump administration, the growing use of HELOCs by homeowners, and single-stock leveraged ETF proliferation. Ben shares personal Bitcoin portfolio rebalancing decisions and vacation observations from the Dominican Republic.

## Key Takeaways

- Technology stocks have dominated for a decade with near-800% returns since 2014, and the top-10 S&P 500 names now account for 36% of market cap — a record not seen as inherently bearish by some analysts.
- MicroStrategy's strategy of issuing near-zero-interest convertible debt to buy Bitcoin is described as one of the greatest financial engineering schemes in Wall Street history, but its premium over Bitcoin NAV is seen as the key vulnerability.
- Ben Carlson sold 25% of his Bitcoin position near $99,000 to rebalance, citing regret minimization as the framework — practicing disciplined portfolio management amid extreme volatility.
- Single-stock leveraged ETFs are attracting massive flows, with a 2X MicroStrategy ETF drawing nearly twice the AUM of established quant firms despite a 1.29% expense ratio.
- Home equity totals $35 trillion nationally; HELOCs are increasingly being used for consumption, and first-time homebuyers have been squeezed from roughly 40% to 24% of transactions.
- Silicon Valley's backing of the Trump administration is framed not as Republican alignment but as a desire to remove regulatory barriers and let innovation proceed unchecked.
- Tariffs proposed on Mexico and Canada at 25% caused minimal initial market reaction, with analysts speculating they may be a negotiating tactic rather than firm policy.

## Detailed Notes

### Stock Market Strength and Concentration [00:00:04]

Hosts discuss the continuing bull market with technology up nearly 800% since 2014, per a sector return chart. Bank of America data shows the top 10 S&P 500 stocks — including Microsoft, Apple, Nvidia, Alphabet, Amazon, Meta, Berkshire, Eli Lilly, JPMorgan, and Broadcom — now represent 36% of total market cap, exceeding the dot-com peak of 27%. Strategists like Nick Colas at DataTrek argue this reflects the index's ability to hold meaningful positions in genuinely superior companies.

### Silicon Valley, DOGE, and Political Realignment [00:00:04]

The hosts reference the book 'Boom: Bubbles and the End of Stagnation' by Byrne Hobart and Tobias Huber, which argues physical progress has stagnated while digital innovation has flourished. They connect the thesis to Silicon Valley's support for Trump, framing it not as partisan but as a desire to dismantle regulatory barriers. Mark Andreessen, Joe Lonsdale, and Travis Kalanick are named as potential DOGE participants. Scott Bessent, incoming Treasury Secretary and former Soros CIO, is praised as a sober, rational choice.

### MicroStrategy and Leveraged Bitcoin Vehicles [00:00:04]

Michael Saylor's MicroStrategy strategy is examined: issuing near-zero convertible debt, immediately deploying proceeds to buy Bitcoin, benefiting from equity premium as Bitcoin rises, then repeating. The stock briefly became the most traded US security, surpassing Tesla and Nvidia. Hosts identify the key risk as competitors replicating the model and eroding MicroStrategy's 3-4x premium to Bitcoin NAV. Single-stock leveraged ETFs tied to MSTR are also proliferating, with one 2X product collecting nearly twice the AUM of established quant firms.

### Bitcoin Portfolio Management and Listener Feedback [00:00:04]

Ben Carlson reveals he sold 25% of his Bitcoin position near $99,000 because it had grown to nearly 10% of his portfolio, using the framework of regret minimization. He plans to sell another tranche if prices rise further and rebalance back in if prices fall sharply. A listener email story of a Harvard graduate investing his parents' $700,000 retirement portfolio heavily in the Defiance ETF and MicroStrategy is cited as an example of excess FOMO.

### HELOCs, Housing, and the US Consumer [00:00:04]

US total home equity stands at $35 trillion. Torsten Slok's daily letter is cited noting homeowners are increasingly liquifying equity gains via HELOCs for consumption spending. First-time homebuyers have declined from roughly 40% of the market historically to around 24% now, a long-running trend the hosts identify as one of the most damaging economic outcomes of the 2020s for younger Americans.

### Tariffs, Trade Policy, and Market Reaction [00:00:04]

Trump's Truth Social post threatening 25% tariffs on all goods from Mexico and Canada prompted only a modest market reaction, with the Mexico ETF (EWW) down less than 2%. Goldman Sachs data from the first Trump term showed tariff-impacted goods prices rose but did not spill over to other core goods. Hosts speculate markets are treating the announcement as a negotiating tactic rather than firm policy.

### Cultural Round: Sports Betting, NIL, and Vacation Observations [00:00:04]

New York and New Jersey lead US sports betting with $2.33 billion wagered; hosts discuss the long-term risks of normalization. NIL money in college athletics is discussed through the lens of Michigan's recruitment of the top quarterback prospect, with Larry Ellison cited as a major donor. Ben's Dominican Republic vacation anecdotes cover income inequality ($8,000 median income), all-inclusive resort economics, and observations on happiness in lower-income societies.
