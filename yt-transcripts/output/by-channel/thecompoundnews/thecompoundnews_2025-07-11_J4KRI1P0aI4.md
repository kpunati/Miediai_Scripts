# Why the Stock Market Goes Higher in the Second Half | TCAF 199

**Channel:** The Compound
**Published:** 2025-07-11
**URL:** https://youtube.com/watch?v=J4KRI1P0aI4
**Duration:** 71:54

## Summary

In episode 199 of The Compound and Friends, hosts Josh Brown and Michael Batnick sit down with Ryan Detrick and Sonu Varghese of Carson Group for a mid-year market review. The guests argue that the absence of a recession, combined with AI-driven earnings growth and powerful breadth thrust signals off the April 2025 lows, supports continued equity outperformance in the second half of the year. They examine the K-shaped consumer economy, noting that upper-income households with appreciating assets are the primary driver of spending and corporate profits, while lower-income cohorts are under meaningful pressure from elevated utility costs and slowing wage growth. Seasonal studies and technical indicators, including advance-decline lines and credit spreads, reinforce a constructive outlook for equities.

## Key Takeaways

- The S&P 500 recovered 25% in three months from the April 2025 lows, matching historic patterns that preceded further gains of 22% on average over the next year.
- AI capital expenditure is the single largest driver of current bull market leadership across technology, industrials, and financials.
- Carson Group's proprietary leading economic indicator shows the US economy growing below trend but clearly not recessionary, supporting an equity overweight stance.
- S&P 500 forward earnings hit a record $281 per share and profit margins reached a new cycle high, with both metrics continuing to expand.
- The consumer economy is deeply bifurcated: upper-income households with strong asset wealth are driving spending, while the bottom two income quintiles show signs of retrenchment visible in Vegas tourism data, airline economy class demand, and credit card spending.
- Seasonal analysis shows above-average second-half returns when the first half lands in the 5-10% gain range and when both May and June are positive.
- Key risk monitors -- credit spreads and advance-decline lines -- remain healthy and are not signaling deterioration.

## Detailed Notes

### April Lows and Bull Market Recovery [00:00:00]

Ryan Detrick noted the S&P 500 gained 25% in three months from April 2025 lows, only the fifth such instance in history. Prior occurrences came off the 1975, 1982, 2009, 2020, and early-1999 lows; one year later stocks were higher every time with a 22% average return. April 9th's breadth day -- where 98% of volume was in advancing stocks -- and a follow-up thrust on April 21st were identified as the definitive bottoming signals.

### AI as the Singular Bull Market Theme [00:00:00]

The panel agreed that AI capital expenditure is driving leadership not just in technology but across industrials (data center construction, earth-moving), financials (IPO revival, M&A, wealth management), and communication services. Josh Brown argued that even Boeing and Caterpillar are AI beneficiaries through physical infrastructure buildout, and that this single theme is 'single-handedly powering this current bull market.'

### K-Shaped Consumer and Economic Divergence [00:00:00]

Josh Brown challenged the utility of 'the consumer' as a single concept, noting the bottom 20% of households by income have stopped discretionary travel and are making dramatic retail substitutions visible in credit card data. Sonu Varghese provided context that the bottom quintile accounts for only about 15 basis points of S&P 500 consumption, so their weakness does not directly impair corporate earnings. Electricity and gas prices rising at a 14.5% annualized rate over three months compound the pressure on lower-income cohorts while wage growth has slowed to 3%.

### Earnings, Margins, and Valuation [00:00:00]

S&P 500 12-month forward earnings reached a record $281 per share and profit margins hit a new cycle high of 13.7%. Breaking down the 112% total return since end-2019, roughly 50% came from sales growth, 26 percentage points from multiple expansion, 20 percentage points from margin expansion, and 17 percentage points from dividends. The panel viewed this as a healthy mix and argued that elevated multiples are rational given record profitability.

### Seasonal Studies and Second-Half Outlook [00:00:00]

Detrick presented two seasonal overlays: when the first half gains are in the 5-10% range, the S&P finishes higher 13 of 15 times; when both May and June are positive, the final six months are higher 15 of 16 times with an average return near 9%, nearly double the typical second-half average. He also noted that year three of bull markets is historically choppy but rarely terminal, and that the five bull markets making it this far averaged eight more years of duration with a five-year minimum.

### Risk Monitors and Portfolio Positioning [00:00:00]

Ryan Detrick watches advance-decline lines and high-yield credit spreads as primary bear-market warnings, both of which remain benign. Carson Group moved to a neutral US-versus-international weight in February-March before liberation day tariffs, citing the weakening dollar and German fiscal stimulus. They maintain a small gold allocation as a hedge and advocate managed futures for commodity supply-shock exposure. The overweight equity stance is supported by the absence of recessionary signals in their proprietary LEI.
