# The Age of Discontentment | Animal Spirits 441

**Channel:** The Compound
**Published:** 2025-12-03
**URL:** https://youtube.com/watch?v=2aBx3TLGTTU
**Duration:** 72:11

## Summary

Animal Spirits episode 441 features Michael Batnick and Ben Carlson discussing the strong 2025 market performance and why plain vanilla diversified portfolios have outperformed. The hosts explore wealth inequality, housing affordability stress, and the psychological roots of widespread economic discontent despite rising prosperity. They also cover crypto weakness and MicroStrategy's liquidity moves, Robinhood's retail investing dominance, AI revenue milestones, and the ongoing shift from legacy media to streaming platforms.

## Key Takeaways

- Plain vanilla three-fund portfolios (US stocks, international stocks, bonds) are having an excellent 2025 with the S&P up ~17% and the aggregate bond market up ~7%.
- Foreign capital inflows into US equities recovered sharply after the April tariff shock and reached record levels by late 2025.
- Economic discontent is driven not just by affordability pressures but by rising expectations and increased visibility of extreme wealth via social media.
- MicroStrategy (Strategy) established a $1.44 billion USD cash reserve to cover preferred dividends, signaling balance sheet caution amid Bitcoin's price decline.
- Robinhood dominates online brokerage daily active users and is credited with bringing a new generation of young investors into the market.
- Legacy broadcast and cable TV fell from 57.5% to 42% of US viewing time in under two years, while YouTube is now larger than Netflix by viewership.
- AI generative revenue has grown ninefold in two years to over $60 billion, and could hit $650 billion by 2029 if growth sustains at 100% per year.

## Detailed Notes

### 2025 Market Performance and Plain Vanilla Portfolios [00:00:46]

The S&P 500 is up roughly 17% year-to-date with only a 1% drawdown. A basic three-fund Vanguard portfolio of US stocks, international stocks, and bonds is performing exceptionally well, with international stocks up nearly 30% and the aggregate bond market up about 7%. The hosts note that narratives about plain vanilla strategies being dead have been proven wrong again.

### Wall Street Strategist Forecasts and Overvaluation Debate [00:04:35]

Wall Street strategists are clustering 2026 S&P price targets between 7,100 and 8,000, representing 4-17% gains. The hosts discuss Jeff Gundlach's claim that almost all financial assets are overvalued, and push back arguing that capital must go somewhere and not all asset classes can be simultaneously overvalued. Foreign capital inflows hit a record $646 billion per Yardeni, rebounding strongly after the April tariff-driven selloff.

### Wealth Inequality and the Age of Discontent [00:21:46]

Citing Allison Schrager, the hosts discuss how household incomes above $150,000 have grown while the middle class has shrunk, largely because people have moved upward. Despite broadly rising prosperity, people feel worse off because expectations have risen, visible extreme wealth has multiplied via social media, and necessities like housing, healthcare, and childcare have become far more expensive relative to income.

### Housing Affordability and Behavioral Effects [00:41:00]

A University of Chicago and Northwestern study found that young adults with little realistic prospect of homeownership show reduced work effort and increased investment in risky financial assets. The hosts note that UK and Australian house price-to-income ratios are significantly worse than the US, and express pessimism that enough housing supply will be built to meaningfully address the problem.

### Crypto Market and MicroStrategy (Strategy) [00:35:42]

Bitcoin and crypto assets have sold off significantly, with investors pulling $6 billion from global crypto funds in November in what is described as the worst monthly ETF exodus on record. MicroStrategy (now branded Strategy) established a $1.44 billion USD reserve funded by stock sales to cover at least 12 months of preferred dividends. The hosts discuss the MNAV premium dynamics and note the stock's strong correlation to Bitcoin's price direction.

### Robinhood and Retail Investor Trends [00:15:22]

Robinhood holds the largest share of daily active users among online platforms, compared to E*Trade, Schwab, and Fidelity, though its share has declined from its 2021 meme-stock peak. The hosts credit Robinhood with democratizing investing, especially among younger generations, noting a Wall Street Journal story about teens saving summer-job income to invest in the stock market. Vlad Tenev is set to be interviewed by the hosts.

### AI Revenue Growth [00:33:00]

Citing Derek Thompson and Tim Lee, the hosts note that generative AI revenue grew ninefold in two years from $7 billion to over $60 billion, excluding circular chip/data center spending. If revenue grows at 100% per year, AI could reach $650 billion annualized by 2029. Data center construction is driving a labor boom, with electricians and project managers earning 25-30% more than before the AI buildout.

### Streaming Entertainment and Legacy Media Decline [00:52:22]

Netflix's net income since 2019 vastly exceeds the combined direct-to-consumer losses of Disney, NBCUniversal, Warner Brothers Discovery, and Paramount. Legacy broadcast and cable TV fell from 57.5% to 42% of US viewing time between January 2023 and late 2025. YouTube Premium has 125 million paid subscribers generating an estimated $21 billion annually. Rumors of Netflix potentially acquiring Warner Brothers are discussed alongside concerns about theater viability.
