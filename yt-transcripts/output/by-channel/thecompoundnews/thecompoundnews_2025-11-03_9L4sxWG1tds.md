# Brian Belski Says "The Bull Market Continues"

**Channel:** The Compound
**Published:** 2025-11-03
**URL:** https://youtube.com/watch?v=9L4sxWG1tds
**Duration:** 37:07

## Summary

Brian Belski announces the launch of Humilis Investment Strategies, his independent portfolio advisory firm focused on large cap, SMID, value, and dividend growth equity strategies with concentrated portfolios of 50 and 65-75 stocks respectively. He makes the case that the current bull market is not in a bubble because profit margins justify elevated valuations, and that broadening market participation beyond AI/Mag 7 will benefit the strategies he emphasizes. Belski and the hosts debate whether AI stocks resemble the 1999-2000 dot-com bubble, concluding the structural differences in earnings, margins, and operating cash flows make a direct comparison inaccurate, though circular AI revenue relationships warrant caution. The discussion closes with Belski defending his year-end S&P 7,000 target set a year prior and his philosophy of conviction-based investing with humility.

## Key Takeaways

- Belski launched Humilis Investment Strategies with a focus on portfolio advisory—large cap (50 stocks), SMID (65-75 stocks), value, and dividend growth—not institutional research.
- His core thesis is that a 25-year secular bull market began in 2009 and has roughly 10 years remaining, with bear markets and recessions possible but not ending the cycle.
- The AI segment is not a bubble by Belski's or Brown's definition because today's companies have real earnings, margins at all-time highs, and genuine operating moats—unlike Lucent and Worldcom in 2000.
- Earnings revision trends for non-Mag-7 S&P companies have been improving faster than large cap tech, supporting the broadening thesis and benefiting value, SMID, and dividend growth strategies.
- Belski argues regional banks face existential competitive pressure from both mega-banks and community banks, predicting a wave of mergers among mid-tier institutions.
- Market timing around S&P price targets is an academic exercise; Belski says the practical message is to own what you own with conviction and not change positioning during volatility.
- The dispersion among Mag 7 stocks quarter to quarter suggests active bets away from them may produce better risk-adjusted outcomes than trying to pick within that group.

## Detailed Notes

### Launch of Humilis Investment Strategies [00:01:53]

Belski reveals his new firm, Humilis Investment Strategies (HIS), whose name derives from the Latin for 'humble.' The tagline is 'investing with conviction and humility.' After decades at Merrill Lynch, Oppenheimer, and BMO, Belski decided to launch his own firm to focus exclusively on separately managed equity portfolios rather than multi-role institutional strategy work. The firm will offer large cap US (50 stocks), SMID (65-75 stocks), value, and dividend growth strategies.

### Portfolio Construction and Stock Market as a Market of Stocks [00:06:25]

Belski argues the stock market is a 'market of stocks' and that too many strategists focus excessively on index-level calls. His US large cap portfolio holds 50 stocks with positions ranging from 1% to 6%; the SMID portfolio holds 65-75 names to accommodate the higher risk spectrum and capture individual stories. He cites long-term holdings like Oracle (since 2014) and Broadcom as examples of finding value outside the Mag 7 spotlight.

### AI Bubble Debate [00:15:36]

The hosts and Belski debate whether AI stocks resemble the 1999-2000 dot-com bubble. Belski distinguishes the current environment—real earnings, record profit margins, genuine moats—from the era of Lucent and Worldcom which had no sustainable fundamentals. Brown introduces the Duality Research argument that when profit margin-adjusted forward PE is calculated, it comes to approximately 17.75x, less than one standard deviation above the 20-year average of 16.2x, suggesting limited bubble characteristics at the index level. Both Brown and Belski acknowledge concern about the circular nature of AI revenue relationships—where companies give each other revenue—as a legitimate risk distinct from pure valuation.

### Market Broadening and Sector Outlook [00:09:05]

Belski contends that earnings revisions for non-Mag-7 S&P companies have been improving faster than large cap tech, driving new index highs more broadly. He favors financials across mega-banks and community banks, believing regional banks will face consolidation pressure. The equal-weight S&P, NASDAQ equal weight, and Russell equal weight are all within 3% of all-time highs despite headline index concentration in AI names, supporting the broadening narrative.

### S&P 7,000 Target and Investment Philosophy [00:30:36]

Belski's bull case for the S&P 500 in 2025 was 7,000, published in early November 2024 shortly after the election; the index stood near 6,856 at interview time. He frames price targets as an academic exercise and says the practical lesson is to maintain positions through volatility rather than capitulate—he made no portfolio changes during the April 2025 drawdown. He believes the 25-year secular bull that started in 2009 has approximately 10 more years to run.
