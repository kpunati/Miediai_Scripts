# Three Signs the Bottom is In | WAYT?

**Channel:** The Compound
**Published:** 2026-04-07
**URL:** https://youtube.com/watch?v=2wZXKyTGqgA
**Duration:** 66:33

## Summary

In this episode of What Are Your Thoughts, Josh Brown and Michael Batnick debate whether markets have bottomed amid escalating US-Iran tensions over the Strait of Hormuz, with Brown citing three technical signals: retail trader panic in options, record cash inflows, and a high percentage of S&P stocks recovering from local lows. The hosts then cover a range of market topics including dividend literacy gaps among retail investors, BlackRock's planned entry into the Nasdaq 100 ETF space with a lower-cost competitor to QQQ, and the historically wide return dispersion between value and growth stocks. They also review JPMorgan's annual shareholder letter highlighting Dimon's track record, OpenAI's record-breaking $122 billion fundraise and strategic acquisition of the TBPN podcast, and conclude with bullish technical setups for biotech ETFs and Netflix.

## Key Takeaways

- Three signs suggesting a market bottom: extreme put buying by small traders, record flows into money market funds, and a high proportion of S&P stocks recovering 5%+ from their lows.
- Surveys suggest only 25% of individual investors correctly understand that a stock price adjusts downward by the dividend amount on the ex-dividend date.
- BlackRock's planned Nasdaq 100 ETF at approximately 12 basis points could compete with QQQ's 20 basis points, with the greatest asset risk coming from institutional non-taxable long-term holders rather than active traders.
- Value has outperformed growth in Q1 2026 by the widest margin since 2001, while the Mag 7 decline has been largely offset by gains in energy, industrials, and semiconductor stocks.
- JPMorgan's 20% ROTCE in 2025, long-term outperformance versus financial peers, and $41 trillion in assets under custody underscore Dimon's record as an exceptional capital allocator.
- OpenAI raised $122 billion at an $852 billion valuation and acquired TBPN, which the hosts interpret partly as a strategic PR and media platform for Sam Altman.
- XBI and IBB biotech ETFs show strong technical setups; Netflix is identified as having favorable risk-reward with a stop around 90 and upside toward 120.

## Detailed Notes

### Market Bottom Debate and Iran Conflict [00:00:18]

Brown and Batnick discuss whether April's market low was the definitive bottom, noting that the S&P was only down 67 basis points despite extreme rhetoric from President Trump threatening to destroy Iran's civilization by an 8 p.m. deadline. Brown frames the Strait of Hormuz as the key geopolitical hostage, and both hosts assign roughly 60% odds the bottom is in absent a major escalation such as a full-scale invasion. Citrini Research attracted attention by physically sending an analyst through the Strait to verify satellite data showing Iran taking tolls rather than fully closing it.

### Three Technical Signs of a Bottom [00:00:18]

Brown cites Jason Getman of SentimentTrader noting that small options traders allocated one-third of volume to opening puts for only the third time ever, matching COVID-washout panic levels. He also highlights record flows into money markets and cash-like ETFs over a 3-month rolling period, and a chart showing 53% of S&P tech stocks are already up 5% from their local lows. Batnick raises the alternative hypothesis that the early-April bounce may simply reflect end-of-quarter rebalancing rather than a true capitulation bottom.

### Dividend Literacy and the Meb Faber Survey [00:00:18]

The hosts highlight a Meb Faber survey showing that only 25% of individual investors correctly understand that a stock price declines by the dividend amount on the ex-date, with even 40% of professionals getting it wrong in broader surveys. Brown argues most retail investors mentally equate dividends to bank interest, not recognizing the price adjustment mechanism. This segment is framed as an argument for why investors misattribute dividend income as 'free money.'

### BlackRock vs. QQQ: ETF Fee Wars [00:00:18]

BlackRock is preparing a competing Nasdaq 100 ETF (ticker IQQ) expected at approximately 12 basis points versus QQQ's 20 basis points. The hosts note QQQ's unique history: the 1999 UIT structure meant Invesco pocketed almost nothing for 26 years while the Nasdaq collected $500 million annually and BNY Mellon earned $109 million as trustee. They compare the dynamic to iShares IEMG displacing EEM ($136 billion vs. $25 billion) and argue that institutional non-taxable accounts are most susceptible to switching, while active traders will remain with QQQ for liquidity and the derivative ecosystem built on it.

### Return Dispersion: Value vs. Growth and the Mag 7 [00:00:18]

Ned Davis Research data shows the benefit of buying winners and the drag from buying losers is more than 5 standard deviations above its long-term average, seen previously only after the dot-com crash, GFC, and COVID reopening. The Mag 7 contributed roughly $2 trillion in losses year-to-date, but this capital rotated into Exxon, Chevron, Micron (now a $430 billion company), Walmart, Caterpillar, and other asset-heavy industrials. Value outperformed growth in Q1 2026 by the widest margin since 2001, while stocks popular with retail (Tesla, Coinbase, Robinhood, Nike, Block) saw 50-80% drawdowns.

### Jamie Dimon and JPMorgan Annual Letter [00:00:18]

Brown reviews highlights from Dimon's 48-page annual letter, noting JPMorgan's 20% ROTCE in 2025 versus 14-15% for Wells Fargo and Bank of America, 23% vs 15% 5-year stock outperformance versus the financials index, and $41 trillion in assets under custody. Brown credits JPMorgan's fortress balance sheet and opportunistic acquisitions during crises (Bear Stearns, Washington Mutual, First Republic) as central to its compounding. He compares the annual letter's investor importance to Larry Fink's BlackRock letters for financial industry participants.

### OpenAI Fundraise and TBPN Acquisition [00:00:18]

OpenAI raised $122 billion at an $852 billion valuation — the largest capital raise in history, dwarfing Saudi Aramco's $25 billion IPO — with Amazon ($50 billion contingent on IPO or milestone), SoftBank ($30 billion), and Nvidia ($30 billion) as lead investors. OpenAI also acquired the TBPN daily tech podcast for approximately $100 million; Ben Thompson of Stratechery criticized the deal sharply. Brown argues the acquisition is a strategic PR platform for Altman, comparing it to Musk's approach with X. Separately, Anthropic reported its annualized revenue run rate surpassed $30 billion, up from $9 billion at end-2025, with over 1,000 enterprise customers spending more than $1 million annually.

### Biotech and Netflix Technical Setups [00:00:18]

Batnick makes a bullish case for biotech, showing XBI (equal-weighted) and IBB (cap-weighted) with XBI outperforming on a relative basis; 130 is identified as the key upside trigger for XBI. Brown and Batnick identify Netflix as the mystery chart, with the stock holding its gap around 90, a price target near 120, and strong fundamentals from recent price increases and expanding live sports content. Both agree Netflix offers favorable risk-reward: risk approximately 9 points to make 20.
