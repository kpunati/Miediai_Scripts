# Everybody in the Pool | Animal Spirits 387

**Channel:** The Compound
**Published:** 2024-11-20
**URL:** https://youtube.com/watch?v=azDDiKvk-iU
**Duration:** 71:55

## Summary

Episode 387 of Animal Spirits finds Michael Batnick and Ben Carlson in a reflective mood after seven years of podcasting, observing that 'animal spirits' have become the phrase of the week across Wall Street following the 2024 election. The hosts weigh genuine bullish fundamentals — strong earnings growth, record ETF inflows, and improving corporate profitability — against the risks of euphoric positioning, stretched large-cap valuations, and elevated earnings expectations for 2025. Bitcoin receives extended discussion as it approaches $100,000, with both hosts acknowledging its maturation as an institutional asset while cautioning late-arriving retail investors. Lighter segments cover declining birth rates and the grandparent experience, the Paul vs. Tyson Netflix event, and movie recommendations.

## Key Takeaways

- Ed Yardeni's S&P 10,000 target by 2030 works out to roughly 10% annually from current levels, which the hosts view as plausible rather than extreme given recent history.
- US equity ETF flows have already broken the 2021 annual record at $913 billion, raising questions about the source of capital given still-elevated money market balances.
- Forward P/E ratios on the S&P 500 are little changed since Biden's 2020 election because earnings growth has kept pace with price appreciation, though 17% EPS growth is now priced in for 2025.
- Bitcoin ETFs crossing $90 billion in assets in under a year marks a structural shift; hosts argue the 80%+ drawdown cycle is likely over while still cautioning against FOMO at current prices.
- ARKK's underperformance — missing the AI trade in Nvidia — is contrasted with the 2x Nvidia ETF (NVDL) now having more AUM than ARK Innovation.
- The DOGE agenda faces structural limits: discretionary non-entitlement spending has not grown and is already lean; meaningful deficit reduction requires touching Social Security, Medicare, or defense.
- Personal finance philosophy discussion: hosts push back on pure wealth-accumulation framing, arguing money is a tool for spending and enjoyment, not just investment maximization.

## Detailed Notes

### Podcast Anniversary and Audience Context [00:00:04]

Animal Spirits marks seven years since its November 2017 launch. The hosts reflect on a live Chicago show with FM Investments and note the challenge of knowing their audience without data from podcast platform gatekeepers. A listener survey is announced.

### Equity Market Sentiment and Valuations [00:04:18]

Ed Yardeni's S&P 10,000 by 2030 call is framed as the catalyst for 'animal spirits' becoming the financial buzzword of the week. The hosts note that the S&P forward P/E has barely moved (21.4 to 21.6) over the past four years even as prices rose 90%, because earnings kept pace. However, the market now prices in 17% EPS growth for 2025. Small and mid-caps are described as still cheap on a relative basis.

### ETF Flows and Market Euphoria [00:09:31]

A nearly $56 billion single-week ETF inflow, the second largest on record, signals broad market participation. Year-to-date ETF flows of $913 billion have already broken the 2021 record. The hosts debate whether euphoric participation should be treated as a warning sign or simply a reflection of favorable fundamentals and policy expectations.

### ARKK, Nvidia, and Market Concentration [00:21:05]

ARKK's failure to own Nvidia is highlighted as the defining irony of innovation investing this cycle. The 2x Nvidia ETF (NVDL) has surpassed ARK in AUM. Nvidia, Apple, and Microsoft together represent nearly 20% of the S&P 500, with Nvidia's market cap approaching the entire Japanese equity market in size.

### Government Spending and DOGE [00:25:23]

A Bank of America chart shows median checking and savings balances remain above inflation-adjusted 2019 levels across all income cohorts. The DOGE initiative is discussed skeptically: the bulk of the federal deficit comes from entitlements and interest expense, which are politically untouchable, while non-discretionary spending is already flat and lean.

### Bitcoin Maturation and ETF Milestones [00:34:46]

Bitcoin ETFs crossed $90 billion in assets and are 72% of the way toward surpassing gold ETFs — a feat gold ETFs achieved over two decades since 2003. ibit reached $40 billion in just 21 days, a record pace. The hosts debate whether 80%+ Bitcoin drawdowns are a thing of the past given institutional adoption, with both leaning toward that structural shift while cautioning late retail entrants. Options on ibit are noted as beginning to trade.

### Social and Demographic Topics [00:44:15]

Robinhood's acquisition of TradePMR, a major RIA custodian, is briefly noted. Real estate affordability is examined by state, with Midwest and Southern states showing median single-family home prices well below the national $385,000 median. A Pew survey finding that two-thirds of Americans say they have achieved or are on track to achieve the American dream is discussed. The declining birth rate and its emotional impact on would-be grandparents is explored through a New York Times article.

### Streaming, Pop Culture, and Recommendations [00:57:45]

The Paul vs. Tyson fight on Netflix drew approximately 60 million viewers — dwarfing prior pay-per-view boxing records — but was marred by widespread buffering. Disney streaming's turn to profitability is noted positively. Movie recommendations include a rewatch of Meet the Parents and the horror films Host (2020) and Kill List.
