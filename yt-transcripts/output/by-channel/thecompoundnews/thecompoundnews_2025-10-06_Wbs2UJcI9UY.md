# Solana Will Win The Race To Tokenize The Stock Market

**Channel:** The Compound
**Published:** 2025-10-06
**URL:** https://youtube.com/watch?v=Wbs2UJcI9UY
**Duration:** 42:00

## Summary

Anatoly Yakovenko, co-founder of Solana, joins Josh Brown and Michael Batnick to explain why Solana was built as a high-performance execution layer and how its proof-of-history clock combined with proof-of-stake enables full bandwidth utilization across validators. Yakovenko argues that Solana's architecture is uniquely suited for tokenizing both private and public equities because products can be launched directly without building Layer 2 infrastructure. He also addresses decentralization, quantum computing risks, memecoin activity, validator revenue economics, and the regulatory path to a Solana ETF.

## Key Takeaways

- Solana's proof-of-history mechanism, inspired by cellular-network time-division multiple access, allows validator nodes to use all available bandwidth rather than leaving gaps from block-collision resolution.
- Yakovenko views Solana as analogous to Linux for trading — an open, transparent, and decentralized execution layer where no party can withhold data or change business models unilaterally.
- XStocks tokenized equities launched on Solana the same day Robinhood announced its Ethereum Layer-2 tokenization product, claiming ~90% of tokenized-equity trading volume shortly after.
- Solana validator tips exceeded $1 billion in the prior year, providing a revenue-based framework for valuing the network similar to analyzing a conventional business.
- Quantum computing poses a theoretical long-term risk to elliptic-curve cryptography used by all major blockchains, but Yakovenko estimates meaningful threats are roughly 20 years away and that the ecosystem would coordinate a fix as the threat became concrete.
- Memecoins account for 10–40% of Solana activity depending on the day; Yakovenko regards them as the blockchain equivalent of mobile 'fart apps' — a valid but low-priority use case on the path to regulated equities.
- SEC filings for a Solana ETF are underway and the current regulatory administration appears more receptive than its predecessor.

## Detailed Notes

### Solana Origin Story and Eureka Moment [00:01:24]

Yakovenko became interested in crypto in 2017 after recognizing that time-division multiple access concepts from cellular networks could solve the block-collision problem in proof-of-work blockchains. He quit Qualcomm in 2017–2018, raised money via a future-token sale, and recruited co-founders from among colleagues facing low morale amid a Broadcom acquisition attempt.

### Proof-of-History and Proof-of-Stake Explained [00:11:56]

Proof-of-work forces energy expenditure to prevent spam blocks but is slow and expensive. Proof-of-stake uses the chain itself for the sybil-resistance problem, making it cheaper but more algorithmically complex. Solana's proof-of-history adds a verifiable clock so every node can use its full bandwidth rather than leaving gaps caused by competing block producers resolving conflicts simultaneously.

### Decentralization, Governance, and Quantum Risk [00:07:07]

Yakovenko places Solana closer to Bitcoin on the decentralization spectrum than to Ripple, drawing an analogy to Linux where influence comes from making persuasive technical arguments rather than from token ownership or founder status. On quantum computing, he estimates cracking 256-bit elliptic-curve cryptography requires roughly one million error-corrected qubits and is likely ~20 years away, giving the ecosystem time to coordinate a protocol upgrade.

### NFT Era, Memecoins, and Ecosystem Growth [00:21:36]

Solana broke into mainstream awareness during the 2021 NFT boom when high Ethereum gas fees blocked small artists; an open-source NFT standard built by a Solana product engineer went viral organically. Today memecoins represent 10–40% of daily activity, which Yakovenko likens to mobile game loot boxes — a stepping stone rather than the end goal. His key KPIs are team runway and whether products on Solana have achieved revenue sustainability.

### Tokenized Equities and the SOL Bull Case [00:29:54]

Yakovenko argues Solana is better positioned than Ethereum for equity tokenization because products can be deployed directly without announcing a Layer-2 first. XStocks launched on Solana the same day as Robinhood's Ethereum-based tokenization announcement and quickly captured the majority of volume. Validator tip revenue exceeded $1 billion annually, supporting a fundamental revenue-based valuation model. A Solana ETF filing with the SEC is in progress and the current administration appears receptive.
