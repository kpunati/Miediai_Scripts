# Why Bubble Talk is Totally Wrong | TCAF 238

**Channel:** The Compound
**Published:** 2026-04-17
**URL:** https://youtube.com/watch?v=w5KlIB4JmKY
**Duration:** 90:46

## Summary

In Episode 238 of The Compound and Friends, Dr. Ankur Crawford of Alger joins Josh Brown and Michael Batnick for a 90-minute deep dive into the AI investment landscape. Crawford, a former semiconductor analyst with 22 years at Alger, argues the current AI buildout is structurally unlike past speculative bubbles because the supply chain is consolidated, the technology is proven, demand is genuinely exponential, and hyperscaler CapEx is recovering within 18 months at current chip pricing. The conversation covers specific positions including Nebius (positioned as a potential next AI-native hyperscaler), QXO (Brad Jacobs-led building-products roll-up), and AppLovin, while Crawford expresses genuine skepticism about the long-term viability of enterprise SaaS business models in an agentic AI world.

## Key Takeaways

- Crawford contends that global compute is still in deep shortage and that demand growth is exponential, not cyclical, making bubble comparisons to dot-com or solar panels structurally incorrect.
- The semiconductor supply chain has consolidated to two or three players in most segments over 20 years, making it unlike solar where the technology barrier was low and China could commoditize supply.
- Hyperscaler CapEx payback is currently around 18 months because H100 chip rental pricing has been rising, not falling, meaning capital deployed earlier is already in the money.
- Crawford is skeptical about enterprise SaaS long-term margins, arguing agentic AI will reduce seat-based expansion and allow enterprises to replicate functionality internally, compressing operating margins from ~40% toward ~20%.
- Nebius (NBIS), originally the non-Russian assets spun out after Yandex's Ukraine-war crisis, is characterized as a potential AI-native hyperscaler built by 1,500 engineers who left Russia with Arkady Volozh.
- All tokens are not created equal: Crawford expects a future tiered token pricing model where high-value tasks such as drug discovery command premium pricing versus routine queries.
- Apple is identified as the most at-risk Mag 7 company from a screenless, agentic future if it cannot deliver a compelling personal AI agent, despite currently benefiting from a 'HALO' effect as a physical device platform.

## Detailed Notes

### Alger's Investment Philosophy and AI Conviction [00:08:14]

Crawford describes Alger's two-bucket growth framework: high-unit-volume growth companies and life-cycle-change companies that are reinventing themselves at low multiples. She cites Microsoft under Satya Nadella, Western Digital, and Nvidia as life-cycle-change examples. Her conviction in AI has increased over three years, with agents arriving roughly two years ahead of her original forecast.

### Why This Is Not a Bubble [00:34:31]

Crawford argues bubble talk often signals the speaker did not understand or missed the trade. Key differentiators from dot-com: the semiconductor supply chain is deeply consolidated with two to three players per segment, technology is already working rather than theoretical, and CapEx is recovering in 18 months at current H100 pricing. The solar analogy also fails because the technological barrier for solar was low, enabling Chinese commoditization, whereas leading-edge silicon fabrication requires decades of cumulative R&D.

### Compute Shortage, Tokens, and Hyperscaler CapEx [00:20:39]

The global compute shortage is real and persisting at least through 2027 to 2028 based on announced CapEx; Crawford does not know the supply-demand picture in 2030. Tokens are framed as units of intelligence, with more complex reasoning tasks consuming exponentially more compute. Crawford expects token pricing to stratify by task value over time. She notes Uber reportedly exhausted its entire 2026 compute budget by April, illustrating unconstrained enterprise demand.

### Specific Holdings: Nebius, QXO, AppLovin, Intel [01:03:45]

Nebius (NBIS) is described as a potential AI-native hyperscaler built from the engineering talent of the former Yandex, listed on NYSE after a compressed weekend process. QXO, led by serial entrepreneur Brad Jacobs, is consolidating the fragmented building-products distribution market with a target of growing EBITDA from roughly $1 billion to $5 billion by 2029-2030. AppLovin is viewed as an AI-driven advertising platform that can monetize mobile inventory and expand into e-commerce. On Intel, Crawford praises new CEO Lip-Bu Tan but is skeptical the foundry business can execute, noting it remains an open existential question.

### SaaS Disruption and Cybersecurity [01:15:00]

Crawford explicitly disagrees with Jensen Huang's view that agents will reinforce existing SaaS moats, arguing that agentic AI will erode seat-based expansion and enable internal software development. Salesforce is cited trading at 10-14x EV/FCF depending on stock-based comp treatment, reflecting the market's skepticism. Platform-oriented security companies such as CrowdStrike are deemed more defensible than point solutions; Atlassian is called out as having zero moat. Crawford estimates SaaS terminal operating margins should compress toward 20% from current ~40% levels.

### Apple and the Screenless Future [01:22:38]

Crawford identifies Apple as at risk if it cannot deliver a compelling personal AI agent before a screenless or alternate form-factor device gains traction. She attributes Apple's delay partly to its privacy-first brand identity creating internal tension around deep AI integration. Despite reduced CapEx, she notes Apple's strategy of free-riding on hyperscaler compute while monetizing through the App Store could be brilliant short-term but leaves the company exposed if the device paradigm shifts.
