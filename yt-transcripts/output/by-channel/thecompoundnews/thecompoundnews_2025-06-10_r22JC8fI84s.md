# The Only Comp for AI Growth is Electricity | WAYT?

**Channel:** The Compound
**Published:** 2025-06-10
**URL:** https://youtube.com/watch?v=r22JC8fI84s
**Duration:** 70:31

## Summary

In this episode of What Are Your Thoughts, Josh Brown and Michael Batnick cover a wide range of market topics beginning with the high-profile Elon Musk-Trump feud and its transient but dramatic impact on Tesla's stock price. The hosts then spend significant time analyzing key insights from Mary Meeker's 340-slide AI trends report, focusing on explosive AI adoption rates, surging hyperscaler capital expenditures, and falling inference costs. Additional segments cover the Starwood Real Estate Income Trust redemption crisis, new US millionaire creation statistics, Josh's thesis on ARR businesses driving market multiple expansion, and a make-the-case for Disney alongside a mystery chart identifying McDonald's amid multiple analyst downgrades.

## Key Takeaways

- Tesla recovered its full Musk-Trump feud losses within three days, reinforcing that Elon headlines rarely produce lasting shareholder damage for long-term holders.
- Mary Meeker's data shows ChatGPT adoption vastly outpaced all prior technology platforms, with the big-six tech companies now spending 15% of revenue on capex vs. 8% a decade ago.
- AI inference costs have fallen 99.7%, creating a demand flywheel where cheaper usage drives higher overall infrastructure spend rather than reducing it.
- Starwood's SREIT has seen its NAV fall 40% from its 2022 peak and faces $850 million in queued redemptions, raising policy questions about illiquid assets in retail-accessible vehicles.
- Josh argues that the single biggest driver of elevated market multiples over the past 20 years is the shift from transactional to ARR-based business models, citing examples from Spotify to Deere.
- Meta's $14.8 billion deal for 49% of Scale AI highlights the extraordinary valuations being assigned to AI-adjacent data and infrastructure businesses.
- McDonald's received multiple analyst downgrades citing eroding pricing power, softening traffic tied to GLP-1 weight-loss drugs, and weakening defensive status.

## Detailed Notes

### Elon Musk-Trump Feud and Tesla Impact [00:02:37]

The public falling-out between Elon Musk and President Trump was triggered partly by disagreements over the Big Beautiful Bill's elimination of EV tax credits, which Tesla relies on for profitability. Tesla lost what hosts characterize as a record single-day market cap decline before recovering fully within three days. Morgan Stanley is reportedly struggling to find buyers for X/Twitter debt amid the fallout, seeking smaller checks.

### Circle IPO and Apple WWDC [00:13:01]

Circle Internet Group, creator of the USDC stablecoin, tripled on its IPO first day reaching $88 per share, making it the seventh-largest IPO since 1980 at a $22 billion market cap trading at 140x earnings. Apple's WWDC was characterized as underwhelming on AI progress, with UBS calling new features 'incremental'; Apple has underperformed the MAG7 ETF by nearly 100 percentage points since early 2023.

### Starwood SREIT Redemption Crisis [00:21:49]

Starwood's non-traded REIT has gated redemptions after being overwhelmed with withdrawal requests; its NAV has fallen 40% from a 2022 peak to $8.8 billion with $850 million in queued redemptions. Hosts debate the appropriateness of packaging illiquid commercial real estate into semi-liquid vehicles accessible to retail investors with minimum investments as low as $2,500.

### Mary Meeker AI Trends Report Deep Dive [00:28:21]

Meeker's 340-slide deck highlights ChatGPT's unprecedented adoption curve, the big-six tech companies' capex reaching $200 billion annually (15% of revenue), AWS spending 49% of its own revenue on AI/ML infrastructure capex, and inference costs falling 99.7%. Closed-source models are 17 months ahead of open-source in compute intensity and growing 5x per year vs. 3.6x for open source. Data center energy usage has tripled since pre-AI, driving hyperscaler investments in nuclear and renewables.

### Scale AI, Physical AI, and Robotics [00:39:00]

Meta's deal to acquire 49% of Scale AI for $14.8 billion underscores the value of AI data labeling infrastructure. Hosts discuss physical AI and robotics as potentially the most important investable theme, with Waymo reaching 27% of San Francisco ride-share market from near zero in 2023. Josh cautions that the money in physical AI will likely be made in the software and services layer, not the hardware manufacturers.

### ARR Business Model Thesis [00:56:20]

Josh presents his thesis that the shift from transactional to annual recurring revenue business models is a primary driver of elevated market multiples over the past two decades. He cites Spotify, Netflix, CrowdStrike, and even Deere's stated goal of converting 10% of its $51 billion in revenue to ARR by 2030 as examples of companies commanding premium valuations for predictable cash flows.

### Make the Case: Disney and Mystery Chart: McDonald's [01:03:54]

Josh makes the case for Disney, which is completing its acquisition of the remaining Hulu stake for $439 million and is approaching a technical breakout level. The mystery chart revealed McDonald's, which has received downgrades from Redburn Atlantic, Loop Capital, and Morgan Stanley citing eroding pricing power, weak same-store sales across the burger category, and growing headwinds from GLP-1 weight-loss drugs reducing fast food consumption.
