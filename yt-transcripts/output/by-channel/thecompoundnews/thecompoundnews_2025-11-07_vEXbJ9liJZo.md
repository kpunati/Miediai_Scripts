# Eric Jackson and the Search for the Next 100 Bagger | TCAF 216

**Channel:** The Compound
**Published:** 2025-11-07
**URL:** https://youtube.com/watch?v=vEXbJ9liJZo
**Duration:** 90:31

## Summary

In this 90-minute interview on The Compound and Friends (episode 216), Eric Jackson of EMJ Capital discusses his evolution from traditional hedge fund manager to high-conviction, social-media-driven investor. Jackson recounts his famous Carvana call and ongoing Opendoor thesis, explaining the analytical framework his AI-powered model uses to identify potential 100-bagger stocks. The episode also dives into OpenAI's chaotic week — including Sam Altman's erratic behavior on a podcast, the CFO's loan guarantee comments, and the company's restructuring — contextualized against the broader AI investment landscape. Jackson then rapid-fires through several speculative positions he believes have 100x potential.

## Key Takeaways

- Jackson's 100-bagger framework looks for companies trading near bankruptcy prices with insider buying, heavy short interest, and a path to profitability, similar to the setup Carvana had in late 2022.
- Opendoor's new CEO (former Shopify COO) and move into mortgage/title are central to Jackson's bull thesis for the stock reaching $82.
- OpenAI's inability to be publicly scrutinized is creating systemic risk for Mag-7 companies whose earnings estimates incorporate OpenAI partnership revenue.
- IREN and Cipher Mining are favored over asset-light hyperscaler middlemen because they own land and power contracts, providing durable competitive advantages in AI infrastructure buildout.
- Jackson views the intersection of retail investor communities on X (formerly Twitter) and low-float speculative stocks as a legitimate strategy, drawing explicit parallels to Hertz and AMC.
- SANA Biotechnology is highlighted as a biotech with potential to cure type 1 diabetes using stem cell therapy, still in early clinical stages.
- Jackson proposes a 'lottery fund' ETF concept — a publicly marketed portfolio of high-asymmetry, high-risk positions — as a product suited to today's retail speculator culture.

## Detailed Notes

### OpenAI's Turbulent Week and AI Landscape [00:09:23]

The hosts and Jackson discuss OpenAI's restructuring for IPO readiness, Sam Altman's confrontational behavior on Brad Gerstner's podcast, the CFO's comments about federal loan guarantees, and growing enterprise AI market share gains by Anthropic and Google at OpenAI's expense. Jackson argues the biggest risk is not Altman's behavior but that publicly-traded companies have baked OpenAI-dependent revenue growth into their earnings models, creating exposure if OpenAI fails to deliver.

### Carvana Call and the 100-Bagger Framework [00:36:00]

Jackson recounts pitching Carvana in June 2022 when it was around $20, which then fell to $3.50, before eventually rising above $400 — approximately a 100x from the low. He explains that his AI model re-identified Carvana as a top position at $11-15 in 2023, and describes the importance of management insider buying as a conviction signal. The key thesis element was Carvana's transition from losses to profitability, which forced short-sellers to cover and attracted new buyers.

### Opendoor Thesis and the Open Army [00:49:20]

Jackson describes his viral campaign of posting daily videos outside Drake's Toronto mansion to promote Opendoor stock, crediting his 16-year-old son with the idea. He argues Opendoor is structurally similar to Carvana — a tech-enabled marketplace that will generate its real economics through adjacent financial products (mortgage, title insurance). The new CEO's 82-million-share equity package and board engagement with Jackson signal institutional alignment with the bull thesis.

### Lightning Round: Speculative Positions [01:08:23]

Jackson reviews Better (AI-powered mortgage originator disrupted by rate hikes but rebuilding with automation), IREN and Cipher (former Bitcoin miners pivoting to own AI data center land and power contracts), SANA Biotechnology (stem cell therapy showing early human results for type 1 diabetes cure), BTQ Technologies (post-quantum cryptography cybersecurity), and DeFi Technologies (European crypto ETF issuer with ambitions in sovereign debt tokenization). Jackson says every position in his portfolio must have a plausible 100x path.

### Retail Investor Philosophy and EMJX Announcement [01:21:11]

Jackson proposes a 'lottery fund' active ETF openly marketed as a portfolio of long-shot bets, arguing retail investors are already doing this with zero-DTE options and sports gambling and deserve a more transparent vehicle. He also announces EMJX, a Gen-2 multi-asset digital asset treasury company launching in December where he will serve as chairman. The discussion ends with Jackson expressing that his goal is to direct middle-class retail interest away from pure gambling toward asymmetric public-market opportunities.
