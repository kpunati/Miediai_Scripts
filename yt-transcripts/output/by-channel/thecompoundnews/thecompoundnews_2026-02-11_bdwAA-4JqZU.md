# What Would You Do With $3 Million? | Animal Spirits 451

**Channel:** The Compound
**Published:** 2026-02-11
**URL:** https://youtube.com/watch?v=bdwAA-4JqZU
**Duration:** 78:05

## Summary

Animal Spirits episode 451 focuses on the dramatic broadening of the U.S. equity market in early 2026, with value, industrials, staples, and equal-weight indexes outperforming while the Mag 7 has gone sideways for roughly six months. The primary driver discussed is the software/momentum unwind following Anthropic's Claude Code release, which raised fears about AI disrupting enterprise SaaS moats. The hosts also cover the knock-on effects for private credit heavily exposed to SaaS borrowers, the emerging market and dollar narrative, a crypto selloff, and a lively personal finance debate on portfolio wealth versus home ownership.

## Key Takeaways

- The S&P 500 equal-weight index hit all-time highs and 66% of S&P 500 companies are outperforming year-to-date, marking a clear rotation away from the Mag 7 concentration trade.
- Anthropic's AI coding advances (Claude Code / agents) triggered a 33% selloff in IGV and raised structural questions about whether enterprise SaaS moats are permanently damaged.
- Private credit is significantly exposed to SaaS companies—one firm classifies 70% of borrowers as software companies—creating risk for BDCs and alternative asset managers like Blackstone, KKR, and Apollo.
- Hyperscaler capex is projected to consume up to 90% of operating cash flow in 2026 (up from 40% in 2017-2023), with Amazon alone guiding to $200 billion in capex.
- The dollar has weakened roughly 12% from its highs and foreign holdings of U.S. debt securities are at historic lows (13% of portfolios vs. 38% in 2007), supporting the bull case for emerging markets.
- Bitcoin and silver both experienced historically severe selloffs, with Bitcoin and IGV moving in lock step—reflecting broad momentum/risk-off liquidation rather than crypto-specific news.
- Companies that mention AI-driven ROI in earnings calls have outperformed the market by roughly 5 percentage points annually, with logistics firm CH Robinson cited as a tangible example.

## Detailed Notes

### Market Rotation: Broad Rally Beneath the Surface [00:03:34]

While the S&P 500 was roughly flat for the week, 337 individual stocks outperformed the index and the average stock was up nearly 2%. Equal-weight and small-cap indexes hit all-time highs. Value over growth posted its third largest single-day outperformance ever, with value in the 99th percentile over six days. Sector funds excluding tech attracted a record $62 billion in inflows in the first five weeks of 2026, surpassing all of 2025.

### Software/Momentum Unwind and AI Disruption [00:16:10]

Anthropic's Claude Code release catalyzed investor fears that AI would erode enterprise SaaS moats, sending IGV down 33% over roughly four months and Microsoft down ~25%. The hosts discuss competing views: Ben Thompson argues software companies will be winners (AI helps them ship faster), while acknowledging that spending growth is now in serious question as customers seek to cut SaaS costs to fund their own AI token budgets. Adobe is highlighted as a stock that failed to recover despite deep losses, reinforcing the 'falling knife' caution.

### Private Credit Exposure to SaaS [00:27:06]

Private credit vehicles are heavily concentrated in SaaS loans because of their recurring, predictable revenues. One firm classifies ~70% of borrowers as software companies representing ~22% of a $1 trillion+ debt exposure. BDCs like ARCC were 'absolutely destroyed' in the selloff. Blue Owl fell 50%, while Blackstone, KKR, and Apollo dropped ~30%, which the hosts view as an overreaction given those firms' diversification.

### Emerging Markets and the Dollar [00:37:05]

Foreign government and central bank holdings of U.S. debt securities have fallen from 38% of portfolios in 2007 to just 13% today. The dollar has weakened ~12% from its highs. The hosts note a ratio chart of EFA vs. VTI (international developed vs. U.S.) that appears to be forming a real bottom, with record flows into non-tech sectors providing further support for the thesis.

### Crypto Selloff and Trading Commentary [00:46:47]

Bitcoin dropped to ~$60,000 before recovering to the $68,000 range; the host bought IBIT during the selloff. The two worst single-day declines for silver in history both occurred within the prior 10 days. Bitcoin and IGV were moving in near-perfect lockstep, reflecting macro momentum liquidation. Both hosts noted they had reduced crypto exposure to approximately 4-5% of liquid net worth following Bitcoin's earlier run to $120,000.

### Personal Finance Debate: $3M Portfolio vs. $3M Home [00:58:31]

Prompted by a tweet from Nick Mullie, the hosts debate whether a $3 million portfolio or a $3 million home represents greater personal utility. Ben argues he would take the home at his current life stage due to the utility value of a nice house with family, while Michael acknowledges the financial logic but understands the preference. The segment highlights the emotional versus financial dimensions of wealth accumulation.

### Hyperscaler Capex and AI Build-out [00:31:13]

The five largest hyperscalers (Amazon, Google, Microsoft, Meta, Oracle) are collectively adding $314 billion in incremental capex in 2026—equivalent to over 1 percentage point of U.S. GDP growth and more than 2% of total U.S. GDP. Amazon alone is guiding to $200 billion. The hosts are skeptical that investors will permit capex to reach 90% of operating cash flow for long, but acknowledge the productivity output from this spending could be transformative.
