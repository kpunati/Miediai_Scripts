# A Sit-Down With the CEO of Charles Schwab | TCAF 233

**Channel:** The Compound
**Published:** 2026-03-13
**URL:** https://youtube.com/watch?v=mPLqLsyo19k
**Duration:** 45:09

## Summary

In this live podcast recorded at the Future Proof conference, Charles Schwab CEO Rick Wurster joins hosts Josh Brown and Michael Batnick to discuss Schwab's dominance in the RIA custody market, its approach to competing or cooperating with independent advisors, and its strategic priorities around Gen Z investors, AI, and private markets. Wurster highlights record asset inflows, growing advisory assets, and Schwab's commitment to the fiduciary RIA model. He addresses competition from Robinhood and Goldman Sachs, changes to the advisor referral program, and AI's transformative role in wealth management.

## Key Takeaways

- Schwab reported $519 billion in core net new assets in the prior year, a record, with advisory assets growing 42% versus 33% for retail.
- Wurster distinguishes Schwab from Robinhood by positioning Schwab as an 'outcomes business' focused on long-term investor success rather than transaction volume.
- The Schwab advisor referral program saw growth from roughly $14 billion to $38 billion in two years, prompting a decision to raise asset minimums to manage program quality.
- One-third of new Schwab client onboards were Gen Z, and the average client age has declined by 10 years, challenging the narrative that young investors only use app-based platforms.
- Wurster argues AI will expand advisor capacity to serve younger and less wealthy clients profitably by automating planning, tax, trust, and rebalancing tasks.
- Schwab acquired a stake in wealth.com and has over 220 internal AI use cases, viewing third-party AI innovation as a benefit rather than a threat to its business model.
- Private markets access via Forge is being rolled out to advisors first before retail, with Wurster noting Forge is three to four times larger than Equity Zen and stronger in direct shares.

## Detailed Notes

### Schwab Scale and Advisory Growth [00:04:20]

Wurster outlines Schwab's scale: $519 billion in core net new assets, 46.5 million new client accounts opened (6% YoY growth), and 22% revenue growth in the prior year. Advisory assets stood at $5.2 trillion representing 44% of the platform, growing 42% versus 33% for retail. Wurster credits advisor success and the fiduciary model for outpacing retail growth.

### RIA Custody Positioning and Competition [00:08:38]

Schwab tracks a transfer-of-asset ratio and claims to win over two times more assets from competitors than it loses. Wurster says Schwab has supported the RIA community for 35 years and offers the most comprehensive platform including APIs, lending, research, and alternatives. He addresses concerns about Schwab competing with advisors by saying it has clear rules of the road, preferences the RIA in client overlap situations, and invites advisors to contact him directly with complaints.

### Referral Program Changes and Mega RIAs [00:15:58]

Schwab raised the minimum asset size for its referral program to $500 million for participating firms and increased the client asset minimum to $2 million, framing this as recalibrating to original program intent as asset growth made old thresholds outdated. The program had grown from $14 billion to $38 billion in referrals over two years. Fewer than half of 1% of the 16,200 supported firms participate, and only six firms gather more than $1 billion per year.

### Gen Z Strategy and Investing vs. Gambling [00:22:53]

One-third of new Schwab onboards were Gen Z and the average client age fell by 10 years to mid-40s. Schwab is the most-followed financial services firm on YouTube. Wurster differentiates Schwab's approach by emphasizing education and thinkorswim's trading platform versus platforms he characterizes as pushing prediction markets and sports betting, which account for over 90% of prediction market volume.

### Private Markets, Forge, and Alternatives [00:30:23]

Schwab will roll out Forge's pre-IPO share capabilities to advisors before retail investors. Forge is three to four times larger than Equity Zen and offers direct shares rather than primarily SPVs. Schwab launched an alternatives platform 18 months prior and notes the sales cycle for introducing clients to alternatives is long, calling for pragmatism about the adoption timeline.

### AI Strategy and Stock Market Reaction [00:35:29]

Schwab has over 220 internal AI use cases, including call recording analysis for advisor coaching and a stake in wealth.com for AI-driven trust and estate services. Wurster says Schwab welcomes AI innovation from external providers and considers itself a beneficiary rather than a victim, calling the sharp stock price decline following AI competitor announcements 'the craziest thing I've ever seen.' He envisions an agentic advisor experience on Schwab Advisor Center within five years.

### Future of Human Advisors and AI Wealth Management [00:40:18]

Wurster advises RIAs to embrace AI to automate manual tasks across planning, tax, trust, and rebalancing, while redirecting people toward relationship management and emotional guidance. He acknowledges AI wealth advisors will emerge but notes robo-advising, despite Schwab running the largest robo at $100 billion AUM, has not displaced RIA growth. He suggests AI will enable advisors to onboard younger, lower-wealth clients earlier and convert them as their financial lives become more complex.
