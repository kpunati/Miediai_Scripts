# The Biggest Risk in 2026 | Animal Spirits 444

**Channel:** The Compound
**Published:** 2025-12-24
**URL:** https://youtube.com/watch?v=He2ndnqDsaI
**Duration:** 65:39

## Summary

In this year-end episode of Animal Spirits (ep. 444), Michael Batnick and Ben Carlson review 2025 markets and argue that despite feeling tumultuous, the year's 17% return and 19% drawdown are historically unremarkable. They examine the relative cheapness of small and mid-cap stocks versus the S&P 500, record household cash balances, and why US GDP growth of 4.3% (Q3) continues to surprise consensus pessimists. The episode also covers structural risks emerging in evergreen private real estate funds, the rise of financial nihilism and online gambling among Gen Z, and cryptocurrency positioning as hosts head into 2026.

## Key Takeaways

- Despite a jarring 19% intraday drawdown, the 2025 stock market year was well within the range of historical norms when viewed on an annual return basis.
- Small and mid-cap stocks trade at roughly a 30% P/E discount to the S&P 500, echoing the setup that preceded international stock outperformance, with lower rates, AI benefits, and inflows cited as potential catalysts.
- US household cash as a percentage of total financial assets sits near the 98th percentile historically, and hosts believe this represents a permanent structural shift rather than dry powder waiting to be deployed.
- Evergreen private fund structures face mounting illiquidity risk: the Blue Rock real estate fund conversion to a listed vehicle resulted in a 40% NAV discount, and hosts expect more friction in the wealth channel as redemption limits become visible.
- AI is framed as a recurring macro offset that has successively neutralised inflation fears, recession predictions, and tariff shocks, and hosts expect this pattern to continue in 2026 against a softening labour market.
- Financial nihilism and online gambling are described as interlinked phenomena driven primarily by technological accessibility rather than genuine economic despair, with Pennsylvania alone collecting $1.05 billion in digital casino taxes.
- Both hosts are trimming cryptocurrency exposure heading into 2026 despite holding long-term positions, citing unclear near-term catalysts even as structural tailwinds (deregulation, institutional adoption) remain intact.

## Detailed Notes

### 2025 Stock Market Review and Drawdown Context [00:00:04]

Batnick and Carlson open by noting the 2025 US equity market posted a 17% annual return alongside a 19% maximum drawdown, arguing this is indistinguishable from a typical year historically. They use the Exhibit A chart (updated daily at Exhibit A for advisors) to illustrate that most years in the stock market feel crazy in real time but look normal in hindsight. A thought experiment shows a 30% crash from current levels would merely reset prices to January 2024, a level investors did not find particularly painful at the time.

### Small and Mid-Cap Opportunity and ETF Flows [00:00:04]

Bank of America data shows midcaps trade at a forward P/E near 15 vs. the S&P 500 at 22, close to the widest discount since the mid-1990s. Hosts draw an analogy to international stocks, which rallied sharply once their valuation gap became extreme, and identify three potential catalysts for small caps: lower interest rates, AI adoption benefits, and renewed inflows. Todd Sohn of Strategas notes equity ETF flows remain at record pace with roughly $900 billion entering equity ETFs in 2025, yet small-cap-specific flows lag even as the Russell 2000 is up approximately 46% from its April lows.

### Macroeconomic Resilience and Financial Nihilism [00:00:04]

US GDP grew at 4.3% annualised in Q3, yet hosts observe this data point is broadly disbelieved by consumers, illustrating what they call the most hated economic expansion on record. They attribute the disconnect to post-pandemic inflation psychology and populist media incentives. The segment transitions to financial nihilism among Gen Z, citing Kyla Scanlon's Wall Street Journal piece: gambling accessibility rather than genuine hopelessness is the primary driver, evidenced by Pennsylvania collecting over $1 billion in digital casino taxes versus $188 million from sports betting.

### AI as a Macro Tailwind and Prediction Markets [00:00:04]

Hosts argue AI has successively neutralised consecutive macro threats since 2022: inflation, recession fears, housing weakness, and tariff disruption. The framing for 2026 is AI versus a softening labour market. A separate discussion of prediction markets (Polymarket) notes that while sports betting dominates volume, the hosts see value in arbitraging perceived mispricings on near-certain outcomes. Benedict Evans data is cited showing only 5% of users pay for AI chatbots, suggesting advertising revenue is an inevitable next step for OpenAI.

### Private Markets Illiquidity Risk [00:00:04]

The Blue Rock private real estate fund case study illustrates structural risks in evergreen funds: after multiple quarters of redemption queues capped at 5% of NAV per quarter, shareholders voted to convert to a listed closed-end fund, which immediately traded at a 40% NAV discount. Hosts note BDCs (business development companies) from Blue Owl and KKR are showing similar NAV discounts. While Blackstone reports record flows, both hosts expect the wealth advisory channel to push back on illiquid structures, slowing private market adoption in 401ks and retail accounts significantly.
