# The Biggest Short Squeeze of All Time | Animal Spirits 467

**Channel:** The Compound
**Published:** 2026-06-03
**URL:** https://youtube.com/watch?v=7ZUqx61qbi4
**Duration:** 66:55

## Summary

Animal Spirits episode 467 (June 3, 2026) finds hosts Michael Batnick and Ben Carlson surveying an exuberant mid-2026 equity market driven by semiconductor and AI stocks, drawing explicit comparisons to the 1999 tech bubble while arguing fundamentals justify much of the run. Major discussion topics include the SpaceX IPO mechanics and index inclusion controversy, Alphabet's unprecedented equity raise, the global spread of the AI trade, and cautionary data on personal savings rates. The episode also covers Roundhill's record-breaking DRAM ETF, South Korea's speculative surge, distress among real estate agents, and a cultural segment on the renaissance of independent cinema.

## Key Takeaways

- Chip stocks delivered a historically rare two-month S&P 500 surge of 16% in April-May 2026, with historical analogs suggesting further upside over the following six months.
- The SpaceX IPO raises structural questions about index inclusion timelines and float manipulation that could force passive funds to chase the stock at inflated prices.
- Alphabet's $80 billion equity offering — unprecedented for a cash-generative company of its size — signals that AI capex demands are outpacing even massive operating cash flows.
- The AI trade has become global, with Chinese AI stocks and emerging-market indices closely tracking the NASDAQ 100 since 2024.
- A bearish counterpoint from TS Lombard's Dario Perkins estimates that 80% of AI revenue is currently circular (hyperscaler capex flowing among a small set of companies) rather than penetrating the broader economy.
- The US personal savings rate dropped to 2.6% in April 2026, consistent with historical patterns where booming asset prices reduce perceived need to save.
- Real estate agents face severe income pressure — those with under two years of experience completed only three transactions in 2024, earning roughly $8,100 in gross income.

## Detailed Notes

### Market Euphoria and Semiconductor Surge [00:06:13]

Hosts describe a market where something 'goes nuclear' every week — Dell, Intel, SanDisk, Western Digital — with some stocks posting four-digit annual returns. Bespoke's comparison of the NASDAQ's trajectory 875 trading days after Netscape's release versus 875 days after ChatGPT's launch shows nearly identical patterns, with the implication that the market may only be in the equivalent of 1998. The S&P 500 historically made another all-time high within one year of an all-time high 99% of the time.

### Micron and the Trillion-Dollar Club [00:16:43]

Micron became the 11th S&P 500 company to reach a $1 trillion market cap and set the record for the fastest move from $500 billion to $1 trillion. Bloomberg's forward earnings chart for Micron shows a projected 10-fold increase in EPS — described as looking 'like a totally different chart.' Roundhill's DRAM ETF, launched in early April, was up 127% and reached $14 billion AUM in roughly five weeks, surpassing $10 billion faster than any ETF in history.

### SpaceX IPO and Index Inclusion Debate [00:28:09]

Hosts examine Matt Levine's 'maximally cynical' scenario in which SpaceX sells a minimal float at a $2 trillion valuation, forcing index funds to chase shares at any price — effectively engineering a short squeeze on passive investors. Cali Cox's analysis suggests SpaceX's float-adjusted weight would land it around the 195th position in the S&P 500, comparable to Amgen or Shopify, not a top-10 weight. The broader IPO pipeline (OpenAI, Anthropic, SpaceX) is projected to bring $250 billion of new supply to market in 2026.

### Alphabet's Equity Raise and AI Capex Demands [00:34:34]

Alphabet announced a roughly $80 billion equity offering — its first since 2005 — despite generating $174 billion of operating cash flow and issuing $85 billion of debt in the prior 12 months. Hosts view this as evidence of extreme AI infrastructure demand, with Alphabet guiding 2026 capex of $180-190 billion and expecting 2027 capex to rise further. Berkshire Hathaway participated via a $10 billion private placement at a ~4% discount.

### AI Bear Case and Global Spread [00:21:36]

Dario Perkins of TS Lombard argues roughly 80% of AI revenue is circular — hyperscaler capex flowing among a small group of companies rather than diffusing into the broader economy. Hosts note the AI trade has gone global: Chinese AI stocks and the emerging markets ETF have tracked the NASDAQ 100 closely since 2024. Hosts also discuss Jevons' paradox as a potential counterargument — cheaper AI tools may create more demand and jobs rather than eliminating them.

### Investor Behavior, South Korea, and Money Markets [00:38:37]

South Korea's EWY ETF has outperformed the S&P 500 over the past decade with nearly all gains coming in the past year. A 2x SKH Hynix ETF in Hong Kong grew AUM 10-fold in 2026 and now represents ~8.5% of that market, the US equivalent of a $1.3 trillion single ETF. US money market funds hold $8.28 trillion and are not rotating into equities despite expectations. Cumulative flows into tech since the March 2026 low total ~$30 billion, while flows ex-tech are negative $4 billion.

### Real Estate, Crypto, and Entertainment [00:48:49]

Strategy (MicroStrategy) sold 32 Bitcoin for ~$2.5 million to fund preferred stock obligations, marking its first Bitcoin sale since the 2022 crypto winter. In real estate, the typical agent with under two years of experience completed only three transactions in 2024 for ~$8,100 gross income; 71% of agents in a 2025 NAR survey said real estate was their only profession, a record low. On entertainment, independent horror films Backrooms and Obsession are outperforming the Star Wars franchise at the box office, with both directed by young filmmakers who built audiences on YouTube.
