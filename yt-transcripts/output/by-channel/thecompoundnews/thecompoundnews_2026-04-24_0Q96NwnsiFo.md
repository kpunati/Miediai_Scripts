# Why Nvidia is Going to 10 Trillion Dollars | TCAF 239

**Channel:** The Compound
**Published:** 2026-04-24
**URL:** https://youtube.com/watch?v=0Q96NwnsiFo
**Duration:** 88:33

## Summary

Episode 239 of The Compound and Friends brings together Adam Parker of Trivariate Research and Rob Sechan of NewEdge Wealth to examine the persistent weakness in enterprise software stocks despite solid revenue metrics, contrasting it with the historic semiconductor rally that saw the SMH post its best 16-day run ever. The conversation broadens into spinoff strategy, summer stock picks across semis, power infrastructure, and healthcare, and closes with a bold bull case for Nvidia reaching a $10 trillion market cap by 2030. Rob Sechan also shares the story behind NewEdge Wealth's growth from $8 billion to $105 billion in AUM and makes the case for Ethereum treasury companies as a bridge between DeFi and traditional finance.

## Key Takeaways

- Software stocks face a structural headcount-growth ceiling as enterprises deploy AI; analysts still model flat margins, making consensus earnings estimates too optimistic for many names.
- Expensive software names with lower technological-obsolescence risk (e.g., security, EDA) are preferred over cheap, slow-growing ones in any eventual rebound.
- The semiconductor rally is historically extreme but argued as justified given Nvidia's free-cash-flow estimates having moved from roughly $1.5B to $80B+ over two years.
- Spinoff co's outperform their industry peers by ~10% on average over two years, and a wave of mid-cap spinoffs is anticipated following a relatively quiet 2026 so far.
- Rob Sechan's summer stock picks balance riding AI infrastructure momentum (AVGO, LRCX), geopolitical energy exposure (APA), power-demand growth (NRG), and healthcare quality (GILD).
- BMNR and similar Ethereum treasury companies are positioned as institutional bridges to tokenized finance, earning staking yields and transaction-validation fees rather than pure ETH price speculation.
- The RIA wirehouse-exodus is accelerating; NewEdge's pipeline is at record levels as advisors seek entrepreneurial structures with full institutional support.

## Detailed Notes

### Software Sector Weakness and the Margin Problem [00:07:57]

Parker argues that software stocks are 'guilty until proven innocent' because sell-side models embed flat gross and net margins through 2028-29 without accounting for the spending required to attach AI tools. He predicts the pattern is: miss on earnings first, then eventually miss on sales. Sechan and Brown note that ServiceNow's 22% revenue growth and strong ACV metrics still resulted in an 18% single-day drop, illustrating the market's zero-tolerance stance on margin guidance cuts.

### Semiconductor Rally and Nvidia's Earnings Revision Story [00:24:38]

Parker highlights that the SMH posted its best 16-day rolling return in history (~35%), yet argues the move is justified given Nvidia's free-cash-flow trajectory went from roughly $1.5B to $80B+ over two years—one of the largest positive estimate revisions in 25 years of market history. He also notes Micron trades at 4.5x next year's earnings despite ~100% earnings growth, making it optically cheap in the context of a high-growth semiconductor upcycle.

### Market Breadth, Multiple Compression, and the Macro Backdrop [00:30:00]

The group reviews tech sector forward P/E compression from ~32x in late 2024 to ~19x by April 2026 as earnings estimates raced higher. Parker dismisses breadth indicators as statistically insignificant for predicting returns, preferring to focus on absolute earnings growth levels. Sechan notes the market's 'stair-step lower' during geopolitical stress—without a capitulation panic—as a bullish signal that kept NewEdge fully invested.

### Spinoff Playbook [00:48:39]

Parker's research shows median spin-co transactions reached a 26-year peak of $3.3B in 2025 across 11 deals, with only one completed in 2026 so far (Comcast/Versent). Historically, spin-co's beat their industry by ~10% over two years while remain-co's lag by ~2%. Parker expects a wave of mid-cap spinoffs, driven by boards seeking value unlocks via focused capital structures and tax-efficient separations.

### Summer Stock Picks: AVGO, LRCX, NRG, APA, GILD [00:55:43]

Sechan's team presents a balanced portfolio: Broadcom for custom silicon and data-center networking leadership with ~60% projected revenue growth; Lam Research for its near-monopoly in chip manufacturing equipment; NRG Energy as an AI-power beneficiary with 'bring-your-own-power' data-center contracts; APA Corp for Middle East-geopolitical optionality and a 12% total yield; and Gilead for HIV dominance and an oncology pipeline with 50+ drugs in development.

### Ethereum Treasury Companies and BMNR [01:17:00]

Sechan, a lead independent director at Bitmine (BMNR), explains the concept as an Ethereum-holding treasury vehicle that earns staking yields of 3-4% by validating transactions, plus equity-like upside from 'moonshot' investments such as Mr. Beast. He frames Ethereum as programmable financial infrastructure rather than a speculative coin, and argues traditional finance is converging toward tokenization on the Ethereum layer.

### Nvidia's Path to $10 Trillion [01:21:28]

Parker argues Nvidia should be thought of as a sector, not a stock, given the near-impossibility of migrating large-scale AI workloads off the CUDA platform. He projects at least 15% annual revenue growth for five years, implying revenues roughly double, and sees the current ~$4.8T market cap as undervalued if the S&P 500 is headed to 10,000. Parker also notes that 12-month consolidation periods in the stock have historically been buying opportunities.
