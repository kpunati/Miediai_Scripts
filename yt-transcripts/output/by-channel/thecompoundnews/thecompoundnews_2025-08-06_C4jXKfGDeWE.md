# Wall Street Strikes Back | Animal Spirits 424

**Channel:** The Compound
**Published:** 2025-08-06
**URL:** https://youtube.com/watch?v=C4jXKfGDeWE
**Duration:** 65:49

## Summary

Animal Spirits episode 424 examines the growing consensus that AI capital expenditure by the major hyperscalers has become a dominant driver of US economic growth in 2025, with hosts Michael Batnick and Ben Carlson discussing data showing AI investment added more to GDP than consumer spending. They explore whether this represents a historical capex bubble analogous to railroads or telecom, or a genuine transformative shift. The episode also covers rising retail investor sophistication and activity in futures and options, the cooling housing market with particular weakness in condos, labor market data integrity debates, and cryptocurrency milestones including the JP Morgan-Coinbase partnership.

## Key Takeaways

- The four largest hyperscalers (Meta, Google, Microsoft, Amazon) are forecast to spend $320 billion on AI capex in 2025, up from $250 billion in 2024, and this spending has outpaced consumer spending in contribution to GDP growth.
- Historical capex bubbles (railroads, telecom) suggest a potential washout scenario where overspending leads to a tech stock correction of 30-50%, even if the underlying technology succeeds.
- Retail investor participation is surging, with CME reporting 90,000 new retail futures traders in Q2 2025 (a 56% increase) and Robinhood generating a quarterly record $265 million in options revenue.
- The US housing market shows stark regional divergence: inventory is rising in Texas and Florida while remaining scarce in the Northeast and Midwest, with condo prices falling nationwide due to rising assessments and insurance costs.
- Bitcoin is showing increased market maturity, with $2 billion in MicroStrategy purchases and $8 billion in Galaxy sales barely moving the price, and crypto ETFs setting a monthly inflow record of $12.8 billion in July.
- Labor market data quality is being politicized, with the administration's response to payroll revisions raising concerns, though a former BLS economist noted revisions are statistically normal and unbiased.
- The JP Morgan-Coinbase strategic partnership enabling direct bank-to-wallet connections and Chase credit card funding of crypto accounts marks a significant mainstream crypto adoption milestone.

## Detailed Notes

### AI Capex as GDP Driver [00:00:04]

Hosts discuss the emerging consensus that AI spending by hyperscalers essentially provided a 'soft landing' for the economy during the inflation period and the 2025 trade war. Data from Financial Times shows Meta, Google, Microsoft, and Amazon spent $150B in 2023, $250B in 2024, and forecast $320B for 2025. A RENMAC chart showed AI capex added more to GDP than consumer spending in 2025 despite consumer spending totaling $16.4 trillion versus AI's $1.4 trillion contribution — the gap in growth rates is the key insight.

### Capex Bubble Risk Debate [00:00:04]

Ben references a Paul Kadoski analysis showing infrastructure capex as a percentage of GDP across historical eras: railroads (~6%), telecom, and now data centers (~1.2% of GDP). The historical playbook suggests expectations overshoot, a spending pullback triggers a washout, and tech stocks could fall 30-50% even if AI ultimately works. Michael argues this 'too easy' historical parallel may be wrong because the current AI technology is genuinely transformative and companies are generating real revenue.

### Retail Investor Activity and Market Structure [00:00:04]

CME reported 90,000 new retail futures traders in Q2 2025, a 56% increase and the fifth consecutive quarter of double-digit growth. Robinhood set a quarterly record of $265 million in options revenue with trading revenue up 65% year-over-year. YieldMax ETFs (ULTY with a 112% dividend yield and MSTY tied to MicroStrategy) appeared in top-10 daily flows, prompting debate about whether retail investors understand yield-erosion products.

### Labor Market and Economic Data Debate [00:00:04]

A 253,000 two-month downward revision to payroll employment — the largest since at least 1979 — prompted market anxiety. Economist Matthew Klein argued the US economy slowed in H1 2025 but unemployment, wage growth, and inflation were not materially impacted. A former BLS economist rebutted claims of data manipulation, noting revisions follow a normal distribution centered at zero. Hosts note decreased immigration may explain most of the observed slowdown in aggregate growth.

### Housing Market Dynamics [00:00:04]

About half of US cities are now seeing falling home prices, with more sellers than buyers emerging for the first time post-pandemic. Condo prices are declining faster than single-family homes due to mandatory building assessments and insurance cost increases, especially in Florida. Mortgage lock-in effects are constraining both supply and demand; Michael describes selling his Long Island house with only two offers despite listing above asking price, attributing limited interest primarily to high interest rates.

### Cryptocurrency Developments [00:00:04]

MicroStrategy purchased 21,000 Bitcoin at $117,000 per coin and Galaxy sold $8 billion worth, with Bitcoin price barely responding — indicating a much deeper, more liquid market than historically. US crypto ETFs set a monthly inflow record of $12.8 billion in July at $600 million per day. JP Morgan and Coinbase announced a strategic partnership enabling bank-to-wallet connections, Chase reward point transfers to Coinbase, and credit card funding of crypto accounts, marking major mainstream institutional endorsement.
