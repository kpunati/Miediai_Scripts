# Does the Stock Market Know Something We Don’t? | TCAF EP204

**Channel:** The Compound
**Published:** 2025-08-15
**URL:** https://youtube.com/watch?v=B3ltiziHoas
**Duration:** 71:18

## Summary

Episode 204 of The Compound and Friends features Michael Batnick with Shannon Saccocia of Neuberger Berman and Todd Sohn of Strategas, analyzing the stock market's continued rise despite persistent macroeconomic uncertainty. The panel identifies three core drivers of the rally: tariff uncertainty has peaked and corporate earnings have significantly beat expectations, rate cuts remain on the horizon, and inflation is broadly under control. They also examine warning signs including extreme index concentration in Nvidia and Microsoft, near-record leveraged ETF assets signaling elevated risk appetite, mixed crypto flows versus retail conversations, and growing labor market stress among younger workers potentially linked to AI adoption.

## Key Takeaways

- Corporate America's ability to protect margins against tariff headwinds was significantly underestimated, leading to one of the widest earnings-beat gaps in a decade.
- All-time highs in equity markets are historically bullish and tend to be followed by persistent positive performance rather than mean reversion.
- Leveraged ETF assets have reached all-time highs around $135 billion with a 10-to-1 leverage-to-inverse ratio, a key real-time sentiment indicator that warrants monitoring.
- S&P 500 concentration risk has reached an extreme: Nvidia at ~8% weight nearly matches entire healthcare and industrials sectors combined, raising structural index concerns.
- Crypto asset flows into ETFs are strong but retail investor conversations about crypto have paradoxically declined versus prior Bitcoin rallies, suggesting institutionalization rather than mass retail enthusiasm.
- Entry-level job displacement from AI is a growing concern, with companies using AI efficiency as justification for reduced hiring of younger workers.
- Immigration policy shifts may create longer-term wage and services inflation pressure as foreign-born worker share declines.

## Detailed Notes

### Why Is the Stock Market Up? Three Drivers [00:00:00]

Batnick frames three reasons for the rally: tariff uncertainty has peaked with the range of outcomes narrowing post-Liberation Day; corporate earnings dramatically beat expectations, with Goldman data showing the widest surprise gap in 10 years; and rate cuts are expected as Fed leadership transitions. Shannon Saccocia adds that corporate management teams honed over a decade of adversity (GFC, 2019 tariffs, COVID) have proven far better at margin protection than analysts anticipated.

### Investor Sentiment: Mixed Signals Across Cohorts [00:00:00]

Todd Sohn notes sentiment entering 2025 was aggressive after back-to-back 25% years, but tariff shock reset it; recovery since has been technically sound with breadth and momentum improving. Schwab data shows retail investors on the largest platform have been net sellers of technology including Nvidia for months, countering the narrative of uniform retail euphoria. Shannon distinguishes three investor buckets: institutional, advisor-led retail, and speculative retail, with advisors increasingly applying institutional frameworks.

### Crypto Adoption Dynamics [00:00:00]

ETF flows into crypto are strong but retail conversations about it have paradoxically declined from prior bull cycles, suggesting the current move is driven more by institutional adoption (Harvard's disclosed position, ETF mechanics) than retail speculation. Shannon notes that advisors find crypto positions too small to move the needle relative to their volatility cost, and many sophisticated early adopters have moved beyond spot ETFs toward tokenization.

### Technical Signals and Near-Term Outlook [00:00:00]

Cyclicals-versus-defensives ratio hit its best 3-month rate of change in 15 years off the April low, historically a risk-on signal for continuation. However, NASDAQ 100 logged all-time highs with fewer than half its members above their 50-day moving average — Thursday's reading was the weakest in history. Leveraged ETF assets near $135 billion at 10-to-1 versus inverse ETFs signal elevated trader risk appetite, though not yet at January 2025 extremes.

### Index Concentration and the MAG7 Capex Question [00:00:00]

Nvidia alone is ~8% of the S&P 500, nearly as large as the combined healthcare and industrials sectors; Nvidia plus Microsoft at ~15% nearly rival all four defensive sectors combined. The largest AI firms are now spending 1.3x earnings on capex, up from 25% of cash flow in 2015 to nearly 45% today. Shannon and Batnick debate whether AI capex will drive the next decade's broadening trade or whether a failure to monetize will trigger a 2000-style unwind.

### IPO Market, M&A, and Interesting Products [00:00:00]

The IPO window has opened after years of backlog; however, recent listings like Circle (IPO high $299, now $140) and Figma (popped to $143, fell to $76 pre-IPO low) illustrate pricing risk. M&A activity is ticking up with Goldman Sachs canceling planned layoffs due to rising banking activity. A USCF filing for an oil-plus-Bitcoin ETF was discussed as a geopolitical doomsday hedge product. ARK ETF saw large inflows likely driven by tax-motivated heartbeat trades rather than genuine sentiment.

### Labor Market and AI Displacement [00:00:00]

July payroll data was so weak and subject to large revisions that the panel views it as unreliable signal. Most concerning is the unemployment rate for the 16-25 cohort approaching double digits. Companies appear to be using AI efficiency gains as cover for reduced entry-level hiring; the panel expresses concern that grunt-work roles being replaced by AI create a structural career-entry problem that won't self-correct for 5-10 years. Separately, declining foreign-born worker share since the Trump presidency (down 3%) is flagged as a medium-term services inflation risk.
