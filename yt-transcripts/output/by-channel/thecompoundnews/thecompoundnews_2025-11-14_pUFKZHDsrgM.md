# Quality in the Streets, Momentum in the Sheets | TCAF 217

**Channel:** The Compound
**Published:** 2025-11-14
**URL:** https://youtube.com/watch?v=pUFKZHDsrgM
**Duration:** 62:11

## Summary

In this live-audience recording, Josh Brown and Michael Batnick interview Joe Terranova about the mid-November 2025 market pullback, framing it as a momentum unwind rather than a structural bear market. Terranova explains how the Federal Reserve's September 2024 rate cut ignited a momentum trade in macro assets — long crypto, long precious metals, short dollar — that is now deleveraging and spreading into equities, with Palantir's negative post-earnings price action serving as a key sentiment signal. The conversation then widens to the secular bull market case (global central bank easing, four consecutive quarters of double-digit earnings growth, bond market calm) before pivoting to a detailed discussion of how the JOET rules-based quality-momentum ETF operates, using Nvidia and Palantir as case studies in the value of removing human emotion from portfolio decisions. The episode closes with midterm-election year seasonality statistics and a personal looking-ahead segment.

## Key Takeaways

- Palantir's blowout earnings but flat price action exemplifies 'good news, bad price action,' a classic signal that speculators are reducing risk rather than adding it.
- Terranova argues current market corrections are more violent but shorter in duration because algorithmic strategies dominate and create rapid, synchronized deleveraging and snapbacks.
- The secular bull market case rests on more than 80% of global central banks last cutting rates, four consecutive quarters of double-digit S&P 500 earnings growth, and historically narrow 10-year Treasury yield ranges.
- Midterm election year seasonality shows a 21-for-21 record of positive S&P 500 returns in the 9-month window starting on election day, with 14 of those years producing double-digit gains.
- Rules-based ETF wrappers like JOET remove behavioral biases — the strategy bought back Nvidia at $27 after selling at $25 and held Palantir from $16 to over $175, trades most discretionary managers would have botched.
- Terranova defines quality with three metrics: return on equity, debt-to-equity ratio, and three-year revenue growth, arguing the inclusion of growth avoids traditional value traps.
- Equal-weight within the portfolio and quarterly reconstitution provide natural risk management, acting as a shock absorber in downturns while keeping pace in bull markets.

## Detailed Notes

### Market Pullback and Momentum Unwind [00:02:53]

Terranova traces the current equity weakness to a macro momentum trade — long crypto, long precious metals, short dollar — that peaked around September 17, 2024, when the dollar reversed, followed by crypto topping on October 6 and precious metals in mid-October. As leverage comes out of these macro positions, it eventually forces equity selling. Palantir's post-earnings price failure (good earnings, flat stock) is cited as the clearest signal that speculators are in risk-reduction mode.

### AI CapEx Skepticism and Market Structure [00:09:30]

Brown notes that high-capex AI stocks have defied historical norms this year (up 45% vs. the typical multi-year underperformance), but the market is beginning to question circular financing schemes, pointing to Oracle's debt-to-equity above 500% and the company's $300 billion OpenAI contract that the market is skeptical OpenAI will honor. Terranova argues current corrections are more violent but shorter-lived due to algorithmic dominance — April 2025's rapid selloff and snapback is offered as evidence.

### Secular Bull Market Thesis [00:21:24]

Terranova makes a multi-factor bull case: over 80% of global central banks last acted with a rate cut, inflation is now a US-specific problem (Europe at 2%, India 1.5%, China slightly negative), four consecutive quarters of double-digit S&P earnings growth, and the 10-year Treasury yield range in 2025 is only about 95 basis points versus 285 basis points in 2022. He also credits deregulation sentiment and lower energy prices (oil near $58-67 vs. above $80 when Trump took office) as positive tailwinds. The equal-weight S&P's underperformance widening by 3.6% over four days — a rarity outside October 2008 and June 2020 — is flagged as a potential short-term top in the AI-concentrated trade.

### JOET ETF: Rules-Based Quality-Momentum Investing [00:42:26]

Terranova explains JOET's framework: 125 names drawn from the 500 largest US companies by market cap, equal-weighted, and reconstituted quarterly using three quality metrics (return on equity, debt-to-equity, three-year revenue growth) combined with a momentum screen. He uses two case studies — selling Nvidia at $25 in January 2023 and buying it back at $27 in April, and holding Palantir from $16 all the way to $175+ — to demonstrate how the ETF wrapper removes the emotional bias that would have caused a discretionary manager to sell prematurely. In 2022, equal-weighting and quality helped limit drawdown; in 2023, momentum and growth drove outperformance. Terranova describes his goal as 'capturing the personality of the market' rather than beating the S&P 500 every quarter.

### Midterm Election Seasonality and Looking Ahead [00:36:00]

Brown presents a statistic Terranova has previously referenced on the show: since World War II, buying the S&P 500 on midterm election day and selling on June 30 of the following year has been profitable in all 21 occurrences, with 14 producing double-digit returns. Terranova notes he used this data to stay constructive in 2022 when markets were down 19%, and expects it to be a useful guide heading into November 2026. The segment closes with personal looking-ahead comments about family milestones and golf.
