# Stocks Break Out All Over The World | TCAF 200

**Channel:** The Compound

## Summary

Episode 200 of The Compound and Friends features JC Parets, founder of TrendLabs, discussing his contrarian technical analysis approach and reviewing global equity markets that are broadly making all-time or multi-year highs across Europe, Asia, Latin America, and beyond. Parets presents short interest data showing hedge funds and asset managers underpositioned in the current rally, arguing this creates an underlying support for the market. The conversation covers Bitcoin dominance declining, altcoin strength, a contrarian case for biotech, and technical setups across the Mag 7 stocks, with Parets favoring Tesla and Amazon as the best setups into year-end.

## Key Takeaways

- Global equity markets from Germany to Japan to Latin America are broadly making all-time or multi-cycle highs simultaneously, echoing the 2017 synchronized global growth environment.
- Short interest data from FINRA and the CFTC Commitment of Traders report shows asset managers and hedge funds are heavily underpositioned in stocks, historically a bullish contrarian signal.
- Bitcoin dominance is declining while Ethereum outperforms, and commercial hedgers (smart money) are net-long Bitcoin at record levels while speculators are shorting at record levels.
- Biotech is the biggest contrarian opportunity: the IBB has returned roughly 1.7% over 10 years versus 249% for the S&P 500, short interest in the XBI and Russell 2000 is at record highs.
- Tesla is Parets's top Mag 7 pick for the second half of 2025 given extreme pessimism, heavy short interest via ARK, and the importance of consumer discretionary to any continued bull market.
- Parets distinguishes between direction and rate of change in interest rates, arguing orderly moves in rates matter less than sudden volatility spikes in bond and forex markets.
- Asset prices trend while volatility mean-reverts — yet humans do the opposite, trying to mean-revert prices and trend-follow volatility, explaining persistent bearish skepticism at market highs.

## Detailed Notes

### TrendLabs and Everybody's Wrong Newsletter [00:06:45]

JC Parets explains the evolution from AllStar Charts (acquired four years ago) to Stock Market Media and then TrendLabs. TrendLabs is his personal research vehicle focused on identifying contrarian extremes in positioning. The daily newsletter 'Everybody's Wrong' targets situations where consensus positioning is heavily one-sided and due to reverse.

### Interest Rates, the Fed, and Market Implications [00:10:32]

Parets argues the rate of change in interest rates matters more than the direction or level. Orderly rate moves do not typically derail equity markets; it is rapid moves in bond and forex markets that cause equity volatility. The 30-year Treasury at 5% is viewed as a news story rather than a market signal. TIPS versus nominal Treasury ratio is being monitored as the bond market's real-time inflation indicator.

### Short Interest Data and Commitment of Traders Analysis [00:27:46]

Parets walks through how TrendLabs processes FINRA short interest data (published every two weeks) and CFTC Commitment of Traders reports (published every Friday) using machine learning to identify names with rising short interest as a percentage of market cap, high days-to-cover, and signs of active short squeezes. Examples of current squeeze candidates include GameStop, OpenDoor, and RCAT (Red Cat Holdings).

### Global Equity Breakouts [00:53:43]

Parets presents a comprehensive tour of global stock markets, with the German DAX, Euro Stoxx 600, FTSE 100, Oslo, Amsterdam, Poland, Italy, Ireland, Spain, Greece, Tel Aviv, Johannesburg, India's Nifty 50, China, and Japan's Nikkei all at or near all-time or multi-decade highs. Japan's breakout above 1989 levels is highlighted as the most significant. The broad participation across regions and sectors is compared favorably to the 2017 synchronized global growth environment.

### Bitcoin Dominance and Crypto Markets [00:46:15]

Total crypto market cap is at all-time highs near $3.8 trillion. Bitcoin dominance is declining, with ETH/BTC breaking out to the upside. Commercial hedgers are the most net-long Bitcoin ever while small and large speculators are shorting at record levels — a configuration Parets reads as bullish. MicroStrategy (Strategy) holds roughly $71B in Bitcoin but trades at approximately a 2x premium to NAV at ~$140B market cap.

### Biotech as Biggest Contrarian Play [01:00:08]

Biotech (IBB/XBI) has returned roughly 1.7% over 10 years versus 249% for the S&P 500. Short interest in the XBI and the Russell 2000 (which contains ~250 biotech names) is at record highs. Ari Wald of Oppenheimer is cited as arguing XBI above $89 would unlock broader market breadth. Alnylam Pharmaceuticals (ALNY) and Gilead (GILD) are highlighted as the two IBB top-10 holdings showing constructive technical setups.

### Mag 7 Technical Setups and Tesla Thesis [01:06:01]

Parets favors Tesla and Amazon as the best Mag 7 setups into year-end. Tesla is down approximately 20% YTD with extreme bearish sentiment and high short interest via ARK. Amazon is forming a golden cross, breaking above 240 resistance, at its lowest-ever P/E of roughly 36 with hockey-stick EPS growth. Amazon represents 22% of the consumer discretionary sector and Tesla about 18%, making their performance integral to any continued bull market leg.
