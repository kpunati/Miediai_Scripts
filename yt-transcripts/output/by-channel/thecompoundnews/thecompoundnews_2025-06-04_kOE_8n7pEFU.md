# Will AI Take All Our Jobs? | Animal Spirits 415

**Channel:** The Compound
**Published:** 2025-06-04
**URL:** https://youtube.com/watch?v=kOE_8n7pEFU
**Duration:** 64:19

## Summary

In episode 415 of Animal Spirits, Michael Batnick and Ben Carlson record from their Chicago office opening and cover a wide range of financial and market topics. They examine whether technical analysis remains valid in a tariff-driven market, note improved investor behavior during the April selloff, and debate when government debt levels should become a genuine concern. The hosts discuss AI's potential for significant job displacement versus its deflationary economic benefits, and assess the housing market slowdown driven by elevated mortgage rates.

## Key Takeaways

- Breadth thrusts — rapid transitions from oversold to overbought — have historically been bullish signals, and the hosts believe technicals deserve the benefit of the doubt even in policy-driven markets.
- Investors demonstrated improved behavior during the April selloff by buying dips aggressively and then slowing purchases on the recovery, rather than panic-selling.
- Research shows only 29% of companies with business deterioration achieve a sustained turnaround, supporting a momentum-over-averaging-down approach for stock pickers.
- AI non-AI tech jobs are down 9% since 2018 while AI-specific jobs are up 448%, signaling a structural labor shift that the hosts believe may break the historical pattern of technology creating net new employment.
- The housing market is seeing the largest seller-to-buyer imbalance since 2013 per Redfin data, but low total inventory and likely rate sensitivity suggest demand could return sharply if mortgage rates fall.
- Consumer spending remains resilient across American Express, Bank of America, Visa, and Mastercard despite historically low consumer sentiment surveys, driven by continued labor market strength.
- Jamie Dimon's repeated warnings about a government bond market crack are noted, but the hosts remain skeptical until market signals — sustained high inflation and rising yields — confirm the risk.

## Detailed Notes

### Stock Market Technicals and Investor Behavior [00:05:34]

The hosts debate whether technical analysis remains valid when market moves are driven by tariff tweets. They cite breadth thrust data showing the S&P recovered from over 18% below its high to within 3% of all-time highs, arguing the market consistently bottoms before macro fundamentals give the all-clear. Equity ETF flows data from Todd Sona showed that investors actually bought the dip heavily and reduced buying on the way back up, indicating improved behavioral discipline.

### Stock-Picking Difficulty and Loss Aversion [00:17:15]

Referencing a Mobison research piece, the hosts note that a large majority of stocks get cut in half at some point, and only 29% of companies with sustained deterioration achieve a true turnaround. Research on retail investor behavior found investors are 50% more likely to buy more of a falling stock, yet averaging down did not improve returns. The hosts argue loss aversion is the most important behavioral concept for investors and that momentum investing, though counterintuitive, is often more rewarding.

### Rates, Government Debt, and Jamie Dimon [00:22:23]

Batnick questions the risk-reward of long-duration bonds, noting TLT is still in a 43% drawdown while short-term rates offer only 1% less yield than 30-year Treasuries. Jamie Dimon's warning of a bond market crack is discussed alongside his 2022 hurricane comment; the hosts question whether these are genuine warnings or perpetual vigilance messaging. Carlson states he will wait for the market itself — via sustained high inflation and high rates — before treating government debt as an actionable risk.

### AI, Jobs, and Deflation [00:36:49]

A New York Times op-ed on AI's impact on entry-level careers prompts discussion of broader labor displacement. Cullen Roche's thesis is cited: AI will first compress services inflation, then robots will reduce goods inflation, potentially leading to ZIRP within 10-20 years. Alphabet's 50x annual token processing increase and Mary Meeker's 300-page report showing AI jobs up 448% since 2018 are highlighted as evidence of the structural shift. The hosts suggest owning stocks — particularly robotics and AI companies — as the pragmatic response.

### Housing Market and Consumer Spending [00:43:29]

April home sales hit the slowest pace for any April in 16 years. Redfin data shows nearly 500,000 more sellers than buyers, the widest gap since 2013. The Toll Brothers CEO noted buyers are not responding to additional incentives above $5,000-$20,000, pointing to a mortgage-rate-driven strike rather than a demand collapse. Consumer spending data from American Express, Bank of America, Visa, and Mastercard all contradict depressed sentiment surveys, with the hosts attributing resilience entirely to labor market strength.

### Waymo, Streaming, and Entertainment [00:42:52]

Waymo's paid ride growth is highlighted as underappreciated: from 10,000 rides per week in August 2023 to over 250,000 per week by mid-2025. The hosts briefly discuss the streaming landscape via Ben Thompson's ESPN analysis and share film and TV recommendations including Naked Gun reboot, The Studio, Crazy Stupid Love, and the Last of Us season 2 finale.
