# The Bubble is Bursting | TCAF 182

**Channel:** The Compound
**Published:** 2025-03-14
**URL:** https://youtube.com/watch?v=x6hS717BGtg
**Duration:** 79:44

## Summary

Rob Arnott, founder of Research Affiliates, joins The Compound and Friends to assess the March 2025 US equity selloff through the lens of valuation and historical precedent. Arnott argues the market may be in the early stages of a bubble bursting, particularly in mega-cap growth and AI stocks, while simultaneously seeing opportunity-rich conditions in international value, small cap value, and multi-asset diversifiers. The conversation covers the mechanics of 'big market delusions,' evolving CAPE ratio interpretation, fiscal and monetary policy under tariff uncertainty, and Arnott's DOGE-related views on deficit spending and government efficiency. Arnott also outlines the rationale behind the Pimco All Asset and All Asset All Authority funds as diversification vehicles for a low-return S&P 500 environment.

## Key Takeaways

- Arnott views the current environment as opportunity-rich for cheap assets like international value and small cap value, even as US large-cap growth continues to derate.
- The EV sector serves as a case study in 'big market delusion' — all nine original EV specialists underperformed the S&P 500, with the median down roughly 80%.
- AI stocks display similar big-market-delusion characteristics; Arnott is on record calling it a bubble but warns against short-selling bubbles, which can persist longer than expected.
- The CAPE ratio long-term average has risen from 17.5 to roughly 25-27 when measured against its own historical trend, meaning 35x still implies meaningful downside risk.
- Research Affiliates' model projects 10-year forward S&P 500 returns of only about 3.5% annually, compared to roughly 10% for international and emerging market value.
- Arnott supports spending cuts over tax increases to address the deficit, citing Research Affiliates research showing a negative 50% correlation between government spending magnitude and per capita real GDP growth.
- The Pimco All Asset All Authority fund can go net short US equities to reduce equity beta, providing more differentiated returns during bear markets.

## Detailed Notes

### Market Selloff and Early Bubble Warning Signs [00:00:00]

Arnott opens by characterizing the March 2025 selloff as potentially the early stages of a bursting bubble, while noting that cheap assets — particularly EM and international value — make the environment opportunity-rich rather than purely bearish. He notes that the last decade favored pure mega-cap indexing, disadvantaging value managers.

### Big Market Delusion: EV Case Study [00:11:57]

Arnott revisits the 'big market delusion' framework co-developed with Ang Deaton and Brad Cornell — a bubble in which multiple companies are priced for the full success of a transformative narrative. The EV sector exemplifies this: nine EV specialists in 2020 saw a composite gain of ~800%, but through 2025 the median is down ~80% and none has beaten the S&P 500.

### AI as the Next Big Market Delusion [00:25:41]

Arnott states publicly that AI stocks are a bubble, but cautions against shorting bubbles given they can extend far beyond what valuation models suggest. He notes AI is genuinely useful internally at Research Affiliates for copy editing, graphics generation, and literature review, but argues most asset managers claiming AI-driven alpha are overstating it, particularly for medium-to-long investment horizons where data scarcity limits AI's edge.

### CAPE Ratio Evolution and US Valuation [00:40:34]

The group examines how the CAPE ratio's long-run average has shifted upward. Arnott acknowledges a wealthier, more mature economy may sustain a higher multiple, but a trend-line analysis still puts fair value closer to 25 times, implying potential downside from the 35 times level prevailing in early 2025. He also highlights that S&P earnings are roughly 50% above their 10-year average — historically, such stretches lead to approximately zero earnings growth over the following decade.

### International and Emerging Market Opportunity [00:57:39]

Arnott has long favored international value; the catalyst arrived as Trump tariffs and European defense spending spurred a sharp rotation. Research Affiliates' asset allocation tool projects ~10% annualized returns for international and EM value versus ~3.5% for the US S&P 500, representing the widest spread in decades. He draws parallels to the 2000-2002 period when Russell 2000 Value gained 53% while the S&P fell 27%.

### Fiscal Policy, DOGE, and Deficit Spending [00:49:04]

Arnott argues the problem is government spending rather than deficits per se, citing RA research showing a -50% correlation between spending magnitude and per capita real GDP growth. He supports DOGE-style spending cuts as necessary long-term medicine despite short-term pain, and views attempting to model specific tariff levels as largely futile given the daily policy reversals under the Trump administration.

### Pimco All Asset and All Asset All Authority [01:10:32]

Arnott describes these funds as one-stop diversification vehicles investing in 'third pillar' assets — commodities, high yield, EM stocks and bonds, TIPS, REITs — priced to return ~7.5% versus ~4.5% for a classic 60/40. All Asset All Authority additionally can short US equities, lowering equity beta; during the global financial crisis it barely declined while the S&P fell sharply.
