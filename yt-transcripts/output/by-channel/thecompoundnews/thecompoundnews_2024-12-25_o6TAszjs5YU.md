# All In on U.S. Stocks | Animal Spirits 392

**Channel:** The Compound
**Published:** 2024-12-25
**URL:** https://youtube.com/watch?v=o6TAszjs5YU
**Duration:** 64:06

## Summary

Animal Spirits episode 392, recorded on Christmas Day 2024, features Michael Batnick and Ben Carlson reviewing a year-end market shake-out and discussing the investment landscape heading into 2025. The hosts assess the Fed's shifting rate-cut trajectory, extreme bearish sentiment toward international stocks, and the growing concentration risk in U.S. large-cap tech. They also explore behavioral finance themes including trading addiction, the MicroStrategy crypto cult, and investor tendencies toward over-customization through direct indexing.

## Key Takeaways

- A brief Dow sell-off of roughly 5-6% is characterized as healthy for a bull market still up approximately 26% on the year.
- Apollo's Torsten Slok estimates a 40% chance the Fed hikes rates in 2025, which both hosts view as a potential catalyst for a 15-20% market correction if realized.
- Investor sentiment toward international, value, and small-cap stocks has reached historically negative extremes, but the hosts caution that sentiment alone is not a reliable timing indicator.
- The top 10 S&P 500 stocks represent about 40% of index market cap, raising concentration concerns that direct-indexing workarounds may not adequately address.
- Private credit is entering retirement accounts in a major wave, but its 4%+ fee structure and opacity warrant significant investor skepticism.
- Trading addiction is documented as a growing clinical problem, with PA gambling hotlines seeing more stock/crypto calls since 2021 than in the prior six years combined.
- AI capital expenditure by major tech firms is unprecedented in scale, and Benedict Evans's annual presentation frames this as a structural break from historical comparisons.

## Detailed Notes

### Market Vibe Shift and Fed Outlook [00:00:04]

The Dow experienced a roughly 12-day losing streak and a 5-6% drawdown following the December Fed press conference. Batnick argues the selloff was a healthy correction in an otherwise strong bull market, with the S&P still up approximately 26% on the year. The Fed signaling fewer rate cuts for 2025 is viewed positively by the hosts because it reflects economic strength, though they note that if inflation re-accelerates and forces rate hikes, a 15-20% correction would be likely.

### International Stocks, Diversification, and Valuation Debate [00:00:04]

Carlson notes he has never seen more negative sentiment toward international stocks, value stocks, and valuation concerns broadly. The hosts debate whether this is a timing opportunity or reflects a structural shift where U.S. large-cap growth has permanently supplanted historical value and international premiums. They reference Peter Bernstein's account of the stock-to-bond yield signal that stopped working in the late 1950s as a caution against assuming historical cycles must repeat. A 50-year analysis cited by Colin Roach at Discipline Funds showed global diversification delivered nearly 6% real annualized returns, close to the U.S.-only return of 6.6%, supporting the case for diversification even if not maximizing returns.

### Trading Addiction and MicroStrategy Crypto Cult [00:00:04]

A Wall Street Journal piece documents Gamblers Anonymous meetings now dominated by stock and options traders, with Pennsylvania hotlines showing a surge in trading-related addiction calls since 2021. The hosts discuss how online communities amplify herd behavior, pointing to MicroStrategy's Discord and Twitter groups with tens of thousands of members. They note that the dollar value of MicroStrategy shares traded in November briefly exceeded every other U.S. stock and ETF except Nvidia, reflecting extreme speculative concentration.

### AI Capital Expenditure and Tech Moats [00:00:04]

Benedict Evans's 2025 annual presentation comparing Apple's pre-iPhone R&D to Meta Reality Labs losses illustrates the unprecedented scale of current AI investment. The hosts argue that no sector in history has produced sustained profits at a scale that allows companies to absorb massive losses and still widen competitive moats, making historical analogies less useful for evaluating current tech valuations.

### Private Credit, Housing, and Retirement Planning [00:00:04]

Jason Zweig's WSJ analysis shows private credit funds averaged 4.12% in total annual expenses in 2023, which the hosts find hard to reconcile with borrower rates of 15-17% and promised investor yields of 10-12%. Despite skepticism about fees and opacity, Batnick expects private credit to continue growing rapidly into 401(k)s and retirement accounts, with fees likely declining over the next decade as competition increases. A separate discussion on aging U.S. housing stock highlights a coming wave of costly renovations as Baby Boomers pass homes to younger generations.

### Miscellaneous: Autonomous Vehicles, Guinness Trend, Recommendations [00:00:04]

Waymo data showing 88% and 92% reductions in property damage and bodily injury claims respectively is cited as underappreciated evidence that autonomous vehicles are already far safer than human drivers. The episode closes with cultural observations including a viral Guinness drinking trend, streaming platform rankings, and the hosts' end-of-year book and show recommendations.
