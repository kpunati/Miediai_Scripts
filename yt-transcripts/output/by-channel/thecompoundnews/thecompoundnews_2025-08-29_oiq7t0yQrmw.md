# How to Make Money in the Bull Market for Electricity | TCAF 206

**Channel:** The Compound
**Published:** 2025-08-29
**URL:** https://youtube.com/watch?v=oiq7t0yQrmw
**Duration:** 66:16

## Summary

In episode 206 of The Compound and Friends, Jan Van Eck (VanEck CEO) joins hosts Josh Brown and Michael Batnick to cover a wide range of macro and market themes. The conversation spans Nvidia's record data center earnings, the structural bull case for electricity demand driven by AI, the US deficit as a portfolio risk and gold/Bitcoin as hedges, the superior returns of owning GP stocks versus LP positions in private equity funds, and the maturing crypto ecosystem including stablecoins and tokenized securities. Jan also marks VanEck's 70th anniversary and discusses the firm's thematic ETF strategy.

## Key Takeaways

- Nvidia's $41 billion data center quarter (up 56% YoY) excludes China revenue, meaning any China re-entry represents pure upside to already-elevated analyst forecasts.
- AI-driven electricity demand is pushing utility stocks to historically high multiples, with nuclear and natural gas identified as the primary beneficiaries of this multi-year infrastructure cycle.
- Foreign central banks now hold more gold than US Treasuries for the first time since 1996, supporting gold's role as a dollar-hedge in diversified portfolios.
- Owning GP stocks of alternative asset managers (Blackstone, KKR, etc.) dramatically outperformed being an LP in their funds over a three-year period, illustrating the value of fee-stream exposure.
- The 2023 stablecoin legislation is viewed as the third most important piece of US banking law, enabling tech companies to enter payments without a bank charter.
- Social Security faces a projected 20% benefit cut around 2033 absent bipartisan action, which Jan frames as a selective sovereign default and a reason to hold portfolio hedges.
- ETH and EVM-compatible blockchains are expected to become the settlement layer Wall Street standardizes on for tokenized securities and stablecoins.

## Detailed Notes

### VanEck 70th Anniversary and ETF Industry Landscape [00:03:20]

Jan discusses VanEck's 70-year history, noting the firm's founding philosophy of identifying macro trends early and creating investable products. The ETF industry now has 300-400 issuers versus ~30 when VanEck entered 19 years ago, but M&A consolidation has not materialized because fees are thin and major distribution platforms have already built in-house families.

### Nvidia Earnings and AI Innovation Supercycle [00:16:54]

Nvidia's data center revenue of $41 billion (up 56% YoY) came in with no China contribution. Analysts have largely caught up to growth, so EPS/revenue beat sizes are shrinking even as the absolute bar rises toward $100 billion per quarter. Jensen Huang's guidance cites $3-4 trillion in AI infrastructure spend by end of decade; AI-native startup revenues have grown from $2 billion to $20 billion in one year. AI is characterized as a winner-take-all scale technology, potentially driving MAG-7 to 60% of the S&P.

### Electricity Bull Market: Nuclear and Natural Gas [00:25:56]

Electricity prices have been rising well above CPI, driven by AI data center demand, and carry a disproportionate burden on low-income households (up to 10-20% of income). Jan identifies nuclear and natural gas as the preferred investable themes via VanEck's nuclear ETF, which is up roughly 45% in 2025. Natural gas companies lag despite demand because US supply is essentially unlimited, keeping prices capped.

### US Deficit, Gold, and Portfolio Hedges [00:33:29]

Jan argues the US deficit at ~6.5% of GDP is historically extreme and frames Bitcoin and gold as working hedges, noting central banks now hold more gold than US Treasuries for the first time since 1996. He warns of a Social Security selective default around 2033 (projected 20% benefit cut) absent bipartisan reform. Bessent is targeting a reduction to 5-5.5% of GDP through tariff revenue, which could allow debt to be grown out of.

### Private Equity: GP Stocks vs. LP Funds [00:42:32]

Analysis by VanEck's team showed that owning the publicly traded stocks of the top alternative asset managers returned $264 per $100 invested versus $113 in the Bloomberg PE index over three years. Jan launched the VanEck Alternative Asset Managers ETF (GPZ) ~4-5 months prior to the episode. He is bullish on private credit as having a structural moat because only a handful of managers can write billion-dollar checks for data centers or leveraged buyouts.

### Crypto, Stablecoins, and Tokenized Securities [00:51:29]

Post-2023, low-cost reliable blockchains (Solana, EVM-compatible chains) made crypto viable for institutional use. The stablecoin bill is cited as the third most important piece of US banking legislation, enabling tech companies to enter payments. ETH/EVM is expected to be the standard Wall Street builds on. Robinhood's tokenized securities initiative faced pushback from companies wanting to control their cap tables. Jan notes Bitcoin has outperformed the S&P 500 on every time frame and frames it as digital gold with limited supply.
