# Time to Get Bearish? | Animal Spirits 401

**Channel:** The Compound
**Published:** 2025-02-26
**URL:** https://youtube.com/watch?v=Eg_ZQa2zOZc
**Duration:** 53:09

## Summary

Episode 401 of Animal Spirits features Michael Batnick and Ben Carlson examining rising bearish sentiment in early 2025, driven by economist Neil Dutta's recession warning, Steve Cohen's cautious hedge fund outlook, and technical weakness in industrial stocks and homebuilders. A central theme is the extreme concentration of wealth and consumption among the top 10%, which the hosts discuss as both the engine of the current expansion and a structural fragility. The episode also touches on Berkshire Hathaway's record tax payments, Robinhood's transformation into a crypto-and-options platform, Bitcoin's pullback amid a bullish regulatory environment, and early AI use cases for book writing assistance.

## Key Takeaways

- Multiple respected market observers including Neil Dutta and Steve Cohen have turned cautious on the economy, citing monetary policy tightening, immigration slowdown, and DOGE-related austerity as headwinds—not just tariffs.
- The top 10% of earners now account for 50% of all US consumption (up from 36% three decades ago) and any sustained bear market or housing price correction could disproportionately impact economic activity.
- Consumer balance sheets remain relatively healthy with debt-service ratios and credit card delinquencies still below long-term averages, giving the economy a buffer even if conditions soften.
- Robinhood has effectively become a crypto and options platform, with equities generating less than 10% of transaction-based revenues, up 200% year-over-year in Q4.
- Bitcoin was approximately 18% off its highs despite bullish regulatory tailwinds including SEC dropping cases against Coinbase and Robinhood, illustrating divergence between fundamentals and price action.
- The wealth effect and housing market are tightly linked—mortgage spreads remain elevated, and hosts argue the Fed could narrow spreads by signaling intent to buy mortgage bonds.
- AI models like Claude and ChatGPT are proving useful for editorial feedback on long-form writing, though hosts note possible repetition in feedback across chapters.

## Detailed Notes

### Bearish Signals and Economic Slowdown Concerns [00:02:10]

Hosts note a wave of market participants turning cautious, with Neil Dutta of Renaissance Macro Research arguing that tariff uncertainty is an ex-post rationalization for a slowdown already underway. Key indicators flagged include rolling housing starts (1.67M to 1.37M peak-to-current), weakness in industrials and homebuilders, and rising quits rates near lows. Kevin Gordon of Schwab notes homebuilders have had their worst four-month relative performance since the 2008 housing crisis.

### Steve Cohen Turns Bearish; DOGE as Austerity [00:06:21]

Steve Cohen publicly stated he is 'pretty negative for the first time in a while,' citing tariffs as a tax, slowing immigration reducing labor force growth, and DOGE spending cuts as a form of austerity analogous to post-GFC European austerity policies. Torsten Slok context: 300,000 federal job cuts appear small against 160 million employed and 5 million monthly job changers, but contractor multiplier could push real impact toward 1 million.

### Wealth Concentration and Consumer Spending Dynamics [00:16:49]

Wall Street Journal data highlighted that the top 10% of earners now account for 50% of consumption (up from 36% three decades ago), hold two-thirds of all wealth, 87% of stocks, and 84% of private businesses. Between September 2023 and September 2024, highest earners increased spending 12% while working- and middle-class households saw spending drop. Hosts note this concentration means consumer health is strongly tied to equity and housing prices, not wages.

### Berkshire Hathaway Annual Letter and Tax Commentary [00:21:04]

Warren Buffett's annual letter disclosed $26.8 billion in 2024 IRS payments, equaling roughly 5% of all corporate taxes. Buffett contextualized the sum as $1 million every 20 minutes for the entire leap year and thanked the government, contrasting sharply with wealthy individuals who complain about tax burdens. Hosts use this to discuss the tackiness of ultra-wealthy tax grievances.

### Robinhood, Crypto, and Bitcoin Market Update [00:28:24]

Robinhood's Q4 transaction-based revenues rose 200% year-over-year to a record, with crypto and options comprising ~90% of that base and equities under 10%. Bitcoin was approximately 18-20% off its highs near $88,000 despite a bullish regulatory environment (SEC dropping Coinbase and Robinhood cases, deregulation signals). Hosts suggest this is a buying opportunity for existing Bitcoin holders but warn against chasing price at higher levels after FOMO.

### Housing Market, Mortgage Spreads, and Fed Policy [00:36:45]

US home values gained $2.5 trillion in 2024 to $49.7 trillion, with a 5.2% appreciation despite 7% mortgage rates. Millennials now account for over 20% of US home market value, rising 18.8% year-over-year. Hosts debate whether the Fed should buy mortgage-backed securities to compress elevated spreads (~150 bps over), which could lower mortgage rates and stimulate housing activity, though acknowledge this risks reigniting inflation.

### AI Use Cases and Recommendations [00:32:35]

Tyler Cowen's argument that human bottlenecks (e.g., FDA drug approval rates) will limit AI productivity gains is discussed approvingly. Batnick shares his use of ChatGPT and Claude for editorial feedback on book chapters, finding value in transitional and structural suggestions even if feedback patterns across chapters feel repetitive. Recommendations include Netflix's 'Zero Day,' Court documentary on the Paris Olympics, and White Lotus Season 3.
