# The Best Stocks in History | Animal Spirits 457

## Summary

In Animal Spirits episode 457, Michael Batnick and Ben Carlson discuss how financial markets appear to be constraining geopolitical decision-making, with the 'Trump put' keeping equities from selling off sharply despite Middle East conflict and rising oil prices. They review an updated Bessembinder study showing even greater stock market wealth concentration than previously documented, with just 46 firms accounting for half of all equity wealth creation through 2025. The hosts examine how AI is intensifying rather than reducing workloads according to a large-scale study, and cover trends in gold sell-offs, housing market stagnation, accidental landlords, crypto tokenization, and tax-aware investing strategies for wealthy clients.

## Key Takeaways

- Markets appear to be acting as a governor on policy, with major geopolitical announcements timed around market open and close, suggesting a 'Trump put' prevents deep sell-offs.
- The updated Bessembinder study shows stock wealth creation has become even more concentrated—46 firms now account for half of $91 trillion in total net wealth creation, reinforcing the case for diversified index investing.
- A large-scale analysis of 164,000 workers found AI tools more than doubled time spent on messaging and raised business management activity by 94%, contradicting the narrative that AI reduces workloads.
- Gold selling during market uncertainty reflects the 'ATM effect'—investors sell their best-performing asset to raise cash, not necessarily a fundamental change in gold's outlook.
- The housing market shows historically low pending home sales, creating a class of 'accidental landlords' who rent out unsold properties, with that share of rental listings at record highs in several cities.
- Tax-aware investing—through direct indexing, exchange funds, private placement life insurance, and deferred distribution strategies—is becoming an increasingly large business as baby boomers begin selling assets.
- Private credit's risk profile is often understated: even in 2008, the Cliffwater Direct Lending Index lost only 6.5%, making it hard to generate the kind of alarm the sector currently attracts.

## Detailed Notes

### Markets as Geopolitical Regulator and the Trump Put [00:02:37]

Batnick and Carlson argue that military announcements are now timed around market open and close, with markets acting as a check on policy. Despite Iran-related conflict, the S&P held gains of around 1% at lows after an initial spike of roughly 2.5%, reflecting investor confidence in the 'Trump put'—the expectation that the administration will act to avoid a market collapse. Forty percent of index constituents are in bear market territory even though the index level has not declined significantly.

### Oil Prices, Inflation Expectations, and the Fed [00:10:33]

Energy spending as a share of US household budgets has fallen from over 6% in the early 1980s to roughly 2%, giving households greater capacity to absorb higher gas prices. Investors shifted from pricing in three rate cuts in 2026 to pricing in a rate hike within three weeks, though the hosts argue rate hikes would be counterproductive in response to an exogenous supply shock. Pantheon Macro estimated that a $150/barrel oil scenario could push inflation to about 6% peak in Q2.

### Bessembinder Study Update: Extreme Concentration of Equity Returns [00:25:23]

The updated Bessembinder research covering through 2025 finds that 46 firms account for half of $91 trillion in cumulative net wealth creation in US equities, up from 89 firms and $43 trillion in the prior version. Nvidia leads all stocks with an annualized 37% return over its 20-plus-year period. The hosts note that many of the top long-run performers are companies most investors have never heard of, reinforcing the case for broad diversification rather than stock-picking.

### AI, Work Intensity, and Labor Displacement [00:41:26]

A Wall Street Journal analysis of 164,000 workers across 443 million hours found that AI adoption more than doubled time spent on email and messaging and raised use of business management tools by 94%. The hosts debate whether this reflects a fear-driven 'fake productivity boom' in which workers labor harder to avoid displacement, or whether output quality is meaningfully improving. Viewer email describes real displacement of marketing, estimating, and project coordination roles at small businesses.

### Gold Sell-Off, Microsoft Decline, and Crypto Developments [00:22:14]

Gold is down roughly 20% intraday at time of recording, which Carlson attributes to the ATM effect: investors sell their best-performing asset to raise cash during uncertainty. Gold ETF flows hit their worst four-day sell-off since 2013 according to Todd Sohn. Microsoft is down 31% from its peak and at its lowest since April 2025, with the hosts seeing it as an overreaction. The New York Stock Exchange announced a partnership with Securitize on tokenized securities, and hyper liquid launched a 'USA 500' index product for crypto-native investors.

### Housing Market, Accidental Landlords, and Real Estate Costs [00:32:55]

Pending home sales are at their lowest level on record, and Home Depot's stock has delivered 0% cumulative return since April 2021 despite $9 trillion in US home sales over that period. About 2.2% of rental listings on Zillow were previously for-sale listings as of November, reaching 4% in Houston, Denver, Austin, and Tampa—all at record highs. Average monthly new car payments reached $774 in January, up from $588 in January 2021, with 20% of new car buyers agreeing to over $1,000 per month.

### Tax-Aware Investing and Personal Finance [01:06:45]

Bloomberg's feature highlights a $1 trillion industry in tax-efficient strategies for wealthy investors including direct indexing, exchange funds, 351 conversions, box ETFs, and private placement life insurance. The hosts argue that as baby boomers sell assets, tax deferral strategies—not alpha generation—will be the defining service of wealth management. They also share a business lesson from a roofer who performs small jobs for free to generate word-of-mouth referrals, and link this to the value of immediate client responsiveness in professional services.
