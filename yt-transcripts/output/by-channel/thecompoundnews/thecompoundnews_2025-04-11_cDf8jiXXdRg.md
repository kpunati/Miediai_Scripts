# The 7 Best Dividend Stocks to Buy Now | TCAF 187

**Channel:** The Compound
**Published:** 2025-04-11
**URL:** https://youtube.com/watch?v=cDf8jiXXdRg
**Duration:** 91:16

## Summary

In episode 187 of The Compound and Friends, Josh Brown and Michael Batnick are joined by Jenny Van Leeuwen Harrington, CEO of Gilman Hill Asset Management, to dissect the dramatic April 2025 tariff pause and its market impact. The conversation covers the bond market's role in forcing Trump's hand, insider-trading concerns around the tariff reversal, and the behavioral pitfalls investors face in volatile markets. Jenny then presents seven high-yield dividend stocks she views as tariff- and recession-resilient, and discusses her book on dividend income investing.

## Key Takeaways

- The reciprocal tariff pause came after only 13 hours, driven by a combination of spiking Treasury yields, CEO pressure, and political self-preservation instinct rather than a single planned strategy.
- The bond market's sharp selloff was the proximate force Trump responded to, given his background in real estate and sensitivity to borrowing costs.
- Stocks historically bottom well before earnings trough, making it nearly impossible to time re-entry by waiting for earnings clarity.
- Selling during a correction locks in permanent damage to long-term returns; Jenny's real-world client example showed an 8%+ annualized performance gap between those who stayed invested versus those who sold in March 2020.
- Jenny's dividend strategy targets a portfolio yield of 5% or better, focusing on US-revenue-dominant companies with long dividend payment histories and low tariff exposure.
- All seven recommended dividend stocks share 100% or near-100% US revenues, making them highly resilient to the current trade-war environment.
- Elevated market volatility (fourth most volatile week in 60 years) does not necessarily predict near-term returns, though one-year forward returns after large down days have historically been positive about 95% of the time.

## Detailed Notes

### Tariff Pause and Market Reaction [00:11:34]

Reciprocal tariffs went live at midnight but were paused just 13 hours later after the bond market sold off sharply overnight. Hosts and Jenny discuss the combination of factors — bond market stress, CEO interventions (Jamie Dimon on Fox Business), and Bessant's advice — that converged to produce Trump's reversal. The resulting S&P rally was the third-largest single-day gain since 1990.

### Bond Market Power and Fiscal Impact [00:30:08]

The panel emphasizes that the 10-year Treasury yield spiked dramatically, illustrating that the bond market remains a binding constraint on policy. Jenny notes that each basis point of yield increase translates to roughly $5 billion in additional annual interest payments, meaning the tariff-induced yield spike was genuinely costly in fiscal terms.

### Insider Trading Concerns and Market Integrity [00:44:37]

The hosts flag unusual options volume in leveraged QQQ ETFs ahead of Trump's 'This is a great time to buy' tweet, raising questions about whether connected parties had advance knowledge. The panel argues the episode breeds distrust regardless of legality, and notes that making the market look like a rigged game show has real costs to market integrity.

### Earnings Outlook and Recession Risk [00:52:13]

S&P 500 2025 EPS consensus has declined from $280 to $269 and may fall further toward Goldman's estimate of $248-$250. The group discusses how CEO guidance withdrawals will be the next major market risk, and how stocks typically bottom 9 months before earnings trough — meaning waiting for earnings confirmation means missing the recovery.

### Investor Behavior and the Case for Staying Invested [01:02:55]

Jenny shares a real client example where the investor who panic-sold in March 2020 achieved only 1% annualized return versus 9%+ for the investor who stayed invested, over a five-year period. The panel agrees the 10 best market days almost always follow the worst days, making market timing effectively impossible.

### Seven Dividend Stock Picks [01:10:02]

Jenny presents Bristol-Myers Squibb (4.7% yield, 92-year dividend history, strong free cash flow), Conagra Brands (5.6% yield, 49-year dividend history, healthier food portfolio), Clearway Energy (7% yield, wind/solar/battery, zero tariff exposure), Dominion Energy (5% yield, utility, 92-year dividend history), Ryman Hospitality (corporate conference REIT, 67% corporate revenues, recession-resilient cancellation fees), Sabra Health Care REIT (7.3% yield, skilled nursing, zero tariff risk), and Verizon (6.4% yield, though Josh Brown expresses skepticism about total return potential).

### Dividend Investing Philosophy and Book [01:18:13]

Jenny explains her equity-income strategy: a 5%+ portfolio yield target, ~36 concentrated holdings, and the behavioral benefit of income payments helping clients stay invested during downturns. She discusses her new book 'Dividend Investing: Dependable Income to Navigate All Market Environments' and the Charlie Ellis 'ocean liner' analogy — building a portfolio large enough that market tides (bull or bear) don't matter to your financial security.
