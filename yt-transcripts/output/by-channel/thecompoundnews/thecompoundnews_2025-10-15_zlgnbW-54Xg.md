# Did the Market Just Top? | Animal Spirits 434

**Channel:** The Compound
**Published:** 2025-10-15
**URL:** https://youtube.com/watch?v=zlgnbW-54Xg
**Duration:** 71:49

## Summary

Animal Spirits episode 434, hosted by Michael Batnick and Ben Carlson, opens with a debate on whether the stock market has topped following a sharp Friday selloff, with both hosts concluding that earnings trends — not elevated valuations — are the only reliable indicator. The episode covers a wide range of topics: gold's 50% 2025 rally, the growing participation of lower-income Americans in equity markets, a record crypto liquidation driven by extreme leverage, skepticism about cash-on-sidelines narratives, and the nuances of private credit as an asset class. The hosts also touch on Delta Air Lines earnings, Hollywood's box office struggles, and book recommendations including Dan Wang's 'Breakneck.'

## Key Takeaways

- Earnings versus estimates is the single most reliable indicator for identifying market tops — valuation alone is insufficient.
- Market concentration at the top 10% of US stocks is at historic highs, but historically concentration peaks in bull markets, not before collapses.
- Gold's 2025 rally is driven by central bank buying and geopolitical uncertainty, not the dollar-collapse thesis gold bugs predict.
- The October 2025 crypto liquidation, estimated at $19B+, was the largest ever and was enabled by 50x leverage on perpetual futures — a structural risk.
- Lower-income and younger Americans are investing in equities at record rates, partially substituting stock market participation for unaffordable home ownership.
- Cash-on-sidelines narratives are misleading because money market balances as a percentage of total equity market value have actually declined as stocks have risen.
- Private credit is not viewed by the hosts as a systemic bubble, but returns are expected to compress as the market grows.

## Detailed Notes

### Can Anyone Call the Top? Earnings as the True Signal [00:00:04]

Batnick argues that although many try to call the market top, nobody can reliably do so, and repeat callers deserve no credit. Ben grounds the discussion by showing that bear markets have historically occurred when projected earnings dramatically overshoot actual earnings — as in the dot-com bust and GFC. The hosts conclude that forward PE alone is insufficient; what matters is whether earnings ultimately disappoint.

### Market Concentration and Valuation Context [00:00:04]

The top 10% of US stocks by market cap now represent roughly 80% of total market cap, the highest ever recorded. A Goldman Sachs comparison shows the current 24-month forward PE of ~27x is well below 52x at the dot-com peak and 67x for Japan's bubble. Normalized for today's superior profit margins, the forward PE is near its 20-year average, supporting the argument that this is not an 80% washout scenario.

### Gold Rally, Central Banks, and Pricing Fallacies [00:00:04]

Gold is up roughly 50% in 2025 — the first decade where both gold and equities have risen strongly simultaneously. The hosts dismiss pricing equities in gold as meaningless, arguing the dollar is the only practical unit. They attribute gold's rise primarily to central bank demand and macro uncertainty rather than dollar collapse theses held by gold bugs.

### Cash on the Sidelines Fallacy and Household Balance Sheets [00:00:04]

Eric Balchunas-style analysis shows that US money market fund assets as a percentage of the S&P 500 are actually declining, debunking the 'dry powder' narrative. Separately, Bank of America data shows households have one dollar in cash for every dollar of debt — the most deleveraged since the early 1990s — giving buffer against a potential downturn.

### Record Crypto Liquidation: Leverage Risk Laid Bare [00:00:04]

A single down day in equities triggered roughly $19-20B in crypto liquidations, the largest in history — dwarfing FTX's $2B event. Brian Armstrong's September announcement raising Coinbase international perpetual futures leverage from 20x to 50x is cited as a structural enabler. The hosts note altcoins such as Ethereum and Solana fell 20%+ intraday while Bitcoin held above $110,000.

### Retail Participation, Wealth Inequality, and Private Markets [00:00:04]

A Wall Street Journal/BlackRock survey shows 54% of Americans earning $30k-$80k now hold taxable investment accounts, with half entering in the last 5 years. JP Morgan data shows 37% of 25-year-olds used investment accounts in 2024, up from 6% in 2015. On private credit, BlackRock reported $8B in inflows; hosts are skeptical of bubble claims but expect return compression. US private company revenues ($40T) now exceed public company revenues ($35T).

### Earnings Season Preview, Delta, and Miscellaneous [00:00:04]

Batnick highlights Delta's earnings as a proxy for corporate health — Delta now represents 60% of airline industry profits and premium cabin revenue is approaching main cabin revenue. The hosts recommend Dan Wang's 'Breakneck' (lawyers vs. engineers framing of US vs. China) and Doris Kearns Goodwin's 'Team of Rivals.' Hollywood's struggles are noted: Tron cost $180M to make but earned only $33M opening weekend.
