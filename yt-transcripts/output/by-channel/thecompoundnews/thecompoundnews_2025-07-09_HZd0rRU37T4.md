# A 25 Year Bull Market | Animal Spirits 420

**Channel:** The Compound
**Published:** 2025-07-09
**URL:** https://youtube.com/watch?v=HZd0rRU37T4
**Duration:** 66:59

## Summary

In episode 420 of Animal Spirits, Michael Batnick and Ben Carlson analyze the stock market's record-speed recovery to all-time highs and discuss the plausibility of a 25-year secular bull market underpinned by AI. They explore the current deregulation wave—drawing an analogy to motorcycle riders without helmets—and its risks alongside its benefits for investors. The episode also covers the weakening dollar, a deteriorating job market for college graduates, the US housing market's supply-demand imbalance, Vanguard's push into private markets, and a range of personal finance pressures including record car payments and rising healthcare costs.

## Key Takeaways

- The 2025 stock market correction and recovery was the fastest on record following a 15%+ decline, driven partly by tariff policy reversal rather than fundamental recession dynamics.
- Hosts argue the US may be in the early stages of a 25-year secular bull market analogous to the post-WWII 1943–1966 cycle, contingent on continued AI-driven growth.
- The current deregulation cycle is expected to produce both innovative investor-friendly products and significant retail investor blowups, similar to the pre-GFC era.
- The US dollar's 10% year-to-date decline returns it to early-2024 levels, representing a round-trip from the Trump-policy spike, but the dollar's 88% share of FX transactions keeps it dominant.
- Entry-level white-collar jobs in accounting, finance, and marketing are disappearing at a faster rate than any prior cycle, which a career development professional describes as worse than COVID-era conditions.
- Vanguard's entry into private markets via a Harborvest partnership is expected to introduce fee pressure to an industry accustomed to 2-and-20 structures.
- One in five new car buyers is committing to payments of $1,000 or more per month, illustrating broader consumer affordability stress despite a surface-level strong economy.

## Detailed Notes

### Stock Market Recovery and Bull Market Thesis [00:02:16]

The S&P 500 returned to all-time highs just months after a 19% decline triggered by tariff announcements. Batnick presents a scatter plot showing symmetry between the length of declines and recoveries, noting 2020 and 2025 both showed V-shaped patterns driven by policy reversal rather than fundamental recessions. Hosts posit the US could be in year 15 of a 25-year bull market comparable to the 1943–1966 period, with AI as the key enabling catalyst.

### Breadth and Mag 7 Divergence [00:06:34]

Market breadth reached its widest point of the year with 7% of Russell 3000 members hitting 52-week highs. Financials and industrials are breaking out, indicating participation beyond mega-cap tech. Within the Mag 7, Apple, Tesla, and Alphabet are lagging while Meta, Microsoft, and Nvidia lead, suggesting the group is fracturing.

### Deregulation Cycle and New Financial Products [00:12:30]

Carlson uses a motorcycle-without-helmet analogy to describe the current deregulation pendulum swinging after post-GFC over-regulation. Robinhood's tokenization of private company securities and the potential inclusion of private equity in 401(k)s are flagged as products that will benefit some investors and harm others. Yield-max ETFs selling covered calls on names like DraftKings are cited as examples of retail products with hidden risks.

### Dollar, Deficits, and Fiscal Policy [00:18:29]

The dollar is down 10% year-to-date, but has essentially round-tripped to early-2024 levels after a post-election spike. The dollar represents 88% of global FX transaction volume, making near-term reserve currency displacement implausible. Hosts criticize recurring deficit-hawk rhetoric, noting that taxes have consistently fallen throughout their adult lifetimes, and question the political will to raise taxes.

### Labor Market for College Graduates and AI Displacement [00:22:26]

A viewer email from a career development professional states the job market for recent college grads—particularly in accounting, finance, and marketing—is the worst in over a decade, exceeding COVID-era difficulty. Two US military branches met recruiting goals three months early with 20% of recruits holding college degrees, cited as a recession signal by the emailer. Batnick counters that the labor market may simply be normalizing from an unusually hot post-COVID peak.

### Housing Market Dynamics [00:37:01]

Mortgage rates have remained in a 6–8% range since late 2022, trapping homeowners with low locked-in rates and suppressing inventory. Cape Coral, Florida, is described as the worst housing market in the US, with prices down 11% from peak—but that peak itself was up 75% during the pandemic. Logan Mosambi's view is that a recession is not the fix because falling rates would immediately swamp still-insufficient inventory with demand.

### Private Markets and Vanguard's Entry [00:44:49]

The Wall Street Journal reported Vanguard's move into private markets via a partnership with Harborvest, struck in 2020. Hosts argue this is net positive for investors because Vanguard's fee-compression track record will force the private equity industry toward lower fees. Verdad Capital's analysis shows public S&P 500 companies have superior EBITDA margins, interest coverage, and free cash flow versus private equity holdings, raising questions about whether the PE premium is simply compensation for leverage and illiquidity.

### Personal Finance: Car Payments, Quality of Life, and Retirement Savings [00:49:29]

Edmunds Q2 data shows 19.3% of new car buyers are committing to $1,000+/month payments, an all-time high, with an average payment of $756. Separately, Gen Z is saving for retirement at higher rates than millennials did at the same age, with more Gen Z women saving than men, who prefer brokerage accounts. A historical quality-of-life retrospective highlights that firewood was 28% of GDP in 1776, and even the 1980s were characterized by pervasive financial anxiety—contrasting with today's abundance mindset.
