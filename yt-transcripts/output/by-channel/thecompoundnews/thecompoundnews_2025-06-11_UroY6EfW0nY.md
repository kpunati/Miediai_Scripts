# A Bubble Is Coming | Animal Spirits 416

**Channel:** The Compound
**Published:** 2025-06-11
**URL:** https://youtube.com/watch?v=UroY6EfW0nY
**Duration:** 73:29

## Summary

In Animal Spirits episode 416, Michael Batnick and Ben Carlson make the case that June 2025 represents the most interesting investing environment of their careers, driven by the convergence of AI disruption, tariff uncertainty, retail investor participation, and geopolitical shifts. The hosts predict a coming AI bubble is historically inevitable — no life-altering technology has escaped one — while debating how to invest responsibly given already-elevated valuations in names like Palantir. They also cover the argument for Fed rate cuts anchored in housing market weakness, the maturation of crypto into traditional finance via ETFs, and growing scrutiny of private market practices. Earnings transcript quotes from Marriott, Tractor Supply, and Dollar General paint a bifurcated consumer picture.

## Key Takeaways

- The hosts argue 2025 is the most interesting investing environment they have lived through, citing AI, tariffs, retail participation, Europe outperformance, and crypto all occurring simultaneously.
- A bubble in AI-related assets is viewed as historically inevitable; the question is whether markets can achieve the productivity handoff without a.com-style collapse.
- The simplest AI investment strategy, per Ben Carlson, is owning the S&P 500 or Nasdaq-100, as roughly 35% of the index directly benefits from AI.
- Bitcoin and crypto are now traditional finance: IBIT alone holds over $70 billion in assets, and registered investment advisors have become the largest holder class of spot Bitcoin ETFs.
- The Fed should cut rates primarily because housing activity has fallen to great-financial-crisis lows, and AI is structurally deflationary.
- Private market scrutiny is rising, with concerns about secondary fund mark-up practices, plunging distributions to 2021-vintage investors, and a wave of new interval fund launches targeting retail advisors.
- Consumer health is bifurcated: travel and experiences remain resilient while lower-income households face real stress, though the lower-income cohort represents only about two percentage points of S&P earnings.

## Detailed Notes

### Most Interesting Market Environment and Retail Investor Growth [00:00:04]

Batnick argues that the convergence of AI, tariffs, retail investor growth, the post-COVID spending shift, Fed uncertainty, and Europe's rise makes 2025 uniquely interesting. Stock ownership is near an all-time high at 62% of US households, up from 52% in 2016, and 401k savings rates hit a record 8% in Q1 2025 per Fidelity. Robinhood now accounts for 13 million first-time brokerage accounts.

### Europe Outperformance and Market Drawdown Dynamics [00:00:04]

Jeffrey Kleintop noted Europe has outperformed US stocks by 30 percentage points since October 2022, a result that would have seemed implausible to both hosts. The hosts debate whether downturns are structurally shorter and more frequent now, with Batnick suggesting 75% of future corrections may resolve more quickly due to faster information processing and corporate agility.

### AI Bubble Thesis and Investment Implications [00:00:04]

Batnick reviews Mary Meeker's 340-slide AI trend report, highlighting AI companies hitting $5M ARR in 24 months versus 37 months for SaaS companies. CoreWeave IPO'd at $40 and is now at $160; Palantir trades at 105 times price-to-sales. Both hosts agree a bubble is coming — history shows no major technology escapes one — and the simplest approach is owning the broad index. The deeper risk is the hyperscalers' surging capex and whether free cash flow margins can recover.

### Federal Reserve, Housing, and Tariffs [00:00:04]

Batnick makes the case for Fed rate cuts, citing the housing market at great-financial-crisis lows in existing home sales and a weakening homebuilder confidence index. The EU has already cut rates eight times. New York Fed surveys show roughly half of service firms plan to pass 100% of tariff costs to consumers. Neil Dutta is quoted warning that slowdowns do not always self-correct and the longer the Fed waits, the more it will need to cut.

### Crypto as Traditional Finance [00:00:04]

Bitcoin hit all-time highs above $110,000 with little retail fanfare. BlackRock's IBIT reached over $70 billion in assets in 341 days — compared to 5,700 days for GLD — and set a monthly flow record in May. Registered investment advisors are now the largest holder class of spot Bitcoin ETFs per 13F filings. The hosts argue crypto is now fully integrated into traditional finance and the original 'wait for institutions' narrative has been fulfilled.

### Private Markets, Consumer Health, and Earnings Transcripts [00:00:04]

Private market scrutiny is rising: Starwood Property Fund had $850 million in withdrawal requests and sold $1.6 billion in assets to meet redemptions. Bill Gurley warns an all-private world will be messier with more opacity. Earnings transcripts show bifurcated consumers: Marriott reports RevPAR up over 4% despite 52-year lows in consumer confidence as travel becomes a perceived necessity; Dollar General reports 25% of core customers have less income than a year ago and 60% expect to sacrifice necessities.
