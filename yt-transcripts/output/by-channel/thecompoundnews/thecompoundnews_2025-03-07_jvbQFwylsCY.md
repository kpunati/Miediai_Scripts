# Tom Lee to the Rescue | TCAF 181

**Channel:** The Compound
**Published:** 2025-03-07
**URL:** https://youtube.com/watch?v=jvbQFwylsCY
**Duration:** 87:47

## Summary

In TCAF episode 181, Tom Lee of Fundstrat joins Josh Brown and Michael Batnick to analyze the market turbulence triggered by the Trump administration's tariffs on Canada, Mexico, and China in early March 2025. Lee draws parallels to Brexit and the 2018 tariff episode, arguing the selloff is creating a buying opportunity rather than signaling a bear market, provided the Federal Reserve and White House both retain their implicit 'puts.' He also covers his Granny Shots ETF strategy, the demographic basis for his long-term bull market thesis, and makes the case for a US Bitcoin strategic reserve as a logical extension of America's digital asset holdings.

## Key Takeaways

- Lee views the tariff-driven correction as analogous to Brexit — disruptive short-term but unlikely to permanently impair the economy or end the bull market.
- Three overlapping 'puts' — the Fed, the White House, and a potential sovereign wealth fund — provide downside support and reduce the probability of a full bear market.
- VIX term structure backwardation (one-month contract above four-month) historically signals short-term panic and a tactical buying opportunity.
- The Granny Shots ETF strategy selects roughly 35 equal-weighted stocks tied to at least two long-duration secular themes (AI, cybersecurity, Millennial spending, Fed cycles), rebalanced quarterly.
- Lee's demographic model, anchored to 20-year birth cohort peaks, puts the next major secular top around 2038, making current volatility a mid-cycle episode.
- A US Bitcoin strategic reserve is defended by analogy to the Strategic Petroleum Reserve — the government could hold Bitcoin to stabilize a highly speculated commodity that underpins an emerging financial system.
- Rapid immigration restriction removes a source of labor supply that previously fed leisure and hospitality growth, but Lee argues net illegal crossings were not generating high employment and the economic effect may be neutral to mildly positive.

## Detailed Notes

### Tariffs, Trade War Framing, and Market Impact [00:13:51]

Lee compares the tariff shock to Brexit, noting the UK market recovered fully and served as a major buying opportunity. He distinguishes between a two-quarter earnings blip (bullish setup) and a permanent regime shift (bearish), leaning strongly toward the former. The S&P was down roughly 6.5% and NASDAQ roughly 10% from highs at time of recording — described as 'orderly' — though the average stock was reportedly down 30% from all-time highs.

### Federal Reserve Dynamics and Recession Risk [00:24:24]

Rate cut odds had risen to a 30% probability of four cuts in 2025. Lee notes the Fed is in a structurally different position than 2018 when it was actively hiking; today it has significant cushion to cut if needed. The Atlanta Fed's GDP Now reading had dropped from positive 2.8% to negative 4%, and a weak jobs print would, in Lee's view, cause a short panic followed by a rally as the Fed put re-engaged.

### Sentiment Indicators and Tactical Signals [00:39:01]

The AAII bearish sentiment reading hit 55%, an extreme rarely seen even during the pandemic, despite the S&P being only modestly off highs. Lee's VIX term structure indicator — one-month contract inverted above four-month — historically marks short-term bottoms and was approaching that signal. JP Morgan data showed a record single-hour retail outflow of $1.2 billion, and the put-call ratio had spiked.

### Granny Shots ETF Strategy [01:08:23]

The Granny Shots ETF holds approximately 35 stocks that appear in at least two of Fundstrat's long-duration thematic buckets (AI, cybersecurity, Millennial spending, PMI seasonality, Fed cycles). It is equal-weighted and rebalanced quarterly. The fund had grown to nearly a billion dollars in assets since launch. Lee distributes a weekly public video to around 35,000 subscribers to help investors contextualize holdings during volatile periods. An international version was under consideration.

### Bitcoin Strategic Reserve and Crypto Outlook [01:16:49]

Lee argues the US should build a Bitcoin strategic reserve by analogy to the Strategic Petroleum Reserve — owning roughly 1 million Bitcoin (adding ~800,000 to the 200,000 already confiscated) through the Exchange Stabilization Fund. He projects Bitcoin could reach $1 million per coin if Millennial allocation flows bring crypto market cap to parity with gold's approximately $21 trillion. The reserve thesis rests on Bitcoin's role as a settlement layer and the likely extreme trading volumes as banks adopt it.

### Bull Market Longevity and Year-Three Seasonality [00:56:50]

Lee's demographic model maps 20-year birth cohort peaks to prior secular market tops (1929, 1974, 1999, 2018) and projects the next major top around 2038. Separately, after back-to-back 20%-plus years the second half of year three historically underperforms, which is consistent with current conditions but does not signal a secular bear market.
