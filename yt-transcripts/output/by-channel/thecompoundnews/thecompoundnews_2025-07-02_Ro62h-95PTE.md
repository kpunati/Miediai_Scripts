# The Top 10 Risks to the Stock Market | Animal Spirits 419

**Channel:** The Compound
**Published:** 2025-07-02
**URL:** https://youtube.com/watch?v=Ro62h-95PTE
**Duration:** 69:38

## Summary

In episode 419 of Animal Spirits, Michael Batnick and Ben Carlson survey the landscape of financial markets in mid-2025, framing discussion around the top risks that could end the bull market. Key themes include the possibility of an AI bubble inflating and eventually bursting, the historically weak US dollar, and an emerging vulnerability in housing. The hosts also celebrate the structural tailwinds from automatic 401(k) enrollment, warn about yield-chasing ETF products, and debate whether AI will eventually displace financial advisors.

## Key Takeaways

- The consensus top risk to the bull market is an AI bubble bursting; hosts believe there is a greater-than-50% chance of an AI bubble forming.
- The US dollar posted its worst first-half performance since 1973, which partly explains significant international equity outperformance versus the US so far in 2025.
- Automatic 401(k) enrollment has expanded dramatically — 61% of plans now auto-enroll, driving record savings participation and providing a structural equity demand tailwind.
- High-yield option-income ETFs offering payouts as high as 100%+ are surging in popularity but hosts warn investors that yield is not the same as total return.
- Housing prices are 68 basis points off all-time highs and hosts see the first credible case for a modest correction, though a slowing economy and lower mortgage rates would quickly reset demand.
- Hosts dismiss the idea that institutional investors are wholesale dumping private equity onto retail; survey data shows only 6% of institutions plan to decrease alt allocations.
- AI is unlikely to replace financial advisors in the near term but will significantly enhance DIY investing tools, much as the internet did.

## Detailed Notes

### Bull Market Risks and the Death of the Permabear [00:01:51]

Hosts identify the top consensus risk as an AI bubble bursting, followed by a potential debt or dollar crisis. They argue that after years of failed doom predictions, permabears have lost credibility and are now quickly dunked on when they resurface. The Liberation Day tariff shock in early April caused a brief 20% drawdown but the market recovered to all-time highs, which hosts view as a healthy release of excess speculation.

### International Outperformance and Dollar Weakness [00:12:00]

More than 50% of ACWI constituents hit new highs — the best reading in over a decade. International developed markets (EPHA) are up roughly 20% versus the S&P 500 at ~6%, the widest outperformance since 2006. The dollar's worst first-half since 1973 is a major driver, and value investing is working well outside the US while US markets remain dominated by mega-cap tech growth.

### Automatic Investing and the 401(k) Revolution [00:16:39]

Vanguard data shows 61% of plans now auto-enroll participants (up from 10% in 2006), with 94% participation rates and two-thirds automatically increasing deferrals annually. The average account holds 80% equities and median holds 89%, creating a durable structural bid for equities. Hosts argue this effectively solves the retirement savings crisis for younger generations.

### Yield-Chasing ETFs and Income Illusions [00:23:31]

A Wall Street Journal article highlighted nearly a dozen ETFs offering payouts over 100% by writing options on single volatile stocks, attracting $6.4 billion in new inflows. Hosts warn that yield is not total return and that if the underlying securities fall, income disappears too. They use the historical example of mortgage REITs with 15% yields that delivered negative total returns.

### AI's Impact on the Economy and Financial Advice [00:30:14]

Hosts explore Peter Thiel's view that AI is roughly on the scale of the internet — adding perhaps 1% to GDP over 10-15 years — rather than a total economic transformation. The debate on AI replacing financial advisors concludes that AI will help DIY investors and eventually blur the lines of human advice, but near-term replacement is unlikely given empathy, personalization, and regulatory barriers. Robo-advisors serve as a precedent: they took assets and forced competition but did not eliminate advisors.

### Housing Market and Alternative Investments [00:39:27]

Case-Shiller national home price index is 68 basis points off its highs, and hosts see the first credible setup for a 3-5% correction given sustained 6.5-7% mortgage rates. A slowing economy could actually help housing by bringing mortgage rates down. On alternatives, 80% of alt managers plan retail product launches and institutional surveys show only 6% plan to reduce alt allocations — hosts push back on the narrative that institutions are dumping private equity onto retail.
