# Investing Isn’t Supposed to Be Fun | Animal Spirits 461

**Channel:** The Compound
**Published:** 2026-04-22
**URL:** https://youtube.com/watch?v=iTflH_E8GLw
**Duration:** 60:32

## Summary

Animal Spirits episode 461 presents Michael Batnick and Ben Carlson's case that the stock market's 2026 recovery is entirely rational, driven by accelerating forward earnings per share estimates rather than government intervention or irrational exuberance. The hosts contextualize the speed of the recent rally against historical bull markets, argue that buy-and-hold investing wins by default despite being psychologically difficult, and observe that consumer spending and labor markets remain resilient. Side topics include Austin's housing supply success story, Hyperliquid's remarkable crypto metrics, the explosion in prediction market volume, and AI productivity trends.

## Key Takeaways

- Markets pricing in earnings growth rather than macro narratives is the Occam's razor explanation for the 2026 rally, per Batnick.
- The S&P 500 made new all-time highs within 11 days of a 5-10% decline, a record not seen since 1928.
- Buy-and-hold beats intuition-based trading for the vast majority of investors, even though the process is emotionally grueling.
- Consumer resilience is supported by a decade of asset price appreciation in stocks and real estate providing a margin of safety.
- Austin's housing reform — adding 30% more units — drove rents 15% lower even with continued population growth, illustrating supply-side solutions.
- Hyperliquid's $900M+ revenue with 11 employees represents a potential proof-of-concept for decentralized finance infrastructure.
- Prediction market volume surged from ~$500M to ~$6B in about a year, driven primarily by sports betting rather than financial markets.

## Detailed Notes

### Why the Stock Market Makes Sense [00:00:04]

Batnick pushes back on a New York Times op-ed arguing markets are irrationally ignoring risk. He contends markets correctly anticipated vaccine rollout, transitory inflation, contained geopolitical risks, and AI's earnings impact. The key data point is accelerating 12-month forward EPS estimates, which Batnick calls the Occam's razor explanation for why prices are rising.

### Speed and Scale of the Bull Market Rally [00:00:04]

Bespoke data shows the S&P made new all-time highs in 11 days or fewer after a 5-10% decline, a historical first since 1928. The current bull market (since 2013) has accumulated nearly 450 all-time highs, approaching the 505 recorded during the entire 1980-2000 bull run. The recent move was described as unusual — stairs down, elevator up — reversing the typical pattern.

### Buy-and-Hold Philosophy and Investor Behavior [00:00:04]

Carlson previews a book framing buy-and-hold as 'the worst form of investing except all the others,' echoing Churchill on democracy. Batnick's rule: when the market does something and most people say it makes no sense, assume the people are wrong. Both hosts acknowledge buy-and-hold is emotionally painful in real time even when it works in hindsight.

### Economy and Consumer Spending [00:00:04]

A new inflation equilibrium around 3-3.5% (vs. the pre-pandemic 2% target) has emerged, accompanied by higher wage and economic growth. Bank of America CEO Brian Moynihan cited $4.5T in annual consumer spending, up 5% year-over-year through Q1 2026. Bank of America's loan book shows consumer credit card and home equity loan exposure down sharply since 2009, suggesting healthier household balance sheets.

### Austin Housing as a Policy Template [00:00:04]

Pew research highlighted Austin's 2015 zoning reforms that encouraged rental development. Adding 30% more housing units caused rents to fall 15% and drop below the national median, even as population grew by 18,000. Hosts argue this is the clear answer to housing affordability and question why other municipalities have not followed suit.

### Crypto, Prediction Markets, and AI [00:00:04]

Hyperliquid — an 11-person decentralized exchange in Singapore with $900M+ revenue and a $10B market cap — is presented as a potential proof-of-concept for crypto delivering real financial infrastructure. Prediction market volume on Polymarket and Kalshi surged from roughly $500M to $6B in about a year, almost entirely driven by sports betting rather than financial markets. On AI, Morgan Stanley data suggests a productivity boom is beginning, mirroring the internet's impact on output per hour.
