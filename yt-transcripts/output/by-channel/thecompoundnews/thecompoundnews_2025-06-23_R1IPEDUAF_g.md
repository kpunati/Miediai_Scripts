# Live to a Hundred Years Old, Allocate 40% to Bitcoin | LFTC

**Channel:** The Compound
**Published:** 2025-06-23
**URL:** https://youtube.com/watch?v=R1IPEDUAF_g
**Duration:** 61:15

## Summary

In this 61-minute episode of Live from The Compound, host Josh Brown interviews Ric Edelman — founder of Edelman Financial Engines and DACFP — on the release of his white paper arguing that the 60/40 portfolio is obsolete and that crypto allocations of 10-40% are now appropriate. Edelman grounds his argument in three pillars: crypto's maturation via ETF adoption, regulatory clarity, and institutional acceptance; a looming longevity revolution driven by biotech and AI that will require heavier equity exposure for decades longer; and the rapid growth of tokenization and stablecoins as the next wave of financial infrastructure. The conversation also examines the political shift that made crypto a Republican issue, the fiduciary obligations of advisers to at least discuss digital assets with clients, and the emerging competitive dynamics among stablecoin issuers including Circle, Tether, major banks, Amazon, and Walmart.

## Key Takeaways

- Edelman argues conservative clients should hold 10% crypto, moderate clients 25%, and aggressive clients 40%, directly replacing part of the traditional equity sleeve.
- A longevity revolution driven by AI, biotech, and genomics means many investors alive in 2030 may live to 100, requiring far longer equity-heavy portfolios than current 60/40 models assume.
- Tokenization is projected to grow into a $16 trillion market by 2030, with BlackRock, Franklin Templeton, McKinsey, and BCG all validating the thesis; it could eventually replace ETFs.
- Stablecoins strengthen rather than compete with the US dollar by pegging to it and buying treasuries; the Genius Act is the first crypto-specific US legislation and will require US-based issuers to conform to disclosure standards.
- Financial advisers are caught between clients who own Bitcoin personally and compliance officers who will not approve recommendations, creating a fiduciary gap Edelman says must be resolved through informed disclosure.
- Crypto now represents roughly 3% of total US wealth; advisers who hold zero exposure are effectively making an active short decision rather than a passive one.
- Ric Edelman first invested in Bitcoin at approximately $700 and has never sold; he founded DACFP in 2015 to educate financial professionals on digital assets.

## Detailed Notes

### Edelman's Background and Bitcoin Journey [00:00:17]

Ric Edelman was introduced to Bitcoin in 2012 and began investing around $700 per coin in 2013. He founded DACFP (Digital Assets Council of Financial Professionals) in 2015 as a crypto education company for financial advisers, not as an investment vehicle. He emphasizes that early skepticism was reasonable given regulatory uncertainty, high volatility, and lack of institutional infrastructure, but conditions have materially changed.

### Political Shift and Regulatory Transformation [00:00:17]

The episode traces how crypto shifted from a bipartisan issue to a strongly Republican one, driven by $200 million in pro-crypto PAC money and 60 million US adult Bitcoin holders who viewed Biden-era enforcement as a threat to their assets. The Trump administration reversed Biden-era prohibitions, replaced Gary Gensler at the SEC, and installed strong crypto supporters across Treasury, Labor, Commerce, CFTC, FDIC, and OCC. Pro-crypto candidates won 57 of 59 contested Congressional races where crypto was a major issue.

### The White Paper: Death of 60/40 and Recommended Allocations [00:00:17]

Edelman's white paper argues the 60/40 model is obsolete on longevity grounds: 90% of everyone in history who ever reached age 90 is alive today, and if current trends hold, anyone alive in 2030 is likely to reach 100. This requires heavy equity exposure well into the 80s, not a bond-heavy glide path. Within that expanded equity sleeve, Edelman recommends 10% crypto for conservative investors, 25% for moderate, and 40% for aggressive, sourced directly from stocks. His illustration shows that even a 25% Bitcoin allocation going to zero still leaves a portfolio slightly above the starting value after 5 years, while upside is dramatically higher.

### Tokenization as the Next Wave [00:00:17]

Edelman projects a $16 trillion tokenization market by 2030 based on McKinsey and BCG estimates. He argues tokenization will replace ETFs by enabling 24/7 frictionless trading, fractional ownership of illiquid assets (real estate, art, exotic cars, individual homes), and elimination of intermediaries. He cites Franklin Templeton and BlackRock as leaders, and notes Larry Fink's shareholder letter identified tokenization as the next major asset management trend. Real estate tokenization is particularly compelling: the global real estate market is three times the size of the global equity market.

### Stablecoins: Circle, Tether, and the Genius Act [00:00:17]

Circle went public as the seventh largest IPO of all time, raising roughly $1 billion, with its stock reportedly quadrupling post-IPO. Tether generated an estimated $13 billion in EBITDA in 2024 — more than McDonald's, BlackRock, and many other large corporations — by collecting treasury yield on customer dollar deposits while returning no interest to holders. Edelman distinguishes Circle's US-regulated, audited model from Tether's offshore opacity. Major banks (JPMorgan, Bank of America, Citi, Wells), Fiserv, Amazon, and Walmart are all launching or planning stablecoins, suggesting a fragmented multi-issuer future with a 'wallet hub' opportunity.

### Fiduciary Obligations and the Adviser Gap [00:00:17]

Edelman argues that 50% of US advisers personally own Bitcoin but only 20% recommend it to clients, largely because compliance officers cannot approve what they do not understand. He frames zero crypto exposure as an active short decision given crypto's 3% share of total US wealth. His challenge to advisers: use updated information, not five-year-old assumptions, and at minimum disclose any conflict of interest that prevents them from discussing digital assets. He frames the CBDA certification and adviser education as the path forward, noting the biggest near-term opportunity is helping clients who already own crypto with tax, estate, and philanthropic planning.
