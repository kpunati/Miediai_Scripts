# Autonomous Driving Is a Trillion Dollar Opportunity

**Channel:** The Compound
**Published:** 2026-02-09
**URL:** https://youtube.com/watch?v=N6t0o-46pW4
**Duration:** 33:26

## Summary

Josh Brown hosts Andrew Macdonald, President and COO of Uber, to discuss the company's record-breaking recent quarter and its long-term autonomous vehicle strategy. Macdonald explains that Uber's competitive advantage lies in network aggregation — the belief that AV technology will be solved by many players globally, making Uber's 200-million-user distribution platform the key bottleneck for AV operators seeking utilization and revenue. The interview covers Uber's asset-light approach, its willingness to use its balance sheet in the short term to seed the AV ecosystem, and early data showing AVs expand rather than cannibalize the existing ride-sharing market.

## Key Takeaways

- Uber added more new customers in Q4 than any quarter since the pandemic, despite conventional wisdom that the platform is already saturated.
- Uber's core thesis is that L4 autonomy will be solved by many companies globally, which validates an aggregation strategy over a vertically integrated approach.
- 70-75% of Uber's US mobility profits originate outside the top 20 cities, challenging the common narrative of geographic concentration.
- Uber argues its delivery business and international markets with low average fares are largely insulated from near-term AV disruption.
- Uber plans to use its balance sheet temporarily to help AV partners and OEMs like Lucid scale production, while maintaining its long-term asset-light model.
- Early Waymo partnership data in Atlanta and Austin shows AV trips grow the overall mobility category rather than just substituting for human-driven rides.
- Uber's nearly 10 million drivers and couriers generate unique edge-case data for AV partners, representing a structural advantage in training and deployment.

## Detailed Notes

### Record Quarterly Earnings Overview [00:02:47]

Uber reported 200 million monthly active users, a $15 billion annual trip run rate, adjusted EBITDA of $2.5 billion (up 35% YoY), and free cash flow growth of 42% for the full year. Quarterly trips grew 22% and gross bookings guidance was set at 17-21% growth. Uber One membership surged 55% YoY and now represents approximately 50% of gross bookings.

### Competitive Position and Geographic Scope [00:06:25]

Macdonald addressed skepticism about geographic concentration by noting 70-75% of US mobility profits come from outside the top 20 cities. Uber operates in 8,000+ US markets covering 95% of the US population. Markets like Germany, Spain, Japan, and Argentina are described as still early-stage for Uber, suggesting significant remaining growth potential.

### AV Strategy: Multi-Player Aggregation Model [00:11:12]

Uber's AV thesis is premised on L4 autonomy being solved by multiple global players rather than one or two dominant firms. With seven live deployments and partners including Waymo, Pony AI, Weide, and others, Uber positions itself as a distribution network that makes AV fleets more efficient — citing roughly 30% higher vehicle utilization on the Uber platform versus first-party channels. Macdonald draws an analogy to McDonald's using both its own app and Uber Eats for delivery.

### AV Market Growth and Category Expansion [00:19:59]

Data from Waymo deployments in Atlanta and Austin shows that 40% of Uber customers now use multiple products, and AV availability is growing overall mobility demand rather than cannibalizing existing trips. Macdonald compares this to how UberX's launch in 2012-2013 grew the entire transportation category beyond prior TAM estimates.

### Uber Fleet and Balance Sheet Strategy [00:25:26]

Uber announced a joint venture with Lucid and Nuro to deploy a branded fleet of 20,000 autonomous vehicles in the Bay Area, marking the first time Uber's logo appears on an AV. While Uber's long-term goal remains asset-light, Macdonald explained the company will use its balance sheet selectively to help OEM partners establish supply chains and give fleets financial confidence. He compared the eventual ownership structure to REITs in the hotel industry.
