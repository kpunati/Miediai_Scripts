# Top Predictions for Crypto in 2025

**Channel:** The Compound
**Published:** 2025-01-27
**URL:** https://youtube.com/watch?v=R1gIB4T2350
**Duration:** 46:42

## Summary

Recorded the day after President Trump signed his executive order on digital assets, this episode features Pantera Capital General Partner Franklin Bi explaining what the regulatory shift means for the crypto industry and laying out three mega-trends for 2025: gateways (connecting traditional finance to blockchain rails), developer growth (scaling from 100,000 to 10 million builders), and applications (moving crypto from Wall Street speculation to mainstream consumer use). Bi draws on Pantera's decade-plus experience and portfolio companies to illustrate each trend with concrete examples including Ondo's tokenized treasuries, Bitso's remittance volumes, and blockchain gaming. The discussion also addresses the prohibition of a central bank digital currency in the EO, Operation Chokepoint banking discrimination, and the tokenization of real-world assets as the most significant near-term catalyst.

## Key Takeaways

- Trump's executive order explicitly prohibits a US central bank digital currency, preserving the commercial banking layer and addressing concerns about government financial surveillance.
- Operation Chokepoint-style banking discrimination against crypto companies is expected to end, which Bi says will be the biggest near-term change — freeing entrepreneurs who have held back from building in the space.
- Gateways connecting the $1-quadrillion traditional financial system to blockchain rails represent the largest opportunity, with tokenized treasuries ($2 billion on-chain) as an early proof of concept.
- Ondo Finance is tokenizing US treasuries and distributing them to foreign investors who lack direct access, competing with BlackRock and Franklin Templeton for this market.
- Blockchain gaming — exemplified by Pantera portfolio company Infinity Gods' King of Destiny with over 2 million downloads — could be crypto's Farmville moment, onboarding mainstream users who don't know they're using blockchain.
- Zero-knowledge proof technology enables privacy-preserving verification (proving age without revealing identity) and is being used in digital voting systems being tested in controversial countries.
- Tokenizing publicly traded securities could unlock global price discovery and make shares more valuable by exposing them to a worldwide pool of buyers rather than just local market participants.

## Detailed Notes

### Trump Executive Order: Key Provisions and Industry Reaction [00:00:13]

Less than 24 hours after Trump signed the executive order on digital assets, Bi describes the industry reaction as 'surreal' after years of regulatory hostility. Key provisions include supporting blockchain deployment, providing regulatory clarity, ensuring fair banking access for crypto companies, and prohibiting a central bank digital currency. Bi credits Coinbase, the Blockchain Association, and David Sacks for shaping the sophisticated language in the order.

### Operation Chokepoint and Banking Discrimination [00:00:13]

Bi describes dozens of Pantera portfolio companies having bank accounts shut down overnight simply for using the words 'blockchain' or 'crypto.' Two crypto-friendly banks were effectively executed by regulators in 2023 as a warning to others. This chilling effect suppressed entrepreneurial activity more than any other factor, and Bi expects its end to unlock a wave of new talent and company formation in the space.

### Mega-Trend 1: Gateways and Real-World Asset Tokenization [00:00:13]

Bi identifies connecting the $3 trillion crypto market to the traditional $1 quadrillion financial system as the primary opportunity. Examples include Bitso processing over 10% of US-Mexico remittances, Ondo Finance tokenizing US treasuries for foreign investors, and Tiplink enabling crypto micro-payments with a Venmo-like user experience. Larry Fink of BlackRock and the CEO of Bank of America are cited as publicly pushing for SEC approval to tokenize stocks and bonds.

### Mega-Trend 2: Developer Growth and Interoperability [00:00:13]

With only about 100,000 developers building on blockchains — half the size of a single large tech company — Bi argues mainstream adoption requires scaling to 10 million developers. Multi-chain interoperability (enabling different blockchains to communicate), zero-knowledge technology for privacy-preserving verification, and no-code tools analogous to Shopify or WordPress are the infrastructure needed to onboard this talent base.

### Mega-Trend 3: Mainstream Applications and Blockchain Gaming [00:00:13]

Approximately 80 million users are on-chain today, nearly all attracted by trading and speculation. Bi argues the next phase requires use cases for non-investors, with gaming as the most promising near-term catalyst. Pantera portfolio company Infinity Gods' King of Destiny game has had 2 million downloads and lets players sell in-game assets to other players globally via blockchain — without users knowing they are using crypto. Bi compares this to Farmville's role in onboarding 80 million users to Facebook.
