# The Stock Market Is Doing Something We’ve Never Seen Before | Animal Spirits 463

**Channel:** The Compound
**Published:** 2026-05-06
**URL:** https://youtube.com/watch?v=TVz4Nz4WjE4
**Duration:** 65:43

## Summary

Animal Spirits episode 463 features Michael Batnick and Ben Carlson debating Paul Tudor Jones's warnings about extreme US stock market valuations against their own more constructive view that rising equity ownership rates structurally justify higher market cap-to-GDP ratios. The hosts review blockbuster Q1 2026 earnings from the Mag 7, the accelerating CapEx arms race among hyperscalers, and Anthropic's extraordinary revenue growth trajectory. They also discuss Jevons paradox as a counterargument to AI-driven job displacement, troubling data from prediction market platforms, and simmering risks in private credit and long-duration bonds.

## Key Takeaways

- Paul Tudor Jones argues the US is 'over-equitized' at 252% market cap-to-GDP with negative 10-year forward returns historically at current P/E levels, but the hosts contend that rising stock ownership rates (now 60% of households vs. 2% in 1929) structurally justify higher readings.
- S&P 500 Q1 2026 earnings growth hit 27% year-over-year — the highest since Q4 2021 — led by communication services (+53%) and tech (+50%), with Mag 7 companies beating EPS estimates by 61% on average.
- Hyperscalers are dramatically accelerating CapEx: the big four plan to spend 77% more than last year's $410 billion, with CapEx expected to exceed 90% of operating cash flow in 2026.
- Jevons paradox suggests AI will likely expand employment in affected industries rather than destroy it, as lower costs create more demand, similar to how spreadsheets quadrupled the number of accountants.
- Prediction market data is deeply unfavorable to retail participants: 67% of Polymarket profits go to 0.1% of accounts, and Kalshi acknowledges 75% of its traders lose money.
- Long-duration Treasury bonds remain in a 40% drawdown with negative 11-year total returns, and US publicly held debt has crossed 100% of GDP.
- Private credit faces growing risks: software exposure in top BDCs exceeds 25% of fair value, non-accrual stress is rising, and single credits appear across five or six BDCs simultaneously.

## Detailed Notes

### Paul Tudor Jones, Market Valuations, and Investor Personality [00:00:36]

Batnick recaps a Paul Tudor Jones interview on Invest Like the Best where Jones warns the US is at 252% stock market cap to GDP — far above prior bubble peaks — and that historical data shows negative 10-year forward returns when buying the S&P at a P/E of 22. The hosts argue Jones's persistent bearishness reflects his trader personality rather than superior forecasting, drawing a parallel to Dalio's erroneous 1982 depression call at the start of the greatest bull market in history.

### Stock Ownership Rates and the Buffett Indicator [00:05:43]

Carlson pushes back on the 'over-equitization' framing: in 1929 only 2% of Americans owned stocks vs. 60% today, so a structurally higher market cap-to-GDP ratio is exactly what one would expect. International data shows the US at 55% household equity ownership versus India at 6% and China at 7%, and both hosts expect global equity participation to continue rising as wealth grows in emerging markets.

### Mag 7 Earnings and Hyperscaler CapEx Surge [00:08:17]

Q1 2026 saw S&P 500 earnings growth of 27% year-over-year, the highest since Q4 2021, driven by communication services (+53%) and technology (+50%); Alphabet beat EPS estimates by over 90%, Amazon by over 70%, and Meta by over 56%. The four biggest hyperscalers plan CapEx 77% above the prior year's $410 billion combined spend, with CapEx as a share of operating cash flow projected to exceed 90% in 2026 — up from under 25% in 2012. Anthropic's revenue growth is described as exceeding Zoom's pandemic-era pace and Standard Oil's Gilded Age growth.

### AI and Labor Markets: Jevons Paradox [00:37:05]

Sam Altman and Ezra Klein are cited arguing that AI will augment rather than replace workers, pointing to historical precedents such as the spreadsheet quadrupling accounting employment and the rise in Philippine call center jobs despite AI advances. The hosts are cautiously optimistic, acknowledging real displacement in some sectors (e.g., Coinbase cutting 14% of staff) while expecting AI to accelerate business formation and create new categories of demand.

### Prediction Markets: Profitability Data [00:46:30]

Bloomberg and the Wall Street Journal independently published analyses showing that on Polymarket, 67% of profits flow to just 0.1% of accounts (roughly 2,000 accounts netting nearly half a billion dollars), strongly suggestive of insider-information advantages. Kalshi released its own data showing 75% of traders lose money — a figure the hosts note is still presented by Kalshi as superior to options (80% losers) and sports books (95% losers). Batnick calls for prohibiting bets on outcomes knowable in advance.

### Bonds, Government Debt, and Private Credit Risks [00:18:13]

Long-duration Treasuries (TLT) are down roughly 40% from early 2020 highs with a negative 11-year total return, a crash the hosts say was easily hedgeable via shorter-duration instruments. US publicly held debt crossed 100% of GDP at $31.265 trillion as of March 31. PitchBook's Q4 2025 BDC analysis flags growing software/AI disruption risk (25%+ of top BDC portfolios), rising non-accrual stress, and deep borrower overlap across multiple BDCs as compounding credit risks.
