# The Momentum Bear Market | TCAF 183

**Channel:** The Compound
**Published:** 2025-03-18
**URL:** https://youtube.com/watch?v=Fsdj092ZjQA
**Duration:** 47:00

## Summary

Recorded live at the Future Proof Miami citywide event, this episode features Joe Terranova joining Josh Brown and Michael Batnick to analyze the early 2025 momentum selloff, in which momentum stocks led a swift 10% market decline within 16 trading days of an all-time high. The discussion covers how the proliferation of quantitative strategies has changed market structure, why momentum alone is insufficient without quality and growth overlays, and how the wealth effect amplifies the impact of equity declines on consumer behavior. Terranova explains the mechanics of his rules-based JOET ETF, including how its quarterly rebalancing navigates momentum breaks without discretionary overrides.

## Key Takeaways

- Intra-year drawdowns averaging 14% are normal for the S&P 500; the 10% drop of early 2025 was fast but historically unremarkable in magnitude.
- The wealth effect is particularly potent now because US household equity allocations are near record highs, making the economy more sensitive to stock declines.
- Quantitative and systematic strategies — estimated at 80-85% of daily volume — drove the momentum unwind, with pod shops like Millennium reducing risk after losses rather than buying dips.
- Pure momentum exposure leaves portfolios vulnerable in corrections; combining momentum with quality (measured by 36-month revenue growth) and growth factors provides a meaningful shock absorber.
- Rules-based investing enforces discipline that discretionary managers cannot replicate: JOET re-entered Nvidia in April 2023 after selling in January, a trade nearly impossible to execute manually.
- The German DAX rising 19% year-to-date in early 2025 illustrates the value of diversification across geographies that advisors can now point to for client reassurance.
- Duration of a drawdown matters more than depth: a prolonged flat market is more economically damaging than a sharp V-shaped correction.

## Detailed Notes

### Market Context: The 2025 Momentum Selloff [00:00:29]

Within 16 trading days of an all-time high, the S&P 500 fell 10%, with momentum names hit hardest. The panel emphasizes that the average intra-year drawdown since 1990 is approximately 14% and markets almost never finish the year at their worst level. The speed of the decline was unusual even if the magnitude was not.

### Wealth Effect and Consumer Risk [00:00:29]

Strategist Warren Pies argues US household record equity overweighting means a 10% correction constitutes a wealth shock equivalent to 12% of GDP. Terranova stresses that both portfolio values and home values drive consumer spending intentions, and that duration — not depth — determines whether a correction infects the real economy. The 2022 bear market did not cause a recession partly because it resolved quickly enough that consumer behavior did not structurally change.

### Sentiment Extremes and Positioning [00:00:29]

AAII bulls fell below 20% for three consecutive weeks, a level seen only twice in the survey's history. Despite bearish rhetoric, Jim Paulson data shows average AAII equity-less-cash positions remain at 50.5% — well above the 21% typical at past market lows — suggesting investors are scared but still invested. Terranova uses sentiment and positioning as a risk-measurement tool rather than a market-timing signal.

### Market Structure and Quant Concentration [00:00:29]

Terranova estimates quantitative strategies now represent 80-85% of daily trading volume, indifferent to fundamentals and focused purely on price. Goldman Sachs reported stock-picking hedge funds had their worst 14-day stretch since May 2022, and Millennium lost 1.3% in February. The simultaneous unwinding of concentrated momentum positions in names like Palanteer, Coinbase, Reddit, and Netflix created cascading selling pressure.

### Quality-Momentum-Growth ETF Strategy (JOET) [00:00:29]

Terranova's JOET ETF, launched in 2020 and with approximately 5 years of track record by the episode date, combines four factors equally weighted: momentum, quality (requiring consistent 36-month revenue growth), and growth. The quarterly rebalance enforces rules without discretion — the strategy exited Nvidia in January 2023 and re-entered April 30, 2023 purely by rules. The worst environment for such a strategy is concentrated bull markets (e.g., 2023 Magnificent Seven dominance); the approach sacrifices some upside capture for better drawdown management.

### Managing Momentum Breaks and Career Lessons [00:00:29]

When momentum breaks, names like Palanteer typically trace a U-shape rather than a V-shape because systematic capital steps back and reduces risk after losses. Terranova's rules-based quarterly reconstitution is designed for exactly these episodes — the April 30 rebalance will rotate the portfolio into whatever sectors are exhibiting momentum. He closes with a career lesson: 'your first loss is your best loss,' and the importance of maintaining perspective because markets have always recovered from periods of volatility.
