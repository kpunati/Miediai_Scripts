# How Sixers Owner David Adelman Lost a Bet But Gained a Real Estate Empire

**Channel:** Ownership by Ritholtz 
**Published:** 2025-02-05
**URL:** https://youtube.com/watch?v=RBHWvBVf87k
**Duration:** 44:32

## Summary

David Adelman traces his path from an 11-year-old losing a bet to Uncle Allen Horwitz to becoming CEO of Campus Apartments, a national student housing platform operating across roughly 18-28 states. He discusses how he scaled the business by consolidating a highly fragmented market, secured a $300 million sovereign wealth fund commitment in 2005, and avoided taking outside equity at the parent company level to preserve operational control. The conversation also covers his venture investments through Darco Capital, his acquisition of an LP stake in the Philadelphia 76ers and New Jersey Devils alongside Michael Rubin, and plans to build a new arena in Philadelphia in partnership with Comcast.

## Key Takeaways

- Student housing is treated as a near-recession-proof asset class because students represent a captive demand base with extremely low default rates even during COVID.
- Adelman's growth strategy mirrored Aramark's outsourcing model—universities do not want to manage off-campus housing, creating a large fragmented market ripe for consolidation.
- Maintaining 100% parent-company equity while partnering with capital at the asset level allowed Adelman to scale without ceding control of hiring and culture.
- His competitive advantage in venture capital is providing liquidity support and strategic distribution leverage through Campus Apartments' 25,000 student residents.
- Acquiring an LP stake in Harris Blitzer (Sixers, Devils, Crystal Palace) came via a 30-second handshake deal with childhood friend Michael Rubin after Rubin was required to divest due to sports betting regulations.
- FS Investments was built to democratize access to alternative investments through RIA and wirehouse channels, growing to approximately $84 billion AUM.
- Adelman and Comcast are jointly pursuing a new arena build in Philadelphia estimated at roughly 30 months of construction after approximately one year of design.

## Detailed Notes

### Origin Story: The Basketball Bet and Early Real Estate Apprenticeship [00:01:07]

At age 11, Adelman lost a basketball bet to family friend Allen Horwitz and had to work off the debt at Campus Apartments stacking lumber and sweeping sawdust. By 13, he invested his $2,000 bar mitzvah money directly into a Campus Apartments student housing property near UPenn, selecting it by size rather than financial metrics.

### Building and Scaling Campus Apartments [00:04:16]

Adelman identified the extreme fragmentation of student housing—every university had its own mom-and-pop operator—and modeled consolidation after Aramark's outsourcing of university dining. After graduating Ohio State on Thursday and starting Monday, he became CEO at 25-26 with roughly 40-50 employees and began a national expansion, eventually operating in 18-28 states. A $300 million sovereign wealth fund commitment in 2005 provided the equity firepower to pursue multiple deals simultaneously rather than one at a time.

### Community Investment and University City District [00:14:41]

When crime around UPenn threatened Campus Apartments' core Philadelphia portfolio, Adelman became the largest private-sector backer of the University City District special services district, contributing $500,000 over 10 years toward a $4.5 million operating budget. He frames it as aligned self-interest: the business cannot prosper if the surrounding neighborhood deteriorates.

### Venture Portfolio and Investment Philosophy at Darco Capital [00:19:58]

Adelman invests pre-revenue, betting on the founder, and seeks portfolio companies adjacent to Campus Apartments where he can add distribution value via 25,000 resident students. Holdings include Credai (fintech/debit-credit product, Starbucks partnership), Margo (women's footwear), Oi Pets, Rise Cold Brew, American Harvest Vodka, and Fanatics LP. He distinguishes himself from VC funds by providing bridge lines of credit during downturns rather than being constrained by fund vintage limitations.

### Sports Ownership: Sixers, Devils, and New Arena [00:27:18]

Adelman purchased his LP stake in Harris Blitzer Sports and Entertainment from Michael Rubin in a brief one-on-one deal after Rubin was forced to divest due to sports betting regulatory requirements. He plans to build a new state-of-the-art arena in Philadelphia jointly with Comcast, targeting a design phase of approximately one year and a 30-month construction timeline, and is also pursuing a WNBA expansion franchise for Philadelphia.

### FS Investments and Alternatives Distribution [00:39:47]

FS Investments (originally Franklin Square, founded 2006) has grown to approximately $84 billion AUM by channeling alternative investment products through RIAs and wirehouses. Adelman emphasizes transparency and early communication of bad news as core to maintaining adviser trust, analogizing it to his philosophy that 'bad news doesn't get better with time.'
