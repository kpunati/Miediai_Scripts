# How Nvidia Employees Became Multi-Millionaires

**Channel:** Ownership by Ritholtz 
**Published:** 2025-01-29
**URL:** https://youtube.com/watch?v=wImMjd397JU
**Duration:** 33:26

## Summary

This episode of Ownership by Ritholtz Wealth Management features Josh Brown and equity compensation expert Joey Fishman walking through how Nvidia employees accumulated substantial wealth through the company's Employee Stock Purchase Plan (ESPP), incentive stock options (ISOs), and restricted stock units (RSUs). The video examines the mechanics of Nvidia's generous ESPP look-back discount, charts showing explosive earnings and stock performance, and the financial planning challenges facing employees who now hold highly concentrated positions. Fishman explains liquidation strategies, tax considerations, and risk management approaches for navigating these concentrated equity positions.

## Key Takeaways

- Nvidia's ESPP allows employees to purchase up to $25,000 of stock per year at a 15% discount applied to the lower of the opening or closing price over a six-month look-back period, one of the most generous plans in tech.
- Employees hired in the 2014-2016 era who combined ISOs with ESPP participation saw the most dramatic wealth creation due to low strike prices and Nvidia's subsequent explosive growth.
- RSUs, which now predominate at Nvidia, offer a safer but lower-upside alternative to ISOs, with a four-year vesting schedule including a one-year cliff before 25% vests.
- Sequence of return risk is a major concern for Nvidia employees living off a concentrated position: even a multi-year downturn in a high-volatility single stock can exhaust wealth that appears sufficient on paper.
- Dollar-cost averaging out and regret minimization are rated the most effective and behaviorally sound liquidation strategies for concentrated positions, often paired with a 10b5-1 plan for insiders.
- A 60/40 portfolio with lower volatility can achieve the same retirement income goals as an undiversified Nvidia position with a significantly smaller asset base, illustrating the volatility tax on concentrated holdings.

## Detailed Notes

### ESPP Mechanics and Nvidia's Generous Structure [00:01:14]

Joey Fishman explains that ESPPs are elective benefits similar to 401k contributions, allowing employees to set aside salary to buy up to $25,000 of company stock per year. Nvidia's plan stands out by applying a 15% discount to the lower of the stock price at the beginning or end of a six-month period (the look-back provision). Most large-cap tech firms offer ESPPs but few are as generous as Nvidia's; Microsoft and Apple are considered second-tier in this regard.

### Nvidia's Stock Performance and Wealth Creation [00:04:23]

Nvidia has been the best-performing stock in the Russell 1000 since its 1999 IPO, with an annualized total return of 37%. The company grew market cap from $500 million to $3.25 trillion and added $2 trillion in market cap in just the 11 months of 2024. ESPP participants over the last 10 years on a $250,000 notional basis accumulated roughly $13.5 million with the discount versus $11.5 million without.

### Equity Compensation Types: ISOs vs. RSUs [00:10:54]

Incentive stock options (ISOs) were prevalent in Nvidia's earlier years, offering favorable tax treatment and high leverage (potential 10-20-30x returns relative to strike price) that attracted talent away from established firms. RSUs, now more common at mature-stage Nvidia, provide guaranteed value upon vesting on a four-year schedule (one-year cliff, then 6.25% quarterly), making them lower-risk but lower-upside than ISOs. Stock-based compensation at Nvidia rose from $2 billion in 2022 to $3.5 billion in 2024.

### Financial Planning: Sequence of Return Risk [00:17:19]

Fishman highlights that sequence of return risk is underappreciated by concentrated equity holders. A 50-year-old withdrawing $200,000 annually from a position with 40-60% annualized volatility faces a meaningful probability of ruin even if the starting balance looks sufficient. A 60/40 portfolio targeting 5% returns can match retirement income objectives with lower required assets than a single Nvidia position targeting 11% but with 40% volatility.

### Liquidation Strategies for Concentrated Positions [00:27:48]

Fishman rates dollar-cost averaging out (DCA out) and regret minimization as the highest-efficacy liquidation approaches. DCA out works by systematically selling over time, with the nuance of avoiding selling into sharp declines. Insiders and control persons should file a 10b5-1 plan to pre-announce trading schedules and avoid insider trading exposure. Regret minimization focuses on identifying worst-case outcomes and derisking accordingly, though it carries the behavioral cost of hindsight regret regardless of which direction the stock moves.
