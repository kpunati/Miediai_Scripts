# How to Sell Stocks Without Getting Crushed by Taxes

**Channel:** Ownership by Ritholtz 
**Published:** 2025-12-17
**URL:** https://youtube.com/watch?v=t50_u1ansv4
**Duration:** 19:39

## Summary

Nick Sapienza uses Ann's real subscriber question to walk through a comprehensive framework for selling a concentrated tech stock position ahead of retirement. Ann holds $1 million in two big tech stocks within a $4.1 million portfolio and faces a narrow window between her current highest-bracket tax situation and a lower post-retirement rate. The video evaluates four liquidation strategies — options collars, exchange funds, 351 exchange funds, and dollar-cost averaging out — ultimately recommending DCA out combined with custom indexing to systematize selling, reinvest immediately, and generate ongoing tax-loss harvesting benefits. The asset drawdown structure layers cash for near-term spending, bonds for medium-term stability, and a diversified equity portfolio for long-term growth, while flagging key retirement milestones that will require plan adjustments.

## Key Takeaways

- The gap between the current high-tax period and the lower post-retirement tax bracket represents an optimal window to plan concentrated stock liquidation, but deferring entirely adds price and concentration risk.
- The 0% capital gains bracket allows tax-free gains up to roughly $64,100 for a single filer in 2025, but meeting that threshold alone is too slow to de-risk a large concentrated position.
- A rules-based DCA-out plan — selling a fixed percentage quarterly without exception — removes emotion and reduces the risk of overriding the plan at the wrong time.
- Custom indexing replaces a traditional ETF portfolio with 200-300 individual stocks, enabling systematic tax-loss harvesting against routine volatility to offset gains from the concentrated position.
- A 351 exchange fund allows immediate diversification without triggering a tax event, but it defers rather than reduces taxes and lacks tax-loss harvesting capabilities.
- Sequence-of-returns risk is the biggest threat to early retirement income; layering cash, bonds, and equities in a bucket structure mitigates this.

## Detailed Notes

### Ann's Situation and Core Trade-offs [00:00:00]

Ann is 55, has worked 15 years in big tech sales, holds $1 million in two concentrated tech stocks with a low cost basis, and manages a total portfolio of $4.1 million including $2.7 million in a taxable account, a $1.1 million pre-tax 401k, and a $340,000 Roth 401k funded via mega backdoor Roth. Her husband is 64 and retired; she plans to retire within two years. The key tension is between paying high taxes now versus carrying concentration risk until retirement.

### Tax Mechanics in Retirement vs. Today [00:03:10]

Currently in the highest federal bracket, Ann would owe 20% long-term capital gains tax plus 3.8% NIIT on gains. In her first year of retirement with zero other income, gains are taxed in layers: the first chunk is tax-free via the standard deduction, the next tranche falls in the 0% bracket, then 15% applies, and gains above $200,000 attract the additional 3.8% NIIT. On $800,000 of gains, delaying until retirement could reduce the tax bill by roughly $40,000.

### Liquidation Strategies: Options, Exchange Funds, and DCA Out [00:06:16]

Options collars (writing calls and buying puts) create a disciplined selling schedule and cap downside, but are expensive and labor-intensive; research by AQR suggests simply selling is often more efficient. Exchange funds provide immediate diversification by pooling concentrated positions but carry a 7-year lockup — unsuitable for Ann's timeline. The 351 exchange fund diversifies without immediate tax but defers rather than reduces the liability and lacks tax-loss harvesting. DCA out on a fixed 24-36 month schedule with immediate reinvestment into custom indexing is the preferred approach.

### Rules-Based DCA Out and Custom Indexing [00:10:34]

The five rules of a disciplined liquidation plan are: (1) sell on a fixed quarterly schedule over 24-36 months; (2) define parameters like share count or dollar amount and never deviate; (3) reinvest proceeds immediately into the target portfolio; (4) create a tax plan that stays within a desired bracket, uses loss harvesting and donor-advised fund gifting; and (5) lock in a one-page decision-making policy reviewed once per year. Custom indexing holds 200-300 individual names, generating tax-loss harvesting opportunities from routine individual-stock volatility.

### Asset Drawdown Structure and Retirement Milestones [00:17:03]

Ann's drawdown plan layers: up to two years of expenses in cash, 3-5 years of spending in bonds, and the remainder in a diversified equity portfolio. Key milestones requiring plan adjustments include age 59.5 (penalty-free 401k access), 62 (Social Security eligibility), 63 (IRMAA income threshold), 65 (Medicare), 67 (full retirement age), and 75 (RMD start and Roth conversion window).
