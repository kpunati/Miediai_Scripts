  Most people roll everything into an IRA without realizing there’s another option — a little-known IRS rule called Net Unrealized Appreciation (NUA).  

  It lets you turn part of your 401(k) into taxable stock, pay income tax only on what you originally paid for it, and pay long-term capital gains on the growth later.  

  That difference can mean tens or even hundreds of thousands of dollars in tax savings.

  ✅  The IRS rules you must follow to qualify
