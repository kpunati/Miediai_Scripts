# Aftermath Of Hurricane Helene Including Insurance

**Channel:** Rebecca Walser
**Published:** 2024-10-02

## Summary

This segment from Fox Business 'Mornings with Maria' (aired September 27, 2024) features Rebecca Walser and other panelists discussing several converging macro and political risks. Topics covered include tight 2024 presidential election polls in Arizona and Georgia, the threat of an East Coast port strike estimated to cost $5 billion per day, China's massive stimulus package and what it signals about the health of its economy, and the Federal Reserve's aggressive 50-basis-point rate cut which Walser views as a warning sign of recession. The episode also briefly addresses Hurricane Helene's damage and the accelerating homeowners insurance crisis in Florida.

## Key Takeaways

- Walser interprets the Fed's 50-bps cut as historically predictive of recession, citing parallels to pre-dot-com-crash and pre-2008-crisis rate actions.
- China's stimulus, which involves cutting the RRR and issuing special sovereign bonds totaling roughly $400 billion, is seen as a reaction to failing bank liquidity rather than a healthy growth strategy.
- An East Coast port strike could disrupt auto parts supply chains for major manufacturers, adding inflationary pressure to an already fragile economy.
- Walser warns of a bifurcated economy where the bottom 50% of earners feel they are already in recession while the top 20% experience prosperity.
- Gold reached an intraday all-time high near $2,700, which Walser views as having substantial further upside.
- Florida homeowners insurance premiums have risen as much as 50% year-over-year with many insurers exiting the market, a problem compounded by Hurricane Helene's category-four storm surge.

## Detailed Notes

### Election Polls and Border Policy [00:00:00]

Fox News polls showed Trump with a two-point edge over Harris in Arizona and Harris with a three-point lead in Georgia, 39 days before the election. Walser and panelists criticized the Biden-Harris administration's immigration policies, particularly the CBP One app and flights of migrants directly into U.S. cities, framing border security as both a national security and economic issue.

### East Coast Port Strike Risk [00:09:32]

With talks stalled, the Biden administration was trying to bring both sides to the table in Washington DC. A strike at East Coast and Gulf ports was estimated to cost up to $5 billion per day, with particular risks to auto parts supply chains including factories for Mercedes, Hyundai, Kia, Nissan, VW, and BMW. Walser noted the risk of a secondary inflation spike if a strike coincided with union demands for a 70% wage increase.

### China Stimulus Analysis [00:15:56]

China's central bank cut its reserve requirement ratio and planned to issue special sovereign bonds, resulting in an estimated $400 billion stimulus package. Walser was skeptical, arguing the RRR cut amounts to liquidity support for failing banks rather than real economic stimulus. Shanghai Composite had its best week since 2008 but Fundstrat's Mark Newton expected the rally to be short-lived.

### Federal Reserve Rate Cut and Recession Signal [00:18:01]

Walser characterized the 50-bps cut as a historical warning sign, having occurred before the dot-com crash in 2001, the 2007-08 financial crisis, and the 2020 COVID shock. She argued the Fed's forward guidance of eight more cuts over two years signals awareness of a deeper economic deterioration, possibly global depression, while public messaging remained upbeat.

### Hurricane Helene and Florida Insurance Crisis [00:30:33]

Hurricane Helene hit Florida as a Category 4, producing an approximately 8-foot storm surge in St. Petersburg. Panelists noted homeowners insurance premiums in Florida had risen up to 50% year-over-year with many insurers withdrawing, and that a 2022 legislative fix had not fully resolved availability and cost issues near the water.
