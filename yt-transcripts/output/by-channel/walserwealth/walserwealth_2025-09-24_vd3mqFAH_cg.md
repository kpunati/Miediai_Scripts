## Summary

Rebecca Walser presents four investment opportunities during a Fox Business segment with host Charles Payne. She emphasizes the market's long runway driven by the fourth industrial revolution and advocates for organic economic growth. The discussion covers Tesla's potential as a leader in EVs and battery technology, Oklo's nuclear energy solutions for AI infrastructure, Netsik's cybersecurity dominance across Fortune 100 companies, and Figur's innovative blockchain-based consumer finance platform.

## Key Takeaways

- Tesla offers exposure to multiple growth drivers including EVs, robotaxi development, and battery technology with strategic backing from Trump administration.
- Oklo addresses critical energy infrastructure gap where AI demand exceeds current energy capacity; nuclear reactor coming 2027-2028.
- Netsik provides cybersecurity with strong revenue growth, Fortune 100 client base, and attractive valuation relative to peers as recent IPO.
- Figur Technologies disrupts home equity lending with blockchain technology, reducing loan closure timeline from industry standard to 10 days.
- Blockchain and stable coins (backed 1:1) will likely replace paper currency as nation addresses debt levels.
- All four picks are recent or new IPOs providing entry points before significant institutional adoption.

Up 565% YTD, reflecting market recognition of nuclear's role in powering AI infrastructure. Company has strong cash reserves and reactor expected operational 2027-2028. Positioned as long-term play not flash-in-pan, with major institutional cash reserves.

IPO showing strong revenue growth with 30 Fortune 100 clients already. Trading at 10x revenue and discount to peer valuations. Walser sees breakout potential; noted as cheap entry point for new IPO investors.

Walser emphasizes blockchain and stable coins (1:1 backed) as solution to national debt levels and currency transition. Trump's position against CBDCs but supporting regulated stable coins seen as favorable environment for cryptocurrency adoption.
