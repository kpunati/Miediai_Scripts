# Bracing for Market Volatility + Long-term Strategy

**Channel:** Rebecca Walser
**Published:** 2026-04-13
**URL:** https://youtube.com/watch?v=mX8U8cMtipQ
**Duration:** 14:01

## Summary

In this Schwab Network interview, Rebecca Walser analyzes current market conditions shaped by an ongoing US-Iran conflict and elevated oil prices near $100 per barrel. She warns investors against expecting rapid resolution and recommends bracing for 3 to 6 months of volatility. For long-term positioning, she highlights three AI infrastructure names—Jabil as a cheap backbone play, Micron for the memory supercycle, and Coherent for optical AI connectivity—while noting the macro headwinds from a new Fed chair, labor market pressures, and geopolitical realignment of capital flows toward the east.

## Key Takeaways

- Markets may be overestimating the speed of US-Iran conflict resolution; a failed deal could cause a sharper downturn than the initial selloff.
- Investors whose risk tolerance cannot withstand a 3 to 6 month pullback should reduce exposure to riskier assets now rather than waiting.
- Gold has been acting as a risk-on rather than risk-off asset, reducing its utility as a safe haven in the current environment.
- Jabil, Micron, and Coherent represent three different entry points into the AI buildout theme across traditional backbone, memory, and optical connectivity.
- The biggest retail investor mistake is going to all-cash—missing both the upside and the timing needed to re-enter effectively.
- Macro headwinds include a new Fed chair, rising yields from oil-driven inflation pressure, labor market issues, and declining international demand for US Treasury debt.

## Detailed Notes

### Geopolitical Risk and Near-Term Market Outlook [00:00:00]

Walser cautions that markets may be pricing in a ceasefire that has not yet fully materialized. If negotiations collapse after getting close to a deal, market sentiment could deteriorate more sharply than the initial shock, as hope would give way to greater uncertainty. She recommends investors not assume complete resolution in the near term.

### Oil, Gold, and Safe Havens [00:02:48]

With US crude near $100 per barrel, Walser notes gold has paradoxically acted as a risk-on asset, returning to $4,800 when the ceasefire was announced rather than maintaining elevated levels. If a deal fails and oil continues rising, oil itself becomes an attractive yield opportunity. December oil futures remain in the high 70s range, suggesting market consensus still expects near-term resolution.

### AI Investment Opportunities [00:05:36]

Walser identifies three AI-related names: Jabil as cheap infrastructure at 0.89 price-to-sales for server and networking connectivity; Micron for the aggressive AI memory supercycle with 700% net income growth and nearly 200% revenue growth year-over-year; and Coherent as a longer-term optical connectivity play with high beta near 2.0 requiring an 18-month holding window. She notes all three are up double digits year-to-date but considers it still early in AI monetization.

### Macro Headwinds and Strategy [00:07:39]

Key macro concerns include: a new Fed chair taking over in May, with implications for monetary policy direction; labor market deterioration with a problematic quit rate; persistent yield pressure from oil-driven inflation; and global capital reallocation toward the east as geopolitical alignment shifts since Russia was removed from Swift in 2022. Walser recommends equal-weight S&P exposure, defensive overall positioning with selective risk, and avoiding going fully to cash. She would trim Mag Seven, buy Apple, sell Microsoft, and favors gold over Bitcoin as a hard asset.
