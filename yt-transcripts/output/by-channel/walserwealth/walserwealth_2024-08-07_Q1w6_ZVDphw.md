# Tech Sellout & Unemployment & Japanese Yen

**Channel:** Rebecca Walser
**Published:** 2024-08-07
**URL:** https://youtube.com/watch?v=Q1w6_ZVDphw
**Duration:** 39:16

## Summary

This Fox Business 'Mornings with Maria' segment aired August 6, 2024 following a major global market selloff that saw the Dow lose over 1,000 points and the S&P 500 record its worst day since 2022. Analysts Rebecca Walser, Quincy Crosby, and Bob Doll discuss the causes — AI stock disappointment, weak labor data, and the unwinding of the Japanese yen carry trade — and assess whether the rebound in futures represents a dead cat bounce or a sustainable recovery. The episode also covers Google's antitrust ruling, Q2 earnings results, and extensively debates Kamala Harris's economic and border security record ahead of the 2024 election.

## Key Takeaways

- The August 5, 2024 selloff was a convergence of multiple factors: AI monetization concerns, weak employment data, PMI contraction, and yen carry trade unwinding.
- Analysts cautioned that an emergency Fed rate cut would signal panic and could worsen market conditions; a 50 basis point cut at the September meeting was seen as more appropriate.
- The yen carry trade unwind, estimated at roughly $4 trillion, had not fully played out and was expected to continue pressuring risk assets.
- Google's antitrust ruling was seen as a significant precedent, though analysts expected years of appeals and noted it would likely not impact consumers directly.
- Q2 earnings were broadly better than expected on an EPS basis but missed on revenue, with analysts warning about back-half earnings disappointments as the economy slows.
- Rebecca Walser characterized Kamala Harris as 'economically unviable' based on prior statements on Medicare for all and open borders, with her economic policies unclear heading into the election.
- Border security data showing nearly 100 migrants on the terror watch list released into the US was highlighted as a political liability for the Harris campaign.

## Detailed Notes

### Global Market Selloff and Initial Reactions [00:00:00]

The Dow lost over 1,000 points and the S&P 500 had its worst day since 2022 on August 5, 2024. The VIX hit its highest level since the 2020 pandemic plunge. The Magnificent Seven companies collectively lost $1 trillion in market value, with Nvidia and Super Micro Computer leading the AI sector decline due to production delays in next-generation chips.

### Causes: AI Selloff, Weak Jobs Data, and Yen Carry Trade [00:04:10]

Analysts attributed the selloff to three converging forces: AI monetization concerns (Nvidia production delays), weak Friday jobs data showing anemic headline growth with 63% of new jobs in government and healthcare, and the unwinding of the Japanese yen carry trade following the Bank of Japan's July 31st rate hike. Quincy Crosby noted the market feared contagion from hedge funds or banks exposed to the carry trade.

### Federal Reserve Rate Cut Debate [00:02:06]

Futures priced in 125 basis points of Fed cuts across September, November, and December meetings. All analysts agreed that an emergency rate cut would be a mistake, likening it to signaling a crisis rather than a policy adjustment. Bob Doll and Rebecca Walser both agreed the yen carry trade was not fully unwound and further risk asset pressure was likely.

### Google Antitrust Ruling [00:22:06]

A federal judge ruled Google illegally monopolized the search and text advertising markets, marking the first such anti-tech ruling in decades. Key finding was that pre-installing Google on Android and Apple devices via paid contracts constituted monopolistic behavior. Analysts expected years of appeals and noted big tech companies have large legal teams prepared for such cases.

### Earnings: Uber, Caterpillar, Yum Brands [00:24:10]

Caterpillar delivered a double beat lifted by infrastructure spending demand. Uber posted strong results with 14% growth in monthly active consumers and 21% trip growth. Yum Brands beat on EPS but missed on revenue with KFC and Pizza Hut same-store sales declining, suggesting consumer cost containment rather than top-line growth. Bob Doll warned of back-half earnings disappointments as the economy slows.

### 2024 Election: Harris Economic Record and Border Security [00:07:19]

The panel discussed Kamala Harris's economic policy uncertainty following Biden's withdrawal, noting the hiring of Jean Sperling as a signal of continuity with Bidenomics. Harris's prior positions on Medicare for all, corporate tax increases, and open borders were contrasted with needed electoral moderation. A House Judiciary report alleging 99 migrants on terror watch lists were released into the US from 2021-2023 was highlighted as a significant campaign vulnerability.
