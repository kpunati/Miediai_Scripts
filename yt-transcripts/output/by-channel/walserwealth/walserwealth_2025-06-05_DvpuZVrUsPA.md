# Three Big Tech Stock Picks

**Channel:** Rebecca Walser
**Published:** 2025-06-05
**URL:** https://youtube.com/watch?v=DvpuZVrUsPA
**Duration:** 4:19

## Summary

Rebecca Walser evaluates three major technology stocks on CNBC's Power Lunch, focusing on fundamental valuations, growth catalysts, and policy headwinds. She recommends a hold or sell on each based on elevated valuations relative to growth prospects, emphasizing caution until tariff policy clarity is achieved and growth metrics justify premium valuations.

## Key Takeaways

- Apple shares are pressured by tariff policy uncertainty and its heavy Asia supply chain exposure, with July 9 as the tariff expiration date creating a key decision point for policy continuation.
- Netflix carries extreme valuation risk at 57x PE (3-year high), with subscriber growth having stopped after Q4, making continued upside dependent on new revenue models or resumed subscriber growth.
- Snowflake trades at 196.91x PE versus 29.52x industry average, representing significant valuation risk if the anticipated multi-year data investment cycle fails to materialize as expected.
- Premium technology stocks are facing valuation compression until either policy clarity (tariffs) is achieved or growth metrics resume to justify current valuations.
- Momentum-driven rallies in tech stocks should be approached cautiously, as most upside appears already priced into current valuations.

## Detailed Notes

### Apple: tariff pressure and supply chain disruption [00:00:01]

Apple is heavily exposed to Asia supply chain disruption from tariff policy. Trump has explicitly stated he wants iPhones made in America. Apple will need major long-term infrastructure investments outside Asia to diversify supply chains. Cell phones may have been exempted from tariffs, but manufacturing localization requirements would be costly. Current valuation trading between 52-week high and low suggests no directional bias until tariff policy is resolved on July 9.

### Netflix: valuation concerns despite analyst upgrades [00:01:41]

Netflix appears as a surprise upside story with UBS raising price target to 1450 and stock up 40% YTD and doubled YoY. However, valuation at 57x PE (highest in 3 years) combined with forward PE of 48 remains elevated. Critically, subscriber growth of 18 million in Q4 (reaching 302 million) ended in Q1, removing the key growth narrative. Future growth depends on content strategy, but password-sharing crackdown benefits have already been captured. Recommendation is hold to sell until subscriber growth resumes.

### Netflix: structural concerns with IP crackdown [00:02:39]

Netflix's IP address-based password-sharing crackdown creates operational issues for subscribers who travel, limiting usage flexibility. This approach differs from other streaming models that allow some flexibility. The subscriber resilience narrative may be overstated if future policy changes create friction in user experience, particularly for international travelers.

### Snowflake: extreme valuation with deal-dependent thesis [00:02:39]

Snowflake represents an extreme valuation case with PE of 196.91x versus industry average of 29.52x. The entire upside thesis is dependent on a specific multi-year data investment cycle deal materializing as expected. If the anticipated deal fails to materialize or delivers disappointing results, significant downside is likely. Current valuation significantly overprices potential scenarios. Built on AWS/Azure infrastructure with singular point dependencies adds operational risk.

### Snowflake: architecture and competitive positioning [00:03:41]

Snowflake has strong technical architecture built on top cloud providers (AWS/Azure), suggesting it has a strong runway for growth. However, this architecture creates dependencies on the underlying cloud providers. The recommendation is to wait for more evidence that the anticipated data investment cycle is materializing before deploying capital, given the extreme valuation premium.
