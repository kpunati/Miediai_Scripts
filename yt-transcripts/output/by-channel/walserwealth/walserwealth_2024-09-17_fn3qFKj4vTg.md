## Summary

This segment from Fox Business 'Mornings with Maria' features Rebecca Walser and Dennis Gartman analyzing major financial and political developments from September 13, 2024. Central topics include the Federal Reserve's upcoming rate decision — with Walser arguing a 50-basis-point cut would be a warning sign — and the Boeing machinists strike that halted 737 MAX production. The show also covers the Harris-Trump debate aftermath, expiration risks for the Tax Cuts and Jobs Act, and government AI policy meetings with tech leaders.

## Key Takeaways

- Walser believes a 50-basis-point Fed cut would signal hidden economic distress, given that CPI and PPI remain elevated.
- The US surpassed $1 trillion in annual interest payments on its $35.3 trillion national debt, constraining future Fed rate policy.
- Boeing faces a significant production crisis as 96% of machinists voted to strike after rejecting a 25% wage increase contract.
- Expiration of the Tax Cuts and Jobs Act would raise taxes for an estimated 62% of US households, averaging $2,800 per household.
- Harris declined to answer directly whether Americans are better off than four years ago during the September 2024 debate.
- Dennis Gartman argues once the Fed starts easing, the fed funds rate will likely fall to 3-3.5% over the following year regardless of whether the initial cut is 25 or 50 basis points.
- Government meetings with OpenAI and Google on AI data centers and energy were characterized as a double-edged sword for investors.

## Detailed Notes

### Presidential Debate Aftermath and Electoral Landscape [00:00:02]

Harris called for a second debate after the September 10, 2024 face-off, but Trump refused. Walser noted that Harris avoided directly answering whether Americans are better off economically, and a Reuters poll of undecided voters showed 60% leaning toward Trump after the debate. Swing states including Arizona, Georgia, Michigan, Nevada, North Carolina, Pennsylvania, and Wisconsin are considered toss-ups.

### Federal Reserve Rate Cut Decision [00:09:26]

Ahead of the September FOMC meeting, debate centered on 25 vs. 50 basis points. Walser argued that hot CPI and PPI data mean a 50-bps cut would signal unknown negative data. Gartman believed rates would ultimately fall to 3-3.5% regardless of the initial cut size, given historical Fed easing patterns. The ECB had already cut rates twice while the Fed had not yet acted.

### Boeing Machinist Strike [00:17:47]

Over 30,000 Boeing machinists went on strike just after midnight, with 96% voting against a tentative agreement that included a 25% wage increase over the contract life (workers sought 40%). The strike halted 737 MAX production and was expected to last at least through November. Gartman warned this could set a precedent encouraging other unions to demand similar increases.

### National Debt and Tax Policy [00:22:59]

The US spent over $1 trillion in interest payments in fiscal year 2024, a 30% year-over-year jump, on a $35.3 trillion national debt. Walser argued this effectively caps future Fed rate hikes, as the government cannot afford higher interest costs. Separately, expiration of the Tax Cuts and Jobs Act at end of 2025 would raise taxes for 62% of households, with an average increase of $2,800 per household according to the Tax Foundation.

### AI Industry and Amazon Alexa Bias Allegations [00:08:22]

Senior Biden administration officials met with OpenAI's Sam Altman and Google's Ruth Porat to discuss AI data centers and energy infrastructure. Separately, Amazon's Alexa drew congressional scrutiny from Rep. Jim Jordan after giving asymmetric responses to questions about Trump vs. Harris, with the company attributing it to an error.
