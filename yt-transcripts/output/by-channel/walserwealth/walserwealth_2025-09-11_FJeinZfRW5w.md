# Rate Cut Outlook

**Channel:** Rebecca Walser
**Published:** 2025-09-11
**URL:** https://youtube.com/watch?v=FJeinZfRW5w
**Duration:** 23:14

## Summary

In this interview-style discussion, Rebecca Walser explains why she believes the Fed is caught between its employment and price stability mandates, a conflict that makes meaningful rate cuts both necessary and dangerous. She traces persistent inflation to the approximately $20 trillion in global COVID-era stimulus and argues the US has locked itself into a cycle of perpetual monetary easing to sustain asset bubbles created by cheap credit. Walser's investment positioning favors equities, gold (citing Basel 3 elevation of gold to tier-one asset status), and Ethereum given its smart contract and stablecoin infrastructure.

## Key Takeaways

- The Fed's dual mandate creates a structural conflict when unemployment rises and inflation remains above target simultaneously.
- Modern monetary theory adopted after 2008 and the $20 trillion in COVID-era stimulus have made 2% inflation essentially unachievable in Walser's view.
- Asset bubbles in real estate and other sectors reflect decades of cheap credit; unwinding requires constant money supply growth, creating a debt spiral.
- Walser favors equities, gold, and crypto as hedges against dollar debasement and recommends Ethereum specifically for its smart contract and stablecoin role.
- NASDAQ's announced tokenization of equities for 24/7 trading could accelerate the convergence of traditional and digital asset markets.
- Massive structural shifts in the global monetary order — including BRICS expansion and dollar multipolarity — are expected within 18-36 months.
- Tariff revenue ($40B/month per Lutnik) is insufficient to close the federal fiscal deficit gap but may partly replace lost tax revenue.

## Detailed Notes

### Fed Rate Cut Outlook and the Dual Mandate Problem [00:00:45]

With core PCE at 2.9% and jobless claims at their highest since October 2021, Walser argues Powell faces conflicting mandates. Markets have already priced in cuts (rates fell before any Fed action). Polymarket shows 88.8% odds of a 25 bps cut on September 17, with the market pricing three to four cuts totaling ~100 bps by January 2026.

### Modern Monetary Theory, COVID Stimulus, and Permanent Inflation [00:03:45]

Walser traces the US shift to modern monetary theory post-2008, arguing it enables unlimited deficit spending as long as the tax base can service interest. Approximately $20 trillion was globally printed during COVID-19, with M2 money supply jumping exponentially from March to June 2020 and never fully retracting — hitting a new all-time high in the most recent reading. This makes transitory inflation impossible and locks the Fed into perpetual easing.

### Investment Positioning: Gold, Equities, and Crypto [00:14:51]

Walser has been a gold advocate since 2020, citing Basel 3's reclassification of gold from a tier-three to tier-one asset driving central bank accumulation, especially from China and Turkey demanding physical delivery. She also favors broad equities and Ethereum specifically for its role in stablecoin infrastructure and smart contracts. Bitcoin is characterized as 'digital gold' while Ethereum is preferred for tokenization upside. NASDAQ's announcement of on-chain equity tokenization for 24/7 trading is viewed as an important catalyst.

### Geopolitical Risks and Dollar Debasement Timeline [00:17:52]

BRICS nations now represent more than 40% of global GDP, surpassing the US-led bloc. Walser expects corporate CFOs globally to begin diversifying away from USD-denominated assets over the next 18 months, with massive structural changes in the monetary order within 36 months. She views crypto, AI, robotics, and quantum computing as the fourth industrial revolution technologies that must grow faster than debt service costs to prevent an unrecoverable debt spiral.
