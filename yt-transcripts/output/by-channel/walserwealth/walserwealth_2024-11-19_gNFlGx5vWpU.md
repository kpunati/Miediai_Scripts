# Global Demand Is Weakening...Look At Crude Oil

**Channel:** Rebecca Walser
**Published:** 2024-11-19
**URL:** https://youtube.com/watch?v=gNFlGx5vWpU
**Duration:** 32:14

## Summary

Rebecca Walser joins 'Mornings With Maria' on Fox Business on November 15, 2024 alongside Dennis Gartman and other commentators to discuss a wide range of topics including Trump's early cabinet picks, weakening global oil demand, and the Fed's cautious stance on further rate cuts. Walser highlights weakening Chinese and German economic conditions as drivers of a potential crude oil price decline toward the $51-$54 range if the $64/barrel support breaks. The panel also discusses the newly announced DOGE initiative led by Elon Musk and Vivek Ramaswamy, with Walser warning about the risks of a debt spiral if fiscal reform does not occur.

## Key Takeaways

- Trump's cabinet nominations including RFK Jr for HHS signal disruptive change to food regulation and vaccine policy oversight.
- Global oil demand is weakening due to economic slowdowns in China and Germany, with Walser flagging $64/barrel as a critical crude oil support level.
- Jay Powell signaled no rush to cut interest rates further, citing a 'stout' economy, contributing to market pullback.
- DOGE's mandate is to cut federal government spending, targeting waste in entitlement programs and soft costs in government contracts.
- Walser warns the US could lose its reserve currency status if the debt spiral is not addressed, citing interest payments as the third-largest federal expense.
- Vaccine manufacturers face regulatory risk as Congress considers removing liability immunity established under Reagan-era legislation.

## Detailed Notes

### Trump Cabinet Nominations and RFK Jr [00:00:00]

Trump nominates RFK Jr as HHS Secretary, focusing on overhauling food production and reexamining vaccine approvals. Walser notes vaccine manufacturers gained blanket immunity under Reagan-era legislation, and Congress is discussing removing that immunity. Other nominees discussed include Doug Collins for Veterans Affairs, Todd Blanche as Deputy AG, and Doug Burgum as Interior Secretary.

### Oil Demand and Global Economic Weakness [00:21:06]

Walser cites China's economic malaise and Germany's manufacturing crisis (Volkswagen considering closing plants for the first time in 87 years) as evidence of weakening global demand reflected in crude oil prices near $68/barrel. She argues that if crude breaks below $64/barrel, prices could fall to $51-$54/barrel. The IEA forecasts global oil supply will exceed demand in 2025 even with OPEC+ cuts in place.

### Federal Reserve and Interest Rates [00:17:56]

Fed Chair Powell stated the economy is performing in a 'stout' manner and the central bank is in no rush to cut rates. Gartman notes futures markets were pricing roughly 50/50 odds of a 25 basis point cut in December. The 10-year Treasury yield sits near 4.4% and gold is at approximately $2,571. Markets pulled back on Powell's comments.

### DOGE and Federal Fiscal Concerns [00:25:15]

The Department of Government Efficiency (DOGE) is accepting applications for cost-cutting roles, with Elon Musk stating the goal is 'super high IQ small government revolutionaries willing to work 80+ hours per week.' The US is $36 trillion in debt. Walser warns that failure to reform Social Security and Medicare—the programs expected to run out of money within a decade—could cause the US to lose its reserve currency status.

### JPMorgan and Treasury Secretary Race [00:23:10]

Trump ruled out Jamie Dimon for Treasury Secretary via Truth Social; Dimon responded at the APEC summit saying he has not had a boss in 25 years. The Treasury Secretary race appeared to be between Scott Bessent and Howard Lutnick, with Walser expressing preference for Bessent.
