## Summary

Rebecca Walser presents an argument that the US-Saudi petrodollar arrangement effectively ended in June 2024 when Saudi Arabia declined to renew the agreement, citing both Western media obfuscation and Saudi Arabia's concurrent entry into the BIS mBridge CBDC project. She situates this development within a broader context of Basel III banking reforms, gold reclassification as a tier-one asset, and the global shift of physical gold from West to East. The episode concludes with Walser drawing parallels between BRICS member nations' symbolic animals and biblical beasts described in Daniel and Revelation, arguing the convergence is statistically improbable without prophetic significance.

## Key Takeaways

- Saudi Arabia did not renew the 50-year petrodollar agreement with the US upon its June 2024 expiration, a development Walser argues was suppressed by Western mainstream media.
- Saudi Arabia joined the BIS-led mBridge project for cross-border CBDC transactions as a full participant just four days before the petrodollar agreement expired.
- Basel III reforms reclassified gold from a tier-three to a tier-one asset in 2021, prompting central banks to take physical gold delivery and move reserves from West to East.
- US banks have resisted Basel III capital requirements as mathematically impossible to meet given the fractional reserve system and inflated money supply, leading Fed Chair Powell to signal rollbacks.
- The BRICS bloc now includes Brazil, Russia, India, China, South Africa, Egypt, Ethiopia, Iran, UAE, and potentially Saudi Arabia, representing a formidable economic challenge to the G7.
- Walser connects the national animals of BRICS nations (jaguar, bear, tiger, panda, lion) to the composite beast described in Daniel 7 and Revelation 13 as a prophetic convergence.

## Detailed Notes

### Petrodollar Agreement Expiration and Media Obfuscation [00:04:14]

Walser claims the US-Saudi petrodollar agreement, originally established in 1974, expired on June 9, 2024 when MBS chose not to renew it. She contrasts a Times of India report confirming the agreement's end with a MarketWatch article dismissing the story as fake news, arguing Western outlets systematically obfuscate such developments to prevent public reaction.

### BRICS Expansion and Saudi Arabia's Membership [00:11:38]

Walser references Bloomberg and BBC reports from January-February 2024 confirming Saudi Arabia joined BRICS alongside Egypt, Ethiopia, Iran, and the UAE effective January 1, 2024. She contrasts this with World Economic Outlook data from April 2024 still listing Saudi Arabia with an asterisk as undecided, characterizing the discrepancy as deliberate obfuscation.

### Basel III, Bank Capital Requirements, and Gold Reclassification [00:15:54]

Walser explains Basel III as a post-2008 international banking reform requiring systematized risk-scoring and higher capital reserves for banks over $100 billion. She emphasizes that gold was upgraded from a tier-three to a tier-one asset under Basel III in 2021, requiring one-to-one allocated physical holdings with no counterparty risk, driving a global surge in central bank physical gold purchases and the movement of gold from Western to Eastern vaults.

### mBridge CBDC Project and Shift Away from the Dollar [00:28:23]

On June 5, 2024, the BIS publicly announced Saudi Arabia joined its mBridge project as a full participant alongside China, Thailand, Hong Kong, and the UAE. Walser notes that 27 observers including the IMF, World Bank, and Goldman Sachs are also participating, and that the project reached minimum viable product stage after three years, indicating meaningful progress toward an alternative cross-border settlement system outside the US dollar.

### Biblical Prophecy and the BRICS Beast Analogy [00:34:41]

In the final segment, Walser reads from Daniel 7 and Revelation 13 describing a composite beast with attributes of a leopard, bear, and lion with ten horns, and maps these symbols onto BRICS member nations (Brazil's jaguar resembling a leopard, Russia and China as bears, India's tiger resembling a leopard, Iran as a lion). She argues the statistical probability of this alignment being coincidental is negligible, suggesting the BRICS convergence fulfills biblical end-times prophecy.
