# META & Trump Settlement, 2025 Most Admired Companies, and More DeepSeek

## Summary

Rebecca Walser appears on Fox Business 'Mornings with Maria' on January 30, 2025, in a wide-ranging segment covering several major news stories. The episode opens with Gordon Chang analyzing the DeepSeek AI model, alleging it was distilled from OpenAI's GPT-4 in violation of intellectual property rights and represents a coordinated Chinese Communist Party propaganda effort. Subsequent segments address Meta's $25 million settlement with President Trump over his 2021 account suspension, Q4 earnings from Meta and Microsoft, the Fed's decision to hold rates steady, and the 2025 Fortune Most Admired Companies list. The broadcast also covers the Washington DC midair collision between an American Airlines regional jet and a military helicopter. Calamos President John Koudounis discusses the firm's newly launched downside-protected Bitcoin ETF suite, and the segment closes with GDP and jobless claims data.

## Key Takeaways

- DeepSeek's R1 model is characterized by US commentators as having been distilled from OpenAI's GPT-4 without authorization, raising IP theft and national security concerns.
- Meta settled with Trump for $25 million without admitting wrongdoing, and Zuckerberg's public alignment with the Trump Administration signals a significant Silicon Valley political realignment.
- Meta Q4 earnings were strong — sales up 21% and net income up 49% — driven in part by AI investments, while Microsoft issued weaker-than-expected cloud guidance.
- The Fed held rates steady and Chair Powell signaled no rush to adjust policy, while Trump publicly criticized the Fed for past inflationary policies.
- Apple, Microsoft, Amazon, Nvidia, and Berkshire Hathaway topped the 2025 Fortune Most Admired Companies list, with tech firms dominating due to their central role in the economy.
- Calamos launched the world's first 100% downside-protected Bitcoin ETF with an 11.65% cap rate, with additional tranches at 90% and 80% protection planned.
- Q4 2024 GDP of 2.3% came in below the 2.6% estimate; Walser alleges prior-administration data was politically manipulated and expects further downward revisions.

## Detailed Notes

### DeepSeek, US-China AI Rivalry, and IP Theft Allegations [00:00:00]

Gordon Chang argues DeepSeek's R1 was distilled from OpenAI's GPT-4, constituting intellectual property theft. He further alleges the January 20 release date — coinciding with Trump's inauguration — was orchestrated by the Chinese Communist Party as a propaganda effort to demonstrate the futility of US sanctions. Chang calls for a near-total ban on chip exports to China, including legacy chips, framing AI as an existential geopolitical struggle.

### Xi Jinping's Weakened Position and Chinese Military Purges [00:03:09]

Maria Bartiromo reports from Davos sources that Xi Jinping suffered a mini-stroke last summer and subsequently lost direct oversight of the Chinese military. Chang corroborates this, noting military propaganda organs are praising 'collective leadership' — a direct challenge to Xi's authoritarian style — and highlights purges in the Rocket Force, which controls China's nuclear arsenal, as a particularly dangerous sign of internal instability.

### Washington DC Plane Collision and Breaking News Coverage [00:08:22]

The segment covers the collision of a PSA Airlines regional jet (American Airlines subsidiary) with a US Army Black Hawk helicopter near Reagan National Airport, with 60 passengers, four crew members, and three soldiers aboard. American Airlines stock is noted as down approximately 2% on the news. No survivors had been found at the time of the broadcast.

### Meta-Trump Settlement and Silicon Valley Realignment [00:16:49]

Meta settled Trump's 2021 lawsuit over his account suspension for $25 million — $22 million to Trump's Presidential Library and the remainder to legal fees — without admitting wrongdoing. Panelists highlight the symbolic significance of Zuckerberg attending Trump's inaugural dinner and publicly praising the administration on Meta's earnings call, describing it as a complete reversal from Silicon Valley's 2016 stance toward Trump.

### Federal Reserve Policy and Earnings from Meta and Microsoft [00:22:03]

The Fed held rates steady at its first 2025 meeting, with Powell citing a less urgent need to adjust policy. Trump publicly attacked the Fed on Truth Social, blaming it for the worst inflation in US history. Meta reported strong Q4 results — sales up 21% year-over-year, net income up 49% at nearly $21 billion — while Microsoft's Azure cloud revenue guidance disappointed, sending its stock down over 4%.

### 2025 Most Admired Companies and CEO Confidence Under Trump [00:26:13]

Korn Ferry's Alan Guarino discusses the 2025 Fortune Most Admired Companies list, with Apple leading followed by Microsoft, Amazon, Nvidia, and Berkshire Hathaway. Tech dominance reflects its central role in the modern economy. Guarino notes CEOs are operating at high RPM — forming SWAT teams to evaluate opportunities and threats from the new administration's rapid executive order activity. Calamos's Koudounis adds that 83% of CEOs feel optimistic about investing, driven primarily by expected deregulation.

### Calamos Bitcoin Downside-Protected ETF and GDP Data [00:35:42]

Calamos launched the first 100% downside-protected Bitcoin ETF with an 11.65% annual cap rate. Two additional tranches are launching the following Tuesday: 90% protected with an estimated 28-31% cap and 80% protected with an estimated 50-55% cap. Separately, Q4 2024 GDP came in at 2.3% — below the 2.6% estimate and weaker than Q3's 3.1% — while initial jobless claims of 207,000 beat expectations. Walser alleges prior-administration data was politically inflated and predicts further downward revisions under the Trump Administration.
