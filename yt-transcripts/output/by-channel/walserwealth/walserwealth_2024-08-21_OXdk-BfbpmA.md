# Economists Should Be Worried About The Remaining Yen/$ Carry Trade

## Summary

Rebecca Walser uses IMF and Federal Reserve research papers to explain Japan's decades-long carry trade, in which the Bank of Japan borrowed at ultra-low short-term rates and invested in longer-duration assets to earn positive yield spread. She argues the Bank of Japan's two 2024 rate increases — to 0.1% in March and 0.25% in July — triggered the massive August 5, 2024 global selloff by forcing margin calls on carry trade positions. Walser draws a parallel between Japan's duration-mismatch strategy and US Treasury Secretary Janet Yellen's reliance on short-term issuance. She concludes the carry trade unwind is far from complete and warns viewers to consider taking profits given three additional pre-election threats: a cyberattack on infrastructure, a WHO pandemic declaration for mpox, and continued market fragility.

## Key Takeaways

- The Bank of Japan's rate hikes in 2024 dramatically increased the cost of yen-funded carry trades, triggering a global margin-call event on August 5.
- JP Morgan estimated $4 trillion in commercial carry trade exposure remained open as of mid-August, separate from the Bank of Japan's estimated $20 trillion institutional position.
- The US Treasury's reliance on short-term debt issuance mirrors Japan's duration mismatch, making US fiscal space vulnerable to rising rates.
- The Bank of Japan has begun shifting from US Treasuries into riskier assets like CLOs to maintain positive yield spread, increasing the probability of default in its portfolio.
- CNN Fear and Greed Index was in extreme fear territory as of August 14, 2024, suggesting markets had not returned to complacency despite a partial recovery.
- Walser advises viewers to consider profit-taking during the three months before the November 2024 US election given compounding geopolitical and financial risks.

## Detailed Notes

### Introduction and Market Context [00:01:03]

Walser provides a general update after a gap since June, noting she has been busy growing her practice. She frames the episode as a market update for August 14, 2024, covering events since her last recording including the volatile August 5 selloff.

### Japan's Carry Trade Mechanics [00:03:10]

Walser references a 2019 IMF paper and a 2024 Federal Reserve Bank of St. Louis working paper to establish the long history of Japan's carry trade. She explains that Japan's government borrows at short-term floating rates and invests in longer-duration assets to earn a yield spread exceeding 3% of GDP. She also introduces the concept of duration mismatch as the core vulnerability.

### US Treasury Parallel Under Janet Yellen [00:07:25]

Walser argues that Janet Yellen's preference for short-term treasury issuance replicates Japan's duration mismatch at the US federal level. She contends this strategy locked in higher refinancing costs after the Fed began raising rates in 2022 and warns this represents a structural fiscal vulnerability for the United States.

### 2024 Bank of Japan Rate Hikes and Market Selloff [00:11:39]

Walser explains the Bank of Japan's first rate hike since 2007 occurred on March 19, 2024, moving from negative rates to a 0-0.1% range. A second hike on July 31 raised the target to 0.25%. Combined with a weaker-than-expected US jobs report showing 114,000 jobs versus an expected 185,000, this double shock triggered the August 5 global selloff. She notes the yen appreciated from approximately 162 to 142-146 per dollar, generating margin calls on dollar-denominated carry trade investments.

### Scale of Unwinding and Bank of Japan Portfolio Risk [00:23:18]

JP Morgan assessed roughly $4 trillion in commercial carry trade transactions as only partially unwound by August 14. Walser distinguishes this from the Bank of Japan's own estimated $20 trillion institutional position. She warns the Bank of Japan has begun replacing safe US Treasuries with collateralized loan obligations and other leveraged debt to maintain yield, substantially increasing its credit risk exposure.

### Pre-Election Risk Outlook [00:29:36]

Walser identifies three threats she expects to materialize before the November 2024 US election: a cyberattack or electrical grid disruption, a WHO declaration of a global mpox pandemic, and continued financial market instability from the incomplete carry trade unwind. She references the CNN Fear and Greed Index sitting at extreme fear territory and recommends viewers consider locking in year-to-date gains.
