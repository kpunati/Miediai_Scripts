# Market Insights on Bloomberg Asia

**Channel:** Rebecca Walser
**Published:** 2025-09-25
**URL:** https://youtube.com/watch?v=gnjdOpNwFfo
**Duration:** 13:57

## Summary

In this Bloomberg Daybreak Asia interview, Rebecca Walser of Walser Wealth Management surveys the post-April market recovery, arguing that financial media overcorrected on tariff fears and caused retail investors to miss significant gains. She expresses concern about a structural US debt spiral driven by compounding interest costs, advocates for nuclear power as the only mathematically viable solution for AI data center energy demand, and argues that the financial system is transitioning toward blockchain-based currencies and tokenized hard assets as fiat currency loses credibility.

## Key Takeaways

- The financial media's April tariff panic caused many retail investors to sell and miss the subsequent market rally of approximately $15 trillion in market cap gains.
- AI monetization is moving beyond productivity apps into blockchain-based financial infrastructure, illustrated by Figure Technologies' blockchain home equity loans.
- Nuclear power is Walser's preferred investment for energy demand driven by AI data centers, citing mathematical insufficiency of coal and natural gas.
- The US federal debt spiral is structural; interest payments surpassed Medicare and Medicaid in May 2024 and may reach $1.2 trillion next year.
- Walser sees China's geopolitical and economic strategy as a long-term threat to US dollar dominance, despite China's current domestic economic struggles.
- Gold prices near record highs are driven by central bank demand following Basel 3 reclassifying gold to tier-one asset status, and eroding confidence in fiat currency systems.

## Detailed Notes

### Market Recovery and Investor Sentiment [00:00:53]

Walser argues that financial media overcorrected on tariff risks in April, causing many investors to sell and miss the subsequent rally. She reports a drift in financial media ratings and a shift toward alternative media and podcasts. The S&P is trading at approximately 25x earnings and is up roughly $15 trillion from April lows, which she acknowledges is expensive but reflective of the AI transformation premium.

### AI Monetization and Technology Investments [00:03:48]

Walser distinguishes between AI as a productivity tool and actual financial monetization on the blockchain. She highlights Figure Technologies as an example of blockchain-based financial products going to market, offering home equity loans processed in 10 days versus the conventional 42-day cycle. She sees stable coin legislation (the Genius Act) as early infrastructure for a new currency system with one-to-one dollar reserves.

### Nuclear Power and Energy Infrastructure [00:05:17]

Walser argues that coal and natural gas power are mathematically insufficient to meet AI data center demand, making nuclear power the only viable solution. She specifically calls out Osclo, a company based in Idaho projecting a nuclear power plant operational between 2027 and 2028. She expresses high conviction that power rationing is a near-certain outcome if AI infrastructure outpaces power grid expansion.

### Debt Spiral and De-Dollarization Risks [00:07:01]

US interest payments became the second-largest budget line item in May 2024, surpassing Medicare and Medicaid, and are projected at nearly $1.2 trillion next year. Walser describes this as a structural debt spiral where interest compounds on itself. She identifies China's belt and road initiative, its takeover of the BIS project m-bridge, and its long-term written plans for economic supremacy as serious de-dollarization threats, though she acknowledges China's current domestic economic difficulties.

### Gold and the End of Fiat Currency [00:12:50]

Gold near record highs is primarily central-bank driven, accelerated by Basel 3 reclassifying gold from tier-three to tier-one asset status. Walser views this as evidence of waning confidence in fiat monetary systems and argues the financial world is moving toward blockchain-based tokenization of hard assets with publicly transparent, traceable reserves.
