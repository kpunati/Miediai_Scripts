# America Is Looking For A Big Change

**Channel:** Rebecca Walser
**Published:** 2025-01-07
**URL:** https://youtube.com/watch?v=Ed5fhFS71KA
**Duration:** 34:44

## Summary

This Fox Business segment from January 6, 2025 covers several major political and economic stories as Congress prepares to certify the 2024 election results. The main legislative debate concerns whether Republicans should pursue one comprehensive reconciliation bill ('one big beautiful bill') covering border security, tax cuts, energy, and the debt ceiling, or split it into two bills. Panelists including Rebecca Walser, Liz Peek, and Kathy Entwistle discuss implications for markets and fiscal policy. The segment also covers the Nippon Steel/US Steel acquisition dispute, Biden's blocking of the deal, and the resulting lawsuits filed by both companies.

## Key Takeaways

- House Speaker Mike Johnson plans one reconciliation bill targeting a House vote by early April and presidential signature by Memorial Day.
- Senator Lindsey Graham and others in the Senate prefer a two-bill approach, with border security first and tax cuts later in the year.
- The SALT deduction cap is a major sticking point for Republican members from high-tax states like New York and California.
- Nippon Steel and US Steel filed lawsuits against Biden, CFIUS, Cleveland Cliffs, and the Steelworkers union seeking a new CFIUS review under the Trump administration.
- Trump aides were reportedly scaling back universal tariff plans to apply only to critical economic sectors rather than all imports.
- Rebecca Walser warned that US 10-year yield levels signal bond market concern about continued deficit spending and debt limit increases.
- Panelists were divided on the Nippon Steel deal, with Liz Peek supporting the acquisition as strengthening US steel and US-Japan relations, while Walser argued for supply chain sovereignty.

## Detailed Notes

### Trump's 'One Big Beautiful Bill' Strategy [00:01:06]

House Speaker Mike Johnson confirmed on Fox Business that Trump prefers a single reconciliation bill covering border security, energy, tax cuts, no tax on tips, and the debt ceiling. Johnson targeted a House vote in the first week of April with a Memorial Day deadline. This approach avoids needing Democratic votes for a 60-vote Senate threshold.

### Senate Opposition and SALT Deduction Battle [00:02:11]

Senator Lindsey Graham expressed concern about the one-bill approach, arguing border security should come first and not be held hostage to tax cuts. The SALT deduction cap is a central conflict: members from high-tax states want it raised, while members from low-tax states like South Carolina oppose subsidizing California and New York. Panelists noted Republicans can lose very few votes in the House and the Senate given thin majorities.

### Nippon Steel / US Steel Deal and Lawsuits [00:18:37]

President Biden blocked Nippon Steel's acquisition of US Steel, citing national security and supply chain concerns. US Steel CEO Dave Burritt called the decision politically corrupt, alleging Biden acted to repay union endorsements. Nippon Steel and US Steel announced a pair of lawsuits targeting Biden, CFIUS, Cleveland Cliffs, and the United Steelworkers union, seeking a new CFIUS review under the Trump administration. Sources suggested the deal would likely be approved under a new review.

### Tariff Plans and Market Implications [00:33:28]

The Washington Post reported Trump aides are narrowing tariff plans to cover only critical import sectors rather than applying universal tariffs. Liz Peek called this the 'obvious outcome,' noting blanket tariffs sacrifice strategic flexibility. Walser raised concern about bond market signals from elevated 10-year yields and the debt limit debate. Futures markets were trading higher at the time of the broadcast, partly in response to the tariff news.
