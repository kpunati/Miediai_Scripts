# Taxes, Retirement & 2026 Global Shifts

**Channel:** Rebecca Walser
**Published:** 2026-02-03
**URL:** https://youtube.com/watch?v=Rtm96m-PgTg
**Duration:** 37:46

## Summary

Rebecca Walser joins Rey Trevino on the Crude Truth Podcast to deliver a wide-ranging financial outlook for 2026, opening with a critique of traditional 401k deferral strategy in the context of a historically low tax environment and an impending baby boomer retirement wave. She argues that Roth conversions and after-tax brokerage accounts offer superior long-term tax efficiency, and extends her analysis to macroeconomic themes including BRICS-driven de-dollarization, stablecoin adoption, and the fourth industrial revolution sectors of AI, quantum computing, robotics, and blockchain. The conversation also covers US energy infrastructure vulnerabilities and practical personal finance tips such as auditing subscriptions.

## Key Takeaways

- The 401k was originally designed for highly compensated executives and was never intended to be the default retirement vehicle for all Americans, contributing to a multi-trillion dollar retirement savings shortfall.
- With the US in its third-lowest tax regime historically, Walser argues this is the ideal window to convert pre-tax retirement funds to Roth accounts before anticipated rate increases.
- After-tax capital gains rates (max 20%) are more favorable than income tax rates, making non-qualified brokerage accounts an often-overlooked complement to employer retirement plans.
- BRICS nations conducting bilateral trade outside the US dollar signals the beginning of de-dollarization, which could eventually reduce foreign demand for US debt and force dramatic federal budget cuts.
- Investors must participate in the fourth industrial revolution (AI, quantum computing, robotics, blockchain) to avoid being left behind, but must do so with risk tolerance that allows them to withstand volatility.
- Oil and gas have a long runway due to energy demands of AI data centers, but new energy technologies will be needed as fossil fuels alone are insufficient for projected AI energy consumption.
- Practical first steps include auditing and canceling unnecessary subscriptions and looking beyond employer retirement plans to tax-advantaged alternatives.

## Detailed Notes

### 2026 Tax Law Changes and 401k Strategy [00:00:01]

Walser highlights that the One Big Beautiful Bill changed catch-up contribution provisions for individuals aged 50 and over and for highly compensated employees, advising listeners to consult a CPA about adjusting their 401k withholdings accordingly. She argues the 401k was a historical accident—designed for executives in the Revenue Act of 1978—that became the default retirement vehicle for all Americans without actuarial testing, resulting in an estimated 18-25 trillion dollar retirement wealth gap.

### The Case for Roth Conversions and After-Tax Accounts [00:00:01]

Walser contends the US is currently in its third-lowest tax environment in history and that the CBO projected middle-class rates could reach the mid-60s percent when the final baby boomer reaches full retirement age in 2030. She argues that deferring taxes into traditional accounts is a 'tax trap' given these trends, and recommends transitioning to Roth accounts (where principal and all growth are permanently tax-free) or taxable brokerage accounts subject to capital gains rates (max 20%), both of which are more favorable than future expected income tax rates.

### De-Dollarization and Global Currency Transition [00:00:01]

Walser points to BRICS nations (Brazil, Russia, India, China, South Africa, and others including Saudi Arabia) conducting bilateral trade in domestic currencies, bypassing the US dollar. She describes this as the beginning of de-dollarization, with LNG already being transacted in euros in Europe. If foreign demand for US dollars declines, the US could lose approximately 2 trillion dollars annually in debt financing capacity, requiring severe federal budget cuts. She advises Americans to position portfolios in hard assets and blockchain currencies as a hedge.

### Fourth Industrial Revolution Investment Thesis [00:00:01]

Walser identifies four sectors as core to the fourth industrial revolution: AI, quantum computing, robotics, and blockchain. She compares the current period to the dot-com era where monetization created volatility before delivering returns, and cautions that investors must stay the course through negative-return years rather than exiting at losses. She pairs this growth orientation with a continuing position in commodities—particularly gold and silver held since 2020—as a hard-asset ballast.

### Energy Outlook and US Infrastructure Vulnerabilities [00:00:01]

Walser argues oil and gas have a long runway due to AI data center energy demands, but that fossil fuels alone will be insufficient and new energy technologies (including suppressed hydrogen-based technologies) will be needed. She raises US electrical grid vulnerability as a national security concern, noting the grid relies on antiquated infrastructure and that a complete disruption—according to war game analyses—could result in catastrophic population loss due to the country's hyper-dependence on electricity for food supply chains.
