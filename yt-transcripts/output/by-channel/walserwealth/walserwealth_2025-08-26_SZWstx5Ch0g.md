# Intel’s Future, Fed Rate Cuts, and Cracker Barrel Logo Backlash

**Channel:** Rebecca Walser
**Published:** 2025-08-26
**URL:** https://youtube.com/watch?v=SZWstx5Ch0g
**Duration:** 16:57

## Summary

This video compiles three separate Newsmax television appearances by Rebecca Walser, tax attorney and president of Walser Wealth Management, alongside market commentators Mark Lesky and Paula Pant. The segments cover the Federal Reserve's August 2025 Jackson Hole signaling toward rate cuts, the Trump administration's equity stake acquisition in Intel through CHIPS Act and DoD funds, allegations of mortgage fraud against Fed governor Lisa Cook, a consumer culture debate around Cracker Barrel's logo change, and Nissan's decision to delay US EV production. Commentary reflects broadly pro-Trump administration perspectives on economic and regulatory developments.

## Key Takeaways

- Jackson Hole 2025 signaled a likely Fed rate cut, with CME futures showing 85% probability, driven by strong earnings, resilient consumer spending, and a revised-downward jobs report.
- The Trump administration converted CHIPS Act grants and DoD funding into equity ownership in Intel, framed as a sovereign wealth fund-style approach that could face Supreme Court challenges.
- Fed governor Lisa Cook faces scrutiny over two primary residence mortgage declarations within weeks of each other, with commentators calling it a breach of public trust.
- Tariffs were widely expected to settle between 10-15% across major trade partners, and corporations absorbed costs rather than passing them to consumers through August 2025.
- Nissan delayed three electric SUV models at its Mississippi plant until 2028, citing removal of EV tax credits under the 'big beautiful bill' as a key contributing factor.
- Walser and Lesky both endorsed the concept of a US sovereign wealth fund as a mechanism for generating returns on government investment.

## Detailed Notes

### Federal Reserve Rate Cut Signals at Jackson Hole [00:00:00]

All three panelists interpreted Powell's Jackson Hole remarks as a clear signal toward a rate cut. Lesky cited a strong earnings season and corporate absorption of tariff costs as justifying conditions; Pant argued Powell has a historical pattern of being slow to act and is now responding to pressure from dissenting Fed governors. Walser added that Powell's inflation concerns at 2% remain a risk in an accommodative environment.

### US Equity Stake in Intel [00:07:03]

Walser explained the deal as converting approximately $8.9 billion in pre-existing CHIPS Act and DoD funding into equity ownership of Intel rather than new spending. The rationale is securing domestic semiconductor manufacturing for both economic and national security reasons. Lesky referenced the drone supply chain vulnerability as concrete evidence of why US chip production matters strategically, noting that most US military drone chips are currently made in China.

### Lisa Cook Mortgage Fraud Allegations [00:04:32]

Commentators discussed allegations that Fed governor Lisa Cook signed two mortgage applications both claiming primary residence within two to three weeks of each other—one for a Michigan property and one in Atlanta. Panelists noted that while nothing has been adjudicated, a Fed governor is held to a higher standard of public trust and the pattern does not have a plausible innocent explanation.

### Cracker Barrel Logo and Nissan EV Delay [00:08:42]

The Cracker Barrel logo discussion centered on whether removing the rocking figure and barrel was a brand mistake rather than a political statement, with Lesky arguing the company lost its core brand identity. The Nissan segment noted that the automaker delayed three electric SUV models at its Mississippi plant to 2028, attributing the decision to removal of EV tax incentives and stronger demand in Japan and Europe.
