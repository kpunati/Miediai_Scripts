# Fed Day on Fox Business

**Channel:** Rebecca Walser
**Published:** 2026-05-01
**URL:** https://youtube.com/watch?v=ti_s-viFzSY
**Duration:** 13:41

## Summary

Rebecca Walser joins Charles Payne on Fox Business on Fed Day to provide macro commentary and stock picks. She argues the $39 trillion US debt burden is effectively insurmountable without a productivity breakthrough from AI, while remaining broadly bullish on equities due to dollar liquidity, M2 growth, earnings, and AI-driven efficiency. The panel discusses Jerome Powell's surprise announcement that he will remain as a Fed governor rather than fully departing, and what that means for incoming Fed Chair Kevin Warsh's ability to lead monetary policy.

## Key Takeaways

- Walser characterizes the US debt situation as structurally irreversible absent an AI-driven productivity revolution.
- Liquidity, as proxied by M2 money supply growth exceeding $1 trillion since mid-prior-year, remains a key bullish driver for equities.
- The UAE's exit from OPEC raises concerns about petrodollar fragmentation and potential yuan-denominated oil trade.
- Powell's decision to remain as Fed governor after his chairmanship ends could create a shadow leadership dynamic complicating Warsh's policy mandate.
- Stock picks discussed include Intuitive Surgical (ISRG), Credo Technology (CRDO) on silicon photonics, and Comfort Systems USA (FIX) for data center HVAC infrastructure.

## Detailed Notes

### Macro Backdrop: Debt, Dollar, and Liquidity [00:00:00]

Walser frames the US debt at approximately $39 trillion as a structural problem that cannot be reversed, only managed through continued monetary expansion or an AI productivity breakthrough. She remains bullish on equities because the dollar retains world reserve currency status and M2 has grown over $1 trillion since mid-prior year, providing the liquidity essential for bull markets.

### Geopolitical Risks: OPEC and Petrodollar [00:01:26]

The panel highlights the UAE leaving OPEC as weakening the cartel and raising the prospect of oil trade denominated in Chinese yuan, a meaningful risk to the petrodollar system. Ongoing blockade negotiations and Gulf tensions are cited as factors the FOMC is watching before committing to rate cuts.

### Stock Picks: ISRG, CRDO, FIX [00:02:28]

Walser recommends Intuitive Surgical (ISRG) as a physical AI play with a recurring revenue model, citing 57% revenue growth last quarter and a 25% discount from its high. She also backs Credo Technology (CRDO) for its Dust Photonix acquisition enabling silicon photonics, projecting over $500 million in optical photonics revenue next year. Comfort Systems USA (FIX) is highlighted for its $12.5 billion data center HVAC backlog.

### Fed Day: Powell Stays as Governor [00:03:45]

The FOMC held rates unchanged with three dissents. Powell announced his intention to remain as a Fed governor after his chairmanship ends, a surprise that panelists interpret as Powell protecting his integrity given what he viewed as insufficient legal language about not pursuing him. Panelists debate whether his presence will make it harder for Warsh to build consensus and establish leadership.

### Market and Policy Reaction [00:11:53]

Following Powell's statement, the 2-year yield rose approximately 10 basis points and the 10-year rose about 7 basis points, producing a bear flattener. The committee signaled two-sided risk to its mandate and roughly equal probability of cutting or hiking, meaning an extended pause is expected.
