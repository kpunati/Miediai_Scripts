# Trumps "Gold Card", FL Governor, and Copper Imports

**Channel:** Rebecca Walser
**Published:** 2025-02-27
**URL:** https://youtube.com/watch?v=xaqFQOcJUJA
**Duration:** 66:51

## Summary

This segment from Fox Business Mornings with Maria (February 26, 2025) features Rebecca Walser, Ryan Payne, and Adam Johnson reacting to a dense set of breaking news items. Central topics include Trump's proposed Gold Card — a $5 million investment-based immigration path replacing EB-5 — and an executive order directing the Commerce Department to investigate copper import tariffs on national security grounds. The panelists also preview Nvidia's after-close earnings amid Microsoft AI-spending channel checks, assess Lowe's and TJX earnings, and react to the dock workers' 62% wage deal.

## Key Takeaways

- Trump's Gold Card program would charge $5 million per visa, replacing EB-5, with proceeds earmarked for deficit reduction and a possible sovereign wealth fund.
- Commerce Secretary Lutnick noted the EB-5 program was 'full of nonsense, make believe, and fraud' and the Gold Card is designed to attract high-quality global investors.
- The copper tariff investigation cites US smelting and refining capacity lagging behind a single foreign producer (China) that controls over 50% of global capacity.
- Ryan Payne argued Nvidia is relatively cheap at 28x forward earnings despite being up ~800% since 2022, with $9 trillion in cash on the sidelines expected to flow into AI stocks.
- Adam Johnson dismissed DeepSeek as a genuine AI threat, citing evidence that it used Nvidia chips and reverse-engineered ChatGPT code despite its low stated cost.
- Dock workers secured a 62% wage increase over six years but the panel criticized the automation restrictions as economically counterproductive.
- House Republicans narrowly passed a budget blueprint unlocking the reconciliation process for tax cuts, border security, and spending cuts.

## Detailed Notes

### Trump Gold Card Immigration Program [00:09:23]

Trump announced a $5 million 'Gold Card' visa to replace the EB-5 program, offering green card privileges and a citizenship pathway. Commerce Secretary Howard Lutnick described EB-5 as fraud-ridden and low-priced; the Gold Card targets wealthy global investors willing to go through vetting. Panelists generally supported the concept, noting many countries already offer citizenship-by-investment programs, but cautioned it should not be the only immigration path.

### Copper Import Tariff Investigation [00:13:35]

Trump ordered the Commerce Department to investigate potential copper tariffs, framing copper as a critical material for national security and technology. The executive order noted that US smelting and refining capacity lags significantly behind a single foreign producer controlling over 50% of global capacity. Rebecca Walser characterized the move as implementing reciprocal — not merely retaliatory — tariffs that other nations already impose on US goods.

### Lowe's and Consumer Earnings Watch [00:00:00]

Lowe's posted a double earnings beat ($1.93 vs $1.84 EPS estimate; $18.6B vs $18.2B revenue) with same-store sales up 2%, sending shares up ~3.5%. Guidance for 2025 comp sales was flat, mirroring Home Depot's cautious macro commentary. TJX was also due to report, with the panel noting consumer sentiment dropped sharply from 71.1 in January to 64 in February, signaling belt-tightening.

### Nvidia Earnings and AI Spending Outlook [00:19:53]

Nvidia was set to report after the close amid concerns from Microsoft channel checks suggesting potential AI capex slowdowns. Ryan Payne countered that hyperscalers plan ~$320 billion in AI infrastructure spend in 2025, viewing Nvidia at 28x forward earnings as relatively inexpensive. Adam Johnson highlighted Sterling Infrastructure's strong data center earnings as contradicting the channel-check narrative, while also dismissing DeepSeek's claimed $6M development cost as misleading.

### Dock Workers Contract and Budget Blueprint [00:15:42]

More than 25,000 ILA dock workers approved a six-year contract with a 62% wage increase and protections barring autonomous machinery (except self-stacking cranes) at East and Gulf Coast ports. Panelists applauded wage gains but criticized automation restrictions as unsustainable. Separately, House Republicans passed a budget blueprint opening reconciliation for tax cuts and border spending; the Senate still needs to align on a single-bill approach.
