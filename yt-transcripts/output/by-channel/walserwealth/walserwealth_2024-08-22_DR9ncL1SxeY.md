# We Are Entering The Convergence Of All Things

**Channel:** Rebecca Walser
**Published:** 2024-08-22
**URL:** https://youtube.com/watch?v=DR9ncL1SxeY
**Duration:** 17:06

## Summary

Rebecca Walser joins Basil Valentine to discuss U.S. macroeconomics and the 2024 presidential race. She traces the current asset bubble environment to the post-2008 adoption of near-zero interest rates and quantitative easing, arguing that artificially cheap capital inflated real estate, equities, and derivative markets far beyond sustainable levels. She contends that economic data has been obscured and that reconciliation to true value is inevitable. The political segment covers Harris's policy positions, alleged media bias, tech censorship, and Trump's demonstrated fortitude following the assassination attempt.

## Key Takeaways

- Walser argues that the Fed's near-zero interest rate policy from 2009 to 2022 created systemic asset bubbles across equities, real estate, and complex debt instruments.
- She characterizes post-2008 policy as Modern Monetary Theory in practice, distinguishing it sharply from Clinton-era fiscal surpluses.
- The Fed's rapid rate hikes in 2022 were presented as the first meaningful response to two decades of money printing, causing the only significant pullback in that period.
- Walser expresses concern about the accuracy of official jobs data, citing revisions that she claims reallocated gains from full-time domestic workers to part-time non-citizens.
- On the election, Walser challenges the lack of transparent policy positions from Harris and predicts potential disruption before November 2024.
- The overall thesis is that systemic fragility has been repeatedly papered over and that a true reconciliation of asset values remains outstanding.

## Detailed Notes

### Post-2008 Monetary Policy and Asset Bubbles [00:02:06]

Walser argues that the TARP program in 2008 began a policy of never allowing economic contraction, and that from 2009 onward the Fed embraced near-zero rates and money printing consistent with Modern Monetary Theory. She contends the ~$20 trillion in global money creation during 2020–2021 inflated all asset classes, citing stock market performance from 2009 to present as evidence.

### Derivative Exposure and Systemic Risk [00:07:25]

Walser claims that artificially low capital costs for 15 years generated CLO and mezzanine debt risks far larger than in 2008, and that the top 10 U.S. banks hold a quadrillion dollars in derivatives. She argues market participants delay the inevitable price reconciliation through incremental liquidity injections, but the underlying fragility is unresolved.

### 2024 Election and Political Commentary [00:09:30]

Walser expresses concern that Harris's policy positions are not being scrutinized, contrasting her prior Senate record with post-nomination messaging. She attributes Trump's resilience to his conduct after the assassination attempt and describes the election contest as the most divisive she has observed. Walser concludes by stating her view that the November 2024 election may not take place, citing unspecified intervening events.
