## Summary

In a UK television interview, wealth strategist Rebecca Walser discusses the 2024 US presidential race with host Basil Valentine, focusing on a second assassination attempt on Donald Trump and its media coverage. Walser argues that corporate media covered the incident inadequately and that powerful global interests, including the United Nations, are working against a Trump presidency. She also contends that the 2020 and 2022 elections contained statistical fraud and that economic data from the Bureau of Labor Statistics has been manipulated. The conversation covers Kamala Harris's economic proposals and Trump's debate performance.

## Key Takeaways

- Walser claims mainstream media initially misreported a second Trump assassination attempt as gang violence before Fox News insiders corrected the narrative.
- She alleges statistical anomalies prove fraud in the 2020 and 2022 elections and warns not to expect a free and fair 2024 election without changes to voting machines.
- Walser argues the Bureau of Labor Statistics has manipulated employment data, citing an 88,000-job downward revision for fiscal 2023.
- She criticizes Kamala Harris's economic proposals — $25,000 home buyer assistance, $6,000 child tax credit, $50,000 small business deduction — as unaffordable given $36 trillion in federal debt.
- Walser expresses disappointment that Trump did not press economic arguments more forcefully during the debate and was baited into showing his temper.

## Detailed Notes

### Election Integrity and Global Opposition to Trump [00:12:47]

Walser asserts that the 2020 and 2022 elections were statistically fraudulent, citing vote counts that she says do not align with registered voter rolls. She argues that the United Nations and other global entities are actively working to prevent a second Trump presidency and that only a landslide margin would make the election difficult to manipulate.

### Economic Data and Harris's Fiscal Proposals [00:13:50]

Walser states that Bureau of Labor Statistics employment data has been repeatedly revised downward — with 88,000 jobs retracted in fiscal 2023 — indicating deliberate upward manipulation. She critiques Harris's economic platform as fiscally irresponsible given nearly $36 trillion in federal debt and argues Trump failed to sufficiently attack these proposals during their debate.
