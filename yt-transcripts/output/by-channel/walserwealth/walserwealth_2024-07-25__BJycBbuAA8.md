# Pre-Election: The World Is Going Crazy

**Channel:** Rebecca Walser
**Published:** 2024-07-25
**URL:** https://youtube.com/watch?v=_BJycBbuAA8
**Duration:** 33:50

## Summary

This Fox Business 'Mornings With Maria' segment from July 25, 2024 features Rebecca Walser and other guests discussing several converging market and political developments. On markets, panelists debate whether the AI/tech rally has become a bubble given extreme concentration in a handful of mega-cap names, whether the rotation into small caps driven by anticipated Fed rate cuts is sustainable, and what weak airline earnings signal about consumer health. On macro data, Q2 GDP surprised to the upside at 2.8% but Walser and others express skepticism about revisions. The segment also extensively covers the political implications of Biden's withdrawal from the presidential race and the path for Kamala Harris.

## Key Takeaways

- AI and large-cap tech concentration in the S&P 500 is at historically unprecedented levels, with the top five stocks absorbing roughly 29 cents of every SPY dollar invested.
- A rotation from large-cap to small-cap stocks is underway driven by expectations of Fed rate cuts in September 2024, but its sustainability is debated given the consumer spending slowdown.
- Q2 2024 GDP printed at 2.8%, above the 2.0% estimate, but panelists including Walser are skeptical and expect downward revision.
- Airline earnings — American Airlines missing on revenue and cutting forecasts, Southwest ditching open seating — signal an uneven consumer demand environment.
- President Biden's withdrawal and Oval Office speech set up Kamala Harris as the likely Democratic nominee, with Obama not yet endorsing and polling showing vulnerability.
- Walser argues Harris's stated policies (Medicare for All, open borders, fracking ban) are economically incoherent and would be disqualifying in key battleground states.
- Panelists broadly agree the labor market is cooling but is not yet in alarm territory, with initial claims at 235,000 in line with expectations.

## Detailed Notes

### AI and Tech Concentration Risk [00:00:00]

Walser opens by flagging irrational exuberance in AI/tech, citing that 29 cents of every SPY dollar goes to the top five S&P 500 stocks. She argues this level of concentration is bubble-like, particularly as monetization timelines for AI remain unclear. Mario Boneroso echoes the concern, noting AI has not yet seen broad meaningful economic applications and the number of investable AI companies remains very small.

### Large-Cap to Small-Cap Rotation [00:14:40]

The prior day's large sell-off in tech (Tesla, Alphabet) and concurrent small-cap rally is analyzed as a rotation driven by approaching Fed rate cuts. Boneroso cites a technical cup-and-handle breakout with small caps up 10% in two weeks, but cautions that the Russell 2000's total market cap is smaller than Apple alone, making percentage moves look dramatic. Walser and Jake Ubini from Piper Sandler both warn that a deteriorating consumer environment makes the small-cap rally fragile given small caps carry four times the debt ratio of large caps.

### Airline Earnings and Consumer Health [00:13:38]

American Airlines beat on earnings but missed revenue and cut its 2024 profit forecast citing uneven demand and overcapacity; Southwest beat but announced a shift to assigned seating from its open-seating model. Both stocks fell sharply. Boneroso characterizes airlines as 'dead money' and notes the CrowdStrike outage and tech disruptions further weighed on travel sentiment.

### Q2 GDP and Economic Data [00:29:16]

Q2 2024 GDP first estimate came in at 2.8% annualized, well above the 2.0% consensus and rebounding from Q1's 1.4%. Durable goods rose 6.6% month-over-month, above the 3% estimate (though ex-transport only +0.5%). Initial jobless claims were 235,000, in line with estimates. Walser dismisses the GDP number as implausible and expects a downward revision, noting the GDP deflator of 2.3% implies an inflation component inflating the headline figure.

### Biden Withdrawal and Harris Candidacy [00:04:08]

Biden's Oval Office address is critiqued by panelists as political rather than candid; they note he did not acknowledge personal health or factual errors about the border and economy. Harris is described as having a difficult path given she was the swing vote on major spending bills and was assigned the border portfolio. Walser argues Harris's progressive economic positions — Medicare for All, fracking ban, open border — are contradictory and would lose Pennsylvania; the segment speculates on Josh Shapiro and Gina Raimondo as potential VP picks with competing strategic trade-offs.
