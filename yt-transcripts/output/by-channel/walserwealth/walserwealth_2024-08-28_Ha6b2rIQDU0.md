# Meta Admits To Being Censored

**Channel:** Rebecca Walser
**Published:** 2024-08-28
**URL:** https://youtube.com/watch?v=Ha6b2rIQDU0
**Duration:** 34:47

## Summary

This Fox Business segment features financial commentator Rebecca Walser alongside other panelists covering three major stories from August 27, 2024. The central story is Mark Zuckerberg's letter to Congress admitting Meta censored COVID-19 content and suppressed the Hunter Biden laptop story under pressure from the Biden-Harris Administration, raising Section 230 liability questions. Financial commentary focuses on Nvidia's anticipated blockbuster earnings as an AI bellwether, Federal Reserve rate cut expectations for September, and the investment strategy of rotating toward defensive sectors amid slowing growth. Political discussion covers Kamala Harris's avoidance of press interviews, the upcoming Trump-Harris debate format dispute, and the impact of RFK Jr.'s endorsement of Trump.

## Key Takeaways

- Zuckerberg's admission that Meta was 'repeatedly pressured' to censor COVID content and the Hunter Biden laptop story raises legal questions about whether Meta's actions undermine its Section 230 liability shield.
- Nvidia enters earnings with massive expectations: 163% year-to-date gains, 113% revenue growth, and 140% EPS growth year-over-year, but panelists caution the pace of triple-digit growth is unlikely to be sustained.
- Investment strategists recommend trimming large-cap tech exposure and adding defensive positions in utilities, healthcare, and consumer staples as growth slows and Fed rate cuts approach.
- Richmond Fed President Thomas Barkin signaled the current low-hiring, low-firing labor market dynamic is unsustainable, with layoffs likely if demand weakens.
- Rebecca Walser anticipates only a 25 basis point cut in September, citing data showing net job losses among American citizens and a shift toward part-time work.
- The Sahm Rule unemployment threshold near 260,000 jobless claims is flagged as a key recession indicator to watch.
- Meta's legal exposure under Section 230 may be challenged given that active content censorship at government request could reclassify it as a publisher subject to broader regulation.

## Detailed Notes

### Meta Censorship Admission and Section 230 Implications [00:00:01]

Mark Zuckerberg's letter to House Judiciary Committee Chair Jim Jordan disclosed that senior Biden-Harris White House officials repeatedly pressured Meta to censor COVID-19 content including satire, and that the Hunter Biden laptop story was suppressed based on what was later proven false Russian disinformation claims. Walser argues this admission potentially invalidates Meta's Section 230 liability protection by demonstrating the company functioned as a publisher making editorial decisions at government direction. Panelists note Jim Jordan's investigative committee work was the catalyst for this disclosure, not voluntary transparency.

### Nvidia Earnings Outlook and AI Sector Assessment [00:07:20]

Nvidia heads into earnings after a 163% year-to-date gain with revenue sales expected to double for the second quarter. Shana Sissel highlights the Blackwell chip backlog as a key metric affecting not just Nvidia but downstream AI producers and ancillary players. Both Sissel and Walser acknowledge that while Nvidia will likely beat, sustaining 300-400% year-over-year growth is mathematically impossible and current 100-110% growth rates may disappoint relative to expectations.

### Federal Reserve Rate Cuts and Labor Market Outlook [00:22:56]

Richmond Fed President Barkin's comments suggest the low-hiring, low-firing equilibrium will not persist, with the direction depending on whether demand holds or weakens further. Walser expects a 25 basis point cut on September 18 given labor market deterioration, citing net negative job creation for American citizens versus foreign-born workers and the shift from full-time to part-time employment. Sissel flags the Sahm Rule threshold and 260,000 jobless claims as key recession signals to monitor, arguing the Fed is deliberately conserving rate-cutting ammunition.

### 2024 Election: Harris Debate Strategy and Political Endorsements [00:13:34]

Discussion covers Harris's refusal to give press interviews 37 days after entering the race and the debate format dispute with the Trump campaign. Walser and panelists argue muted mics were agreed upon previously but Harris's team is pushing for open mics to recreate the 'I'm speaking' moment from the 2020 Pence debate. RFK Jr.'s endorsement of Trump is assessed as potentially marginal given his prior 7% polling, while Montana Senator Jon Tester's decision not to endorse Harris is characterized as a significant indicator of her drift toward what critics call extreme policy positions.
