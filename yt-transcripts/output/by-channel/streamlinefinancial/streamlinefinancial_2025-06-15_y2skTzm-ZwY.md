# This Can Make or Break Your Retirement

**Channel:** Streamline Financial
**Published:** 2025-06-15
**URL:** https://youtube.com/watch?v=y2skTzm-ZwY
**Duration:** 5:26

## Summary

Dave Zoller introduces Martin Seligman's PERMA model (Positive emotion, Engagement, Relationships, Meaning, Achievement) as a framework for retirement fulfillment beyond money. References his prior interview with psychologist Daniel Crosby. Argues most retirement planning focuses only on the P (positive experiences / fun money), which is why some millionaires feel miserable in retirement while modestly-saving retirees thrive — all five elements must work together.

## Key Takeaways

- PERMA model: Positive emotion, Engagement, Relationships, Meaning, Achievement — all five needed for retirement to thrive.
- Most planning focuses only on P (fun, leisure, golf, travel) — necessary but not sufficient.
- Engagement = flow-state activities where you lose track of time; high achievers especially struggle without it.
- Relationships are the #1 predictor of happiness in retirement, per research.
- Meaning = something bigger than yourself (volunteering, mentoring, faith, family legacy).
- Achievement = setting and reaching goals; humans are growth-oriented and stagnate without progress.
- Action: assess which of the five elements has the biggest gap right now and address it.

## Detailed Notes

### Intro and PERMA overview [00:00:00]

Dave Zoller introduces Martin Seligman's PERMA model as a way to make retirement the best stage of life. References a prior conversation with Daniel Crosby. Five elements separate thriving retirees from struggling ones.

### P — Positive experiences [00:00:33]

What most people picture: travel, golf, hobbies, leisure, food. Most retirement planning focuses entirely here. But research shows the other four elements are needed to flourish.

### E — Engagement [00:01:34]

Deep involvement in flow-state activities. Without it, retirees feel bored — successful career-driven people especially struggle. Examples: meaningful projects, learning piano/painting, teaching, mentoring.

### R — Relationships [00:02:38]

Love, connection, meaningful bonds. Research shows relationships are the #1 predictor of happiness in retirement. Many retirees lose work friendships and slide into isolation — actively avoid this.

### M — Meaning [00:02:38]

Working for something bigger than self. Without meaning, retirement feels empty despite money and time. Examples: volunteering for causes, mentoring, religious/spiritual practice, contributing to community or family legacy.

### A — Achievement [00:03:39]

Setting and accomplishing goals. Humans are growth-oriented; stagnation without progress. Retirement examples: new skills, certifications, creative projects (book, memoir), mastering hobbies/sports, personal milestones.

### Action step [00:04:38]

Assess which of the five PERMA elements needs the most attention right now. Identify the gaps. Retirement isn't just about money — it's about building a life rich in all five.
