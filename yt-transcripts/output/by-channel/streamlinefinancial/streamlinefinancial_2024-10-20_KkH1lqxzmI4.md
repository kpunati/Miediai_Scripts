# Successful Retirees Track FOUR Numbers

**Channel:** Streamline Financial
**Published:** 2024-10-20
**URL:** https://youtube.com/watch?v=KkH1lqxzmI4
**Duration:** 9:35

## Summary

Dave Zoller identifies the four numbers successful retirees track: (1) fixed costs / essentials — needs vs. wants for the income plan; (2) net worth — quarterly snapshot of overall financial health; (3) investment allocation — stocks/bonds/cash mix by account; (4) years of safe-money balance — conservative assets converted into years of withdrawals (e.g., $180K / $60K = 3 years). Recommends Monarch Money, YNAB, or Tiller for expense tracking.

## Key Takeaways

- Track fixed costs (essentials) separately from wants — the income plan must cover essentials.
- Net worth is the single best one-number snapshot of overall financial health.
- Use Monarch Money, YNAB, or Tiller to auto-categorize expenses heading into retirement.
- Investment allocation: know stocks/bonds/cash mix by account, and consider differentiating across account types (Roth more aggressive, 401(k) more conservative).
- Years of safe-money balance = conservative assets ÷ annual withdrawal — translates risk into a tangible peace-of-mind metric.
- Knowing you have 7 years in safe money can shift decisions during a bear market.

## Detailed Notes

### The four numbers framing [00:00:00]

Four numbers are the 80/20 of retirement tracking. Accumulation rules (savings rate, growth) don't carry directly into decumulation.

### Number 1: fixed costs / essentials [00:01:02]

Pre-retirees haven't typically tracked monthly outflows. Overestimating leads to 'one more year' syndrome; underestimating wrecks early retirement. Separate needs (housing, utilities, groceries, insurance) from wants (vacations, dining out).

### Expense-tracking tools: Monarch, YNAB, Tiller [00:03:09]

Auto-categorizing expense trackers reduce friction. Recommends Monarch Money, YNAB, Tiller for clients who don't want to do it manually.

### Number 2: net worth [00:04:11]

Subscriber asked: if I only had 5 minutes a month, what would I track? Answer: net worth. Net worth = assets − liabilities. Track quarterly in a spreadsheet or auto-tool.

### Number 3: investment allocation by account [00:05:15]

Know each account's mix of stocks/bonds/cash. Common mistake: same allocation across all accounts when different account purposes warrant different aggressiveness (Roth higher-growth, 401(k) for first-decade withdrawals more conservative).

### Number 4: years of safe-money balance [00:07:22]

Translate conservative assets into years of withdrawals. Example: $180K conservative / $60K annual withdrawal = 3 years.

### Peace of mind from 7 years of safe money [00:08:26]

The March 2020 client story — seven years of withdrawals in conservative assets removed the urgency to sell equities at the bottom.

### Tools and resources to act on this [00:09:27]

Pointers to a deeper-dive free-tool walkthrough video at 4:16 of that linked piece.
