# Winning at What Truly Matters with John Olinger

**Channel:** Streamline Financial
**Published:** 2025-06-05
**URL:** https://youtube.com/watch?v=XOf_0gK8eJA
**Duration:** 57:50

## Summary

Tim Meisenheimer interviews John Olinger (10 years at Nike, author of Worthy Wins) on aligning time, energy, and attention. Olinger shares his dad's death at 74 (realizing at 37 he might be half-done), leading Nike's marketing for Kobe Bryant's legacy after the 2020 crash, and his framework: a meaningful story (personal, intentional, challenging, purposeful) plus a strategic plan. Example: 'wine Wednesdays' + Friday planning grew weekly couple touch-points from 1 to 3.

## Key Takeaways

- Worthy Wins core thesis: winning is subjective — if you don't define it extremely clearly, you can't win at what actually matters.
- The Harvard 84-year study finds the happiest people anchor around high-quality relationships.
- Risk: overinvesting in accomplishments and underinvesting in relationships.
- A 'meaningful story' has four pillars: personal, intentional, challenging, purposeful.
- Strategic example: expanded weekly relational touch-points from 1 (date night, often a logistics dump) to 3 (date night + wine Wednesday + Friday planning meeting) — 52 → 156/year.
- Quantity-of-time-only metric of relational success guarantees failure; quality of intentional moments matters.
- After leading Kobe's posthumous marketing work and Nike reorg, decided to leave for Florida — life-is-short clarity.
- Declined a Disney offer because the 2-hour commute / overnight stay tradeoff wasn't worth it.

## Detailed Notes

### Nike origin: 100 informational interviews [00:01:36]

Grew up 45 min from Nike HQ. Played basketball in Europe for 2 years. Returned and did ~100 informational interviews over 1.5 years until a friend's promoted role opened up — Nike Basketball social media in the early days of Instagram/Twitter.

### Clearness committee: choosing Nike over United Way [00:05:53]

Same week, offered Nike role and 5-year-commitment United Way executive director role. Used a 'clearness committee' — 8 friends asked him questions for 3-4 hours. Realized he didn't want to spend his life asking people for money.

### Dad's death at 74; John at 37 [00:11:20]

John's mom died when he was 11; dad died unexpectedly at 74. John was 37 — realized he might be halfway through life. Born the newsletter that became the book Worthy Wins.

### Worthy Wins thesis: winning is subjective [00:23:08]

10 years at Nike taught him: winning is subjective. If you don't define winning with extreme clarity, you can't win — and you end up spending your time/energy/attention ('T') on the wrong things.

### 4 pillars of a meaningful story [00:35:12]

(1) Personal — every person is unique. (2) Intentional — purposely directed. (3) Challenging — challenge drives growth (Robert McKee: stories move via conflict). (4) Purposeful — connects to a larger why (Simon Sinek-style).

### Relationships first: Harvard 84-year study + 'who and what' [00:25:23]

Per the Harvard 84-year study, the happiest people all anchor on high-quality relationships. Worthy wins are about both 'who and what' — risk is overinvesting in accomplishments and underinvesting in relationships.

### Quality vs quantity of time + weekly touch-points [00:28:08]

Quantity-of-time-only as the metric guarantees feeling like a relational failure. Aaron told him 'these dates are dumps.' They moved from 1 weekly touch-point (date night) to 3 (date + wine Wednesday + Friday planning meeting) — 52 → 156/year.

### Kobe Bryant marketing after the 2020 crash [00:41:01]

John was one of few at Nike with Kobe in his title when the helicopter crash happened. Got everyone aligned on one objective slide: 'pass Mamba mentality to next-gen Gen Z athletes, starting with girls. If your idea doesn't do that, we're not doing it.'

### Leaving Nike: dad's death + the 40-day prayer [00:45:48]

Day 5 of a 40-day prayer about possibly moving east, his dad had a freak fall and traumatic brain injury. Day 40 Nike offered a project job (he'd asked for a people job). 20 days later his dad passed. Accepted the offer but knew he'd leave. Within a year decided to move closer to Aaron's parents.

### Money decisions: declining Disney [00:48:44]

Disney offered a Florida-or-NYC role — but the office was a 2-hour drive each way, 3 days a week. Declined despite compensation. Not worth the relational tradeoff. 'Compensation is a pursuit, but not the most important.'

### Living in the short-term/long-term tension [00:52:39]

Don't know how much time you have. Live in the tension between short term and long term — relational investment now ensures the long-term is pointing where you want.

### Closing: wrestling time with three boys [00:53:40]

John's 'one thing': wrestling time before bed — 10 min timer, 2 older boys vs him + the younger. Tim's: watching his oldest daughter Avery play varsity water polo after chronic knee surgeries.
