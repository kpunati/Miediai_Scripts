# Don't Retire Until You Hit These 5 Goals

**Channel:** Streamline Financial
**Published:** 2025-06-22
**URL:** https://youtube.com/watch?v=DU8wWFWLUNc
**Duration:** 7:18

## Summary

Dave Zoller lists five goals to hit before retiring beyond just the financial number: (1) strengthen relationships now because work friendships vanish — studies show retirees lose 25% of social connections in the first 2 years; (2) build a life plan for the 168 hours/week of new free time; (3) clarify your identity beyond work; (4) honestly assess and address health impact of your job; (5) have a detailed retirement financial plan covering taxes, income, and estate planning.

## Key Takeaways

- Most planning focuses only on money — five non-financial goals matter equally.
- Goal 1: rebuild relationships now — retirees lose ~25% of social connections in the first 2 years.
- Goal 2: build a life plan, not just a financial plan — retirement gives you 168 hours/week to fill.
- Goal 3: clarify your identity beyond work; high-achieving men especially struggle when titles disappear.
- Use vacation days as a retirement laboratory — test hobbies and activities to find what energizes you.
- Goal 4: honestly assess your health and how your job impacts it; start small habits now.
- Goal 5: build a real retirement plan (tax, income, estate) — not just a napkin or basic Excel.

## Detailed Notes

### The unhappiest retirees are isolated, not broke [00:00:00]

After 16 years advising retirees, Dave finds the unhappiest aren't those with the least money — they're the most isolated. Work relationships often vanish when work ends.

### Goal 1 — Strengthen relationships before retirement [00:00:42]

Studies show retirees lose 25% of social connections in the first two years. Action: reach out now to old friends from college, former neighbors, distant family. Awkward at first, but they're almost always glad to hear from you.

### Goal 2 — A life plan, not just a financial plan [00:01:41]

Retirement gives you 168 waking hours/week to fill. Use 'life buckets' or 5-year time segments (60-65, 65-70, etc.). Write down major accomplishments and goals — many people prioritize travel in their 60s-70s while still mobile.

### Goal 3 — Identity beyond work [00:02:43]

Biggest retirement challenge isn't financial — it's figuring out who you are when not working. Watching strong, successful people (especially men) lose themselves after 30-40 years of career-defined identity.

### Vacation as retirement laboratory [00:03:20]

About half of Americans don't use full vacation. Use vacation days to experiment with hobbies (pottery, welding, volunteering, cooking, hiking groups) — find what energizes vs drains you. That's exactly what retirement will be, but every week.

### Goal 4 — Honestly assess health [00:04:09]

Every job has stress, deadlines, time costs. After 30-40 years, the cumulative health toll goes unnoticed. Be brutally honest about how your job impacts your health and start small fixes now or plan them for retirement.

### Goal 5 — Detailed financial plan [00:05:44]

Pre-retirement is about savings rate and growth; retirement shifts to tax-efficient withdrawal, income planning, and syncing investment plan with income plan. Don't use a back-of-napkin plan — work with an advisor or specialized retirement software, and don't forget tax planning and estate planning.

### Closing [00:06:41]

Asks which of the five goals viewers will work on first. Retirement isn't just about enough money — it's about retiring well.
