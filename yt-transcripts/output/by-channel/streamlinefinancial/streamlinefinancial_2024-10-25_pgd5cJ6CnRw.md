# IRS Releases New 2025 Tax Brackets + Tax Sunsets

**Channel:** Streamline Financial
**Published:** 2024-10-25
**URL:** https://youtube.com/watch?v=pgd5cJ6CnRw
**Duration:** 7:42

## Summary

Dave Zoller summarizes the IRS's 2025 inflation adjustments — a smaller-than-usual 2.8% bracket bump, $30,000 standard deduction for married filing jointly, and a $19,000 gift tax exclusion. Then walks through seven Tax Cuts and Jobs Act provisions scheduled to sunset at the end of 2025: rising rates (negative), smaller standard deduction (negative), returning personal exemption (positive), lower child tax credit (negative), uncapped SALT deduction (positive for.

## Key Takeaways

- 2025 brackets rose only ~2.8% — small bump reflecting cooler inflation.
- 2025 standard deduction: $30K for MFJ, ~$15K for single, ~$22.5K head of household.
- Annual gift exclusion rises to $19K per donor per recipient in 2025.
- Social Security COLA for 2025 is 2.5%.
- TCJA sunset (end of 2025) would raise tax rates, shrink the standard deduction, and lower the child tax credit.
- TCJA sunset would also uncap SALT (positive in high-tax states) and reinstate the personal exemption.
- Estate tax exemption would roughly halve at sunset — material for HNW estate plans.
- Bonus depreciation set to fully expire — negative for business owners investing in equipment.

## Detailed Notes

### 2025 IRS inflation adjustments overview [00:00:00]

Smallest bracket bump in years due to cooling inflation. Higher brackets mean less income taxed at lower rates.

### Single-filer brackets for 2025 [00:01:02]

Bump ~2.8% from 2024. Reminder about marginal vs. effective tax rates.

### MFJ brackets, deductions, gift exclusion [00:02:05]

Standard deduction up to $30K for MFJ, ~$15K single, ~$22.5K HOH. Annual gift exclusion up to $19K — example client gifts to married daughter/son-in-law across two spouses.

### Social Security COLA: 2.5% for 2025 [00:03:08]

Cost of living adjustment announced earlier.

### TCJA sunset overview [00:04:12]

Tax Cuts and Jobs Act provisions scheduled to sunset end of 2025. Walks through seven changes.

### Sunset changes 1-4: rates, deduction, exemption, child credit [00:05:15]

Tax rates rise (negative), standard deduction shrinks (negative), personal exemption returns (positive), child tax credit shrinks (negative).

### Sunset changes 5-6: SALT uncap and estate exemption drop [00:06:19]

SALT cap of $10K removed — positive for high-tax states. Estate tax exemption (~$13M single / ~$26M MFJ) drops to roughly half — affects HNW estate plans.

### Sunset change 7: bonus depreciation expires [00:06:19]

Set to fully expire for business owners. Less attractive to invest in new equipment with full first-year deduction.

### Closing: 2025 will be a planning year [00:07:21]

Things can change — don't make big decisions based on predicted sunsets. But pay attention as the year unfolds.
