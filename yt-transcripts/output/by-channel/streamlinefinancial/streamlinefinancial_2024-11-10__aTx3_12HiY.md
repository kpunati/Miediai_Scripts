# New 401K Rules Are Coming

**Channel:** Streamline Financial
**Published:** 2024-11-10
**URL:** https://youtube.com/watch?v=_aTx3_12HiY
**Duration:** 5:44

## Summary

Dave Zoller walks through three big 401(k) changes coming in 2025 from the SECURE Act 2.0: (1) ages 60-63 can contribute an extra $11,250 catch-up bringing the cap to $34,750; (2) a US Department of Labor searchable database for forgotten stranded 401(k)s (~$1.3 trillion estimated); (3) employer match can be directed to student loans, an HSA, or an HRA instead of the 401(k).

## Key Takeaways

- 2025 standard 401(k) limit is $23,500 plus $7,500 catch-up at 50+ = $31,000.
- Ages 60-63 get an enhanced $11,250 catch-up — total $34,750 in 2025.
- Update your contribution amount with your employer to capture the enhanced 60-63 catch-up.
- Estimated $1.3 trillion sits in forgotten 401(k)s — the DOL searchable database in 2025 makes them easier to find.
- Employer-match flexibility (student loans / HSA / HRA) starts in 2025 but only at employers that opt in — ask HR.
- Year-end actions: capture the full employer match, raise contributions during bonus periods, review investment mix, confirm beneficiaries, max out if possible.

## Detailed Notes

### 2025 401(k) contribution limits [00:00:00]

Base: $23,500. Standard 50+ catch-up: $7,500 (total $31,000). Applies to 401(k), 403(b), TSP, and 457 plans.

### Ages 60-63 enhanced catch-up [00:01:05]

From SECURE Act 2.0: $11,250 catch-up for ages 60-63 brings 2025 cap to $34,750 — $3,750 more than the standard 50+ catch-up. Update contribution with employer to capture it.

### Stranded 401(k) database in 2025 [00:02:07]

Estimated $1.3 trillion in forgotten 401(k)s nationally. US Department of Labor required to build a searchable database in 2025. Worth checking even if you think rollovers are complete.

### Employer match redirected to loans/HSA/HRA [00:03:11]

From 2025, employer can direct match to student loans, HSA, or HRA in addition to the 401(k) — but only for employers that file with IRS. Will be norm in 5 years; in 2025 only opt-in employers participate.

### Year-end action items: capture full match, bonus-period boost [00:04:15]

Top action: ensure you're capturing the full employer match (free money). Raise contributions in the last pay periods if you receive year-end bonuses. Max out if affordable.

### Closing: review beneficiaries and investment mix [00:05:18]

Five year-end items: full match, bonus-period boost, review investment mix (stocks/bonds risk), update beneficiaries, max out if possible.
