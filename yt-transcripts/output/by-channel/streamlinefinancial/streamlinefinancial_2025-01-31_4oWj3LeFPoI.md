# This Is The BEST Year To Retire - Here's Why

**Channel:** Streamline Financial
**Published:** 2025-01-31
**URL:** https://youtube.com/watch?v=4oWj3LeFPoI
**Duration:** 17:26

## Summary

Dave Zoller argues 2025 is the best year to retire and reframes retirement as a 'Renaissance' — the convergence of resources, freedom, and time. Tackles three barriers: (1) the spending paradox (~67% of retirees double their principal under 4%); (2) health span vs lifespan (per Peter Attia: lifespan ~78, health span ~64); (3) global uncertainty (the timeline of 'reasons not to retire' is endless — uncertainty is a feature, not a bug). Recommends Boldin for DIY planning.

## Key Takeaways

- Reframe retirement as a 'Renaissance' — the rare convergence of resources, freedom of thought, and time.
- Spending paradox: ~67% of retirees following the 4% rule end up with double their starting principal — most underspend in the go-go years.
- Plan around a smile curve: go-go, slow-go, no-go years — separate needs (inflation-adjusted) from wants (time-limited 'what-ifs').
- Health span (~64) ends well before lifespan (~78) — the period where retirement money, time, and health all align is short.
- Peter Attia's three pillars of health span: physical, cognitive, emotional.
- There is never a perfect time to retire — every year has 'reasons not to.' Uncertainty is a feature, not a bug.
- Build resilience via the bucket strategy: e.g., 7 years of conservative bucket let one client retire in March 2020 with peace of mind.

## Detailed Notes

### Open: 2025 is the year, here's why [00:00:00]

Dave previews three reasons to retire this year and three barriers people use to delay. Promises a new framework: the 'retirement Renaissance.'

### Reason 1: the retirement Renaissance [00:01:02]

Renaissance literally means 'rebirth.' First time in history many can combine resources, freedom of thought, and time. Reframe retirement as a chance to use wisdom and experience in new ways — not running from work, but running toward something.

### Reason 2: the spending paradox [00:03:09]

Data shows ~67% of retirees following the 4% rule double their starting principal. Most under-spend. Plan around a smile curve: go-go years (travel, energy), slow-go years (content at home, expenses drop), no-go years (medical expenses may rise).

### Separating needs from wants in the plan [00:05:12]

Needs (housing, food, transport, insurance) grow with inflation each year. Wants (travel, weddings, gifts) are time-limited and modeled separately as 'what-if' scenarios. Plus a potential long-term-care spike at end of life.

### Reason 3: extend health span, not just lifespan [00:07:18]

Per Peter Attia: lifespan ~78 average; health span ends around 64. Three pillars: physical (muscle mass, cardio), cognitive (memory, processing), emotional (purpose, fulfillment, relationships).

### Client John: building health span via retirement [00:08:20]

Client John made maximizing health span part of his retirement purpose: morning reading/Bible, strength + cardio, 2-3 lunches with friends/family per week, walking + cooking with wife, regular meditation.

### Magic triangles of life and the fourth triangle [00:09:22]

Three life stages have different mixes of time/health/money. The 'magic fourth triangle' is the brief retirement window where all three align — the sweet spot to retire.

### Barrier: global uncertainty — the 16-year timeline [00:11:28]

Timeline of 'reasons not to retire' over 16 years: global financial crisis, debt crisis, Brexit, pandemic, 2022 inflation, 2023 banking crisis. Clients retired through each and are still enjoying retirement. Uncertainty is a feature.

### Building the resilient plan: streamline investment system [00:13:32]

March 2020 client needed $10K/month and had 7 years in the conservative bucket at +3% YTD — instant relief during the COVID drawdown. Streamline system: income plan first, take the least risk needed, asset classes for every economic season, trigger-based rebalancing.

### DIY tool: Boldin [00:15:42]

If not ready for an advisor, Dave recommends Boldin (link in description) — set recurring expenses with inflation, model what-if travel/medical scenarios, see Monte Carlo. Get familiar with your plan before talking to a CFP.
