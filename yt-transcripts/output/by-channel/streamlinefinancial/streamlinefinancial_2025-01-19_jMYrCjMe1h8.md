# The One Cost You Must Cut In Retirement

**Channel:** Streamline Financial
**Published:** 2025-01-19
**URL:** https://youtube.com/watch?v=jMYrCjMe1h8
**Duration:** 6:34

## Summary

Dave Zoller covers small-but-recurring retirement money leaks: (1) call your bank — many retirees earn ~0% on cash that could earn $5-10K/yr more in a high-yield option; (2) audit monthly subscriptions using Monarch Money or Rocket Money; (3) use AARP discounts (30% car rentals, 20% hotels) even if you 'feel young'; (4) the 'must cut' item — the Amazon one-click habit. Suggests a 24-hour rule before purchases.

## Key Takeaways

- Call your bank — earning near-0% on a conservative bucket can cost thousands; high-yield savings accounts are easy to set up.
- Audit subscriptions with Monarch Money or Rocket Money — Dave admits finding three kids' apps still being charged.
- AARP membership (over 50) offers real discounts on car rentals, hotels, flights, restaurants.
- The must-cut item: Amazon one-click purchases — they accumulate stuff retirees later try to downsize.
- Use a 24-hour cart rule: add to cart, wait a day; if you still want it, buy it.
- Most retirement money damage isn't from one big expense — it's the small recurring ones that drain savings.

## Detailed Notes

### Setup: small leaks beat big expenses [00:00:00]

Most retirees worry about running out of money from big expenses. After 16 years advising, Dave finds it's the small recurring expenses that drain accounts. Promises hacks worth thousands.

### Hack 1: call the bank to earn real interest [00:01:03]

A 5-10 minute call to your bank can move conservative-bucket cash to a higher-yielding account. Some clients earn an extra $5-10K/yr just from this move. Online high-yield options are easy via a quick search.

### Hack 2: audit monthly subscriptions [00:02:05]

Monarch Money will show all recurring subscriptions on one page after a month of expense data. Dave admits to finding three kids' learning apps still being charged. Rocket Money is another tool.

### Hack 3: AARP discounts after 50 [00:03:06]

AARP discounts can produce real savings even if you 'feel young': ~30% off car rentals, ~20% off hotels, flights, restaurant discounts. The card doesn't age you — paying full price does.

### Hack 4 (must cut): the Amazon one-click habit [00:04:10]

The must-cut item: little brown boxes daily from Amazon one-click. Retirement is usually about simplifying and downsizing; this habit works against that. Many retirees are trying to downsize possessions later.

### Tip: the 24-hour cart rule [00:05:14]

If you see something you want on Amazon, add it to cart and wait 24 hours. If you still want it the same way, buy it. Small habit, meaningful savings.

### Wrap-up [00:06:18]

Take 10-30 minutes to review checking-account rates and recurring expenses. Small leaks add up over decades.
