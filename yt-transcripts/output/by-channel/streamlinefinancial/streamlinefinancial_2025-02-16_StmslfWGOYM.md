# Give Me 10 Minutes, I'll Transform Your Retirement Plan

**Channel:** Streamline Financial

## Summary

Dave Zoller introduces a neuroscience-based 'Retirement Mastery' worksheet for the non-financial side of retirement planning. Top half contrasts the ideal retirement (specific morning, hobbies, contributions, perfect Tuesday) against the 'anti-retirement' (worst-case version) to engage both carrot and stick in the limbic system. Bottom half identifies daily/weekly habits to start and to stop. Closes with the Streamline C3 model: creativity/curiosity, connection, contribution.

## Key Takeaways

- Most financial-industry retirement advice ignores the limbic system — the part of the brain that actually drives behavior.
- The Retirement Mastery worksheet uses both ideal and anti-retirement visualizations to motivate change.
- Specificity matters: 'wake at 7am without an alarm, coffee + reading, walk or swim, weights 3x/week, lunch with friends 2-3x/week.'
- Anti-retirement: write the worst version (passive TV, doomscrolling, lost relationships) — the brain needs pain to commit.
- Identify daily/weekly habits to start AND habits to stop — the brain automates whatever you repeat.
- C3 model: creativity/curiosity, connection, contribution — the three pillars of a fulfilling retirement.
- Curiosity is the fountain of youth; learning Spanish, photography, writing, community college classes all qualify.

## Detailed Notes

### Why traditional retirement planning misses the brain [00:00:00]

Even financially-set retirees feel uncertainty because the financial industry doesn't engage the limbic system. Introduces a 'Retirement Mastery worksheet' to fix that.

### Top half of the worksheet: ideal retirement [00:01:03]

Get specific. Example: wake at 7am no alarm, coffee + reading + learning, morning walk or swim, weights 3x/week, lunch with family/friends 2-3x/week, pick up grandkids Thursdays. Treat as a brain dump; refine later.

### Five prompts for the ideal retirement [00:02:06]

Five prompts: what time you wake; what skills/hobbies to try; three adventures if you had perfect health; how to contribute; what your perfect random Tuesday looks like; what you want grandkids/loved-ones to say about you.

### Anti-retirement: the worst version (right side) [00:03:08]

Imagine waking up 5 years in to find you've drifted into TV all day, doomscrolling, faded friends, no purpose. The brain needs the pain to commit. Four prompts: fading relationships, health issues, lost purpose, regrets.

### Why anti-retirement works [00:04:11]

Writing down what you really don't want produces motivation to prevent it. You just need to identify it first.

### Bottom half: habits to start and habits to stop [00:05:13]

Daily/weekly habits create the retirement. Start: morning stretching, calling family member, weekly coffee with friends, blocking Tuesdays for volunteering, learning Spanish 30 min/day for Barcelona trip. Stop: TV-asleep, hours of social media, constant portfolio checking.

### C3 model: creativity, connection, contribution [00:07:19]

Streamline's three pillars. (1) Creativity/curiosity — Spanish, photography, writing, community college; curiosity is the fountain of youth. (2) Connection — relationships needed biologically; cognitive decline accelerates without them. (3) Contribution — mentoring, volunteering, teaching grandkids life skills.

### Closing: PDF link and DIY plan resource [00:09:26]

Links below: the Retirement Mastery PDF, plus Dave's favorite retirement planning calculator (Boldin) for the financial side.
