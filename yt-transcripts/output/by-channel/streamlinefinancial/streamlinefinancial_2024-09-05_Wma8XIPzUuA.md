# The Intersection of Wellness and Leadership with Jackie Glass

**Channel:** Streamline Financial

## Summary

Tim hosts executive wellness coach Jackie Glass on Wealth Beyond Riches. They explore why leaders are loneliest during transitions (not at the top), introduce Carey Lohrenz's prepare/perform/prevail framework for life change, and discuss the practice of 'margin' and the principle of working from a place of rest rather than resting from a place of work. Closes with stories from the Global Leadership Summit, including hosting Joni Eareckson Tada.

## Key Takeaways

- Leaders aren't loneliest at the top — they're loneliest in transition; build a support structure for change.
- Carey Lohrenz's framework for transitions: prepare, perform, prevail.
- Ask 'what am I taking with me and what am I leaving behind?' when changing roles, retiring, or selling a business.
- Even when a title doesn't change (founder becoming non-CEO), the role still shifts and old tools need to be set down.
- Margin (the blank space on the notebook page) is where rest and dreaming happen — adulthood squeezes it out.
- Work from a place of rest rather than rest from a place of work — countercultural but life-sustaining.
- Balance is stagnant; movement and shifting between domains is the actual goal.

### The unique wellness challenges of women in leadership [00:05:15]

Women experience more frequent vocational shifts — wife/mother life-stage transitions plus career changes. Often overlooked or under-coached at the top of their organizations.

### Carey Lohrenz's prepare/perform/prevail framework [00:08:24]

From Span of Control by the first female F-14 fighter pilot. Coaches use this for any major transition: retirement, role change, entrepreneurship, founder stepping aside.

### Loneliness is highest in transition, not at the top [00:09:27]

Research she cites: leaders feel loneliest during the change, not when they reach the destination. Plan for who's coming with you and what your proximity changes.

### Six-month change-integration period [00:11:33]

Major change takes about 6 months to fully settle. Acknowledging that you're in transition is itself part of the work.

### Title doesn't change but role does: founder transitions [00:13:39]

Even when a LinkedIn title stays the same (e.g., founder hires a CEO), the actual role and identity shift. Tools you've used (your 'tool belt') don't go away but can be set down.

### Handing tools to others: generosity in leadership [00:15:44]

Letting someone else pick up your tools is a form of generosity — it multiplies your impact and frees you to step into the next season.

### Balance is stagnant — movement is the goal [00:17:49]

Pursue movement, not balance. Always lean into one domain, then the other. The dotted line down the middle isn't where energy comes from.

### Margin: the notebook-paper analogy for rest [00:19:55]

Childhood notebook paper had wide margins for doodling and dreaming. Adulthood squeezes margin out because we're always showing people the middle of the page (resume, accomplishments).

### Work from a place of rest, not rest from a place of work [00:22:02]

Counter-cultural principle. Rest can mean physical rest, hobbies, walks, or just protected morning hours. No one will fight to protect your rest except you and your closest people.

### Personalize rest — no formula [00:24:09]

Rest is 'one-size-fits-one.' Try things, evaluate, adjust as seasons change. Permission to experiment.

### Effective vs. efficient: rest is effective [00:26:17]

Tim's framing: rest isn't efficient on a time-cost basis but it's highly effective. Work flows better after dreaming and rest.
