# 3 Shocking Retirement Spending Surprises from 280,000 Retirees

**Channel:** Streamline Financial
**Published:** 2025-08-25
**URL:** https://youtube.com/watch?v=b9zqRQ1chSc
**Duration:** 6:46

## Summary

Dave Zoller summarizes JP Morgan's research on 280,000+ retirees: (1) retirement spending decreases over time, peaking in late 40s/early 50s — actual inflation is 1.65%/yr not 3%, suggesting savings needs may be 20-25% lower than typical models; (2) over half of households don't retire all at once and partial retirees spend ~$5K more than fully retired; (3) 60% of retirees experience major spending fluctuations in the first few years — only 18% are 'steady Eddie'.

## Key Takeaways

- Retirement spending actually decreases over time — peaks in late 40s/early 50s.
- JP Morgan data shows real inflation is 1.65%/yr — meaning savings needs could be 20-25% lower than traditional models suggest.
- Over half of households don't retire all at once — partial retirement is the norm.
- Partial retirees spend ~$5K/yr more than fully-retired households at the same income.
- Only 18% of retirees are 'steady Eddie' spenders — 82% experience spending volatility.
- Volatility + market downturn early in retirement = elevated sequence-of-returns risk.
- Build flexibility into the plan; use a bucket strategy to avoid forced selling in down markets.

## Detailed Notes

### Why retirement plans miss reality [00:00:00]

Most planning software assumes spending rises 3%/yr with inflation. JP Morgan's study of 280,000+ retirees tells a different story — three insights follow.

### Insight 1 — Spending decreases [00:01:01]

Retirement spending follows a predictable curve that peaks in late 40s/early 50s and gradually declines. Healthcare rises, but travel/entertainment/transportation decreases more than offset it.

### Real inflation is 1.65% — not 3% [00:01:43]

JP Morgan data: actual annual increase is 1.65%, not the planning standard of 3%. Implication: savings need may be 20-25% lower than traditional models suggest. Six retirement spending patterns identified.

### Insight 2 — Partial retirement is the norm [00:02:31]

Over half of households don't retire all at once — one spouse keeps working or both reduce hours. Partial retirees spend ~$5K/yr more during the transition than fully-retired peers.

### Insight 3 — Most retirees have spending volatility [00:03:34]

Only 18% are steady spenders. 82% experience meaningful fluctuation, especially in the first few years. Six distinct spending patterns emerged in JP Morgan's data.

### Sequence-of-returns risk and the bucket fix [00:04:36]

Volatility + early-retirement market downturn can permanently damage portfolio viability. Streamline builds bear-market protection into every plan. Bucket strategy lets you avoid selling investments in down markets.

### Closing [00:05:38]

Traditional planning assumptions don't match reality. Retirement planning isn't just accumulating — it's creating a life strategy that gives confidence to enjoy what you've saved.
