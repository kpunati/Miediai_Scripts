# No Tax On Social Security Income?

**Channel:** Streamline Financial
**Published:** 2025-09-01
**URL:** https://youtube.com/watch?v=EREFXww07BI
**Duration:** 3:45

## Summary

Dave Zoller clarifies the new 2025 tax bill's 'no tax on Social Security' provision: it's actually a new $6,000-per-person deduction for those 65+, not an exemption of Social Security income. Available 2025-2028 only. Phases out at $75K AGI single / $150K married, gone at $175K / $250K. Available whether you itemize or take the standard deduction. Warning: don't change Social Security claiming strategy based on this new deduction alone.

## Key Takeaways

- 'No tax on Social Security' is misleading — it's actually a new $6,000 deduction for those 65+.
- Has nothing to do with Social Security directly — it's a senior bonus deduction.
- Available 2025-2028 only (temporary, four-year window).
- If 62-64 and taking SS, you cannot claim the deduction — must be 65+.
- Phases out at $75K AGI single / $150K married; gone at $175K / $250K.
- Works whether you itemize or take the standard deduction.
- Married 65+ standard deduction can total $46,700; single 65+ = $23,750.
- Don't change your Social Security claiming strategy based on this deduction alone.

## Detailed Notes

### Clearing up the 'no tax on Social Security' confusion [00:00:00]

The 2025 tax bill positioned 'no tax on Social Security' — but it's really a $6,000 senior bonus deduction. People hear the headline and consider taking SS earlier. The actual rule is age-based, not SS-based.

### Income limits [00:01:04]

Phases out at $75K modified AGI single / $150K married (wages, taxable SS, pensions, traditional IRA withdrawals). Disappears at $175K / $250K.

### Itemize or standard — both work [00:01:38]

Common question: do I get the deduction if I itemize? Answer: yes — it's available either way. Rob example: single, 66, $60K AGI gets $23,750 total standard deduction ($15,750 + $2,000 age + $6,000 senior).

### Married 65+ math [00:02:30]

Married both 65+: $31,500 + $3,200 age-based + $12,000 senior bonus = $46,700 total standard deduction.

### Don't change SS strategy on this alone [00:02:30]

There may be other reasons to time SS differently under the new bill — tax-efficient withdrawal strategy matters more. Don't make the SS decision based on this deduction in isolation.
