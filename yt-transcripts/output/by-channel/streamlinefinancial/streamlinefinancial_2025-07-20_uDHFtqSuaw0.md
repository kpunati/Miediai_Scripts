# The Retirement Assets Most People Ignore

**Channel:** Streamline Financial
**Published:** 2025-07-20
**URL:** https://youtube.com/watch?v=uDHFtqSuaw0
**Duration:** 4:22

## Summary

Dave Zoller shares the four-assets exercise learned from Ron Bulis at LifeWorks Advisors. Beyond financial assets, there are three others: human (faith, health, relationships, character, time), intellectual (skills, wisdom, experience), and community (church, neighbors, volunteer work, legacy). Less than 10% of clients pick financial as one of their top two when asked which two they'd keep — yet advisors spend 90% of their time on the financial bucket.

## Key Takeaways

- Everyone manages four asset types: financial, human, intellectual, community.
- Human assets: faith, health, relationships, family, character, time — the 'who you are' assets.
- Intellectual assets: skills, wisdom, experience — especially valuable into retirement.
- Community assets: church, neighborhood, volunteer work, legacy.
- Exercise: if you could only pass on two, which two would you choose? <10% pick financial.
- Most advisors talk only about financial — Streamline aligns money to serve the other three.
- The right question isn't 'how much do you need' — it's 'what kind of life do you want your money to make possible?'

## Detailed Notes

### Hook — 'I've been retired 5 years, why am I just learning this now?' [00:00:00]

A prospect who had been retired five years was floored by this exercise. Dave introduces the four-assets framework he learned from Ron Bulis at LifeWorks Advisors.

### Asset 1 — Financial assets [00:00:00]

401(k), IRAs, savings, real estate, stocks — the tangible. Necessary, but only one of four.

### Asset 2 — Human assets [00:01:01]

Faith, health, relationships, family, character, time. What makes you, you. Money without health or relationships isn't wealth.

### Asset 3 — Intellectual assets [00:01:01]

Skills, wisdom, experience, insights from career and life. Especially valuable for retirees who think they're 'done contributing' — they're not.

### Asset 4 — Community assets [00:01:01]

Church involvement, neighborhood connections, volunteer work, legacy, impact. Many retirees feel isolated because they never invested here.

### The exercise — pick two to keep [00:01:59]

If you could pass on only two of the four to those you care about most, which two? Less than 10% pick financial. Most pick human, intellectual, or community.

### The misalignment problem [00:03:03]

Most advisors spend 90% of time on the one asset class clients value least (financial). Streamline's question: how do we use financial assets to serve human, intellectual, and community assets?

### Closing [00:04:06]

Money is just a tool. The question is what you're trying to build with it.
