# How Recognizing Your Strengths Can Transform Your Life with Jackie Glass

**Channel:** Streamline Financial

## Summary

Tim continues with Jackie Glass on Wealth Beyond Riches, focused on Patrick Lencioni's Working Genius assessment (Wonder, Invention, Discernment, Galvanizing, Enablement, Tenacity). Jackie reveals Tim's two geniuses are Enablement and Tenacity, his competencies are Wonder and Discernment, and his frustrations are Galvanizing and Invention.

## Key Takeaways

- Working Genius (Lencioni): six stages — Wonder, Invention, Discernment, Galvanizing, Enablement, Tenacity. Each person has 2 geniuses, 2 competencies, 2 frustrations.
- Aim for ~51% genius time, ~30% competency time, ~19% frustration time at work.
- Burnout most often comes from over-using competencies (things you're good at but don't energize you), not frustrations.
- Tim's geniuses (Enablement + Tenacity) = implementation stage: showing up alongside people and ensuring results land.
- Generosity practice breaks money's unhealthy hold; spending on others delivers more joy than buying things or experiences alone.
- Don't box yourself into your top genius — shift environments to access weaker stages when needed (e.g., walks for Wonder).
- Currently loving: '10x Is Easier Than 2x' (Dan Sullivan / Benjamin Hardy) — focus on gains, not gaps.

### Episode intro and Working Genius setup [00:00:00]

Tim took the Working Genius assessment in 15 minutes; Jackie now reveals results live. He hasn't seen them yet.

### Working Genius vs personality assessments [00:03:10]

Most assessments (Enneagram, DISC, StrengthsFinder) are personality-focused. Working Genius is 80% productivity and 20% personality — built for team workflow.

### Why post-COVID Wellness + recognition + productivity matter [00:04:12]

Loneliness epidemic compared to smoking 15 cigarettes a day. Gallup: recognition disproportionately keeps top performers.

### Six stages of work: W-I-D-G-E-T [00:07:24]

Wonder (sense what could change), Invention (generate ideas), Discernment (evaluate ideas), Galvanizing (rally others), Enablement (come alongside and help), Tenacity (ensure results).

### Tim's results: Enablement and Tenacity [00:09:32]

Tim's two geniuses are Enablement and Tenacity — the implementation stage. Brought the cinnamon rolls to his daughter's swim team because he loves moving things forward and ensuring delivery.

### Genius / Competency / Frustration buckets [00:10:34]

Six stages split: 2 geniuses (lid-on coffee cup, natural energy), 2 competencies (cup with no lid, gets lukewarm), 2 frustrations (cup with hole at bottom).

### Tim's tenacity in action [00:11:37]

Streamline team would recognize Tim as the one who shows up next to colleagues to move work forward. Tenacity = lands the plane.

### Implementation-stage advisors and generosity [00:13:42]

Tim energized by working alongside clients. Money has unhealthy hold; practicing generosity breaks that hold.

### Three joys of spending: things, experiences, others [00:15:47]

Research: spending on things gives some joy, experiences more, spending on others most. Tim says enabling others is where his joy peaks.

### Reframing 'enablement' as a positive word [00:17:56]

Notes the term has a negative connotation in pop psychology but in Working Genius it's a strength.

### Time allocation guidance: 51 / 30 / 19 [00:18:59]

~51% genius, ~30% competency, ~19% frustration. Even at 19%, that's one hour per five — still tolerable.

### Tim's competencies: Wonder and Discernment [00:18:59]

Wonder = sensing what could change ('I wonder if we could…'). Discernment = picking the right idea to move forward. Lukewarm — good but expirable.

### Tim's frustrations: Galvanizing and Invention [00:20:05]

Galvanizing (rallying as outspoken mouthpiece) and Invention (generating many ideas) drain Tim's energy.

### Pair up with complementary types [00:21:08]

Dave Zoller is Invention; pairs naturally with Tim's frustrations. The team-fit principle of Working Genius.

### Burnout comes from over-use of competencies [00:24:18]

Biggest research surprise: people burn out from over-doing what they're competent at (because they've been recognized for it), not from time in frustrations.

### Don't box yourself in — shift environment for weaker stages [00:25:20]

Want to grow in Wonder? Change environment. Tim accesses Wonder while walking, biking, or by a river.

### Wonder happens by the river [00:27:26]

Tim describes rivers (alive, moving) as the place his Wonder is set free.

### Don't pick a career by category — be yourself in any role [00:28:31]

Working Genius cautions against picking a career based on a single genius. Every profession needs all six types.
