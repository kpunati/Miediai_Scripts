# How The New Tax Law Impacts Your Retirement - Big Beautiful Bill

**Channel:** Streamline Financial
**Published:** 2025-07-13
**URL:** https://youtube.com/watch?v=zRFasWAl6dA
**Duration:** 7:41

## Summary

Dave Zoller breaks down the new 2025 tax bill in plain English. Lower brackets are now permanent (the 2017 cuts were set to expire). Standard deduction rises to $31,500 married / $15,750 single. New senior bonus deduction ($6K per person 65+, phased out at higher AGI) runs 2025-2028 only. SALT cap raised from $10K to $40K. Charitable deduction available even for non-itemizers starting 2026 ($2K married / $1K single). Estate tax exemption raised to $15M per person.

## Key Takeaways

- 2017 tax brackets are now permanent — no 2026 increase as previously scheduled.
- 2025 standard deduction: $31,500 married / $15,750 single, plus extra $1,600/person if 65+.
- New senior bonus deduction: $6,000/person 65+, available 2025-2028 only — phases out at higher AGI.
- Combined 2025 deductions for married 65+ couple can total $46,700; with charitable deduction, up to ~$49,000.
- SALT cap quadrupled from $10K to $40K — big win for high-tax states (NY, CA, NJ, IL).
- Starting 2026: charitable deduction available even for non-itemizers ($2K married / $1K single).
- Estate tax exemption raised to $15M per person / $30M per couple.
- Three planning strategies: Roth conversions, IRA withdrawal smoothing, capital gains harvesting — 2025-2028 is a golden window.

## Detailed Notes

### Tax brackets stay low [00:00:00]

The 2017 lower tax brackets were set to revert in 2026 — the new bill keeps them in place. Lower-bracket years to come, opening tax-planning opportunities.

### Standard deduction increases [00:01:02]

2025 married: $31,500 (up from $30K). Single: $15,750 (up from $15K). Additional $1,600/person for 65+ married ($3,200 combined), or $2,000 for single 65+.

### Senior bonus deduction [00:01:51]

Brand new: $6,000/person 65+ ($12K married). Stacks on top of standard. Temporary — only 2025-2028. Phases out at $75K single / $150K married, gone at $175K single / $250K married AGI. Example: married 65+ couple at $100K income gets $46,700 in deductions, only $53,300 taxable.

### Charitable deduction for standard deductors [00:03:29]

Starting 2026: even non-itemizers can deduct up to $2,000 married / $1,000 single in charitable gifts. Total married 65+ standard deduction package: ~$49,000.

### SALT cap raised to $40,000 [00:04:32]

Previous $10K cap raised to $40K. Big benefit in high-tax states (CA, NY, NJ, IL). Phases out above $500K modified AGI, eventually back to $10K above that.

### Estate tax exemption raised [00:05:20]

Previously set to revert to ~$6.8M/person. New exemption: $15M/person, $30M/couple. Major estate-planning shift.

### Planning strategies — the 2025-2028 window [00:06:23]

Three strategies for the four-year window: (1) Roth conversions while rates are low; (2) IRA withdrawal smoothing — use traditional IRA income now to control future RMD-driven taxes; (3) capital gains harvesting — selling appreciated assets at low or zero tax.
