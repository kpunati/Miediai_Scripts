# 7 Investments That Will REDUCE Your Taxes Immediately

**Channel:** Streamline Financial
**Published:** 2024-05-23
**URL:** https://youtube.com/watch?v=GqzciqIgKPQ
**Duration:** 26:08

## Summary

Dave Zoller walks through seven investments that reduce taxes. Four common ones (oil and gas partnerships, real estate, municipal bonds, traditional IRA/401k) and three less-discussed (HSA, small business, donor-advised fund / impact investing). For each, he scores tax deduction on contribution, tax on income, and tax on sale. Recommends impact investing via a DAF as the best-deduction option since the full contribution is deductible immediately.

## Key Takeaways

- Seven tax-advantaged investments scored on three criteria: deductible contribution, tax-free income, tax-free sale.
- Oil-and-gas partnerships offer big year-1 deductions (IDCs ~60-80%) but carry high commodity and regulatory risk.
- Real estate offers depreciation deductions; passive-activity loss limits apply unless you qualify as a real-estate professional; 1031 exchanges defer gain at sale and step-up at death wipes basis.
- Municipal bonds: tax-free federal income but no deduction; common over-allocation risk in high-net-worth portfolios; munis in IRAs waste their tax shield (asset-location mistake).
- Traditional IRAs/401ks: deductible contribution, deferred growth, taxed at withdrawal; Roth = reverse.
- HSA = triple-tax-advantaged (deductible, tax-free growth, tax-free qualified withdrawals); usable post-65 for medical expenses even after stopping contributions at Medicare enrollment.
- Small businesses let you deduct part of lifestyle (home office, vehicle, supplies) for legitimate income-producing activity.
- Donor-advised funds + impact investing: full deduction on contribution, tax-free growth, tax-free distribution to charity — best-tax-treatment option, and adds an 'investing for others' dimension.

## Detailed Notes

### Framing: seven investments scored on three tax dimensions [00:00:00]

Dave Zoller introduces the framework: seven investments that reduce taxes or create tax-free growth/income, each scored on (1) deductibility, (2) tax on income, (3) tax on sale. Four are commonly known; three are less-discussed. Mentions running both Streamline Financial (wealth) and Zoller Swanson (tax planning).

### #1 Oil and gas exploration & drilling [00:01:03]

Partnership/fund structure. Big tax advantage: intangible drilling costs (IDCs) equal ~60-80% of the investment, deductible in year one against other income (not just passive). 15% of income is shielded by depletion. Gain at sale is taxable. High volatility — commodity prices, regulation, drilling uncertainty.

### #2 Real estate (income-producing) [00:04:10]

Depreciation deductions, accelerated depreciation strategies. Passive activity losses generally trapped unless you're an active participant (deduction up to $25K below $100K AGI, phases out to $150K) or qualify as a real-estate professional (50%+ work hours). 1031 exchanges defer gain at sale; step-up in basis at death wipes the gain entirely.

### #3 Municipal bonds [00:09:27]

Federal tax-free income but no deduction. 43 of 50 states tax out-of-state muni interest. Watch over-allocation: munis often don't beat inflation. Asset-location mistake: holding munis inside an IRA wastes the tax shield.

### #4 Traditional IRA / 401k / Solo 401k [00:12:38]

Deductible contribution (income limits apply for traditional IRA), tax-deferred growth, taxed at withdrawal as ordinary income. Roth = no deduction, tax-free growth and withdrawal. Almost any investment can sit inside.

### #5 HSA (triple tax-advantaged) [00:14:46]

Deductible (~$8K MFJ family / ~$4K single), tax-free growth, tax-free qualified withdrawals. Requires high-deductible health plan, no Medicare enrollment. Post-65 can still spend on medical expenses tax-free; possible Roth-rollover scenarios.

### #6 Small business / side business [00:16:53]

Deduct home office, vehicle, supplies, travel where legitimate. Net profit is taxed; equity sale is taxable. Examples: weekend artist, gardener-farmers selling at markets, freelance writer, consultant. Lets retirees deduct part of their lifestyle while staying engaged.

### #7 Donor-advised fund + impact investing [00:20:04]

Full deduction on contribution to a DAF. Money stays invested tax-free (no tax on income or sale). 'Impact investing' = grant DAF dollars to businesses doing good (example: Uganda motorbike company); financial returns flow back into the DAF for re-granting. Like a foundation without foundation costs.

### Closing: investments for self vs investments for others [00:24:13]

Frames the seven as a spectrum from for-self (oil/gas, real estate, munis) to for-others (DAF/impact). Mentions personal involvement with Flourish Rwanda. Streamline helps clients build giving into the income plan.
