# Stop Letting Phones Steal Your Days: Joey Odom on Digital Wellness, Time Freedom, and Leadership

**Channel:** Streamline Financial
**Published:** 2025-01-09
**URL:** https://youtube.com/watch?v=LUc-f6jhn1A
**Duration:** 68:17

## Summary

Tim Meisenheimer interviews Joey Odom (co-founder of Aro) on Wealth Beyond Riches about digital wellness. Odom shares his pivot from commercial real estate to Aro, the 5 S's framework (start with self, two hours phone-free a day, sacred times/places, search for moments of connection, stop at nothing), and Aro's shift to a B2B church/wellness-benefit model. Aro users average ~1 month/year reclaimed off their phones; the company has logged over 2 million phone-free hours.

## Key Takeaways

- Phones have staked claims in five areas: attention, moments, time, relationships, futures.
- 5 S's framework: start with self, spend two hours phone-free a day, set sacred times/places, search for moments of connection, stop at nothing.
- The 'six powerful words' when a bid for connection appears: 'Let me put away my phone.'
- Aro shifted to a B2B / wellness-benefit model serving church and faith-driven organizations (with family membership included).
- The Digital Fast (Darren Whitehead-led, partnered with Aro/Think Media/Gabe Lyons) is a 28-day church program: Detach, Discover, Delight, Determine.
- The number one predictor of how a child uses a phone is how the parent uses theirs today.
- Odom's view on money: 'luxury is overrated; a little margin is underrated' — they've gone ~4 years without taking a salary while continuing to give.
- Donor-advised funds are useful for lumpy income years — get the tax deduction but distribute over time.

## Detailed Notes

### Cold open and game with Joey's intros [00:00:00]

Opens with Odom on shame as 'great indicator, terrible motivator.' Tim plays an intro-writing game: ChatGPT-Joey vs Tim's-own. Joey is co-founder of Aro, a tech company that helps people put down phones.

### Joey's career: childhood scarcity to commercial real estate to Aro [00:06:24]

At 12 his dad's small business struggled and his parents told the boys they'd cover basics but the rest was up to them. Umpired, mowed lawns, etc. Eventually Reebok sales, then 15 years in commercial real estate, then a sense at his wife's 40th-birthday massage that he was being called to Aro.

### Aro's mission and the five areas phones have claimed [00:12:40]

Aro helps people reclaim time from phones. Five areas: attention, moments, time, relationships, futures. Started DTC with the Aro Box charging station + app; tens of thousands of users.

### Pivot to B2B / wellness benefit for churches and faith orgs [00:13:44]

Aro is shifting to a B2B model — providing Aro as a wellness benefit to church staffs and faith-driven organizations. Each membership covers the whole family. Helps with burnout in ministry.

### Modeling vs mandating: the three Ms [00:19:58]

Parents model bad phone behavior to kids, then give them phones, then get angry when kids mimic. Statistically the number one predictor of a child's phone use is the parent's phone use today. Smoking in youth fell ~78% as adult smoking fell.

### The 5 S's framework [00:23:04]

(1) Start with yourself. (2) Spend 2 hours/day physically away from your phone (Aro user average; 91% of people have phone with them 24/7; 2 hours = 1 month/year). (3) Sacred times and places (e.g., dinner table). (4) Search for moments of connection — Gottman bids; the six powerful words 'let me put away my phone.' (5) Stop at nothing to get it right — spend money on the problem.

### Environment beats willpower; decision fatigue [00:30:22]

Cites James Clear: environment is stronger than willpower. The largest source of decision fatigue is the constant phone-or-not decision; removing the phone removes hundreds of micro-decisions a day.

### Deep work and the cost of distraction [00:33:32]

Gloria Mark (UC Irvine): 23 min 15 sec to refocus after distraction. The two main Aro use-tags are family time and deep work (Cal Newport). Aro users have collectively reclaimed 2M+ hours off their phones.

### Digital Fast: 28-day church program [00:42:56]

Darren Whitehead (Church of the City Nashville) wrote The Digital Fast. Partnered with Aro/Think Media/Gabe Lyons. 28 days, four phases: Detach (remove distracting apps), Discover, Delight (dopamine reset), Determine. Available at digitalfast2025.com.

### Career transition wisdom: walking through open doors [00:50:15]

Looking back, the move from real estate to Aro was a slow open-door process with his wife. Living in the imagined future (Wall Street Journal Mansion section, West Village fantasies) was robbing him of presence — moving to Aro forced him into the present.

### Money: luxury overrated, margin underrated; giving even without salary [00:55:28]

Saved well in real-estate career; haven't taken a salary in ~4 years. Found luxury is overrated and a little margin is underrated. Continue to give from investments and savings as an act of faith — kids see it modeled.

### Closing: college football transfer portal vs driving daughter to school [01:03:53]

Joey's 'one thing I'm loving': scrolling the college football transfer portal (Oklahoma Sooners fan). Tim's: driving his 16-year-old daughter to school because there aren't many days left of that.
