# Build A Retirement Paycheck That Works in ANY Market

**Channel:** Streamline Financial
**Published:** 2025-08-18
**URL:** https://youtube.com/watch?v=Sg7elkgxElw
**Duration:** 5:10

## Summary

Dave Zoller walks through the three-bucket retirement income strategy used during March 2020 with a client who'd just retired and panicked during the COVID crash. Bucket 1 = 1-3 years of expenses in cash/cash-equivalents; Bucket 2 = 5-8 years in income-producing/conservative investments (dividends and interest can refill bucket 1); Bucket 3 = growth equities for 10+ year horizon. The system provided peace of mind without selling into the down market.

## Key Takeaways

- Three-bucket system: cash (1-3 years), conservative income (5-8 years), growth (10+ years).
- March 2020 example: client called panicked; reviewing buckets showed multiple years of safe withdrawals — relief was immediate.
- Bucket 1 is the retirement checking account on steroids — calculate annual spend × 1-3 years.
- Bucket 2 holds income-producing investments; dividends and interest can refill bucket 1 manually or automatically.
- Bucket 3 = growth equities for inflation protection over 10-30 year retirement horizons.
- Quarterly check-in: rebalance from bucket 3 to bucket 1/2 when stocks are up; let bucket 3 recover when down.
- The system delivers sleep-soundly peace of mind in both good and bad markets.

## Detailed Notes

### The system that survived March 2020 [00:00:00]

Three-bucket strategy used with a client who'd just retired when COVID hit. Reviewing the buckets revealed multiple years of safe withdrawals — relief was immediate.

### Bucket 1 — Cash and equivalents [00:00:53]

1-3 years of annual expenses in cash or cash-like investments. Example: $50K/yr spender holds $50K-$150K here. Won't move when markets drop.

### Bucket 2 — Conservative income [00:01:51]

5-8 years of expenses in income-producing investments. Some fluctuation, but lower volatility than equities. Dividends and interest can refill bucket 1 manually or automatically.

### Recap of buckets 1 and 2 [00:02:52]

Bucket 1 = retirement checking on steroids. Bucket 2 = conservative portfolio. Together cover 6-11 years of expenses.

### Bucket 3 — Growth [00:02:52]

10+ year horizon for retirees living into their 80s-90s. Inflation averages 2-3%/yr; over 20-30 years that's huge. Growth equities protect purchasing power. Bucket 3 fluctuates but you don't need it for a decade.

### Putting it together [00:03:54]

Draw from bucket 1 for monthly expenses. Quarterly check-in: rebalance buckets when stocks are up; let bucket 3 recover when stocks are down. Live off cash cushion in bad markets.

### Closing [00:04:56]

Talk to your advisor — this isn't personalized advice. Retirement isn't just about numbers — it's about sleeping soundly with a system that works in any market.
