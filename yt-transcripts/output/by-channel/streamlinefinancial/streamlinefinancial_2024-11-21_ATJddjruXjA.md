# Burnout, Sabbatical, and Renewed Leadership with Kristen Welch

**Channel:** Streamline Financial
**Published:** 2024-11-21
**URL:** https://youtube.com/watch?v=ATJddjruXjA
**Duration:** 34:07

## Summary

Tim continues the conversation with Kristen Welch on Wealth Beyond Riches about her three-month sabbatical taken right after becoming CEO of Mercy House Global. After 13 years of nonprofit founding and a series of crises (warehouse collapse, fire at a Kenya home), she stepped away — spent the first 10 minutes in silence having a panic attack, broke her foot on day one, then unwound layers of anger and reactivity.

## Key Takeaways

- Kristen took her first sabbatical 13 years after founding Mercy House — wishes she had taken one sooner.
- Warning sign progression: 'I have answers' → 'I don't know' → 'I don't care' — the last is when you must act.
- Sabbatical was mostly silence and solitude; first 10 minutes triggered a panic attack and she broke her foot on day one.
- Layered anger and defensiveness surfaced and dissolved over the 3-month sabbatical — family noticed.
- Mercy House now offers full sabbatical at 7 years tenure, paid weekly Clarity breaks, paid therapy, trauma-informed culture.
- Eugene Peterson: workplace is #1 place for spiritual formation — leaders shape culture for good or ill.
- Hurry is violence against your own soul; turning down the noise allows you to hear the whisper.
- James 1 reframe: 'consider it joy when you face trials' — joy and difficulty coexist; trials drive perspective.

## Detailed Notes

### Stepping into CEO already burned out [00:00:00]

Became CEO after 12 years as Executive Director — already deeply burned out from COVID, fundraising crises, and growth pains. Started CEO role at empty.

### Why sabbatical was her first CEO ask [00:02:06]

Most CEOs answer to a board unfamiliar with sabbaticals. Kristen made it her first ask because she had nothing left to give.

### Vulnerability crisis: warehouse collapse and Kenya fire [00:03:10]

Within 2 years of CEO role: a fire destroyed one of the Kenya homes and the Texas warehouse collapsed. Organization felt existentially vulnerable.

### 'I'm burned out' = spiritual death (Nouwen) [00:05:20]

Henri Nouwen frame: burnout is a convenient psychological term for spiritual death. Realized 'doing things for God' is not the same as 'being with God.'

### Three-stage warning sign: answers → don't know → don't care [00:06:25]

Founders go from 'I have all the answers' to 'I don't know' to 'I don't care' — the last is the dangerous one and triggered her sabbatical.

### Silence and solitude as the sabbatical core [00:07:27]

Ruth Haley Barton's 'Invitation to Solitude and Silence' was the framing book. Husband traveled three weeks to Egypt; kids in college; mostly isolated.

### Panic attack, broken foot, no phone [00:08:30]

First 10 minutes of practicing silence without her phone triggered a panic attack. Broke her foot day one — couldn't drive, couldn't escape.

### Layered anger surfacing and dissolving [00:13:51]

Layer after layer of defensive anger surfaced from childhood — old reactivity that resolved by week 6-8. Husband: 'that anger you used to flash is just gone.'

### Sabbatical policy at Mercy House Global [00:15:59]

Full sabbatical at 7 years of tenure. Currently 4 staff in the pipeline; takes ~6 months of preparation and handoff. Modeled after retired Sabbath traditions.

### Other team practices: Clarity breaks, therapy, trauma-informed [00:15:59]

Paid weekly 1-hour Clarity breaks (no questions asked). Paid therapy visits. Trauma-informed workplace because team works with overcomers of trafficking.

### Eugene Peterson: workplace = #1 place for spiritual formation [00:17:03]

Stopped Kristen in her tracks. Leaders shape spiritual formation in the workplace — for good or ill.

### Two-hour weekly spiritual formation class at work [00:18:06]

Optional, by invitation. Reads books together. Recognizes that work shapes people whether intentionally or not; chose to shape it on purpose.

### Book recommendations: Comer, Barton, Scazzero [00:21:20]

John Mark Comer ('The Ruthless Elimination of Hurry', 'Practicing the Way', 'Live No Lies'), Peter Scazzero ('Emotionally Healthy Spirituality'), Ruth Haley Barton ('From Sabbath to Sabbatical').

### Smartphones, silence, and turning down the noise [00:24:30]

Kristen: 'I can't hear the whisper if I don't turn down the noise.' Daughter spontaneously said 'I think I'm addicted to my phone' — soul recognizing the cost.

### Training for Grand Canyon Rim-to-Rim at 50 [00:26:36]

Decided to hike the Rim-to-Rim 11 months out. Training on stair master in scorching heat. Parallels spiritual discipline — practice prepares you for the experience.

### Loving: nature, breeze, slowing down [00:30:50]

What's bringing her joy: noticing the breeze, the birds, slowing down to enjoy the natural world.

### James 1: consider it joy when you face trials [00:31:53]

Hard year personally has driven her to deeper dependence on God. Considers it joy in a weird depressing way — joy and trial coexist.

### Loving: fireplace morning routine [00:31:53]

Tim removed the TV from the fireplace room; now uses it for morning reading, coffee, his 19-year-old beagle. Kids and friends also gather there now.
