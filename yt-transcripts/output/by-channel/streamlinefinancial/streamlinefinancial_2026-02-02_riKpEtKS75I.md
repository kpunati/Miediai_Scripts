# Many People Will Overpay in Retirement Taxes

**Channel:** Streamline Financial
**Published:** 2026-02-02
**URL:** https://youtube.com/watch?v=riKpEtKS75I
**Duration:** 5:17

## Summary

Dave Zoller explains how married couples can withdraw over $120K/year (~$80K for singles) in retirement and pay zero federal tax by stacking the 0% long-term capital gains bracket on top of the standard deduction. Strategy requires holding investments over a year in taxable brokerage accounts (focused on low-dividend growth investments), funded aggressively during working years alongside normal 401(k) saving. Window: typically pre-Social-Security, pre-RMD retirement years.

## Key Takeaways

- Married couples can withdraw over $120K/year (singles ~$80K) federally tax-free via the 0% long-term capital gains bracket plus standard deduction.
- Requires holding investments over one year for long-term capital gains treatment.
- Aggressively fund a taxable brokerage alongside 401(k) — taxable accounts have no early withdrawal penalty, no RMD, and sometimes better tax treatment.
- Tax-location: growth-oriented (low-dividend) investments in taxable; dividend-heavy investments in tax-deferred or Roth.
- Any other income (dividends, interest) reduces the available tax-free capital-gains space.
- Strategy works best in pre-Social-Security, pre-RMD retirement years.
- Wealth isn't what you earn — it's what you keep.

## Detailed Notes

### The headline strategy [00:00:00]

Married couples can withdraw over $120K/year (singles ~$80K) federally tax-free in retirement. Built into the tax code via the 0% long-term capital gains bracket plus the standard deduction.

### Why long-term capital gains are different [00:01:03]

Married filing jointly under ~$96K taxable income (singles under ~$48K) pay 0% on long-term capital gains. Adding the standard deduction creates over $120K of federally tax-free access. Requires investments held over one year in ETFs/index funds/individual stocks — generally low-dividend.

### Funding the taxable brokerage during working years [00:02:06]

While working, max 401(k) normally for the match and deduction — then ALSO build a taxable brokerage aggressively. Advantages: no early withdrawal penalty, no RMDs, different tax treatment in retirement.

### Tax-location: what goes where [00:02:06]

Growth-oriented investments in taxable accounts (you get long-term capital gains treatment). Dividend-heavy investments in tax-deferred or Roth, where dividends don't trigger annual taxation.

### Hold for 1+ year for LTCG treatment [00:02:55]

Critical: hold investments at least one year to qualify for long-term capital gains. Without that, the whole strategy collapses to ordinary income.

### Example: age 60 retiree [00:02:55]

Imagine retiring at 60 with a built-up taxable portfolio. From 60 to 65 — before Social Security and RMDs — withdraw $100K annually tax-free with no other work income, no SS, no 401(k) distributions, no penalties.

### Pitfalls and constraints [00:03:34]

Other income (dividends, interest) reduces the tax-free space. The earlier you start funding the taxable account, the more tax-free retirement wealth is possible. Keep dividend-heavy assets in tax-deferred/Roth so capital gains stay 0%-eligible. Wealth is what you keep, not what you earn.
