# Redefining Success: Dave Zoller's Approach to Money and Life

## Summary

Tim continues with Dave Zoller on Wealth Beyond Riches. Dave discusses his philosophy that money is important but should never be the main thing — treat it as a steward and helper rather than master. Covers servant mindset learned from his dad Steve, three Cs as the framework for retirement fulfillment, finding strengths via StrengthsFinder, AI's coming impact on financial advice (par-planner roles at risk; relational human-first advisors will thrive), and the.

## Key Takeaways

- Money is important but shouldn't be the main thing — treat it as a steward/helper, not master.
- Bronze-medalist mindset: a small change in framing (bronze vs. silver) flips comparison from gap to gain.
- Practice gratitude on both receiving AND spending — even on expenses like a sump-pump replacement.
- Servant mindset compounds: Dave learned it from his dad Steve, then modeled it for the next generation.
- Retirement fulfillment formula: find your unique ability (StrengthsFinder), then use a small % of time to help others.
- Three Cs: creativity/curiosity, connection, contribution — and 'churos' as the bonus C joke.
- AI will displace par-planners but elevate human-first advisors who pair relationship with technical work.
- Connecting clients to each other (founder to founder, retiree to retiree) is a high-leverage form of service.
