# The Retirement Sweet Spot: Stop Contributing & Coast

**Channel:** Streamline Financial
**Published:** 2026-05-04
**URL:** https://youtube.com/watch?v=kC-Ui8givg0
**Duration:** 9:29

## Summary

Dave Zoller introduces two retirement milestones that shift the psychology of saving: (1) the crossover point — where annual portfolio growth exceeds annual contributions; (2) the coast number — the amount invested today that, with compound growth alone, hits the retirement income goal without future contributions.

## Key Takeaways

- Two psychology-shifting milestones: the crossover point and the coast number.
- Crossover point: when annual portfolio growth exceeds annual contributions.
- Sarah example: $25K/yr contributions, 7% returns — crossover around $750K invested.
- Coast number: amount needed today so compound growth alone reaches the retirement goal.
- Four-step coast-number calculation: income need, minus guaranteed income, target portfolio at 4%, discount back at 5% real return.
- Reaching these milestones shifts retirement from 'pushing a boulder uphill' to 'working with gravity.'
- Volatility and missed contributions feel less dangerous after these milestones.

## Detailed Notes

### Two milestones most people miss [00:00:00]

Not the first $500K or $1M. The two milestones that matter psychologically: the crossover point and the coast number. They turn retirement planning from pushing a boulder uphill into working with gravity (the Sisyphus reference).

### Two engines of wealth [00:01:36]

Engine 1: contributions. Engine 2: compound growth. Early career, contributions do 90% of the work. At some point, the math flips and growth becomes the primary driver.

### Sarah's crossover walkthrough [00:02:36]

Sarah, 45, contributes $25K/yr, 7% return. At $200K: ~$14K growth. At $350K: ~$24K. At $500K: ~$35K. At $750K: $50K+ growth — twice her contribution. That's the crossover. Contributions become supporting actor, not main actor.

### Reaching the crossover changes the psychology [00:03:28]

She stops feeling solely responsible. Doesn't mean stop contributing — just means she can breathe easier.

### The coast number defined [00:04:01]

Amount invested today such that even without another contribution, compound growth alone hits the retirement goal. Not about early retirement — about peace of mind.

### Four-step coast-number calculation [00:04:56]

(1) Retirement income need in today's dollars — say $80K. (2) Subtract guaranteed income — say $30K SS = $50K from investments. (3) Target portfolio at 4% rule = $1.25M. (4) Discount back at 5% real return over 15 years: $1.25M / 1.05^15 ≈ $600K. So $600K today is the coast number.

### $5M client story [00:06:14]

Type-A doctor client kept asking 'how much until I get to $5M?' The real question: what does $5M unlock? Answer: confidence his wife would be okay if he died. A what-if scenario in the plan showed she already had enough — eased his mind without needing the $5M number.

### The psychological shift [00:06:47]

Before these milestones, every market drop feels dangerous, every missed contribution feels catastrophic. After: volatility becomes manageable; contributions feel like bonus security. Retirement transforms from distant hope to within-reach reality.

### Action steps [00:07:49]

Calculate income need realistically. Determine portfolio target using 4% rule as a starting point. Calculate coast number based on years to retirement. Identify where you are on the crossover. Adjust as life changes.
