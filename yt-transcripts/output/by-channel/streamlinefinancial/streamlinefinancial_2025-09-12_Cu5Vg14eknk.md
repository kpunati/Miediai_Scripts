# Building a Legacy That Lasts: Hobby Lobby CEO David Green & Author Bill High

**Channel:** Streamline Financial
**Published:** 2025-09-12
**URL:** https://youtube.com/watch?v=Cu5Vg14eknk
**Duration:** 56:07

## Summary

Interview with David Green (founder/CEO of Hobby Lobby) and family consultant Bill Hy on the Wealth Beyond Riches podcast. Hobby Lobby started with a $600 loan, now 1,000+ stores and $7.5B+ revenue. Green gives 50% of profits to ministry, takes only a salary based on his job (no profit distributions).

## Key Takeaways

- Hobby Lobby was started with a $600 loan in 1972 — now 1,000+ stores and $7.5B+ in revenue.
- Green family takes only salaries; 50% of profits go to ministry, with no family profit distributions.
- Family vision/mission/values are more important than the business — yet most families never write them down.
- Monthly family giving meetings (7 family members, Gen 1 and Gen 2) decide ministry gifts; Gen 3 sits in.
- Retire TO something, not FROM something — purpose matters more than the date.
- Wealth consumer vs wealth creator — pass on too much without values and the family fails.
- Storytelling preserves family culture across generations — keep telling the story.
- Family meetings should include vision/mission/values review, giving rhythm, conflict-on-the-table.
- Closed Sundays and 8pm close at Hobby Lobby = guardrails so employees can prioritize family.

## Detailed Notes

### Cold open — keeping the family together [00:00:00]

David Green talks about how vision, mission, and values have held the family together. They meet annually to celebrate and reinforce who they are.

### Tim introduces Hobby Lobby's story [00:01:03]

$600 loan, now 1,000+ stores and $7.5B+ in revenue. Family gives all profits away — invests in new stores and gives the rest. New book The Legacy Life releases October 14.

### How Hobby Lobby started [00:02:08]

Borrowed $600 to manufacture small picture frames. Kids (7 and 9) glued frames together. Wife ran the business while David kept his TG&Y job. First store opened 1972 — 600 sq ft in Oklahoma City. Now over 1,000 stores.

### First year revenue [00:04:00]

Made $30,000 in the first year. David left TG&Y in 1975. Lots of hard times early on.

### Lessons from his parents [00:04:37]

Parents were pastors of small churches. Main lesson: giving — paid tithe with vegetables in the 1940s farming economy. Values matter more than business skill.

### Legacy mindset and marriage values [00:06:56]

When David and Barbara married, three goals: marriage that lasts, kids who serve the Lord, successful business. Now expanded to telling great-grandchildren about Jesus.

### Why families don't articulate values [00:08:34]

Bill Hy: thousands spent on company vision/mission/values consulting — but families rarely do it. Three to five family values, vision, mission statement = legacy infrastructure.

### Closing on Sundays — the guardrail [00:12:50]

Hobby Lobby closes Sundays and at 8pm so employees can prioritize family. Walk the walk: religious practice, generosity, family time.

### 50% of profits to ministry [00:23:00]

Family takes salaries only — no profit distributions. 'God owns everything.' 50% to ministries, then taxes, then open new stores, then investment portfolio.

### Del Webb and the retirement mindset [00:27:19]

Bill: 1960, 72% of families were mom, dad, kids around the dinner table. 1962, Del Webb was Time Man of the Year — promoting 'it's my time' move-away retirement. Today only ~37% of families are traditional configuration. Retirement-mindset detachment contributed to family decline.

### David on retirement [00:24:21]

He'd rather work 6 days/week than retire. 'Retire TO something, not FROM something.' If you have something to retire to, God bless you. He has people who can do his job — but he likes working.

### Wealth creator vs consumer [00:35:06]

Bill on Tim's listening audience: passing too much wealth without work-ethic and values transmission destroys families. 'Be a wealth creator, not just a wealth consumer.'

### Family meeting rhythm [00:18:01]

Green family: 7 members of Gen 1 and Gen 2 meet monthly to decide ministry giving; Gen 3 grandkids observe. Annual gathering reviews vision/mission/values. Bill suggests families with young kids cover updates, schedule, vision/mission, goals, giving rhythm, and conflict topics.

### Generosity destinations and ministry partners [00:48:05]

Investments include One Hope, Every Home for Christ, He Gets Us, The Chosen, Museum of the Bible, Bible translation efforts (aiming for 6,000 languages by 2033).

### David's personal rhythm [00:32:35]

Main thing = buying and selling merchandise (80% of his time with buyers). Coffee + bagel mornings, splits a napkin with Barbara, no laptop or computer, uses a pad with restaurants, family names, scriptures, ministries, buyers.
