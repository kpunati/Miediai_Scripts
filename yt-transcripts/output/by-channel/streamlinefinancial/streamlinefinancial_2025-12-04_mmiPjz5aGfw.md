## Summary

Long-form Wealth Unriches interview between Dave Zoller and Riley Fuller, founder of nonprofit Humanity and Hope United and Generous Coffee. Fuller recounts a seven-generation Indiana food-business family, a derailment into addiction and arrests, a derivatives-trading career, a 2008 night-of-success crisis that re-surfaced a 2007 Honduras vision, and the founding of a hope-based development nonprofit.

## Key Takeaways

- Riley Fuller grew up as seventh-generation in his family's Indiana food business (Little Crow Foods).
- A 2006 Honduras trip introduced the 'birth lottery' framing — geographic and family circumstances we don't choose.
- After arrests and addiction, a 2008 derivatives-trading celebration plus an encounter with a homeless man crystallized a sense of life-value crisis.
- Humanity and Hope United uses hope-based development — measuring and building hope as the primary lever for community outcomes.
- Hope is defined as acting and believing the best is yet to come, built on goal + pathway + agency.
- Servant leadership (Global Leadership Summit framing) replaced 'manage by force' for Fuller.
- Best ways to get involved: visit Honduras with a trip, follow on Instagram, donate, or help fund and build a home.

## Detailed Notes

### Cold open: anger at oppressive poverty [00:00:00]

Fuller opens describing how seeing severe Honduran poverty made him angry and sad — feeling the typical solutions were a 'non sequitur' to the depth of the problem.

### Intro: who Riley is and what to listen for [00:00:46]

Zoller introduces Riley Fuller and the Humanity and Hope United partnership. Foreshadows the 'birth lottery' concept and servant leadership replacing manage-by-force.

### Seven-generation family food business in Indiana [00:04:32]

Little Crow Foods milled corn/wheat in Indiana for 100+ years. Became the country's largest cornstarch private labeler. Fuller worked there from ~age 15 and absorbed family-atmosphere business values.

### Dreams of pro soccer, then a downfall [00:10:12]

Got a D1 soccer offer, downgraded to D3 for a girlfriend, was cheated on, started numbing the pain. Eleven arrests in three years; got kicked out of school while captain of the soccer team.

### Sitting in jail; dad's invitation to Honduras [00:13:35]

While interning for Lincoln Financial, Fuller did a month in county jail. After release at 21, his dad invited him to Honduras a second time — Fuller accepted this time.

### First Honduras trip: the birth lottery [00:15:52]

Saw communities where, born there, education ends between 3rd and 6th grade, girls can expect victimization with impunity, and families earn $1-10/day. Frames the 'birth lottery' — the unchosen landing spot that determines opportunity.

### Returns to USA; numbs through college and starts derivatives job [00:23:37]

Fuller treats Honduras as a hallucination, finishes senior year, takes a derivatives-trading job at Lincoln Financial.

### Honduras summer for sobriety [00:24:52]

Moves to Honduras for a summer to avoid drug/jail wreckage, volunteers for multiple organizations including being hazed by Hondurans. Gets giardia from filtering river water through a shirt; gets fired for wearing sandals.

### 2008 celebration night and homeless encounter [00:29:50]

Big credit-default-swap trade. Late-night dinner with the UBS executive who created CDS during the time he was testifying to Congress. Gave a homeless man food on the way home — 'you must be some kind of angel' shattered Fuller's self-image.

### Existential crisis and resurfaced Honduras vision [00:33:42]

A few days of darkness; out of nowhere the Honduras vision returns. Got the vision for what is now Humanity and Hope in 2007; started pursuing it in 2010 after the crisis.

### Generous Coffee plug and 100% profit donation [00:02:59]

Generous Coffee gives away 100% of profits to nonprofits.

### What Humanity and Hope does today [00:41:08]

15-year-old nonprofit. Communities where members had no electricity, no education, no income — kids Fuller met at 6-7 are now professional university grads serving their own communities. Builds 'hope' as a mindset.

### Defining hope as a 3-legged stool [00:44:37]

Hope = acting and believing the best is yet to come. Three legs: goal, pathway, agency (Holy Spirit). Once built, hope reinforces itself neurologically and is contagious.

### Six pillars of community development [00:46:16]

H&H supports community leadership boards across six pillars (health care, education, economy, community leadership, etc.). Heavy investment in leadership training, including bringing locals to the Global Leadership Summit.

### Global Leadership Summit and servant leadership [00:49:09]

Pre-Honduras, Fuller managed by force/manipulation to hit Wall Street quarterly goals. GLS (since 2013) reframed leadership as servant leadership — people love to follow.

### Measuring hope with Brookings fellow [00:53:18]

Co-developed an 11-question participatory survey with a Brookings fellow originally from Peru. First measurements in 2024, second this year; goal is to link hope scores to outcomes across the six pillars (income, education, age of first pregnancy, etc.).

### Home-building trips, women-entrepreneur preschool combo, recovery [00:56:35]

Trips let donors finance and build a home in Honduras. New pilot: chicken-business + preschool for first-time women entrepreneurs with kids 0-5. Future: bringing recovery work to Honduras (Fuller is 3 years sober formally).

### How to get involved; closing quote [01:01:12]

Best: visit on a trip (run year-round). Or follow on Instagram (@humanityandhope) and humanityandhopeunited.org. Closes with 'what the world needs is people who have come alive' — start with one small intentional act and get 1% better.
