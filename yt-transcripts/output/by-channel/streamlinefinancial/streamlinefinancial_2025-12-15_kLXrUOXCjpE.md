# Don't Retire If This Is You - 5 Warning Signs

**Channel:** Streamline Financial
**Published:** 2025-12-15
**URL:** https://youtube.com/watch?v=kLXrUOXCjpE
**Duration:** 11:14

## Summary

Dave Zoller lists five warning signs that someone shouldn't retire yet even if they're financially ready. Three are non-financial: your job currently delivers the three Cs (connection, contribution, creativity); retiring from the business rather than retiring in it; retiring from something rather than to something. Two are financial: not knowing your monthly spending within a reasonable range, and carrying credit-card or auto-loan debt into retirement.

## Key Takeaways

- Five warning signs to delay retirement even if you're financially ready.
- Sign 1: your job currently delivers the three Cs (connection, contribution, creativity).
- Sign 2: if you're a business owner, consider retiring in your business (keeping the energizing parts) rather than selling.
- Sign 3: retiring from something (toxic job) instead of to something concrete sets up retirement depression.
- Sign 4: not knowing what you spent last month on groceries/dining/gas means you can't predict retirement spending.
- Sign 5: carrying credit-card or auto-loan debt into retirement creates outsized psychological stress.
- Tools: Monarch Money or a spreadsheet for 6+ months of expense data; split into needs vs wants.

## Detailed Notes

### Warning sign 1: your job already provides the three Cs [00:00:36]

Three Cs: connection, contribution, creativity. If your work currently delivers them, retirement may leave you empty. Don't let the 'normal at 65' social pressure push you out — but if work also has high stress impacting health, consider modifying the role rather than walking away.

### Warning sign 2: retire IN your business, not from it [00:02:20]

Tom (late 50s, burned out from a successful company) initially wanted to sell everything. Instead he hired a GM, stepped back from day-to-day, stayed on as chairman, kept ownership/income, and focused on strategic clients, products, and mentoring.

### Warning sign 3: retire TO something, not FROM something [00:05:21]

Biggest red flag. Mark hated his job; retired to escape — when the honeymoon ended he had no purpose, structure, or progress. Test: 'I'm excited to retire because I want to ____.' Specifics matter.

### Warning sign 4: can't describe your monthly spending [00:06:40]

If you can't say what you spent last month on groceries, dining out, entertainment, gas — you can't predict retirement spending. Use Monarch Money or a spreadsheet to track at least 6 months. Split expenses into needs vs wants for peace of mind.

### Warning sign 5: debt into retirement [00:08:22]

Especially credit card and auto loans. A $500/month credit card payment over 20 years of retirement = $120K of nest egg consumed plus interest. Psychological stress amplifies — debt feels worse without an incoming paycheck. Consider working slightly longer to clear debt before retiring.

### Final test: from or to? [00:10:27]

If you can clearly articulate what you're excited to do in retirement — purpose, plans, passion — these warning signs likely don't apply. If retirement just means 'not working,' do more planning first.
