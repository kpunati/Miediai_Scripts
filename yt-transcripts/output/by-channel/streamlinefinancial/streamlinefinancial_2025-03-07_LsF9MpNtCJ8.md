# This Makes Retirement SO Much Better

## Summary

Dave Zoller introduces the 'retirement dress rehearsal' (credited to Cody Garrett of Measure Twice Financial): 6-12 months before retiring, direct your paycheck to brokerage and set a monthly transfer to checking equal to your planned retirement income. Tests the budget, eases the saver-to-spender switch, and provides error-correction time while employment safety-nets the simulation. Built around John and Mary, who 3 months in were only using half of what they could afford.

## Key Takeaways

- Most retirees flip a switch from saving to spending after 30 years of conditioning — that's psychologically hard.
- John and Mary case: 3 months in, only spending ~half of what they could afford.
- The retirement dress rehearsal (Cody Garrett, Measure Twice Financial): 6-12 months before retiring, route your paycheck to brokerage and a monthly transfer to checking equal to planned retirement income.
- Benefits: real-world budget testing, psychological adjustment, confidence building, smoother emotional transition.
- Error-correction opportunity: discover wrong assumptions while employment income is still flowing.
- Retirement is a psychological transition as much as a financial one.

## Detailed Notes

### Open: the saver-to-spender problem [00:00:00]

After 40 years of regular paychecks, retirees suddenly need to start withdrawing. The 30-year saving habit is hard to break.

### John and Mary: spending half of what they could [00:01:02]

Sample client couple: 3 months into retirement, only using ~half of what they could spend. Couldn't shake the feeling of depleting the nest egg — leading to unnecessarily restricted travel/dining.

### The retirement dress rehearsal mechanic [00:02:04]

Credited to Cody Garrett of Measure Twice Financial. 6-12 months before retirement: (1) direct your paycheck to brokerage, (2) set a monthly auto-transfer from brokerage to checking equal to your planned retirement income.

### Four benefits: testing, psychology, confidence, transition [00:03:06]

(1) Real-world budget test — discover quickly if your number is right. (2) Psychological adjustment time while still employed. (3) Confidence builds month by month. (4) Smoother emotional transition — no spike of worry at retirement day.

### Error correction while still earning [00:04:09]

Major benefit: if your budget assumptions are wrong, you have time to adjust your retirement date, savings rate, or withdrawal rate while still drawing employment income.
