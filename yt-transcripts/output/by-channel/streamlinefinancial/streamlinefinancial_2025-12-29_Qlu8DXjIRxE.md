# Most Retirees Don't Plan For This (And It Costs Them)

**Channel:** Streamline Financial
**Published:** 2025-12-29
**URL:** https://youtube.com/watch?v=Qlu8DXjIRxE
**Duration:** 4:46

## Summary

Dave Zoller introduces the 'You Inc.' frame: instead of retirement, treat the next chapter as 'rehirement' as CEO of the most important company you'll ever run — yourself. Cites Dr. Peter Attia on three healthspan components (physical, cognitive, emotional) and recommends maximizing healthspan across three departments: physical operations (strength, walks, balance), cognitive development (learning, social connection, sleep), and executive wellness (mission.

## Key Takeaways

- Lifespan vs healthspan: 25 years of life may include only ~7 years of vibrant living.
- Reframe retirement as 'rehirement' — CEO of You Inc., a company maximizing healthspan.
- Peter Attia's three healthspan components: physical, cognitive, emotional.
- Physical: strength training 2-3x/week, daily walks, balance practice (one-foot brushing).
- Cognitive: monthly new learning, social connections, prioritize sleep.
- Executive wellness: mission statement, relationship 'board of directors,' daily gratitude.
- 70-80 year olds hiking mountains and learning languages took control of their health 20 years earlier.

## Detailed Notes

### Healthspan vs lifespan [00:00:00]

Most retirement plans measure the years from retirement to death. The under-asked question is how many of those years will be healthy. Example: someone retired at 65, by 72 couldn't travel, play with grandkids, or drive — 15-20 years of life but only 7 of living.

### You Inc. — CEO of the most important company [00:01:03]

Peter Attia's three components: physical, cognitive, emotional. Emotional is hardest to measure but most important. Reframes retirement as 'rehirement' to a job whose mission is maximizing healthspan across these three departments.

### Physical operations department [00:01:41]

Strength training 2-3x/week (even body weight counts). Quarterly review: can you get up from a chair without using your hands? Daily walks starting at 10 minutes, scaling to 30. Balance practice: stand on one foot while brushing teeth.

### Cognitive development department [00:02:42]

Learn something new monthly (language apps, cooking classes, brain games). Schedule social connections like executive meetings — loneliness is toxic. Prioritize quality sleep — overnight brain maintenance.

### Executive wellness — the C-suite priority [00:02:42]

Most important per Attia. Find your company's new mission statement — what would you do for the next 20-25 years if you couldn't fail? Nurture relationships (board of directors). Daily gratitude — three small wins or three thanks per day. Scripture, prayer, or meditation.

### Closing: 70-80 year olds still thriving [00:03:46]

People in their 70s and 80s still hiking mountains, learning languages, with vibrant social connections — they took control 20 years earlier. They didn't plan to exist longer; they planned to thrive longer.
