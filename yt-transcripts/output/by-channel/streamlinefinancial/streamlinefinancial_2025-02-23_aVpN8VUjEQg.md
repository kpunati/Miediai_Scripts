# This Secret Money Habit Changes Everything

**Channel:** Streamline Financial
**Published:** 2025-02-23
**URL:** https://youtube.com/watch?v=aVpN8VUjEQg
**Duration:** 3:26

## Summary

Dave Zoller shares the one trait of the financially happiest retirees he's worked with: gratitude. Specifically, the practice of 'thank you money' (Ken Honda's Arigato money) — taking 3 seconds to mentally thank money both when it comes in (Social Security, distributions) AND when it goes out (paying bills, treating a friend to coffee). Argues many retirees thank God; others the universe; the practice shifts money from a stressor to a friend.

## Key Takeaways

- The trait shared by the happiest retirees Streamline has worked with is gratitude.
- Specific practice (Ken Honda's Arigato money / 'thank you money'): 3 seconds of gratitude when money arrives.
- Harder and more powerful: 3 seconds of gratitude when money LEAVES your pocket (paying bills, treating a friend).
- Many practitioners thank God; others thank the universe — the recipient of gratitude is personal.
- Practicing this shifts the felt relationship with money from stressor to friend passing through your life.
- No downside to trying — costs nothing, takes 3 seconds.

## Detailed Notes

### Setup: the trait and the practice [00:00:00]

After 16 years advising retirees, Dave sees one trait among the most fulfilled: gratitude. Notes this isn't correlated with most money — many wealthy people are unhappy. Promises a specific 3-second practice.

### Arigato money / thank you money [00:01:01]

Ken Honda's 'Arigato money' practice. When money arrives (Social Security deposit, retirement distribution), take 3 seconds to feel thanks. When money leaves (paying bills, treating a friend to coffee), do the same — the harder direction.

### Who is being thanked and the shift in relationship [00:02:04]

Many thank God ('he owns it all anyway'); others thank the universe. The practice shifts money from a stressful thing to a friend you're grateful for during its time in your life — and grateful for as it moves on.

### Closing: try it and comment [00:03:06]

Invitation to try it next time money comes in or goes out. Low-cost experiment with potentially big mental effect.
