# One BAD Decision Ruined His Retirement

**Channel:** Streamline Financial
**Published:** 2024-09-09
**URL:** https://youtube.com/watch?v=EDereyJCifE
**Duration:** 15:59

## Summary

Dave Zoller tells the story of two retirees who started March 2020 with similar plans: Jane and 'Kevin.' Both panicked during the 30%+ drop, but Jane focused on the seven-year safe-money bucket and held; Kevin sold to cash and never got back in. Jane then layered three winning moves — tax-loss harvesting, a Roth conversion at depressed values, and a system-driven rebalance from bonds into stocks. Kevin missed the recovery and his plan suffered.

## Key Takeaways

- In retirement, the same shock means different things to two retirees — process, not raw return, drives the outcome.
- Focus on the gain (safe-money buckets) not the gap (total portfolio drawdown).
- Having multiple years of withdrawals in conservative assets removes the need to sell equities at a low.
- Selling to cash in panic locks in losses and creates the bigger problem: when to re-enter.
- Market downturns enable strategic moves — tax-loss harvesting, Roth conversions at lower values, and systematic rebalancing.
- Wash-sale rule (30 days, substantially identical securities) is the key constraint when harvesting losses.
- Following a written rebalancing system removes emotion from the hardest decisions.

### Jane's three winning moves: TLH, Roth, system rebalance [00:11:29]

TLH = tax-loss harvest (sell at loss, buy similar — keep allocation, capture deduction). Note the 30-day wash-sale rule. Roth conversion at depressed values converts more shares per dollar of tax.

### Tax-loss harvesting in detail [00:12:32]
