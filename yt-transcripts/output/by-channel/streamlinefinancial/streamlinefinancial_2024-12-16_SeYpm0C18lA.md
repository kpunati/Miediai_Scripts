# DON'T Retire Yet - There's Another Way

**Channel:** Streamline Financial
**Published:** 2024-12-16
**URL:** https://youtube.com/watch?v=SeYpm0C18lA
**Duration:** 9:23

## Summary

Dave Zoller argues phased retirement is an under-used alternative to a full stop. Walks through financial benefits (delay withdrawals, keep contributing, 6-8%/yr Social Security boost from delayed filing) and non-financial benefits (purpose, structure, social contact). Three client examples: a 65-year-old extrovert who took a flexible Trader Joe's job, a man who started a small side business with a friend, and a 60s business owner who restructured to work 15 hours a week.

## Key Takeaways

- Phased retirement reduces the all-or-nothing cliff and tends to improve happiness compared to going cold turkey.
- Financial benefits: delay portfolio withdrawals, optionally keep contributing, gain 6-8%/yr on Social Security from delayed filing.
- Non-financial benefits: continued purpose, structure, physical activity, regular social contact.
- Cold-turkey retirement often produces a honeymoon period followed by a boredom or depression phase.
- Phased work doesn't have to be with the current employer (example: 65-year-old extrovert took a flexible Trader Joe's job).
- A side business in retirement can be a money-making endeavor with tax deductibility benefits.
- Tool: write a 'do not do' list to identify and offload draining activities (six business parts framework: marketing, sales, product, operations, finance, people management).

## Detailed Notes

### Intro: phased retirement as an alternative [00:00:00]

Dave Zoller introduces phased retirement as an under-used alternative to the all-or-nothing retirement decision. Previews three client examples and addresses concerns about employer pushback.

### What is phased retirement and why people consider it [00:01:02]

Phased retirement is a gradual transition reducing hours or responsibilities while keeping purpose, income, and flexibility. Many clients say work stress or pace is escalating and impacting other parts of life.

### How work stress impacts health and family [00:02:05]

Real example: a subscriber's wife told Dave the work stress was affecting his health, exercise time, and time with her. Outlines financial benefits: delay portfolio withdrawals, continue contributing, higher Social Security from each year of delay (6-8% per year).

### Cold turkey: honeymoon then boredom/depression [00:03:10]

Cold turkey retirement often produces a honeymoon period followed by a boredom or depression phase. Work supplies purpose, social contact, physical activity, and routine — all of which evaporate if not deliberately replaced.

### Client 1: 65-year-old extrovert at Trader Joe's [00:04:12]

A 65-year-old extrovert wanted phased retirement but her employer wouldn't allow it. She took a flexible Trader Joe's job that allowed working a few weeks then taking weeks off to travel — funded bucket-list trips and eased her transition.

### Client 2: cold turkey then side business with friend [00:05:14]

A client went cold turkey then started a small side business with a friend. Wrote out expectations, non-negotiables, and business plan in advance. Benefits included tax-deductible lifestyle expenses tied to the business.

### Client 3: business owner restructured to 15 hours/week [00:06:15]

60s business owner identified the people-management/coaching part of his business as most fascinating. Wrote down the six parts of the business (marketing, sales, product, operations, finance, people management), hired from within for the other parts, and went from 50 hours/week to 15 hours/week.

### True owner vs employee-owner [00:07:21]

Most business owners are really employees of their own business. Restructuring delegation made him a true owner — lower profit and take-home pay, but worth it for the stress reduction and happiness gain.

### Do-not-do list and closing [00:08:22]

Practical tool: a 'do not do' list of energy-draining tasks. Look at the last week or day, identify what drains you, and make room for what adds energy. Closes with a pointer to a related '9 things to stop in your 50s' video.
