# The Currency of Influence: Why Generosity Wins in Leadership with Brad Lomenick

## Summary

Interview with Brad Lomenick — strategic advisor to CEOs, leader of leaders, former Catalyst leader — on the Wealth Beyond Riches podcast. Topics: connector vs networker (long-game generosity vs transactional), three connector skills (curiosity, encouragement, pulling gold), Pebb Jackson's model of becoming a student of others, calling vs assignment (river vs the vessel on it), four quadrants for vocational discernment (wiring, passion, God's story,.

## Key Takeaways

- Connector vs networker: connectors play the long game and add value; networkers are transactional.
- Three connector skills: curiosity (good questions), encouragement (add value), pulling gold (calling out what's true).
- Generosity isn't keeping score — connectors don't track favors.
- Pebb Jackson became a student of what other people were experts in — preparation for any conversation.
- Calling = the river and banks (directional, mostly fixed); assignment = the vessel you're in (can change many times).
- Four quadrants of vocational discernment: wiring, passion, God's story, sustainability.
- Find your calling by looking at childhood patterns — what you gravitated toward before pressure or money.
- Modern talent dynamic: hire-and-keep top performers requires real responsibility, vulnerability, and a clear path.

## Detailed Notes

### Cold open and connector definition [00:00:00]

Brad opens: 'a lack of clarity is why we burn out, get depressed, are anxious.' Tim introduces Brad — connector to leaders nationally and internationally, behind many of the podcast's guests.

### Connector vs networker [00:01:50]

Connectors are generous, abundance-minded, long-game. Networkers are transactional, scarcity-minded. You know which is which when they walk in a room — networkers have a 'look at me' neon sign.

### The benefits of being a connector [00:06:57]

You build equity, currency, trust. People want to introduce you because trust is built. Generosity isn't transactional — Maxwell taught Brad that adding value period is the entire game.

### Practical posture shift [00:13:35]

Three skills: curiosity (good questions), encouragement (add value), pulling gold (calling out what's true). In a 45-minute call, Brad spent 40 minutes asking questions of an influential guest.

### Pebb Jackson — Brad's model [00:18:50]

Pebb raised more money in Christian nonprofit space than anyone Brad knew. His secret: became a student of what other people cared about — so any conversation could be a volley.

### Lost Valley as fertile soil [00:22:47]

Brad's first head-wrangler job — 23-year-old leading 45-year-old fortune-500 CEOs on rides. Learned to surprise them by knowing their industry, ask about their day, pull gold.

### Calling vs assignment [00:31:32]

Calling = the river and its banks (directional purpose). Assignment = the vessel you're in (changes many times). Today's 20-somethings will have 15-25 assignments over a career. Confusing calling with assignment causes crisis during transition.

### Four quadrants of vocational discernment [00:36:17]

(1) Wiring: intrinsic gifts. (2) Passion: emotional pull from experience or pain. (3) God's story: what's true for this season. (4) Sustainability: what creates income. Hitting 3-4 of 4 is the sweet spot.

### Finding the through line [00:35:10]

Through lines emerge by reverse engineering. Look at childhood for intrinsic wiring before parental pressure. Brad's example: managing recess games. Ask people what's true about you that you don't even know.

### Working yourself out of a job [00:42:45]

Great leaders keep moving toward the role only they can fill. Squatting on a role = stagnation. People who fear coasting won't coast. Watching is most concerning when someone is content to coast.

### Modern talent dynamics [00:51:15]

The talent fight: how to find, develop, retain. Younger workers expect more responsibility sooner and a clear path. Old-school 'be happy you have a job' doesn't work anymore.

### Coaching parallels and authentic leadership [00:55:30]

Sports as cultural microcosm — old-school coaches retiring because they don't understand the new rules. Authentic vulnerable leaders are followed more than 'I have it all figured out' leaders.

### Brad's loves — YouVersion, Acquired, walking [00:57:33]

Bobby Gruenewald's YouVersion Bible app approaching 1 billion users. Acquired podcast for deep tech/business stories. Walking habit for 10 years — even on Zoom calls (turn the video off and walk).
