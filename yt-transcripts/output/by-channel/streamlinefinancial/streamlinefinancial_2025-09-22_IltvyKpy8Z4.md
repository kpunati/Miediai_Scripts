# Sell These 5 Things BEFORE You Retire

**Channel:** Streamline Financial
**Published:** 2025-09-22
**URL:** https://youtube.com/watch?v=IltvyKpy8Z4
**Duration:** 11:53

## Summary

Dave Zoller lists five things to consider selling or letting go of in retirement: (1) the oversized empty-nest house — client freed $200K equity and cut $15K/yr expenses; (2) over-supporting adult children at expense of retirement security; (3) expensive toys (Tom's classic-car story — kept one of five, freed $180K and $9K/yr); (4) the second car; (5) the work identity itself. Closes with a three-question framework and a 90-day test to evaluate any potential sale.

## Key Takeaways

- Consider selling the oversized empty-nest house — one client freed $200K and cut $15K/yr.
- Stop over-supporting adult children at your own retirement's expense — kids have loans, retirement has none.
- Tom's classic-car lesson: kept his favorite, sold 4 others — freed $180K and $9K/yr.
- Couples can often sell the second car after the commute ends — test it with a 60-day trial.
- Let go of work identity in retirement — Dr. Michael moved from 'doctor' to woodworking and grandfather.
- Three-question framework: (1) does this serve my new life or old life? (2) what's it really costing me? (3) what becomes possible if I let it go?
- Use the 90-day test before any major sale or change.

## Detailed Notes

### Setting up the five things [00:00:00]

Most retirement advice focuses on buying — Dave focuses on strategic letting-go. Two types of viewers: 'am I ready?' and 'I know I'm ready, how do I maximize the go-go years?'

### Thing 1 — Oversized house [00:01:02]

Client raised 5 kids in their house — empty nest, but loved the location. Sold, used equity to buy a condo in the same area for summers, lived in warmer climate in winter. Freed $200K equity, cut $15K/yr in maintenance.

### Thing 2 — Over-supporting adult kids [00:02:05]

At 60, you have less than 1,000 weeks of healthy active time. Adult kids have decades. Clients delayed retirement 3 years to help a daughter buy a house — she could have gotten a mortgage. Kids have loans available — retirement doesn't.

### Thing 3 — Tom's classic cars [00:03:41]

Tom restored 5 cars over 20 years — $12K/yr in storage, insurance, maintenance for ~6 drives/year ($2K per drive). After a year of soul-searching, kept his favorite, sold 4 — freed $180K and $9K/yr. Lesson: sometimes keeping everything prevents enjoying anything.

### Thing 4 — The second car [00:05:59]

Without commuting, do you need two cars? One couple test-ran with one car for 2 months — easier than expected. Sold one, freed funds for travel.

### Thing 5 — Work identity [00:07:02]

Dr. Michael (surgeon, 30 years) felt invisible six months into retirement. Started volunteering at free clinic just to be 'Dr. Michael' again. Breakthrough: stop trying to recreate work identity, discover the new one (woodworking, mentoring younger doctors, becoming Grandpa Michael).

### Three-question framework [00:09:06]

Q1: Does this serve my new life or my old life? Q2: What's it really costing me (direct, opportunity, energy, space costs)? Q3: If I let this go, what becomes possible?

### 90-day test and closing [00:10:08]

Before selling, live as if it's already gone for 60-90 days. See if you miss it or feel relieved. Retirement isn't deprivation — it's optimization. Letting go of what no longer serves frees attention, money, energy for what matters.
