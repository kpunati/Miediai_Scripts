# RETIREMENT EXPERT: They'll Be Caught Off Guard When This Happens In Retirement - Ric Edelman

**Channel:** Streamline Financial
**Published:** 2024-08-27
**URL:** https://youtube.com/watch?v=AcPKO-znLH4
**Duration:** 31:45

## Summary

Dave Zoller interviews Ric Edelman (founder of Edelman Financial Engines, ~$250B AUM) on the future of retirement. Edelman argues longevity (likely 95-100 for those alive in 2030) makes the traditional 60s retirement and 60/40 portfolio obsolete. Recommends 70/30 or 75/25 well into the 80s, ~20% allocation to exponential technologies (AI, robotics, biotech, blockchain/crypto, including 1-3% in Bitcoin), and reinventing for the next 30-40-year chapter via new.

## Key Takeaways

- Longevity is reshaping retirement: many alive today will live to 95-100 — financial advice built on age-85 assumptions is obsolete.
- Non-financial unfulfillment, not money, is the most overlooked retirement failure mode — reinvent yourself for a 30-40-year next chapter.
- Ageism is the next workplace discrimination to overcome; over-50 entrepreneurship is now more common than under-20 entrepreneurship.
- Edelman recommends 70/30 or 75/25 equity/bond allocation maintained well into the 80s — not the traditional 60/40 glide path down.
- Allocate meaningfully (~20%) to exponential technologies: AI, robotics, 3D printing, nanotech, biotech, fintech, blockchain/digital assets.
- A small 1-3% Bitcoin allocation is reasonable; refusing to learn about it is an extreme position given mainstream adoption.
- Both developers (e.g., NVIDIA) and users (e.g., Pizza Hut's mobile/GPS ordering) of technology are investable themes.
- Philanthropy and contribution (steam education, Alzheimer's research) are highlighted as the most fulfilling use of accumulated wealth.

## Detailed Notes

### The big retirement problem: longevity, not money [00:00:00]

Edelman opens with the thesis that the overlooked retirement risk is longevity-driven dissatisfaction, not financial security. People with perfect plans can still be miserable in their 60s and 70s.

### Background on Edelman Financial Engines [00:01:03]

Founded one of the largest independent RIAs in the US, ~$250B AUM. NYT bestselling author. Frames the three interview topics: non-financial retirement, longevity preparation, investment ideas.

### Non-financial side of retirement: why money-only plans fail [00:02:06]

Many retirees have ample money but are miserable. Goal of retirement planning has shifted from financial security alone to something broader.

### Longevity revolution: from age 23 (1776) to 47 (1900) to mid-80s today [00:03:12]

Life expectancy has shifted dramatically. Edelman cites the Milken Institute, Stanford Center on Longevity, and MIT AgeLab.

### If alive in 2030, high odds of living to 100 [00:04:16]

Medical technology is eliminating leading causes of death (heart disease, cancer, diabetes, obesity). Cites the 105-year-old American living with her 95-year-old daughter.

### Retirement planning is built on age-85 assumptions — already obsolete [00:06:22]

Most advisers still plan to age 90 or 95; longevity research suggests that's inadequate. A 40-year retirement requires rethinking the financial plan.

### Why retirees are unfulfilled: identity tied to career [00:07:25]

After 40 years of career-defined identity, retirees reach the milestone and ask 'is this it?' The departure from career is now an opportunity to enter a next chapter.

### Reinvention and entrepreneurship over 50 [00:09:29]

Entrepreneurs are now more likely to be over 50 than under 20. People in their 60s have wisdom AND money — they reinvent themselves.

### Going back to college and athleticism in retirement [00:10:31]

Universities now have many over-50 students; people earn law degrees in their 60s and 70s. Senior Olympics features 90-something gymnasts and 100-year-old track athletes.

### Three Cs alignment: creativity, connection, contribution [00:11:34]

Dave Zoller maps Edelman's reinvention message onto the Streamline three-Cs framework.

### Ageism is the next workplace discrimination [00:12:36]

Hiring managers in their 20s-40s avoid hiring 60+ candidates. This pushes older Americans into entrepreneurship, often part-time lifestyle businesses.

### Edelman's three current businesses [00:13:37]

Crypto education company, daily podcast/media company, Alzheimer's research company. All run part-time leveraging staff — applying decades of operating experience.

### Plan for go-go years that last longer than expected [00:14:40]

Traditional go-go/slow-go/no-go framework needs extending — go-go years now stretch deep into the 70s. First-few-years spending may be higher.

### Philanthropy as retirement fulfillment [00:15:42]

The most fun the Edelmans have is philanthropic spending. Decades involved in Alzheimer's fight.

### Rowan University: Edelman Planetarium for STEAM education [00:16:46]

Funds K-12 student visits to a planetarium to get them onto a college campus and excited about science.

### Edelman Fossil Park museum at Rowan [00:17:50]

A 65-million-year-old dinosaur dig site where visitors find and keep fossils. Smithsonian named it a top-5 anticipated museum opening of the year.

### College scholarships and time-based philanthropy [00:18:52]

Funds dozens of full-ride scholarships at Rowan. Notes retirees spend 10+ hours/week watching TV — that time can be redirected to community impact.

### Recap: plan for longer healthy years and non-financial reinvention [00:18:52]

Transitions from non-financial to investment topics.

### Investment fear in 60s is misplaced — you still have 30+ years [00:19:55]

Dave asks how to think about retirement allocation given longer life. Edelman's view: if you have 30 years left, market dips matter as little as they did in your 20s and 30s.

### 60/40 is out of date — recommend 70/30 or 75/25 into the 80s [00:22:01]

Longevity demands more equity exposure for inflation protection and multi-generational wealth transfer.

### Exponential technologies: invest where the future lives [00:23:04]

We're in the 21st century — invest in AI, robotics, 3D printing, nanotech, biotech, fintech, agtech, and blockchain. Most investors' experience is from a 20th-century world that's over.

### ~20% exponential-tech allocation, 1-3% Bitcoin [00:25:10]

Recommends meaningful weighting (20%+) to exponential technologies. Argues the S&P 500's top concentration captures some of this; suggests QQQ as more tech-tilted alternative.

### Developers AND users of technology are both investable [00:26:14]

Examples: NVIDIA as developer; Pizza Hut as user (mobile app + GPS-timed pizza delivery). Want exposure to companies doing one or both.

### Bitcoin: 15 years old, mainstream, 500M owners worldwide [00:28:18]

Bitcoin is no longer new. Pension funds and university endowments now invest. A 1-3% allocation can't materially hurt but offers asymmetric upside.

### CBDA certification for advisers and investors [00:29:23]

Edelman's Digital Assets Council of Financial Professionals offers the CBDA designation — five tracks including a separate investor track.

### Closing [00:31:27]

Dave thanks Edelman for sharing futurist thinking on retirement.
