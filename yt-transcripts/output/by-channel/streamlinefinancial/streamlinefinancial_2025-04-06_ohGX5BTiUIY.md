# This Hidden Retirement Trap Ruins Your Best Years

**Channel:** Streamline Financial
**Published:** 2025-04-06
**URL:** https://youtube.com/watch?v=ohGX5BTiUIY
**Duration:** 5:14

## Summary

Dave Zoller addresses the 'retirement trap' — studies show 20-40% of retirees experience depression after leaving work. Introduces a 'retirement purpose portfolio' diversified across health, relationships, growth, spiritual, contribution, and leisure — parallel to a diversified financial portfolio. Notes the hedonic-treadmill problem and that meaningful part-time work without financial stress is a valid path.

## Key Takeaways

- 20-40% of retirees experience depression — financial freedom alone doesn't fix it.
- Build a 'retirement purpose portfolio' diversified across health, relationships, growth, spiritual, contribution, leisure.
- Many retirees try to escape work without defining what they're retiring TO.
- The hedonic treadmill: if you don't define your income number, 'one more year' creep continues indefinitely.
- Find what fulfills you — experiences and relationships > more wealth past a point.
- Retirement doesn't have to mean never working again — meaningful part-time work without financial stress is a valid path.
- The 'Retirement Mastery' worksheet (referenced from earlier video) helps design the non-financial side.

## Detailed Notes

### The retirement trap: 20-40% depression [00:00:00]

Studies show 20-40% of retirees experience depression. Money alone doesn't create happiness. Introduces the 'retirement purpose portfolio' concept.

### The honeymoon → boredom → emptiness pattern [00:01:01]

Honeymoon phase fades into mornings with nothing meaningful to do; days blend; nagging emptiness. Working wasn't the issue — lack of purpose was. Most retirees define what they're escaping FROM, not retiring TO.

### The Retirement Purpose Portfolio [00:01:47]

Diversify purpose like a portfolio: health (exercise, nutrition, mental well-being), relationships (family, friends, community), growth (learning, challenge, curiosity), spiritual (closeness to God), contribution (volunteering, mentoring), leisure (hobbies, travel).

### Hedonic treadmill: define the income number or it grows [00:02:48]

Past a certain point, more money doesn't equal more happiness, but if you don't define your needed income number, it keeps rising. 'One more year' creep is a hedonic-treadmill problem.

### Work doesn't have to end completely [00:03:52]

Retirees report it's OK to shift to enjoyable work — even unpaid or part-time. Money as the only motivator is the trap.
