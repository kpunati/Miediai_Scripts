# Psychology Expert: 5 Simple Steps To Retire Happy

**Channel:** Streamline Financial
**Published:** 2024-12-01
**URL:** https://youtube.com/watch?v=kz3U6TaAzfg
**Duration:** 29:58

## Summary

Dave Zoller interviews Dr. Daniel Crosby (chief behavioral officer, author of 'The Soul of Wealth') on five psychological keys to retiring happy. Covers Martin Seligman's PERMA model (Positive emotion, Engagement, Relationships, Meaning, Achievement) and how retirement removes E/R/M/A — even when P is funded; the 'premeditation of evils' (Stoic premortem) for risk-proofing a plan; pairing spending with relationships (the car-club example) for enduring happiness;.

## Key Takeaways

- PERMA model: retirement breaks E (engagement), R (relationships), M (meaning), and A (achievement) — be intentional or money alone won't bring happiness.
- Especially for men, work provides social connection by default — retirement requires actively rebuilding it.
- Premortem / premeditation of evils: imagine your plan failed 10 years out — what's the cause? Then take that risk off the table.
- Bear markets average ~19 months — keep ~2 years of cash to weather them without selling.
- Hedonic adaptation: we habituate to abundance just as we habituate to pain — buying things rarely sustains happiness.
- Spending pairs with happiness best when tied to relationships (e.g., car-club membership > just owning the car).
- Buy novelty (travel) over repeat indulgences — novel experiences resist habituation longer.
- Outsource dreaded tasks to buy back time and freedom — Crosby outsources lawn care without apology.

## Detailed Notes

### Most important financial decision isn't financial [00:01:04]

Crosby's framing: every life decision touches money (e.g., who you marry). Working with an advisor correlates with better emergency preparedness, lower divorce rates, higher happiness.

### PERMA: the framework for retirement wholeness [00:03:10]

Seligman's PERMA: Positive emotion, Engagement (flow), Relationships, Meaning, Achievement. Money funds P; work provides the others by default — retirement must intentionally rebuild them.

### Engagement: meaningful work and flow [00:04:15]

Engagement = flow state. Doesn't have to be paid work. Many retirees forget to plan for engagement.

### Relationships: especially critical for men [00:05:17]

Men have weaker social networks outside of work — relationships are the most fragile leg of PERMA in retirement.

### Meaning: working for something bigger than self [00:06:20]

Religion, spirituality, volunteerism, philanthropy. Work provides corporate mission by default; retirement must replace that.

### Achievement: progress and learning [00:06:20]

Want to be better today than yesterday. Work structures this; retirement needs intentional goal-setting (learn an instrument, write a book, fitness goals).

### Strengths broadly: take a step back from specialization [00:09:27]

Crosby's example: 'taking complex topics and simplifying them' is the broader skill, applicable to financial-literacy teaching, widow education, etc.

### Mental models for filtering news noise [00:11:37]

Do I have reason to trust this source's accuracy? What's their motivation? Will I care in 5 years? Does this matter for what I care about?

### Crosby's TV-producer story: 'Don't be a nerd' [00:12:40]

Recounted being on cable financial news with a producer counting him down 'don't be a nerd' — illustrating how the news incentivizes spice over accuracy.

### Premeditation of evils / premortem [00:19:00]

Stoic and behavioral psychology technique. Imagine 10 years from now your plan failed — what was the cause? Then take it off the table now (insurance, savings, time with kids).

### Memento mori bracelet [00:19:00]

Crosby wears a 'memento mori' bracelet from his daughter — Roman soldiers had slaves whisper 'remember you're going to die' during triumphs. Used to prompt clarity.

### Hyundai's GFC promise pays off [00:21:04]

Hyundai during the 2008 GFC: buy a car, lose your job, return for full refund. Resulted in their best quarter ever — taking the worst case off the table.

### Bear market ~19 months; keep two years of cash [00:22:07]

Crosby's bucket equivalent: ~2 years of cash. When the bear arrives, you've prepared — and can take appropriate risk with the rest.

### Hedonic adaptation: we habituate to abundance [00:24:15]

We adjust to a Porsche the same way we adjust to a beautiful home — it becomes 'where I throw my dirty socks.' Buying alone rarely sustains happiness.

### Pair spending with relationships (car-club example) [00:25:19]

Joining a Porsche Club gives the car enduring meaning via relationships — pop the hood, share coffee, plan trips. Pair consumption with PERMA's R.

### Buy novelty over repeat hedonism [00:27:26]

Travel buys happiness because novelty resists habituation. Could afford a lot of novelty with the cost of a fancy car.

### Buy your time back; outsource dread [00:28:29]

Charitable giving, novelty, time with people you love, getting out of work you hate. Crosby outsources lawn care without apology.

### Close: thanks for sharing The Soul of Wealth [00:29:32]

Dave recommends the book — money + meaning is central to retirement.
