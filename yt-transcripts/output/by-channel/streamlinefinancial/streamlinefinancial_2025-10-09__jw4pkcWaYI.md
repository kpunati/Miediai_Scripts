# Leading from the Inside Out: All Things Together with Heath Hardesty

## Summary

Interview with Heath Hardesty — pastor, author, former plumber's apprentice — on the Wealth Beyond Riches podcast. Discusses his book All Things Together (out October 14) on apprenticeship as a metaphor for human formation. Topics: imagination as the organ of integration, the disenchanted/dissociated/discontextualized/dislocated modern condition, the practice of joyful generosity, the Proverbs 22:9 bountiful-eye principle, Sabbath rhythms, and why generosity shapes.

## Key Takeaways

- Heath's path: plumber's apprentice, then existential crisis at 27 led to Christian faith, then pastor.
- Apprenticeship paradigm = union → abiding → obeying → becoming like the master.
- Six disintegrations of modernity: disenchanted, dissociated, discontextualized, dislocated, asynchronous, disembodied.
- Imagination is the 'organ of integration' (CS Lewis) — not just fiction-generation but how reality is made coherent.
- Joyful generosity = living in accordance with the bountiful, abundant heart of reality.
- Tithing isn't a ceiling — Old Testament practices ran ~23%, and 'grace giving' should be felt.
- Giving shapes the giver (echocardiogram vs exercise metaphor) — it's not just diagnostic, it's formative.
- Streamline practices Lectio Divina on Friday afternoons — 30 minutes of unhurried scripture reading.

## Detailed Notes

### Cold open and intro to Heath [00:00:00]

Heath quotes Proverbs-style: 'to live generously is to live in accordance with the deepest heart of reality.' Tim introduces Heath — pastor, author, plumber. Book All Things Together releases October 14.

### Heath's journey [00:02:29]

Grew up blue-collar, father a master plumber. Studied literature and religious studies at CU Boulder. Played music, worked at a bookstore. Became plumber's apprentice for the paycheck. Existential crisis at 27, became a believer in April 2007.

### From apprentice to pastor [00:04:35]

Mentored by Pastor Tom Hov. Connected to Chuck Colson's Washington DC theology program. Moved to Silicon Valley to plant a church for 20s/30s. Eventually became lead pastor of integrated community.

### Apprenticeship as paradigm [00:11:41]

Union → abiding → obeying → imaging the master. Heath uses 'apprentice' instead of 'disciple' to keep faith embodied. Apprenticeship isn't invented — it's how humans are designed to be formed.

### Wonder and reality [00:14:38]

World loaded with treasures; childlike wonder erodes through pain, suffering, sin. Christian faith brightens the brights and darkens the darks — sees reality as designed for goodness, beauty, truth (the transcendentals).

### Six disintegrations [00:17:45]

Modern condition: disenchanted (broken relationship with creator), dissociated (uncomfortable in own skin), discontextualized (digital scroll-feed cross-connection), dislocated (more aware of distant news than local), asynchronous, disembodied. Windshield-with-cracks metaphor.

### Story and imagination [00:22:45]

We are story-shaped beings. CS Lewis: reason = organ of meaning; imagination = organ of integration. Fiction serves reality — Narnia, Lord of the Rings, Moby Dick help us understand humanity.

### Practices of apprenticeship [00:28:55]

Scripture meditation, unceasing prayer, life together (relational), unhurried presence, joyful generosity. Tim shares Streamline's Lectio Divina rhythm — 30 minutes of dim-lit, ambient-music, meditative scripture reading.

### Joyful generosity [00:33:13]

Heath's mentor Pastor Tom modeled glad-hearted generosity. Generosity is living in accordance with reality — the creator God is a generous, joyful being. Joy is centrifugal.

### Proverbs 22:9 and the bountiful eye [00:36:00]

Hebrew 'tov' = good/beautiful. The bountiful eye sees abundance and shares. Covey's abundance vs scarcity mindset — Jesus beat Covey by 2,000 years (birds and lilies).

### Tithing, costliness, and joy [00:44:55]

Tithe 10% is low-hanging fruit — Old Testament ran ~23%. CS Lewis: charity should have some sting. Heath and family give to local church, kingdom, Compassion International (Guatemala center).

### Money shapes the heart [00:48:14]

Jesus' 'where your treasure is, there your heart will be also' — two sides. Money is diagnostic AND formative. Greed/generosity isn't an echocardiogram, it's exercise — actively shaping you.

### River vs stagnant pond [00:40:54]

Tim's metaphor: a thriving river vs a stagnant side eddy. Generous people are in the flow; Ebenezer Scrooge bound to the dead pool until his Christmas conversion.

### What Heath is loving — Ruby [00:52:59]

His youngest daughter Ruby (born late last year). Each family member loves her; childlike wonder of a baby eating a blueberry is its own theology.
