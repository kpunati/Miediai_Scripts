# Retirement BEFORE 60 - What They Don't Talk About (Real Example)

**Channel:** Streamline Financial
**Published:** 2025-03-02
**URL:** https://youtube.com/watch?v=vlU2r3jpRes
**Duration:** 7:23

## Summary

Dave Zoller walks through client John, mid-50s tech designer with two teens, burned out after a 25-year startup-to-corp arc. Wanted to retire but it didn't feel right. After a 30-minute call, reframed retirement as a 'sabbatical' (his wife's word) focused on what's 'fascinating and motivating' (AI). Recommended modeling 40 years as if no future paycheck — freedom to pick work without economics as driver. Introduces the 'mini retirements' concept.

## Key Takeaways

- Under 60, retirement often looks like a sabbatical or a 'mini retirement' — not a full stop.
- The two key words for the next chapter: 'fascinating' and 'motivating' — write down anything that hits both.
- Modeling the plan as if there's NO future income lets you choose work without economic pressure.
- Once independent of the paycheck, you can choose by what you love, what you're good at, what the world needs — the four-circle Ikigai-style filter.
- What-if scenarios: model returning to part-time AI work for 5-10 years and see what happens to the plan.
- Mini retirements pattern: education -> 15-20 years work -> mini retirement -> re-educate in a new field -> repeat.

## Detailed Notes

### Client John: burnt out tech designer at 50s [00:00:00]

John, ~mid-50s, two teens, 25 years in tech, started in a startup that grew into a massive corp. Promoted into management — now spends 80% of time managing people/systems and only 10-20% on the design work he loves.

### John's wife: it's a sabbatical, not a retirement [00:02:05]

John told his wife he wanted to retire; she said with a smile this wouldn't be retirement — it would be a sabbatical because he'd want to keep doing something. He realized she was right.

### Reframe on fascinating + motivating; AI as next chapter [00:03:09]

Conversation arrived at two words: fascinating and motivating. For John, AI companies and the pace of change fit both. Side note: keep a running list of what fascinates you for your next-chapter prompts.

### Model the plan as if no more paychecks [00:04:12]

Dave's recommendation: model the next 40 years as if John never has another paycheck. If the plan works, he can be picky about what's next. Removes the economics-as-driver problem of career choices.

### The four-circle decision filter [00:04:12]

If economics is no longer the driver, choose by what you love + what you're good at + what the world needs. The fourth (economics) becomes secondary.

### Model what-if: 5-10 more years at lower pay [00:05:15]

Run a what-if where John returns to work in 6 months (or whenever) at the AI work — for another 5-10 years. The goal: work because he wants to, not because he has to.

### Mini retirements as the future pattern [00:06:17]

Predicts the future pattern: educate → work 15-20 years → mini retirement to re-educate in a new field → return to work → repeat. May not happen for everyone, but resonated with John.
