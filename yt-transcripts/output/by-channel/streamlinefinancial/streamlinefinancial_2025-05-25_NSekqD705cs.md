# The 3 Types of Retirees: Which One Are You?

**Channel:** Streamline Financial
**Published:** 2025-05-25
**URL:** https://youtube.com/watch?v=NSekqD705cs
**Duration:** 4:43

## Summary

Dave Zoller presents three retirement-purpose personalities: the Discoverer (purpose already exists, needs uncovering — start with prayer, life story, talk to old friends), the Creator (purpose is a blank canvas — write a vision statement, list 5 new experiences, do 30-day experiments), and the Pragmatist (no grand purpose needed — focus on sustainable daily routines and ordinary joy). Argues the mistake isn't choosing the wrong path — it's never choosing one and getting paralyzed.

## Key Takeaways

- Three retirement-purpose personalities: Discoverer, Creator, Pragmatist.
- Discoverer: purpose already exists — uncover via prayer/meditation, life-story review, old friends' input.
- Creator: purpose is a blank canvas — write a vision statement, list 5 new experiences for year one, design 30-day experiments.
- Pragmatist: no grand purpose needed — build sustainable daily routines for health, relationships, finances.
- The real tragedy isn't choosing the wrong path — it's never choosing one and staying paralyzed.
- You can be a blend of all three paths — pick the one that resonates most and start.
- Retirement is about having enough meaning, not just enough money.

## Detailed Notes

### Three retirement-purpose paths [00:00:00]

After 16 years advising retirees, three personalities: Discoverer (purpose already exists, uncover it), Creator (blank canvas, design it), Pragmatist (no grand purpose, focus on contentment).

### The hidden trait of the happiest retirees [00:01:02]

After working with hundreds of retirees, the common trait isn't financial — it's consciously choosing one of the three paths instead of drifting and hoping purpose finds them.

### Discoverer action plan: prayer, life story, old friends, volunteering [00:02:05]

If you're a Discoverer: begin with prayer/meditation if you believe in God. Revisit your life story for recurring themes. What brought you joy in your 30s/40s before career pressures? Talk to long-time friends about gifts you may not see in yourself. Explore volunteer alignment with deep values.

### Creator action plan: vision statement, 5 experiences, 30-day experiments [00:02:05]

If you're a Creator: write a vision statement for your ideal day. List 5 new experiences for year one (or first six months). Join groups stretching you beyond your career identity. Design small experiments — try a role for 30 days, then re-evaluate.

### Pragmatist action plan: routines and ordinary joy [00:02:05]

If you're a Pragmatist: build sustainable routines for health, relationships, finances. Embrace freedom from needing a grand purpose. Find joy in ordinary moments without the pressure of meaning-making.

### The real mistake: never choosing a path [00:03:08]

Many spend years paralyzed waiting for clarity. The real tragedy isn't picking the wrong philosophy — it's never picking one. You can be a blend; just start walking.

### Closing: meaning > money [00:03:43]

Retirement isn't about enough money — it's about enough meaning. The journey starts with knowing which path calls to you most.
