# 9 Things to STOP DOING After 55 To Enjoy Life More

**Channel:** Streamline Financial
**Published:** 2024-06-23
**URL:** https://youtube.com/watch?v=Zhm-2Ju60Mg
**Duration:** 12:17

## Summary

Dave Zoller lists nine things the happiest retirees they've worked with STOPPED doing after 55 to increase retirement satisfaction. The list: stop taking time for granted (life-in-weeks visualization), stop saving unnecessarily, stop compromising health, stop worrying so much, stop spending time with draining people, stop spending on things not aligned with values, stop over-supporting adult children, stop pretending to be someone you're not, stop living with regret.

## Key Takeaways

- The happiest retirees got happier by what they STOPPED doing, not what they added.
- Stop taking time for granted — Bill Perkins' 'life in weeks' visualization makes the limited time vivid.
- Stop saving unnecessarily — past a certain point, adding another year of work feels like security but rarely improves outcomes.
- Stop compromising health for work or income — sleep, nutrition, and exercise compound over decades.
- Stop worrying so much — most worries never happen; control what you can (allocation, spending, news intake).
- Stop spending time with draining people — be honest, set boundaries, distance if needed (harder with family).
- Stop spending on things misaligned with your values — invest in experiences and relationships.
- Stop over-supporting adult children at the expense of your retirement — they have more time to recover.
- Stop pretending to be someone you're not — Bronnie Ware's #1 deathbed regret.
- Stop living with regret — peak life satisfaction data shows 60s-70s as among the happiest decades.

## Detailed Notes

### Stopped > started [00:00:00]

The happiest retirees Streamline has helped over 15 years became happier from what they STOPPED doing, not what they added. Lists nine such practices.

### Stop taking time for granted [00:01:03]

Bill Perkins' wealth-vs-health visualization: wealth grows with age but health declines. 'Life in weeks' visualization (each week = one box) makes limited time tangible. Use weeks meaningfully.

### Stop saving unnecessarily [00:02:05]

Past sufficiency, the temptation is 'just one more year' for security. But the amount never feels enough. Confirm the plan works (98% vs 99% isn't worth another year of life). Enjoy fruits of labor.

### Stop compromising health [00:03:11]

Real client: spouse pointed out the job was hurting husband — late nights, no exercise, impacted sleep. Health stops being negotiable past 55. Invest in sleep, nutrition, exercise.

### Stop worrying so much [00:04:15]

Anxiety = worry over imagined future danger. Mark Twain: 'I've had a lot of worries in my life, most of which never happened.' Can't control markets/inflation/elections — CAN control allocation, expenses, news intake. Lighter news consumption correlates with happiness.

### Stop spending time with draining people [00:05:20]

Negative people sap energy from positive ones. Solutions: honest conversation about how their topics impact you, distance if needed (hardest with family). View yourself as the light driving out darkness.

### Stop spending on misaligned things [00:06:25]

Do the values exercise (linked separately). Audit recent spending — invest in experiences and relationships (lasting joy) rather than material things (limited joy).

### Stop over-supporting adult children [00:07:28]

Often creates dependence. Your retirement window is shorter than theirs. Make sure your plan is solid before extending financial support — they have more time and means to recover.

### Stop pretending to be someone you're not [00:08:31]

Bronnie Ware's #1 deathbed regret: 'I wish I had the courage to be true to myself.' Be authentic. Quote: '20, you care what everyone thinks; 40, you stop caring; 60, you realize no one was thinking about you anyway.'

### Stop living with regret [00:09:34]

Let go of past regrets. Live in present and future.

### Peak satisfaction data [00:11:41]

Life-satisfaction curves show 60s-70s as among the highest contentment periods. Forgive past mistakes; make the most of the time you have.
