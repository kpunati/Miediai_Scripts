# What Retirement Net Worth Puts You In The Top 1%

**Channel:** Streamline Financial
**Published:** 2025-10-13
**URL:** https://youtube.com/watch?v=ZV2eRmA5DhA
**Duration:** 9:04

## Summary

Dave Zoller breaks down retirement net-worth percentiles using Survey of Consumer Finances data: median household income for 65-69 ~$50K/yr; top 25% net worth = $395K to $1.16M (include home equity); top 10% ~$2.9M; top 1% ~$21.7M. But 86% of seniors with $50-100K total savings report being comfortable (Federal Reserve data).

## Key Takeaways

- Median 65-69 retirement income is just over $50K/yr (Social Security + pensions + withdrawals).
- Top 25% net worth = $395K to $1.16M (includes home equity); top 10% ~$2.9M; top 1% ~$21.7M.
- Only ~3.5% of Americans reach $1M+ in net worth — yet most surveys say people need $1.2M to feel comfortable.
- 86% of seniors with $50K-$100K total savings report being satisfied — happiness comes from the gap between needs and wants, not absolute wealth.
- Robert vs Linda: $150K vs $60K retirement income — Linda was happier because she had 'enough thinking' instead of 'more thinking.'
- Three mindset shifts: define what enough means for you, design around experiences (not rankings), let go of comparison.
- Four practical steps: track expenses for 3 months, build complete income picture, use proper planning software, define enough personally.
