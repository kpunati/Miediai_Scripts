# 7 Things To Do Before 2024 Ends

**Channel:** Streamline Financial
**Published:** 2024-11-24
**URL:** https://youtube.com/watch?v=RAt0kMKiVwk
**Duration:** 10:33

## Summary

Dave Zoller covers seven year-end retirement actions for 2024: (1) review Medicare/ACA open enrollment (Oct 15 to Dec 7); (2) tax-gain harvesting (0% LTCG bracket for MFJ ≤ ~$94K) and tax-loss harvesting (offsets gains; mind wash-sale rule); (3) check mutual-fund year-end capital-gain distributions before they hit your 1099; (4) charitable giving via Donor-Advised Funds for current-year deduction; (5) Qualified Charitable Distributions for those 70+ to satisfy RMDs.

## Key Takeaways

- Review Medicare/ACA coverage during the Oct 15–Dec 7 open enrollment window.
- If MFJ taxable income is ≤ $94K, harvest long-term gains at 0% federal rate and reset cost basis.
- Tax-loss harvesting can offset gains — mind the 30-day wash-sale rule.
- Check fund providers' year-end capital gain distribution announcements before they hit your 1099.
- Donor-Advised Funds: deduct now, distribute later — useful for high-income or business-sale years.
- Qualified Charitable Distributions (QCDs) from IRAs at 70.5+ satisfy RMDs tax-free.
- Confirm you're getting the full 401(k) employer match — even small gaps compound to $10K+ over years.
- Consider maxing out 401(k), IRA, Roth IRA, and HSA — IRA/HSA deadline is tax-filing date, not Dec 31.

## Detailed Notes

### Action 1: Medicare/ACA open enrollment (Oct 15–Dec 7) [00:00:00]

Annual window to review coverage and budget fit. If over 65, review with an agent.

### Tax gain harvesting: 0% LTCG bracket [00:01:02]

Example: bought investment for $50K, now worth $100K — $50K gain. If MFJ taxable income ≤ ~$94K, harvest at 0% federal. Sell, optionally buy back to reset cost basis higher.

### Tax loss harvesting and wash-sale rule [00:03:09]

Sell at a loss to offset gains — buy something similar but not 'substantially identical' within 30 days. Example: $20K gain offset by $25K loss = ~$8K tax saved.

### Mutual fund year-end distributions catch people off guard [00:05:13]

Mutual funds and index funds often pass through capital gains and dividends at year-end. Check fund-provider websites or call before tax season to avoid surprises.

### Charitable giving via Donor-Advised Fund [00:06:16]

Donate cash or stock to a DAF this year, deduct in full this year, distribute to charities over future years. Useful for high-income or business-sale years.

### QCDs for those 70.5+ to satisfy RMDs [00:07:20]

Send IRA money directly to a 501(c)(3). Satisfies RMD requirement; never hits your taxable income. Bypasses the RMD tax.

### 401(k) full employer match — John's $10K mistake [00:08:22]

John contributed only 3% when his employer matched 50% on first 6%. Missed $1,500/year for 5 years — ~$10,000 with growth. Contact HR to confirm match terms.

### Maxing other retirement accounts [00:09:24]

Max 401(k) by Dec 31 if affordable. Traditional/Roth IRA and HSA contributions can be made up to tax-filing date — extra months of flexibility.
