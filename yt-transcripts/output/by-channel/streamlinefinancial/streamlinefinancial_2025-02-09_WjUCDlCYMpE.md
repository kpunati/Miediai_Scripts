# Can Double-Digit Returns Happen Again in 2025?

**Channel:** Streamline Financial
**Published:** 2025-02-09
**URL:** https://youtube.com/watch?v=WjUCDlCYMpE
**Duration:** 7:40

## Summary

Dave Zoller addresses a subscriber question on whether 2025 could deliver a third straight year of double-digit S&P 500 returns (2023: ~26%, 2024: ~23%). Historical data: seven instances of three-or-more consecutive double-digit-return years since 1950, averaging every ~9.5 years with a 30-year gap from 1965-1995. Recommends focusing on what you can control: all-weather portfolio, conservative-bucket allocation, regular rebalancing.

## Key Takeaways

- Three-or-more consecutive double-digit S&P 500 years have happened seven times since 1950, averaging every ~9.5 years.
- There was a 30-year gap (1965-1995) with no such streak — uncommon doesn't mean impossible.
- Markets follow cycles of fear/confidence — not calendar years. Don't try to predict cycles; build an all-weather portfolio.
- Four economic seasons: high/low inflation × high/low growth — stress-test the portfolio across them.
- Conservative bucket protects against forced selling during prolonged bear markets.
- Rebalancing matters in retirement: drift after a strong run shifts your risk allocation away from plan.
- Carl Richards' Behavior Gap distillation: 'greed buy, fear sell, repeat until broke.'

## Detailed Notes

### Setup: subscriber question and 2023/2024 returns [00:00:00]

Subscriber asks if 2025 makes three years of double-digit S&P 500 returns (2023 ~26%, 2024 ~23%). Dave promises historical context plus three actions.

### Markets follow cycles, not calendar years [00:01:03]

Markets cycle on fear/confidence/Euphoria/anxiety — not Jan 1 to Dec 31. Cites the cycle-of-emotions chart and Carl Richards' Behavior Gap: greed-buy, fear-sell, repeat.

### Seven historical instances since 1950 [00:02:08]

Three-or-more consecutive double-digit S&P 500 years: 1950-52, 1963-65, 1995-99 (five overlapping in the late '90s), 2012-14, 2019-21. Range from +12.4% to +37%. Average every ~9.5 years.

### What to do: focus on timeline and goals [00:03:12]

Three ideas: (1) focus on your investment timeline not calendar years, (2) build strategy around your goals, (3) patience is an ally.

### Action 1: all-weather portfolio across the four economic seasons [00:04:15]

Four economic seasons (high/low inflation × high/low growth). Run portfolios through each — 2022's higher-than-expected inflation surprised many 60/40 portfolios.

### Action 2: review conservative-bucket allocation [00:05:19]

Total up conservative/cash/short-term bond holdings. Short-term money lets you avoid selling during downturns in retirement.

### Action 3: rebalancing matters [00:06:22]

Time in market beats timing the market — but only with disciplined rebalancing. If you started 60/40 and drifted to 75% stocks, you've taken risk you didn't intend. Streamline runs a rules-based rebalancing system.

### Closing: control what matters [00:06:22]

Can't control markets, inflation, news, elections. Can control allocation, taxes, expenses, news intake. Retirement is more than investments — peace of mind matters.
