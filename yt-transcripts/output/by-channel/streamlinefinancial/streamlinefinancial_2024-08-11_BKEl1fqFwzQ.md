# 2025 Social Security Increase

**Channel:** Streamline Financial
**Published:** 2024-08-11
**URL:** https://youtube.com/watch?v=BKEl1fqFwzQ
**Duration:** 10:12

## Summary

Dave Zoller delivers the 2025 Social Security COLA estimate (~2.6%, down from 3.2% in 2024 and 8.7% in 2023) and walks through three core retirement-income risks: longevity (running out of money), inflation, and exposure to bad markets. Notes Social Security helps with all three; pensions and certain annuities address one or two. Recap of the 1982 Social Security fix as evidence Congress will likely paper over the 2033 trust-fund shortfall.

## Key Takeaways

- 2025 Social Security COLA estimate: ~2.6% (down from 3.2% in 2024, 8.7% in 2023).
- Three retirement-income risks: longevity, inflation, exposure to bad markets — Social Security helps with all three.
- Social Security solvency: the 1982 fix raised full retirement age from 65 to 67 right before insolvency; 2033 likely follows a similar pattern.
- Pensions help with longevity but not always with inflation; annuities address some risks but carry costs/hidden risks.
- Inflation fighting: hold asset classes that historically outpace inflation (growth bucket in the three-bucket strategy).
- Four-economic-seasons coverage: build a portfolio that survives any combination of inflation/growth changes.
- March 2020 example: conservative-bucket positivity (~3% YTD) eased a recently-retired client's anxiety during a 30% market drop.

## Detailed Notes

### 2025 COLA estimate [00:00:00]

Initial 2025 estimate was under 2%; latest revision puts it near 2.6%. Down from 3.2% (2024) and 8.7% (2023). Final number released at year-end.

### Three retirement-income risks [00:01:05]

Risk 1: longevity / running out of money. Risk 2: inflation. Risk 3: bad markets and economic uncertainty. Walk through how Social Security and other sources address each.

### Risk 1: longevity and Social Security solvency [00:01:44]

Social Security lasts as long as you do — helps with longevity. Addresses the 'it'll be insolvent' worry by recounting how 1982 ended with a similar trust-fund cliff.

### The 1982 fix as a template for 2033 [00:02:45]

In 1982, Congress was months from insolvency; they raised full retirement age from 65 to 67, taxed Social Security benefits for some, and added government workers. Dave predicts a similar last-minute fix in 2033 affecting younger workers.

### Annuities caveat [00:03:48]

Annuities can address longevity but carry costs and hidden risks. Streamline recently helped a subscriber avoid an unsuitable annuity sale — always get second opinions.

### Risk 2: inflation [00:04:51]

Social Security's COLA partially addresses inflation, though many argue the official adjustment lags real cost-of-living. Most annuities lack COLAs (can be added at cost); pensions sometimes do.

### Concrete dollar example [00:05:40]

Based on Dec 2023 average benefit, a 2.6% COLA adds ~$46/month per person — modest but compounds. Need additional inflation-fighting asset classes in the portfolio.

### Bucket strategy + four economic seasons [00:06:42]

Three-bucket: conservative, moderate, growth. Growth bucket historically outpaces inflation. Four-season framework: rising/slowing × inflation/growth — hold assets that perform across all combinations.

### Risk 3: bad markets [00:07:33]

Social Security is unaffected by market drops; pensions and some annuities similarly insulated. Conservative bucket smooths short-term shocks.

### March 2020 client anecdote [00:08:28]

Recently-retired client called during the ~30% Covid drawdown. Her conservative bucket was up ~3% YTD — visible green numbers lifted her financial fear instantly. Argument for advisors who actually plan for downturns.
