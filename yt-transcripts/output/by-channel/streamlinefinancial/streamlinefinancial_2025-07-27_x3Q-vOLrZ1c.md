# This Tax "Bunching" Tip Could Save You Thousands

**Channel:** Streamline Financial
**Published:** 2025-07-27
**URL:** https://youtube.com/watch?v=x3Q-vOLrZ1c
**Duration:** 3:28

## Summary

Dave Zoller explains how the new SALT cap increase (from $10K to $40K under the 2025 tax bill) combined with a 'bunching' strategy can save thousands. Bunching = alternating years between itemizing and standard deduction. Examples: prepay next year's property tax to itemize in the current year, or pre-fund 2-3 years of charitable giving via a donor-advised fund to take a single large deduction in one year while still satisfying the charity over time.

## Key Takeaways

- SALT cap increased from $10K to $40K — major benefit in high-tax states.
- If no state income tax, you can use sales tax (actual or IRS estimator).
- Bunching strategy: alternate years between standard deduction and a loaded-up itemized deduction year.
- Prepay next year's property tax in the current year (check with county) to frontload deductions.
- Donor-advised funds let you pre-fund 2-3 years of charitable giving for one large deduction now, while the charity still receives over time.
- Especially impactful for retirees in high-tax states — can save thousands per year.

## Detailed Notes

### SALT cap increase [00:00:00]

New bill raises SALT cap from $10K to $40K. Example: Illinois resident with $5K state income tax and $9K property tax used to only deduct $10K — now deducts the full $14K. If no state income tax, use sales tax (actual or IRS estimator).

### The bunching strategy [00:01:01]

Alternate years between standard and loaded-up itemized deductions. Frontload deductions into one year, take the standard the next.

### Prepaying property tax [00:01:01]

Check with county whether you can prepay next year's property tax. Combine two years of payments into one to maximize a single-year itemized deduction.

### Donor-advised fund example [00:01:59]

If you normally give $10K/yr to charity, pre-fund 3 years ($30K) into a donor-advised fund this year. Take the full $30K deduction now; the DAF grants out to charities over the following years. Nothing changes for the charity, but you maximize the deduction.

### Bring it to your CPA [00:01:59]

Talk to your CPA or advisor before year-end. In high-tax states, this can save thousands annually.
