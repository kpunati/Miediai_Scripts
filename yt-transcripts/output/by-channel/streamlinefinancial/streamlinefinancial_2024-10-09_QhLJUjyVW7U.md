# If you're in your 50s or 60s, watch this. Life Lessons from 70-year-olds

**Channel:** Streamline Financial
**Published:** 2024-10-09
**URL:** https://youtube.com/watch?v=QhLJUjyVW7U
**Duration:** 21:13

## Summary

Dave Zoller compiles 10 pieces of life advice from 70-year-old clients to people in their 50s: don't miss the magical equal-sided wealth/health/time triangle window; prioritize three types of time (career, relationships, recovery); learn to say no; time is more valuable than money; invest in fitness and mental health (one 69-year-old client looked a decade younger after restarting yoga and meditation); avoid the status-symbol trap (Porsche client wished he had.

## Key Takeaways

- The 50s-60s equal-sided wealth/health/time triangle is short — use it before health starts to slip.
- Three types of time matter: career, relationships, and recovery/renewal — the third is most often ignored.
- Learn to say no in the second half of life; it's how you protect recovery time.
- Time is more valuable than money — money makes money, but money doesn't make time.
- Health habits in the 50s can materially extend healthy years (the 69-year-old yoga+meditation client added a decade).
- Buy back your time by outsourcing tasks you dislike — it provides for others too.
- Status symbols (cars, watches) rarely deliver lasting joy — try renting the experience first.
- Track expenses now (Monarch Money or similar) — expenses often go UP early in retirement, not down.
- Nurture old friendships; deep relationships predict late-life satisfaction.
- Plan retirement of purpose, not just leisure — running TO something matters more than running FROM stress.
