# Navigating Business Exits and Leadership with Travis Hall

**Channel:** Streamline Financial
**Published:** 2025-02-06
**URL:** https://youtube.com/watch?v=OJ_m3cjwhZ0
**Duration:** 69:19

## Summary

Tim Meisenheimer interviews Travis Hall (serial entrepreneur, 11 startups, exited Remodel Health) on the day his sale closes. Covers the emotional reality of exit (identity, grief, money mattering less than expected), why founders need a next thing before selling, transparency with employees, sharing proceeds with non-cap-table staff, Friday Fails as culture practice, and pre-funding a donor-advised fund before the transaction closes.

## Key Takeaways

- Founder identity is wrapped up in the company — you have to let that go at exit.
- Define what you're moving TOWARD before the deal closes — emotions can derail otherwise.
- Be transparent with staff during the sale process — they're professionals; they can handle real news.
- Sharing exit proceeds with non-cap-table employees materially changes lives (down payments, replacement cars).
- Friday Fails: weekly ritual to surface what didn't work — reduces hiding, accelerates camaraderie, models that trying matters.
- Use one-on-ones to ask Bob Goff's question: 'What's it like to feel like {their name} today?' — it bypasses the standard 'fine' answer.
- Set up a donor-advised fund BEFORE the transaction; pre-decide allocation by need, not by reflex tax response.
- Travis's must-haves / should-haves / nice-to-haves framework keeps deal evaluation non-emotional.

## Detailed Notes

### Opening: mental pain is real; the day of the sale [00:00:00]

Travis on the day his exit from Remodel Health closes. Mental pain is as real as physical pain — Travis credits asking about feelings as the key to getting better work from people.

### The raw day: identity, money, and 'now what?' [00:03:08]

Three things founders don't realize at exit: (1) you've created something and have to let it go; (2) financial freedom doesn't fix Monday morning's 'what now?'; (3) zeros and commas matter less than expected — but enabling giving matters a lot.

### Why you need a 'next thing' before exit [00:07:21]

Travis coaches founders that you have to have a destination before exit — without it, deal emotions kill the transaction. His next was Data Elution (data analytics for founder-led organizations).

### Personal sustainability: business coach, life coach, counselor [00:13:43]

Travis is open about having a business coach, life coach, and counselor — they speak truth into his life because his staff have a conflict of interest.

### Remodel Health exit communication: transparency with team [00:19:54]

Unconventional choice: kept the team in the loop through the sale process (not play-by-play but where in the process). Past failed deals proved the team can take hard news and lock arms.

### Sharing proceeds with non-cap-table staff [00:23:00]

Significant portion of staff (even short tenure) was included financially. Real impacts: down payments, replacing aging cars, lifestyle improvements — and money cascading into local nonprofits.

### Friday Fails as a culture practice [00:30:23]

Weekly ritual where everyone shares what they failed at this week. Two benefits: nothing stays hidden (staff aren't afraid of consequences); team rallies to help each other. Encourages experimentation.

### Bob Goff's question and listening with empathy [00:33:35]

From Bob Goff: 'What's it like to feel like {name} today?' Used in one-on-ones — bypasses the standard 'fine.' Travis says feelings are real and naming them defuses anxiety.

### Tactical employee appreciation: birthday cakes, candy, handwritten notes [00:36:45]

Get to know each staff member's favorite candy, salty snack, and cake. Surprise rotation. A handwritten note that takes 30 seconds creates outsized appreciation. Each person has their own 'work love language.'

### Exit emotions: every exit is hard [00:42:02]

Even Travis's multiple exits don't get easier — the day-to-day team relationship is hard to lose. Likens it to The Prestige's teleport-by-dying.

### DAF set up before the deal; must-have/should-have/nice-to-have [00:48:17]

Family set up donor-advised fund and pre-decided allocations before close. Travis runs deals using must-have/should-have/nice-to-have criteria for non-emotional evaluation. Cites stat that a major new-car purchase often follows large transactions within ~13-17 days.

### Why give first: faith and joy of relational giving [00:51:26]

As a Christian, giving back a tenth is core. Don't just write a check blindly — go in person and find out what nonprofits actually need. Family travels and hosts international guests to keep proximate to need.

### Hosting internationally; reframing complaints [00:56:40]

Hosts international guests in their basement; kids ask great questions about life elsewhere. Quote from Bryan Stevenson via Tim: 'the opposite of poverty is not wealth — it's justice; proximity teaches.'

### Closing: loving DAFs and family dart night [01:03:57]

Travis's one thing: DAFs (don't have to decide where everything goes at once). Tim: family dartboard playing 301/Cricket.
