# Leadership, Transitions and Legacy Impact w/ Bill Holbrook

**Channel:** Streamline Financial
**Published:** 2024-06-07

**Duration:** 50:30

## Summary

Long-form Wealth Beyond Riches podcast interview with Bill Holbrook, executive coach to Streamline Financial. Covers Bill's path from youth pastor to executive coach, how he uses StrengthsFinder/Myers-Briggs/Enneagram as dynamic assessments rather than boxes, identity loss in retirement (the 'RW word'), the importance of leaving a leadership legacy via succession planning (2-4 year timeline), defining 'enough' to resist scaling, and modeling rhythms of rest (daily/weekly/sabbatical vacations).

## Key Takeaways

- Wealth has a time dimension, not just money — defining 'enough' lets you resist scale-for-its-own-sake.
- Personality assessments (StrengthsFinder, Myers-Briggs, Enneagram) are most useful when treated dynamically — language for patterns, not boxes.
- Retirement triggers an identity question: 'what am I worth if I can't produce?' — work through the 'move-to-what' and 'what's my succession' questions before the date.
- Succession plans take 2-4 years — identify, train, delegate, debrief, then sideline.
- Communication is information received: the sender owns landing the message on good soil, not just transmitting.
- Vacations come in three rhythms: daily (an hour of journaling/exercise), weekly (Sabbath day, phone-down windows), and longer breaks — living vacation-to-vacation rarely satisfies.
- Great leaders take time off — proves the organization can function without them and builds resilience.

## Detailed Notes

### Setup: Bill Holbrook joins as exec coach [00:00:00]

Tim introduces Bill, his executive coach since 2019/2020. Frames the show around reclaiming relationships with work and money. Bill came in during Streamline's buyout of the founder and has remained as a part-time coach to the team.

### From youth pastor to executive coach [00:07:20]

Bill has a theology + psychology degree, spent a decade in youth ministry, found his real love was 1:1 staff development. A 'is this eating you up?' whisper at a StrengthsFinder workshop pushed him into coaching — used a 0% credit card to fund Gallup certification, paid off in 6 months.

### Using assessments dynamically [00:11:35]

StrengthsFinder = operating system (how you process info); Myers-Briggs = hands (how you intersect with the world); Enneagram = core motivation/heart. Used as common language for patterns, not as static labels. People can then choose whether to keep or change a pattern.

### Identity, retirement, and the 'RW' word [00:18:51]

When clients hear 'retirement,' an identity panic surfaces: what am I worth if I can't produce? Two main conversations: (1) what are you moving on to that's meaningful, (2) is your succession plan robust enough that the company can run without you.

### Succession plan timelines [00:21:59]

Minimum 2 years, max 3-4 for a real transition: identify, find, train, delegate task, delegate authority, debrief feedback loop, then sit in the background and cheer. Ken Blanchard's leadership-square: I do/you watch → I do/you help → you do/I help → you do/I cheer.

### Generosity: time over money [00:23:01]

Bill keeps 1-2 unpaid coaching clients at all times (usually small nonprofits with community focus). Argues money is now easier to give than time — auto-debit charity is simpler than blocking four hours for a workshop.

### Defining 'enough' and resisting scale [00:33:27]

When a major bank offered to bring Bill on nationally, his Enneagram-3 achievement drive said yes. He chose time wealth instead — he has young kids, max travel would steal years. Cites Tim Ferriss' framing: who's wealthier, $1M/80hrs or $150K/30hrs?

### Why no formal retirement [00:36:36]

Bill, 42, doesn't expect to 'retire.' If lifestyle, spending, and generosity stay in order, he can keep coaching for decades — what would he retire from? Impact is his barometer.

### Sabbath rhythms: daily, weekly, longer [00:42:47]

Living vacation-to-vacation never satisfies. Practice daily/weekly micro-vacations: 1hr/day exercise or journaling, phone down Friday 4pm to Saturday 4pm, gym = decompression. Tim shares his UFC-gym routine (3x/week) and Sunday family-day commitment.

### Outro and contact [00:48:59]

Bill's links: billdholbrook.com and sequel retreat organization (seq.org). Pickleball anecdote about Tim's culdesac roller-hockey weekend. Show closes with the standard share/review CTA.
