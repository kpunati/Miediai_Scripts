# Most Retirees Don’t Realize This - Fritz Gilbert

**Channel:** Streamline Financial
**Published:** 2025-05-11
**URL:** https://youtube.com/watch?v=tumvlIIDB44
**Duration:** 38:46

## Summary

Dave Zoller interviews Fritz Gilbert (The Retirement Manifesto, author of Keys to a Successful Retirement) seven years into retirement. Covers the shock of unstructured days, the 9/10 rule (planning is 90% financial; retirement flips to 90% non-financial), the bucket strategy (3 years cash, 6 years bonds in a ladder, 70% growth), the 'force-yourself-to-spend' year-end rule, and 28% retiree depression vs 72% happiness anchored on 3.6 close friends.

## Key Takeaways

- Biggest surprise in retirement: the loss of structure. Add structured rituals (morning gym, writing block) early on.
- 9/10 rule: 90% of pre-retirement focus is financial; first year of retirement flips to 90% non-financial.
- Happy retirees average 3.6 close friends; unhappy retirees 2.6 — be intentional about relationships pre-retirement.
- Bucket strategy at 3.3% withdrawal rate: ~10% cash (3 years), ~20% bonds via ladder, 70% growth.
- After 2020 (when stocks and bonds dropped together), switched from bond funds to a bond ladder for 6-month rungs.
- Force-yourself-to-spend rule: at year-end, any unspent checking balance must be given away.
- Average retiree following the 4% rule ends with ~double their starting balance after 20 years.
- Younger Next Year: 45 min/day of exercise extends health span — 70s and even 80s can look like 30s/40s.
- Set a date for retirement; don't let 'one more year' creep.

## Detailed Notes

### Retirement 2.0: scaling back the blog [00:00:00]

Seven years retired (since 2018). Now scaling back The Retirement Manifesto blog after a decade of weekly posts. Wants to spend more time outside.

### Biggest surprise: the loss of structure [00:02:34]

You lose five things at retirement beyond paycheck: identity, purpose, relationships, structure, sense of accomplishment. Structure was the surprise — totally unstructured days are disorienting. Added gym classes and morning writing block.

### Relationships: 28% depressed vs 72% happy + 3.6 close friends [00:07:19]

Per Wes Moss research: happy retirees have 3.6 close friends on average; unhappy 2.6. Build relationships outside work BEFORE retiring. Mastermind groups, volunteer organizations (Freedom for Fido), and intentional lunches.

### The 9/10 rule: focus flips from money to life [00:12:33]

90% of pre-retirement planning is financial. First year in, focus flips to 90% non-financial. Once your spending rhythm is set, money becomes a non-issue. Even Fritz the spreadsheet nerd now only updates net worth once a year.

### Bucket strategy at 3.3% withdrawal rate [00:16:51]

30 years saved = ~3.3% withdrawal rate. Bucket 1: 3 years cash (10%). Bucket 2: 6 years bonds (~20%) via ladder maturing every 6 months (post-2020 switch). Bucket 3: rest in growth — stocks, REITs, commodities, precious metals (70%).

### Why retirees end with more: the math of the safe withdrawal rate [00:20:02]

Bergen/Trinity research built the safe withdrawal rate around the WORST historical market environment. Anything better produces a surplus. Average retiree ends with ~2x starting balance after 20 years.

### Force-yourself-to-spend rule [00:21:47]

End-of-year rule: any growth in checking balance must be given away. Forces spending or giving — choose the kitchen remodel or release the funds, but don't accumulate.

### Health span: Younger Next Year — 45 min/day [00:23:33]

Cites Younger Next Year (doctor + client). 45 min/day of exercise (cardio + weights) extends health span. 70s and even early 80s can still do what 30s/40s could — skiing black diamonds at 72.

### One-more-year and how to break it [00:25:32]

If the math works and you're still dragging your feet, it's not financial — it's change-resistance. Set a hard date (e.g. April 1, 2026). Have the cash bucket pre-built (don't sell stocks in the last year — start a money-market push instead).

### Planning for no supplemental income [00:29:16]

Fritz planned for $0 supplemental income. Started blog 3 years before retirement just for love; monetized at year 2 of retirement when expenses crept up. Healthcare paid for since 55.

### 10 Commandments of Retirement: mindset over plan [00:32:29]

His pre-retirement post: 10 Commandments of Retirement. All mindset (pursue curiosity, find ways to help others, gratitude). Tactics (woodworking, charity work) emerged unexpectedly from the mindset — retirement is exactly the mindset he expected, but nothing like the tactics he predicted.

### Biggest advice: plan the non-financial side [00:34:35]

Single most predictive factor for successful retirees: how much time spent planning BEFORE retirement, especially on the non-financial side. Take a week, get away, talk through what you want retirement to be.
