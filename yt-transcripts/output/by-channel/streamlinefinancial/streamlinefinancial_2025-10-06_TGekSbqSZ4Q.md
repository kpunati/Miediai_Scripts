# When Retirement Can Hurt Your Marriage

**Channel:** Streamline Financial
**Published:** 2025-10-06
**URL:** https://youtube.com/watch?v=TGekSbqSZ4Q
**Duration:** 6:27

## Summary

Dave Zoller warns that divorce rates over 50 have doubled since the 1990s — and tripled for those 65+ — even as divorce among younger adults has declined. Three retirement relationship traps: (1) the business owner taking management style into the household (Paul example); (2) being together 24/7 destroys individual identity (Ron Blue: 'I married you for better or for worse, but not for lunch'); (3) loss of professional identity drives one spouse to take over the.

## Key Takeaways

- Gray divorce is rising: doubled since the '90s for 50+, tripled for 65+.
- Paul's story: 30 years as a business owner — brought his management style home and started 'optimizing' his wife's household.
- Wife isn't an employee; she's a companion — retirement requires partnership, not CEO mode.
- 24/7 together can damage marriages — Ron Blue quote: 'I married you for better or for worse, but not for lunch.'
- Some separation makes time together more meaningful — keep separate hobbies and friendships.
- Loss of professional identity often makes one spouse try to take over the other's domain.
- Viewer's framework: hobbies + helping — explore curiosities and find ways to use your skills to serve.

## Detailed Notes

### The gray divorce statistic [00:00:00]

Divorce rates for over-50 have doubled since the '90s; tripled for 65+. Meanwhile, younger-adult divorce has declined. Retirement reveals marriage challenges previous generations didn't face.

### Paul — the business-owner trap [00:01:02]

Paul sold his successful 30-year business and came home to his retired wife's established household rhythm. He started 'optimizing' her domain — suggesting improvements, questioning systems. She didn't need a CEO; she needed a companion.

### The 24/7 problem [00:02:17]

Retirement requires partnership, patience, and letting go of control. Decades of natural separation (separate work, colleagues, challenges) suddenly disappear. Ron Blue's wife: 'I married you for better or for worse, but not for lunch.'

### Some separation strengthens [00:03:25]

Couples who think they need to do everything together struggle. Thriving couples maintain individual hobbies, friendships, interests — then come back with something to talk about.

### Loss of identity drives domain takeover [00:03:25]

When one spouse loses professional identity overnight, they often try to refind it by taking over the other's territory. Solution: build a NEW purpose that's uniquely yours.

### Hobbies and helping [00:04:12]

Subscriber's framework before retiring: figure out your HOBBIES (curiosities, things you've always wanted to learn) and your HELPING (causes that break your heart, places to serve). Together, these form a new identity that doesn't trample your spouse's world.
