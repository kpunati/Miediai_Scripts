# The Journey of Reinvention: Mikey Taylor's Path from Pro Skateboarding to Real Estate

**Channel:** Streamline Financial
**Published:** 2024-07-04
**URL:** https://youtube.com/watch?v=7gvY7jZZ6KA
**Duration:** 54:23

## Summary

Wealth Beyond Riches interview with Mikey Taylor — former pro skateboarder, Saint Archer co-founder (sold to MillerCoors), now real-estate fund manager at Commune Capital. Covers his identity collapse after losing his skate sponsor, year-long Bible reading reset, what financial freedom means, building parallel brand equity, real-estate macro (multifamily, storage, hospitality), and his next chapter on marriage retreats.

## Key Takeaways

- Identity built on a career is fragile — Mikey's post-skate collapse pushed him to ground identity in something larger (year-long Bible read) rather than the next business.
- Build parallel brand equity during your primary career — Mikey's brewery (Saint Archer) was already growing while he was still pro.
- Financial freedom = passive income covering lifestyle, not the absence of work; Mikey expects to work for life.
- Tithing is best built as a discipline before the windfall — giving when it's hard rewires the habit for when it's harder.
- Real-estate cycles favor disciplined long-term holders; timing the market requires being right twice (out and back in).
- Multifamily and storage are softening into buying opportunity; office still hasn't fully corrected; hospitality may snap back vs Airbnb.
- Generational marriage example matters more to children than the perfect parent — show the conflict resolution, not just the wins.

## Detailed Notes

### Setup: meeting Mikey [00:00:00]

Tim introduces Mikey: former pro skater, 2M+ social followers, real-estate fund founder, councilman, father of four. Quick story of meeting at an event after a mutual friend introduced Tim to Mikey's content.

### Money as a young pro skater [00:04:12]

Started making $50K/yr at 19 — had to learn ego management. By 25-26, had a more disciplined investing system. Saw skate income as time-bounded, so prioritized investments to bridge post-career years.

### Reaching financial freedom at 34 [00:05:14]

Saint Archer beer business (founded 2011-2012) sold to MillerCoors gave the windfall. Financial freedom achieved at 34 — but identity collapse followed when sponsors dropped him.

### Identity reset via Bible reading [00:08:23]

Initially tried to start a shoe company in spite — pulled back at the last minute. Spent 45min/day for a year reading the Bible. Result: identity grounded in calling and giftedness, not in any specific business — businesses are now expressions of that, not the source.

### Lessons for daughters and son [00:13:37]

Wants daughters to see what a man looks like in marriage, in conflict, in failure. Son: same lessons via example. Aims to model failure recovery, not perfection.

### Saint Archer arc and the shoe-company exit lesson [00:15:44]

Built personal brand as a skater in the last quarter of the career — discovered personal IP could sell more product than skill alone. Saint Archer sold to MillerCoors. The shoe-company-revenge plan was a pain-response; backing out was wisdom.

### Handling negative criticism online [00:19:55]

Early Spain anecdote about message boards taught Mikey to defensively skate-for-likes, which nearly tanked his career. Marriage and kids re-anchored identity. Now: hate is part of the engagement curve — if no one hates you, no one truly loves you either.

### Defining financial freedom [00:25:08]

Strict definition: passive income covers lifestyle so work becomes optional. But Mikey expects to work his whole life — he wanted the option, not the off-ramp.

### Commune Capital today [00:27:12]

Private-equity real-estate firm focused on multifamily and storage. Raises capital, develops, leases up, distributes income. Mikey also serves on city council (~20-25 hrs/month).

### Tithing discipline and the windfall year [00:34:33]

Started tithing 10% out of obligation/calling, didn't see fruits until the Saint Archer sale year — letters from sponsored kids and ministries launched with his giving made the loop visible. Harder to give when scraping by; surprisingly hard to give from a windfall too.

### Real-estate macro outlook [00:39:48]

Office hasn't corrected enough yet. Multifamily cap rates softened 0.5-0.75% but rent growth offset some value loss; capital is harder to find than deals — buyer's market. Storage finally softening (saturated → opportunity). Hospitality snapback as Airbnb pricing converges with hotels. Mobile-home/affordable housing = opportunity. Commune buys through cycles — timing requires being right twice.

### Dividend feedback loop from physical assets [00:43:59]

Mikey describes the visceral motivation of getting a cash-flow check from a building he can drive past — felt more valuable than dividend from a stock even at equal numbers. Drove the discipline to keep allocating more to real estate.

### Next chapter: marriage retreats [00:46:05]

Sees the next venture as around marriage — coaching/retreats for type-A entrepreneurs and athletes. Triggered by repeated requests from struggling friends; feels like a confirmed direction.

### Outro [00:50:16]

Listening-music for skate parts (Dinosaur Jr, The National, The Doves, Jay-Z compilation), pickleball anecdote, and standard sign-off.
